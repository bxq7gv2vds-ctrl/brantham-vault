## CRITICAL SUCCESS FACTORS (CSF) — The "Why"

### CSF #1: Founder-Led Negotiation
**Why:** Only founder can make non-financial decisions (autonomy, role, culture fit)
**How:** Founder = lead on all buyer calls; CFO/lawyer = support, not lead
**Risk if ignored:** Deal doesn't match founder's vision; founder leaves M3; earnout at risk

### CSF #2: Detailed Data Room (Pre-Prepared)
**Why:** 60% of due diligence time is "finding documents," not "reading them"
**How:** Organize by function; use standardized folder structure; curate top 200 docs
**Risk if ignored:** DD delays 4-6 weeks; buyer loses confidence; deal dies

### CSF #3: Locked Earnout Formula (Down to 2 Decimals)
**Why:** Vague KPIs = post-close litigation; specific KPIs = automatic payment
**How:** Write formula in Excel (auditable); define revenue/NRR with 100% clarity
**Risk if ignored:** €1-3M escrow battle over "what counts as revenue"

### CSF #4: Customer Consent Letters Pre-Close
**Why:** Late consents = delayed close = earnout clock ticking anyway
**How:** Contact top 20 customers by D30; have 80% consent by D90
**Risk if ignored:** Close delays 8-12 weeks; earnout window shifts; seasonality changes target

### CSF #5: Key Person Retention Agreements
**Why:** CTO/VP Sales seeing new reporting lines = flight risk
**How:** Lock retention bonuses, autonomy, and reporting line in SPA
**Risk if ignored:** Key person leaves M2-M4; team exodus; revenue declines; earnout missed

### CSF #6: Transparent Pipeline Disclosure
**Why:** Buyer due diligence will call prospects; hidden deals = credibility loss
**How:** Tier pipeline (Likely/Probable/Exploratory); buyer calls 3-5 refs; transparency wins
**Risk if ignored:** Escrow clawback if buyer finds deals were fake or cold

### CSF #7: Tech DD Scorecard (Keep Separate vs Merge)
**Why:** Integration risk can kill value post-close
**How:** Fill scorecard pre-LOI; decide: portfolio approach vs consolidation
**Risk if ignored:** Post-close tech chaos; team fights; product quality drops; retention falls

### CSF #8: IP Assignments (Founder → Company)
**Why:** If founder developed code without assignment, buyer owns €0 IP
**How:** Get signed IP assignment agreement before LOI
**Risk if ignored:** 20% escrow holdback; post-close litigation risk; deal may rescind

## Checklist: Before You Start (Pre-Day 0)

- [ ] **Financial Prep (4-6 weeks before outreach)**
  - [ ] 3 years audited financials ready (clean Excel files)
  - [ ] Cap table organized (all shares, options, vesting)
  - [ ] Tax returns clean (no open audits)
  - [ ] Data room folder structure created

- [ ] **Legal Prep (4-6 weeks before outreach)**
  - [ ] IP assignment agreement (founder → company) signed
  - [ ] All employee agreements signed
  - [ ] Key customer contracts organized
  - [ ] NDA template ready (use vault template, save 2 weeks)

- [ ] **Team Prep (2-4 weeks before outreach)**
  - [ ] Founder alignment on: Price expectations, earnout terms, post-close role
  - [ ] CFO + Lawyer identified (advisors)
  - [ ] CTO ready for technical due diligence
  - [ ] VP Sales ready for customer references

- [ ] **Product Prep (Anytime)**
  - [ ] Tech debt assessed (know your liabilities)
  - [ ] Customer concentration analyzed (know your risk)
  - [ ] Product roadmap documented (will be buyer's question)
  - [ ] Architecture diagram prepared (for tech DD)

## Red Flags During Process — Abort Signals

🛑 **Stop and Reassess If:**

- Buyer goes silent >5 days (they lost interest)
- DD findings reveal unknown liabilities >10% of deal value (renegotiate price)
- Key customer refuses to consent (confirm if deal-breaker)
- Buyer's CFO contradicts CFO on earnout formula (get it in writing)
- Key engineer starts interviewing elsewhere (retention failing)
- Buyer's board doesn't approve mandate (deal unlikely)

## Post-Close Success = Earnout Success

**Earnout is Real Money (50-40% of total deal).**

Your job doesn't end at closing—it intensifies.

| Month | Earnout Tracking | Your Job |
|-------|---|---|
| M0-M3 | Baseline (no targets yet) | Execute flawlessly; build credibility |
| M4-M9 | Quarterly reviews (tracking to target) | Hit every milestone; over-deliver |
| M9-M12 | Monthly tracking (final push) | Lock down final quarter revenue; call all close deals |
| M12 | **MEASUREMENT DAY** | Final revenue certified; earnout paid (if KPIs met) |

**To Hit Earnout:**
- Stay focused (no distraction by integration politics)
- Over-communicate with Buyer (monthly reviews)
- Call every customer at M10 (make sure renewals close in time)
- Lock down new deals early (don't wait until M11)

**If You Miss Earnout:**
- €1-2M at risk
- Team morale collapses (they sacrificed for this)
- Founder regrets deal (even if upfront was good)

**If You Hit/Exceed Earnout:**
- Clear conscience (you earned it)
- Buyer trusts you for next integration phase
- Team stays (success breeds retention)

## The Golden Rule

> **"Everything ambiguous = will be fought in escrow."**
> **Write it down now in SPA, or pay lawyers later to fight it out.**

**Examples of "ambiguous":**
- "Revenue targets" (unclear if new or retention)
- "Successful integration" (who judges success?)
- "Customer retention ≥95%" (95% of what baseline?)
- "Key person stays" (what if they get promoted, or change roles?)

**Fix it pre-close:** 2 hours of negotiation = saves €1M in post-close disputes

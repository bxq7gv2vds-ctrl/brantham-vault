# Acquisition Criteria — 1-Pager

## Profil cible

| Critère | Min | Idéal | Max | Poids |
|---------|-----|-------|-----|-------|
| **Revenue** | €500k | €2-5M | €20M | 25% |
| **EBITDA margin** | 15% | 25-35% | 50%+ | 25% |
| **YoY Growth** | 5% | 15-30% | 100%+ | 20% |
| **Profitability** | Break-even | Profitable | N/A | 15% |
| **Customer concentration** | <40% top 3 | <20% top 3 | <5% | 10% |
| **Debt/EBITDA** | <3x | <1.5x | <0.5x | 5% |

## Secteurs prioritaires (shortlist)

1. **SaaS/Software** (recurring revenue, scalability)
2. **Managed Services** (recurring, margin, team)
3. **E-commerce/Retail** (assets, customer base)
4. **Niche B2B Services** (recurring, margin, consolidation opps)
5. **Digital Assets** (low capex, IP-driven)

## Géographies

- **Primaire** : France, UK, Germany, Benelux
- **Secondaire** : Southern Europe, Nordic
- **Tertiaire** : US (si very strong fit)

## Deal-breakers (automatique non)

- Declining revenue >2 years
- Legal/regulatory red flags
- Key person dependencies (>40% value)
- Overleveraged (debt >3x EBITDA)
- Loss-making 3+ years
- Concentrated customer base (>50% from 1 client)

## Nice-to-haves

- Recurring revenue model
- Experienced management team
- Proven growth trajectory
- Strong customer retention (>90%)
- Proprietary IP/tech
- International presence

## Exclusions

- Highly cyclical businesses
- Regulated industries (banking, pharma) without specific expertise
- Declining market segments
- Turnarounds requiring >2 years
- Negative working capital models

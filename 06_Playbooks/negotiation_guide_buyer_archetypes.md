# Guide de Négociation — 5 Archetypes d'Acquéreurs

**Usage:** Ajuster tactiques de negotiation selon profil du buyer. Chaque archetype a lever points différents + pressure points.

---

## Archetype 1: STRATEGIC BUYER (Gros acquéreur consolidant le marché)

**Exemples:** Salesforce, HubSpot, Shopify, Twilio

### Profile
- **Cash position:** Very High ($2B+)
- **M&A pace:** 10-20 deals/year (bulk buyer)
- **Decision speed:** Slow (4+ layers approval)
- **Price sensitivity:** Low (will pay for fit)
- **Post-close priority:** Integration speed & revenue retention

### Lever Points (Positifs)
✅ Large cash reserves → can close quickly
✅ Strategic fit = highest valuation
✅ Established playbook (integration = smooth)
✅ Long-term hold (won't flip you in 3y)

### Pressure Points (Exploitables)
🔴 Board approval slow (add 20-30 days to close)
🔴 Integration teams stretched (reps quality may be lower)
🔴 Will use "strategic fit" against you ("We'll pay less because fit is high")
🔴 May sunset your product (consolidate feature into theirs)

### Negotiation Tactics

**1. Value Prop = Strategic Complementarity**
```
NOT: "We have 100 customers, $2M ARR"
YES: "We own [verticals/geos/use-case] your product doesn't. 
      Acquisition = unlock $50M market segment for you in 18mo"
```

**2. Anchor on "All-In Price" (Not Revenue Multiple)**
- Buyer wants low multiple ("We'll pay 5x, standard for SaaS")
- Counter: "All-in integration value = $20M over 3y post-close"
- Forces them to see beyond P&L

**3. Lock in "No Sunset" Clause Early (LOI)**
```
In LOI, add:
  "Seller's product will be maintained as distinct offering for ≥3 years 
   post-close, or acquirer pays [2x earnout] as penalty"
  
Why? Prevents buyer from saying "Actually, we're killing this in M12"
```

**4. Negotiate Earnout Heavily**
- Strategic buyer = long hold = predictable revenue
- Push earnout to **18-24 months** (not 12mo)
- Tie to **gross revenue**, not EBITDA (buyer can't cheat margins)

```
Example:
  Upfront: $10M
  Earnout Y1: Revenue >$2.5M = $5M
  Earnout Y2: Revenue >$3M & NRR >100% = $5M
  Total @ plan: $20M (vs. $10M if no earnout)
```

**5. Demand Founder Role in Roadmap**
- Strategic buyer = will slot you on their roadmap
- Negotiate: Founder = seat on product council (3y minimum)
- Prevents you from becoming "just a feature"

### Deal Structure (Typical)
```
Upfront cash:     50-60%
Earnout (18-24mo): 30-40%
Equity (if wanted): 0-10%
Retention bonus:  $500K-$2M (key execs)

Reps & Warranties Insurance: Buyer usually pays
Escrow: 10% × 12mo
```

## Archetype 2: FINANCIAL BUYER (PE Firm, Growth Equity, Acquihire)

**Exemples:** Insight, Thoma Bravo, Menlo Ventures, Foundry

### Profile
- **Cash position:** Medium-High ($500M+ funds)
- **M&A pace:** 3-8 deals/year (selective)
- **Decision speed:** Fast (30-45 days if aligned)
- **Price sensitivity:** HIGH (will negotiate hard)
- **Post-close priority:** Revenue growth & quick exit (3-7y hold)

### Lever Points (Positifs)
✅ Fast decision if metrics aligned
✅ Will use financial leverage (3-5x LBO) = higher valuation
✅ Portfolio approach (won't consolidate you immediately)
✅ Growth-focused (not cost-cutting acquisition)

### Pressure Points (Exploitables)
🔴 Price = 5-7x EBITDA (lower than strategic)
🔴 Will load debt on company (+ 2-3% interest annually)
🔴 Demanding unit economics (KPIs monthly review)
🔴 "Flipping" mentality (might sell you on in 5y for $100M)

### Negotiation Tactics

**1. Lead with Unit Economics, Not Revenue**
```
NOT: "We have $5M ARR"
YES: "LTV:CAC = 4:1 (industry avg = 3:1). Gross margin = 78% 
      (scalable SaaS). Path to $20M ARR = 45% YoY growth achievable 
      with $2M marketing spend (we've modeled it)"
```

**2. Build Detailed 3-Year Pro Forma**
- PE buyers are **ADDICTED to models**
- Provide: Monthly revenue forecast + EBITDA bridge + sensitivity
- Format: Excel with clear assumptions (not Word doc)

```
Example:
  Y1: $5M → $7.5M (50% growth, industry norm)
  Y2: $7.5M → $12M (60% growth, improved ops)
  Y3: $12M → $18M (50% growth, market saturation)
  
  EBITDA margins: Y1=20%, Y2=35%, Y3=45%
  ⇒ EBITDA Y3 = $8.1M
  ⇒ Exit valuation (6x EBITDA) = $48.6M
```

**3. Negotiate on "Multiple at Exit"**
- PE firm want to buy at 5x, sell at 6-7x (arbitrage)
- Counter: "We'll tie earnout to exit valuation, not annual revenue"

```
Structure:
  Upfront: $15M (3x current EBITDA)
  Earnout: Based on exit price in Y5
    - If exit ≥ $60M: You get $5M bonus
    - If exit ≥ $80M: You get $8M bonus
  
  Why? You're incentivized to help them *grow before exit*
  (Not just hit boring revenue targets)
```

**4. Push for Management Retention, Not Replacement**
- PE often wants to bring in "professional CEO"
- Negotiate: "Founder stays as Chief Product Officer, new CEO brought in but reports to Founder" (hybrid model)
- Prevents complete loss of control

**5. Lock in "No Fire Sale" Clause**
```
"If PE firm needs to sell company within 3 years at < $40M 
 (due to poor performance), Founder + team get last-look 
 rights at pro-rata ownership"
 
Why? Prevents PE from panic-selling cheap.
```

### Deal Structure (Typical)
```
Upfront cash:     40-50%
Earnout (3-5y):   20-30% (tied to exit valuation)
Founder equity:   5-15% (rollover into new capital structure)
Management team:  1-2% (key hires get equity)

Reps & Warranties Insurance: Founder usually pays (2% of price)
Escrow: 15% × 18mo (PE is cautious)
Debt load: 2-3x EBITDA (added by PE)
```

## Archetype 3: COMPETITOR / CONSOLIDATOR (Smaller strategic player)

**Exemples:** Mid-market SaaS consolidators, private companies, industry players

### Profile
- **Cash position:** Low-Medium ($50-500M)
- **M&A pace:** 1-3 deals/year (opportunistic)
- **Decision speed:** Medium (45-90 days)
- **Price sensitivity:** VERY HIGH (will compete on price)
- **Post-close priority:** Cost synergies + sales rep reuse

### Lever Points (Positifs)
✅ Genuine product/customer fit (understands your space)
✅ Synergies are clear & achievable (cost cuts, cross-sell)
✅ May keep your brand/product separate (portfolio value)
✅ Can close fast if board-approved

### Pressure Points (Exploitables)
🔴 Will negotiate hard on price ("We could build this ourselves")
🔴 May lay off your team (cost synergies = redundancies)
🔴 Integration risk HIGH (will try to force quick consolidation)
🔴 Post-close often regrets (product conflicts, culture clash)

### Negotiation Tactics

**1. Emphasize "Build vs Buy" Economic Advantage**
```
"To replicate our product + customer base:
  - Engineering cost: $3M/year × 2y = $6M
  - Opportunity cost (lost sales): $5M
  - Time to market: 24+ months
  
  We save you $11M + 2 years. Fair price = $18M all-in"
```

**2. Demand "Stand-Alone" Operating Plan**
- Consolidators always claim "huge synergies" (often overestimated)
- Negotiate: 3-year standalone operating agreement

```
"Seller's product kept distinct for ≥18 months. 
 Consolidation only after revenue targets met.
 
 Violate this = earnout automatically paid in full."
```

**3. Lock Key Customer Contracts**
- Competitors often lose customers post-deal (see same-company logo, paranoid)
- Negotiate: **30-day change-of-control notification + mutual consent window**

```
Top 10 customers = separate negotiation:
  "We'll introduce you to [Competitor CEO]. You decide if you 
   want to stay. Competitor will honor price & terms."
   
Why? Prevents customer exodus, fair to all sides.
```

**4. Push Earnout Based on "Retention"**
- Don't tie to revenue (competitor may steal it after consolidation)
- Tie to **customer NRR + headcount retention**

```
Earnout KPIs:
  Y1: Customer retention ≥95% & all key staff stay = $3M
  Y2: NRR ≥110% & zero key departures = $2M
  
Why? Aligns incentives: competitor wants you to succeed, 
not just extract value.
```

### Deal Structure (Typical)
```
Upfront cash:     70-80% (lower due to price pressure)
Earnout (12-24mo): 15-25% (tied to retention KPIs)
Equity:           0-5% (less common)

Reps & Warranties Insurance: Often splits cost 50/50
Escrow: 20% × 18mo (competitor cautious)
Retention: $500K-$1M key personnel
```

## Archetype 4: PRIVATE EQUITY (Acquihire / Talent Play)

**Exemples:** Tech talent shops, consulting firms adding capabilities, small PE

### Profile
- **Cash position:** Low ($20-100M)
- **M&A pace:** Ad-hoc (as opportunities arise)
- **Decision speed:** Slow (60-120 days, board approval)
- **Price sensitivity:** HIGHEST (negotiate on person, not product)
- **Post-close priority:** Retain talent, integrate into client delivery

### Lever Points (Positifs)
✅ Less due diligence (they care about people, not P&L)
✅ Can be flexible on earnout (willing to overpay upfront for guaranteed team)
✅ Founder often gets C-suite role in combined entity

### Pressure Points (Exploitables)
🔴 Will only pay for team value (~$200K-$500K per engineer)
🔴 Product/revenue deemed worthless (acquiring talent, not revenue)
🔴 Post-close: rapid pivoting, loss of product autonomy
🔴 May integrate into billable services (consultants, not products)

### Negotiation Tactics

**1. Reframe Deal: "Talent Acq" Doesn't Mean "Product Valueless"**
```
NOT: "We're paying for the 8 engineers, ignore the revenue"
YES: "The 8 engineers are worth $3M + this product generates 
      $500K ARR (recurring revenue adds $3M NPV)"
      
Total value = $6M (vs. $3M if team-only)
```

**2. Demand "Consulting Revenue Sharing"**
- Acquirer will use your team to land consulting deals
- Negotiate: Team gets 20-40% of new consulting ARR created

```
Example:
  Your team lands $2M consulting deal for acquirer
  You get $400K-$800K upfront (or equity equivalent)
  
Why? Motivates team to sell, acquirer gets better ROI
```

**3. Founder Gets "Product Authority"**
- Acquirer wants to fold you into delivery org
- Counter: Founder = "Chief of Product" or "VP Technology"
- Prevents being treated as junior consultant

**4. Shorter Earnout (Talent = High Flight Risk)**
- Acquirer fears team leaving in Y2
- Offer: **Shorter earnout (12mo) + higher upfront retention bonus**

```
"We'll take $5M upfront + $2M earnout (Y1) 
 instead of $3M + $4M (Y1-Y2)"
 
Why? Simpler, lower risk for everyone, faster certainty.
```

### Deal Structure (Typical)
```
Upfront cash:     80-90% (talent deals move fast)
Earnout (12mo):   10-20% (tied to key person retention)
Equity:           2-5% (sometimes, in roll-up)

Reps & Warranties: Minimal (they don't care about liabilities)
Escrow: 5-10% × 12mo (already confident on people)
Retention: $1M-$3M per key engineer (huge for talent plays)
Consulting revenue share: 20-40% of new ARR created
```

## Archetype 5: VENTURE / GROWTH EQUITY (Strategic Minority Investor)

**Exemples:** Sequoia, Lightspeed, Accel, Bessemer buying minority stakes

### Profile
- **Cash position:** High ($50M+ available per deal)
- **M&A pace:** 2-5 partial acquisitions/year
- **Decision speed:** Fast (if you're their ideal portfolio company)
- **Price sensitivity:** Low (will overpay for control/board seat)
- **Post-close priority:** Revenue growth, category dominance, IPO prep

### Lever Points (Positivos)
✅ Will not try to consolidate (keeping you independent = upside)
✅ Brings strategic value (cap table, talent, customers)
✅ Fast decision (if you're in their sweet spot)
✅ Usually brings co-investors (more capital available)

### Pressure Points (Exploitables)
🔴 Will demand heavy board influence
🔴 May push aggressive growth targets (burn rate)
🔴 Will want founder to stay (can't exit in 5y)
🔴 Post-close: new CFO, new board dynamics, less autonomy

### Negotiation Tactics

**1. Offer Them "Growth Equity Ownership," Not Controlling Stake**
```
NOT: "We'll sell you 51% for $20M"
YES: "We'll sell you 30% for $12M, with board seat + 1x liquidation pref"

Why? VC keeps you independent (they don't want to own ops), 
you maintain control
```

**2. Negotiate "Independence Clause"**
```
"VC has right to appoint 1 board member, but not majority. 
 All major decisions (product direction, new product launch) 
 require Founder sign-off."
 
Why? Prevents VC from forcing strategy changes.
```

**3. Lock in "Follow-On Funding Commitment"**
- VC invests $X now; negotiate their commitment to follow-on rounds

```
"Series B will be led by you (with $30M commitment) 
 or we have right of refusal before external investors. 
 Same 1x liquidation pref carries forward."
```

**4. Shorter Board Oversight, More Founder Autonomy**
- VCs love meetings
- Negotiate: Monthly board meetings only (vs. weekly updates)
- Founder = day-to-day decision maker (VC input on Q strategy only)

### Deal Structure (Typical)
```
Minority stake:   20-40% ownership
Valuation:        $40-100M (depends on growth rate)
Board seat:       1 for VC (+ 2 Founder)
Liquidation pref: 1x non-participating (VC doesn't double-dip on exit)
Follow-on commit: $10-30M for Series B

Reps & Warranties: Standard (VC will due diligence)
Escrow: 5% × 12mo (minority stakes have low dispute risk)
Earnout: N/A (ongoing ownership, not time-bound)
```

## Quick Reference: Negotiation Levers by Archetype

| Lever | Strategic | PE | Competitor | Acquihire | Growth VC |
|-------|-----------|----|-----------|---------|---------| 
| **Price sensitivity** | Low | High | Very High | High | Low |
| **Decision speed** | Slow | Fast | Medium | Slow | Fast |
| **Earnout length** | 18-24mo | 36-60mo | 12-24mo | 12mo | N/A |
| **Price multiple** | 7-10x EBITDA | 5-7x | 4-6x | $300K/eng | Stock + board |
| **Key pressure** | Board approval | Growth targets | Cost synergies | Team retention | Independence |
| **Winning tactic** | Lock "No Sunset" | Build model | Prevent consolidation | Minimize scrutiny | Negotiate board |

# Competitive Response Playbook — Counter Lowball Offers

**When:** Buyer submits initial offer 20–30% below your expectations (LOI stage)  
**Goal:** Reframe conversation, maintain optionality, reset price expectations  
**Principle:** Never negotiate off a low anchor; create external pressure instead.

---

## FRAMEWORK: 3-STEP RESPONSE PROTOCOL

### STEP 1: Internal Alignment (24 hours)

**Do NOT respond emotionally.** Assemble team:
- Founder + Board + Advisor
- Review offer line-by-line
- Identify buyer's "walk-away" signals in their proposal
- Decide: negotiate or walk?

**Questions to ask:**
- Is offer 15%+ below market? → Likely opening gambit, respond firm
- Did buyer add hostile terms (clawback, aggressive warranties)? → Red flag, consider walking
- Do we have other offers cooking? → Stronger position to counter
- Is buyer credible (funded, track record)? → Serious buyer, engagement justified

**Decision rule:**
- **Lowball price + reasonable terms** → Counter aggressively, maintain optionality
- **Lowball price + hostile terms** → Consider walk; this buyer is trouble
- **Strong price + lowball terms** → Negotiate terms harder

---

### STEP 2: Data Preparation (2–3 days)

**Assemble 3-part rebuttal:**

#### Part A: Market Benchmark
```
Recent comparable exits in [category]:
- [Company] [date], $[X]M revenue × [Y] multiple = $[Z]M valuation
- [Company] [date], $[X]M revenue × [Y] multiple = $[Z]M valuation
- [Company] [date], $[X]M revenue × [Y] multiple = $[Z]M valuation

Market median: [Y]x revenue

Your normalized revenue: $[X]M
Fair valuation: $[X]M × [Y]x = $[Z]M
Buyer's offer: $[Z']M ([Y']x)

Gap: $[X]M below median (you're [Z]% undervalued)
```

**Source:** Crunchbase, PitchBook, your own research, investment banker reports.

#### Part B: Synergy Analysis
```
Buyer stated synergies (from their data room request): [List]
Our conservative estimate of synergies: $[X]M annually

Our valuation: $[Y]M
Buyer's offer: $[Y']M
Implied buyer valuation of synergies: $[Z]M

We propose splitting synergies: You keep [X]%, we ask for [Y]%
Revised offer: $[Z]M (splits difference, aligns incentives)
```

#### Part C: Growth Trajectory
```
LTM Revenue: $[X]M
Projected 12-month revenue (in offer period): $[X']M (+[Y]%)
Buyer's offer assumes: [Z] multiple

At offer price, buyer is paying for [historical/future] revenue?
Recommend: Use forward-looking revenue (12M from now)
Revised offer: $[Z]M (applied to projected revenue)
```

**Outcome:** 15–25 page data package showing buyer is anchored too low.

---

### STEP 3: Response Letter (Draft, Review, Send)

**Tone:** Professional, analytical, never emotional or aggressive.

---

## ALTERNATIVE RESPONSES (By Buyer Type)

### If Buyer Is PE Firm (Focused on IRR)

**Lever:** Cash-on-cash multiple  
**Response:** "We're comfortable with earnout (lowers your initial cash), but only if metrics are achievable. Let's agree on realistic KPI targets first."

**Tactic:** Propose 40% cash at close + 60% earnout, but with aggressive earnout targets (show confidence).

---

### If Buyer Is Strategic (Synergy-Focused)

**Lever:** Show integration risks if undervalued  
**Response:** "Our team retention is key to unlocking synergies. At $[X']M, we lose [key person], which kills [Z]% of your synergy value."

**Tactic:** Link your price to synergy achievement. "Pay us fairly now, we'll deliver synergies together."

---

### If Buyer Claims "No More Money"

**Lever:** Creative deal structure  
**Response:** "Understood. What if we structure as [70% cash + 30% earnout] or [stock-for-stock]? That unlocks value for both sides."

**Tactic:** Offer seller financing, equity roll (if strategic), earnout upside (if confident).

---

## TACTICAL MOVES (Deploy Sequentially)

### Move 1: Create Optionality (Week 1)

**If you have another buyer in pipeline:**
"We're excited about your offer. We're also in final talks with [Buyer B] (can't name yet, but similar vertical). Their indicative range is $[Z]M–$[Z']M.

To keep this moving, could we reset expectations to $[Z]M? That lets us move fast without losing ground to [Buyer B]."

**If you don't have another buyer (yet):**
"We're actively working with 3 other potential acquirers (can't disclose). We want to pick the best strategic fit, not the highest price. But we do need to be at fair market value ($[Z]M) to move forward."

### Move 2: Extend Timeline (Week 2)

**If buyer is pushing for quick closure:**
"We love the energy, but if the valuation is off-market, we'll need to explore other options. That'll take 4–6 weeks.

If you can get to $[Z]M, we can close in 6 weeks. Otherwise, we continue conversations with [Buyer B] and [Buyer C]."

**Outcome:** Buyer accelerates internal approvals to match price.

### Move 3: Walk Away (Week 3)

**If buyer won't move after 2 rounds of counter:**
"Thanks for your time. We're convinced the valuation gap is real (market is paying [Y]x, you're offering [Y']x).

We'll pursue other options. If you'd like to revisit after 6 months, we're open, but we can't accept this range now."

**Why this works:** Founders who walk away often get better terms (buyer fears losing asset; comes back 2–4 weeks later with higher offer).

## REDLINE TACTICS (SPA Negotiation)

**If you're stuck on price, shift to terms:**

| Term | Low-Price Position | High-Price Position |
|---|---|---|
| **Earnout** | Higher % (offset price) | Lower % (price reflects upside) |
| **Holdback** | Smaller (10% max) | Standard (15–20%) |
| **Reps Escrow** | Shorter period (18 mo) | Longer (24–36 mo) |
| **Indemnification** | Buyer-friendly (lower cap) | Seller-friendly (higher cap) |
| **Founder role** | Higher title (Co-CEO) | Less demanding (Advisor) |

**Tactic:** "If we can't move on price, let's improve terms for us post-close."

---

## RED FLAGS (Walk Away If You See These)

- [ ] Buyer slashes offer >30% after NDA (not serious)
- [ ] Buyer demands exclusive while dragging feet (controlling, not buying)
- [ ] Multiple rounds of lowballing + extending timeline (time-kill tactic)
- [ ] Buyer won't acknowledge your comps data (dishonest negotiator)
- [ ] CEO absent from calls; all interaction with lawyer (org not aligned)

**If 2+ red flags:** Stop negotiations, move to next buyer.

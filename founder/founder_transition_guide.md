# Founder Personal Transition Guide — Post-Acquisition

**For:** Founder/CEO selling their company  
**When:** Negotiate role in SPA, execute M0-M6, measure success  
**Outcome:** Maximize personal outcome + team retention + earnout payout

---

## DECISION MATRIX: Which Role?

Before close, negotiate your post-acquisition role in the SPA.

| Role | Stay Duration | Involvement | Earnout Likelihood | Exit Post-Earnout |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Co-CEO** (1–2 yr) | 24 months | 100% (reporting to buyer CEO) | 90%+ | Yes, negotiate equity stake |
| **Product Lead** (6–12 mo) | 12 months | 80% (product roadmap only) | 75% | Yes, stay for earnout |
| **Advisor** (3–6 mo) | 6 months | 20% (quarterly calls) | 50% | Yes, pursue next venture |
| **Clean Exit** (immediate) | 0 months | 0% | 10% | Yes, walk with cash |
| **Earn-in** (new role) | 24 months | 100% (new compensation) | 20% | Maybe, if successful |

**Rule:** Co-CEO = highest earnout payout chance (buyer needs you); clean exit = instant freedom.

---

## 10-POINT PERSONAL TRANSITION PLAN

### 1. **Negotiate Role BEFORE SPA Sign** (D75-D90)

**What to secure:**
- **Title & reporting line** (to buyer CEO? Buyer product? Board seat?)
- **Salary + bonus** (baseline + performance kickers)
- **Equity/earnout structure** (cash vs deferred; milestones)
- **Decision authority** (what decisions are yours? escalation?)
- **Exit optionality** (can you leave after Y1 without penalty?)
- **Severance clause** (if buyer changes strategy, you get [X])

**Template clause:**
```
Founder shall serve as [Chief Product Officer | Advisor] for [24 months].
Compensation: $[X]/year salary + [Y]% of earnout success.
Severance: If buyer initiates separation after M6, founder receives [3 months salary].
Exit: After M18, founder may transition to advisor role with [Z]% reduced earnout.
```

**Negotiation lever:** "I'll only stay if I see a path to [metric]. Otherwise, I risk 24mo for uncertain earnout."

---

### 2. **Prepare Knowledge Transfer (M0-M3)**

**Documentation sprint (first 2 weeks):**
- Product roadmap (next 18 months, assumptions)
- Customer relationships map (top 10 accounts, key contacts)
- Technical architecture decisions (why we chose [stack])
- Culture doc (how we hire, onboard, fire)
- Financial model (unit economics, cohorts)
- Strategic priorities (market expansion, product-market fit next chapter)

**Cadence:** 2h/day × 10 days = 20 hours. Compress into wiki/Notion page per topic.

**Outcome:** Buyer doesn't need you for ops questions (you can focus on strategy).

---

### 3. **Set M0-M3 Quick Wins** (M0: Week 1)

**Day 1–7 of close:** Buyer's team is excited, listening.

**Secure these commitments:**
- [ ] Founder-led customer call series (top 10 accounts, "we're now part of [Buyer]")
- [ ] Team all-hands (announce role, vision for integration)
- [ ] 90-day roadmap for product (3–5 quick wins, shipped by M3)
- [ ] Earnout KPI dashboard (weekly review with buyer)

**Why:** Build momentum, show impact early (important for earnout credibility).

---

### 4. **Build Trust with Buyer CEO (M0-M6)**

**Weekly 1:1 with buyer CEO (30min):**
- Roadmap progress
- Blockers + how founder is helping
- Team morale signals
- Early earnout metrics

**Monthly board meeting (30min):**
- Full earnout status
- Integration risks & mitigation
- Market feedback from customers

**Tone:** Collaborative, transparent. Never: "The buyer doesn't understand our market." Always: "Here's how we can accelerate integration."

**Outcome:** CEO confidence that earnout will be paid (increases personal payout credibility).

---

### 5. **Protect Your Earnout (M3-M24)**

**Critical actions:**
- [ ] Weekly KPI review with buyer CFO (no surprises at earnout measurement)
- [ ] Customer NPS/retention tracking (if that's your earnout metric)
- [ ] Escalate data integrity issues immediately (don't let buyer manipulate metrics)
- [ ] Document all performance in earnout schedule

**Red flag:** If buyer changes metric definition mid-way, escalate to lawyer immediately.

**Sample clause protection:**
```
Earnout metric: "Customer Retention ≥ 85% (measured Q4 per [specific formula])."
No changes to measurement methodology without written founder consent.
```

---

### 6. **Manage Your Team (M0-M12)**

**Key people retention:**
- [ ] Offer them [X]% raise post-close (funded by buyer)
- [ ] Get them on earnout structure too (if possible)
- [ ] Weekly all-hands (from your lips, not buyer comms)
- [ ] Shield them from buyer org chaos (onboarding takes 3–6 months)

**Attrition risk:** If 2+ key people leave M0-M6, your earnout is at risk (buyer will blame you).

**Strategy:** "I'll deliver [product goal] if my team stays intact."

---

### 7. **Create 18-Month Personal Plan (M1)** 

**Career decision tree (pick one):**

**Option A: Build next venture inside buyer (stay 24mo)**
- Goal: New product line, new market expansion
- Success = earnout paid + equity appreciation + board seat
- Exit: Launch as spin-out or separate P&L

**Option B: Advisor → Angel investor (transition M18)**
- Goal: Earn-in phase (24mo), then shift to advisor (20% time)
- Success = earnout paid, mentor next product leader, invest in new founders
- Exit: Pursue 2–3 new investments, build VC network

**Option C: Clean break (leave after earnout)**
- Goal: Year off, travel, reflection
- Success = earnout paid, team thriving, handoff clean
- Exit: Start new company, no distraction

**Decision:** Choose by M1, communicate to buyer CEO.

---

### 8. **Plan Your Personal Outcome** (M6)

**Tax optimization (work with advisor):**
- Estimate total deal value (upfront + earnout + equity)
- Structure personal compensation (salary vs capital gain)
- Plan 2027/2028 tax liabilities
- Consider geographic relocation (if moving, affects taxes)

**Example math:**
```
Total deal: $[X]M
Upfront: $[Y]M (take-home after tax: ~55–60%)
Earnout (probable): $[Z]M over 24mo (take-home: 60%–70%)
Target personal NW delta: $[X]M
```

---

### 9. **Track Buyer Integration Health (M6, M12, M18)**

**Quarterly report card:**

| Metric | Target | M3 | M6 | M12 | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Product roadmap on-track | 95% | ✅ | ⚠️ | ? | Q2 miss on feature X |
| Team retention | 95% | ✅ | ✅ | ⏳ | 1 departure Q2 (not critical) |
| Customer retention | ≥85% | 88% | 87% | ⏳ | Churn increased +2% (monitor) |
| Earnout metrics on-track | 90%+ | ✅ | ⚠️ | ? | On-track for 80%+ payout |

**Red flag threshold:** If 2+ metrics slip, re-negotiate earnout or exit terms.

---

### 10. **Execute M18–M24 Exit Plan**

**Months 18–24:**
- [ ] Transition product role to permanent hire (identify by M12)
- [ ] Document [your successor] onboarding (M15–M18)
- [ ] Shift to advisor cadence (monthly vs weekly)
- [ ] Prepare earnout final measurement (M20–M22)
- [ ] Plan next move (new venture, investor, sabbatical)

**M24 celebration:**
- Earnout payout + close-out
- Team handoff celebration
- Board thank-you
- Personal reflection

```
4. Weekly reporting on KPI progress

Success Metrics:
- Product roadmap 95%+ on-time
- Customer retention ≥ [X]%
- Team retention ≥ [Y]%
- Earnout payout ≥ [Z]%

- After M12: Founder may transition to advisor (20% time)
- After M18: Founder may leave without penalty

```

## SUCCESS METRICS

✅ **Earnout payout ≥ 80%** — You stayed, buyer succeeded  
✅ **Team retention ≥ 90%** — Leadership credibility maintained  
✅ **Personal satisfaction ≥ 8/10** — Worth the 24 months  
✅ **Next venture/role clarity by M18** — Planned transition

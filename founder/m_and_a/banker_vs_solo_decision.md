# Banker / Advisor Decision — DIY vs. Professional

**Hire investment banker IF:**
- Revenue > $3M ARR (complexity justify cost)
- Multiple potential buyers (creates competition)
- Want maximum price (banker negotiates harder)
- Don't have M&A experience

**DIY soft shop IF:**
- Revenue $300k-$2M (fees too high)
- Know your customers + acquirers
- Have lawyer + accountant
- Can manage 12-week process yourself

---

## **Typical Fees**

| Service | % of Deal | Min Fee | Typical Deal |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Investment Bank (M&A)** | 5% | $75k | $2M+ deals |
| **Advisor/Consultant** | 2-3% | $50k | $1.5M+ |
| **Lawyer (M&A)** | Fixed | $50-100k | All deals |
| **Accountant (Financial DD)** | Fixed | $15-30k | All deals |

**Math:** $5M deal × 5% banker = $250k cost
- You get: $5M vs maybe $4.2M solo = $800k+ uplift easily justifies $250k fee

**But:** If $1M deal, $50k banker fee = 5% of proceeds = hurts

---

## **Recommendation:**
- **< $2M ARR:** DIY or lightweight advisor ($20k fixed)
- **$2M-$5M ARR:** Professional banker (5%)
- **> $5M ARR:** Top-tier banker (3-5%)

# Founder Transition Guide — Post-Acquisition Playbook

**Objectif :** Roadmap founder 12–24 mois post-close (nouveau rôle, KPI, relation avec buyer, whenToLeave).

---

## 0–3 MONTHS POST-CLOSE — THE HONEYMOON PHASE

**Founder Role:** VP of Product [or VP of [Team]] (reporting to [BUYER_EXEC])

**Responsibilities (Week 1–4):**
- [ ] Lead team transition: explain buyer's vision, answer questions
- [ ] Customer reassurance: call top 10 customers + reassure re: product continuity
- [ ] Technical integration: begin API/data migration to buyer's infrastructure (non-disruptive)
- [ ] Retention: lock in 3–5 key employees with retention bonuses (if not already)
- [ ] Roadmap alignment: propose product roadmap to buyer leadership (Q1–Q2 integration)

**KPI Tracking (Monthly):**
- Customer churn (target: <2%)
- Employee retention (target: >95%)
- Product velocity (continue ~same velocity pre-close)
- Earnout metric progress (ARR, NRR, etc.)

**Mindset:** You are building trust with Buyer leadership. Small wins = credibility.

---

## 3–6 MONTHS POST-CLOSE — INTEGRATION PHASE

**Founder Focus:** Product roadmap + customer success

**Wins to Target:**
- [ ] One successful product release (small but meaningful)
- [ ] Land 3–5 new logos (using Buyer's distribution)
- [ ] Retain 95%+ of top customer base
- [ ] Integrate 2–3 key data streams into Buyer's platform

**Relationship Building:**
- Monthly 1:1 with Buyer CEO/COO (transparent on progress)
- Weekly sync with direct manager (alignment on priorities)
- Quarterly review of earnout progress (with finance team)

**What NOT to Do:**
- ❌ Complain that Buyer "doesn't understand our product"
- ❌ Avoid buyer's meetings (show up, be visible)
- ❌ Hire aggressively without buy-in (shows distrust)
- ❌ Suggest cutting product corners (lose Buyer trust)

**Danger Signals (Watch For):**
- 🚩 Buyer ignores roadmap you proposed
- 🚩 Buyer shifts team to other projects (understaffing)
- 🚩 Buyer starts talking about sunsetting your product
- 🚩 Buyer cuts marketing budget for your product line

**If Signal Detected:** Flag immediately to Buyer (direct conversation). Don't assume; ask.

---

## 6–12 MONTHS POST-CLOSE — MOMENTUM PHASE

**Founder Goal:** Hit earnout Y1 targets

**Earnout KPI Status Check (Month 6):**
- [ ] ARR on track for $[6.0]M? (Actual today: $__)
- [ ] NRR > 110%? (Actual: __%)
- [ ] Churn < 5%? (Actual: _%)

**If On-Track:**
- Continue current strategy
- Monthly Buyer sync remains in place
- Team morale high = retention strong

**If Behind:**
- Emergency all-hands with Buyer on missed KPI
- Propose corrective action (+ resources needed from Buyer)
- Ensure Buyer buys into fix (not blamed on Founder)
- Escalate if Buyer refuses to invest in fix

---

## 12–18 MONTHS POST-CLOSE — DECISION POINT

**Critical Question:** Does Founder want to stay 12+ more months?

### Path A: Founder Stays (Extension)

**If Y1 Earnout Hit:**
- Buyer likely wants you to stay (you've proven value)
- Negotiate Y2 earnout (same or higher targets)
- Discuss new title/equity (VP → SVP, or equity refresher)

**New Employment Terms:**
- 12–24 month extension
- Target bonus (tied to company KPI, not just your product)
- Equity refresh (4-year vest starting fresh from close date)
- Increased salary (buyer often increases post-earnout hit)

**If Y1 Earnout Partially Hit:**
- Buyer likely keeps you (you're valuable) but skeptical
- Negotiate new earnout with lower targets
- Focus on "what will make you successful" convo

**If Y1 Earnout Missed:**
- Buyer may ask you to leave (or reassign you)
- If you want to stay: negotiate fix-it plan + new earnout
- If Buyer wants you out: negotiate severance (6–12 months typical)

### Path B: Founder Leaves (18–24 months)

**Timing:** After Y1 earnout paid (if hit), before Y2 commitment

**Severance (Negotiate):**
- Accrued PTO paid out
- 6–12 months base salary
- Acceleration of unvested equity (if any vesting left)
- Health insurance continuation (COBRA or buyer pays 6 months)

**Non-Compete Implications:**
- Non-compete period: typically 12 months post-departure
- Non-solicit employees: 12–24 months
- Non-disparagement: indefinite (legal, not contractual)

**Messaging:**
- Joint press release: "Founder [NAME] pursuing next chapter / advisory role"
- Internal email: "Founder stepping down; [SUCCESSOR] now leading product"
- Careful NOT to badmouth Buyer (you still have earnout at risk!)

---

## 18–24 MONTHS POST-CLOSE — LONG-TERM POSITION

**If Founder Staying:**
- Likely promoted to SVP or C-level (based on contributions)
- May transition to "Chief Architect" or "Advisor" role (less hands-on)
- Equity earns back out (4-year vest on new grant)

**If Earnout Y2 On Track:**
- Buyer extends you 2+ more years (or makes acquisition permanent)
- You become part of Buyer's leadership team

**If Earnout Y2 Missed:**
- Buyer may start transition plan (replaces you within 6–12 months)
- Negotiate graceful exit / severance before things go sour

## EARNOUT RISK MANAGEMENT (FOUNDER PERSPECTIVE)

**Risk #1: Buyer Cuts Budget Post-Earnout, Kills KPI**

Mitigation:
- Minimum investment clause in earnout (see Earnout SLA template)
- Monthly budget reviews (confirm spend)
- Escalate to CEO if budget cut (not your fault = Buyer bears risk)

**Risk #2: Buyer Replaces You Pre-Earnout Hit**

Mitigation:
- Lock in employment agreement (12–24 months minimum)
- Severance = unpaid earnout (if fired without cause)
- Make yourself indispensable (deep relationships with customers/team)

**Risk #3: Earnout KPI Becomes Impossible (Market Crash, Competitor)**

Mitigation:
- Include force majeure clause (earnout adjusts if market condition changes)
- Quarterly reviews + escalation process (flag issues early)
- Consider negotiate lower Y2 earnout if Y1 miss (better than zero)

## THE "WHEN TO LEAVE" DECISION TREE

```
Y1 Earnout Hit ✅
├── Buyer wants you to stay 2+ years
│   ├── Excited about new product roadmap? → YES → Stay
│   └── Want to do your own thing? → YES → Negotiate exit (6–12mo notice)
└── Buyer wants you to go
    ├── Negotiated severance acceptable? → YES → Leave gracefully
    └── Severance too low? → Lawyer up; push back
    
Y1 Earnout Partially Hit 50%
├── Buyer wants fix-it plan (12 more months)
│   ├── Believe in fix? → YES → Stay for Y2
│   └── Market shifted; impossible? → Negotiate severance + leave
└── Buyer wants you out
    ├── Take severance + leave
    └── Counter: stay for Y2 earnout (Buyer gets upside if you deliver)

Y1 Earnout Missed
├── Buyer: "Restructure or leave"
│   ├── Want to fix it? → Stay; new earnout targets
│   └── Want out? → Severance negotiation
└── Your option: I want to stay/leave?
    ├── Stay: Need new earnout terms (lower targets)
    └── Leave: Walk away; keep severance + health insurance
```

## HARD TRUTH: THE FOUNDER WINDOW

Most founders leave 18–24 months post-acquisition. Why?

1. **Earnout incentive expires** (Y1 bonus insufficient for Y2 grind)
2. **Buyer's roadmap diverges** from Founder's vision (cultural friction)
3. **Founder restless** (wants to build, not integrate)
4. **Buyer wants fresh leadership** (especially if new CEO/CTO post-close)
5. **Financial security achieved** (earnout hit = Founder can walk)

**Realistic expectation:** Plan exit strategy by month 12. Even if staying longer, nice to have options.

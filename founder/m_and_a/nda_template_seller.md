# NDA — Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement

**For:** Pre-M&A discussions (soft shop, initial buyer conversations)

---

## MUTUAL NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

This Agreement is entered into as of [DATE] between:

**[COMPANY] ("Disclosing Party")** and **[BUYER] ("Receiving Party")**

---

## 1. Definition of Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means all non-public information disclosed by one party to the other, including:
- Business plans, financial data, customer lists, churn rates
- Product roadmap, architecture, technical details
- Customer contracts, vendor agreements
- Pricing models, unit economics
- Employee information

**Excluded:** Information that (a) is publicly available, (b) was independently developed, (c) is received from third parties without confidentiality obligation.

---

## 2. Obligations

Each party agrees to:
- Use Confidential Information solely for evaluating the potential transaction
- Keep information confidential (don't disclose to anyone except advisors under NDA)
- Restrict access to [5-10] personnel with need-to-know

**Advisors** may include lawyers, accountants, investment bankers, board members.

---

## 3. Return or Destruction

Upon request or termination, Receiving Party will:
- Return or destroy all Confidential Information
- Certify destruction in writing

**Exception:** May retain one copy for legal compliance records (still under confidentiality obligation).

---

## 4. Term

This NDA survives for **[2-3 years]** after termination, even if transaction doesn't close.

---

## 5. No Obligation to Transact

Neither party is obligated to complete a transaction. This is purely exploratory.

---

## 6. Seller-Friendly Clauses

### Standstill Clause (Seller Protection)
Buyer agrees not to:
- Solicit employees (except those you introduce)
- Poach customers
- Disclose that negotiations are occurring (subject to regulatory requirements)

### Permitted Disclosure
- Seller may disclose to board members, advisors, potential financing sources
- Buyer may disclose to [board / parent company] only under separate NDA

### No License
Disclosure of information does not grant Buyer any license or rights to Seller's IP.

---

## 7. Governing Law & Dispute Resolution

- **Governing Law:** [State]
- **Disputes:** [Binding arbitration / Mediation first, then litigation]
- **Prevailing Party:** Attorney fees paid by non-prevailing party

## Pre-NDA Checklist

- [ ] Have NDA signed **before** sharing any material info
- [ ] "Confidential Information" definition is clear (don't leave room for interpretation)
- [ ] Survival is 2-3 years (not forever)
- [ ] Buyer's obligation to limit disclosures is explicit
- [ ] Return/destruction clause is included
- [ ] You have attorney review (non-negotiable)
- [ ] Advise buyer: "This is standard; we expect you to sign without changes"

---

## Key Points for Founders

1. **Sign before sharing** — Don't share financial data or customer list without NDA
2. **Mutual is better** — Both parties have obligations (not one-way)
3. **2-year survival** — Covers the negotiation period + 12 months post-fail
4. **Standstill clause** — Prevents buyer from raiding employees/customers if deal falls through
5. **Advisor carve-out** — You can share with your lawyers, accountants, investors (they sign their own NDA)

---

## Red Flags to Avoid

| ❌ Bad | ✅ Good |
|--------|--------|
| One-way NDA (only you have obligations) | Mutual NDA |
| "We keep info for 5 years" | "Return/destroy after 12 months" |
| No standstill clause | Explicit non-solicitation clause |
| Buyer can disclose widely | "Only to [limited list] on need-to-know" |
| Vague confidentiality period | "For 2 years from termination" |

---

## Usage Timing

- **Soft shop:** Send NDA first thing (before LOI call)
- **Strategic buyer:** NDA in first email
- **Follow-up conversations:** Reference NDA; assume it's in effect

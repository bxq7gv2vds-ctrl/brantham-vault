## E. CAC payback calculation

```
CAC = Customer acquisition cost
Payback period = CAC / (monthly gross profit per customer)

Example:
- Total sales & marketing spend Y1: $2.1M
- New customers acquired Y1: 250
- CAC = $2.1M / 250 = $8.4k per customer

Monthly gross profit:
- ASP (average selling price): $1,240/month
- Gross margin: 80%
- Monthly gross profit = $1,240 × 80% = $992

CAC payback = $8.4k / $992 = 8.5 months

Benchmark: <12 months is good, <9 months is excellent.
Your 8.5 months = strong indicator.
```

## F. Net Revenue Retention (NRR) deep-dive

```
Formula:
NRR = (ARR_end - churned_ARR + expansion_ARR) / ARR_start

Example:
Jan 1, 2025: ARR = $8.0M
During 2025:
- Customers churned: $320k
- Expansion (upsells): $520k
- New customers: $2.8M

Dec 31, 2025: ARR = $8.0M - $320k + $520k + $2.8M = $11.0M

Organic NRR = (8.0M - 0.32M + 0.52M) / 8.0M = 105% ✅
(This means existing customers grew 5% in aggregate)

Magic # = NRR without new customer acquisition
        = (Existing + Expansion - Churn) / Prior ARR
        = 1.05x = excellent retention
```

## G. Gross vs Net Retention

```
GRR (Gross) = (ARR_start - churn) / ARR_start
              = (8.0M - 0.32M) / 8.0M = 96%

NRR (Net) = (ARR_start - churn + expansion) / ARR_start
          = (8.0M - 0.32M + 0.52M) / 8.0M = 105%

GRR 96% = 4% monthly churn (normal)
NRR 105% = expansion revenue offsets churn + 5% growth

Buyer likes: NRR >110% = strong expansion, sticky product
```

is growing within product. Recession-proof.

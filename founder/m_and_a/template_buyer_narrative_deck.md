# Buyer Narrative Deck — "Why Buy Us?"

**Purpose:** Answer the buyer's core question: *What problem does this acquisition solve for my company?*

**Format:** 5 slides, 1 deck, 3-5 min verbal pitch or 1-page written narrative.

---

## SLIDE 1: THE BUYER'S PROBLEM (Not Your Problem)

**What this slide must do:** Frame the buyer's pain, not yours. Don't say "we built X" — say "your growth is blocked by Y."

### Template

**Headline:** `[Your buyer type] has a [specific pain]`

**Example (Strategic Buyer — Martech Platform):**
> "You're the #2 email platform in market, but 40% of your churn is customers switching to competitors with native SMS integration. Your roadmap shows SMS Q4 2027, but Twilio already has SMS + WhatsApp. You're losing $2M/year in MRR."

**Example (PE Buyer — SaaS Roll-up):**
> "Your roll-up strategy targets marketing automation. You have HubSpot + Marketo, but neither has lead scoring. You need a 4th asset that fills the gap and contributes 10% organic growth to hit exit multiples."

**Not this:**
> ❌ "We built an AI-powered lead scorer that saved customers 20 hours/month."

---

## SLIDE 2: OUR SOLUTION = THEIR SOLUTION

**What this slide must do:** Position your company as the *cheapest, fastest, lowest-risk* way to solve their problem.

### Template

**Headline:** `[Your company] = [their strategic objective]`

**Your value prop: (ranked by buyer type priority)**

| Buyer Type | Why They Buy You | Proof Points |
|---|---|---|
| **Strategic** | Fills product gap → grow flagship revenue | 2-3 customer names or use cases |
| **PE** | Fills platform gap + organic growth engine | $XM ARR, YY% growth, ZZ% margin |
| **Tuck-in** | Accelerates integration (vs building) | Time saved (quarters), cost saved ($M) |

**Example (Strategic):**
> "We've spent 3 years perfecting lead scoring + buyer intent detection for [your market]. Instead of building it in 12-18 months and burning $3M, acquire us for $15M, integrate in 90 days, and ship to customers Q3. Saves you 6 months + $2M dev cost."

**Not this:**
> ❌ "We're a great company with great metrics."

---

## SLIDE 3: MARKET TIMING (Why Now?)

**What this slide must do:** Create urgency without desperation. Answer: "Why not wait 18 months?"

### Template

**Headline:** `[Market signal] means [buyer's window is closing]`

**Example (Competitive Pressure):**
> "Salesforce acquired Slack for $28B in Q1 2021. Every platform now bundles chat/collaboration. Your gap in [feature] is visible to customers and your sales team worries monthly. Your top 3 accounts renewed with commitments to 'better integrations.' Window closes Q4 2026."

**Example (Customer Demand):**
> "30% of your customers have asked for [your feature] in the last 6 months. Your sales team is writing custom roadmap commitments. RFP loss rate on [feature gap] is 8% annually = $500K churn. This acquisition closes that gap immediately."

**Example (Talent Window):**
> "Our founding team (CEO, CTO, Head of Product) is available for 24-month earn-in now. If you wait, we'll (a) raise Series A, (b) hire CEO #2, or (c) get acquired by competitor. Lock in our team + code now."

**Not this:**
> ❌ "We're a fast-growing company and won't stay available forever."

---

## SLIDE 4: INTEGRATION (Easy + Low-Risk)

**What this slide must do:** De-risk the acquisition. Show you're not a 200-person engineering nightmare.

### Template

**Headline:** `[Simple] integration roadmap = [buyer confidence]`

| Phase | Timeline | Dependencies | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Due Diligence** | 4 weeks | Financial audit, tech audit, customer calls | Buyer |
| **Systems Integration** | 4 weeks | API → their platform, data migration test | Your CTO + their tech |
| **Customer Onboarding** | 8 weeks | Migrate customers, deprecate our standalone product | Your CS + their PM |
| **Sunset Standalone** | Week 12 | Turn off servers, archive docs | Your ops |

**Example narrative:**
> "We built our product on a clean API. Your platform has OpenAPI-ready webhooks. Integration is straightforward: (1) ingest our customer data into your warehouse, (2) expose our scoring engine as an API endpoint, (3) retire our login page. 12 weeks, one engineer. Your customers won't notice the transition."

**Not this:**
> ❌ "It's a complex system and will require careful integration planning."

---

## SLIDE 5: THE ASK (Price + Structure)

**What this slide must do:** Anchor price reasonably. Leave room for negotiation.

### Template

**Headline:** `Acquisition at $[X]M + [structure] = [buyer outcome]`

| Component | Amount | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Upfront cash** | $X.0M | Covers: 10x revenue / 8x EBITDA / peer comps [cite: Crunchbase] |
| **Earnout** | $X.0M (Y% of upfront) | 24-month integration success: $MRR + integration milestones |
| **Founder retention bonus** | $X.0M | 24-month stay clause, key person de-risk |
| **Total deal value** | $XM | Per slide 4: buyer gets [outcome] at [ROI] over 24 months |

**Example narrative:**
> "We're at $2M ARR, 70% margins, 150% NRR, growing 35% YoY. Comparable acquisitions in our space (HubSpot → Observado, $60M; Segment → Twilio, $3.2B) trade at 8-12x revenue. We're asking 8x = $16M upfront + $2M earnout (24 months, tied to retention). You get a 35% compounder that fills your product gap immediately."

**Not this:**
> ❌ "We're a high-growth SaaS and should be valued like one."

---

## DELIVERY INSTRUCTIONS

### Scenario A: Cold Email Intro
Attach **none** of this. Reference in call: *"Happy to walk you through why this makes sense for [your company] — what's your biggest pain around [their pain]?"*

### Scenario B: Warm Intro (Advisor / Broker)
Send **1-page narrative** (this deck compressed to prose). No slides. No logo heavy watermark.

### Scenario C: LOI / Late Stage
Send **full 5-slide deck** in partner brand guidelines. Customize buyer's name / logo on title slide.

### Scenario D: RFI Response
Send **slide 3 + 4 only** (market timing + integration). Leaves defensibility, doesn't tip full valuation.

## CUSTOMIZATION CHECKLIST

- [ ] Buyer company name + CEO name in slide 1
- [ ] Their actual pain point (not generic version)
- [ ] Specific competitor threat they face
- [ ] Customer quote or use case (if warm intro)
- [ ] Your YTD metrics + growth rate (slide 5)
- [ ] Peer acquisitions with announced price (if available)
- [ ] Your team bios (1-line each)
- [ ] CTA: next call date + decision maker email

---

## ANTI-PATTERNS (DON'T DO THIS)

- ❌ "We'll be acquired for $[X]M within 2 years" (arrogant)
- ❌ "If you don't buy us, [competitor] will" (desperation)
- ❌ 10+ slides or >5 minutes (lose attention)
- ❌ Using their 10-K or earnings call against them (petty)
- ❌ Mentioning other bidders (not yet)
- ❌ Asking for valuation before understanding their pain (backwards)

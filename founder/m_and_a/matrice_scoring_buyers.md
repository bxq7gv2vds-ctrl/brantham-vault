# Buyer Scoring Matrix (10 Criteria)

**Use this to rank competing buyers / decide which to prioritize.**

---

## THE MATRIX

Score each criterion: **1 = Poor** | **2 = Fair** | **3 = Good** | **4 = Excellent**

| Criterion | Weight | Buyer A | Buyer B | Buyer C |
|-----------|--------|---------|---------|---------|
| **Price/Valuation** | 20% | 3 ($25M) | 4 ($30M) | 2 ($18M) |
| **Strategic Fit** | 15% | 4 (perfect synergy) | 2 (meh) | 3 (decent) |
| **Speed to Close** | 10% | 3 (60 days) | 2 (120 days) | 4 (45 days) |
| **Earnout Terms** | 10% | 2 (aggressive) | 3 (fair) | 4 (conservative) |
| **Product Integration** | 10% | 4 (can fold in) | 3 (requires work) | 2 (risky) |
| **Founder Retention** | 10% | 3 (stay 2 yrs) | 4 (stay + role) | 2 (want to replace) |
| **Customer Retention** | 10% | 3 (keep them) | 4 (upsell them) | 2 (likely churn) |
| **Financing Certainty** | 5% | 4 (all cash) | 3 (committed) | 1 (pending approval) |
| **Cultural Fit** | 5% | 3 (okay) | 2 (meh) | 4 (great) |
| **Downside Protection** | 5% | 2 (low escrow) | 4 (good reps) | 3 (moderate) |
| **TOTAL** | **100%** | **3.2** | **3.1** | **2.6** |

---

## CRITERIA EXPLAINED

### 1. **Price/Valuation** (Weight: 20%)

- **4** = Highest offer, exceeds your walk-away price
- **3** = Competitive, within range
- **2** = Below range, need to negotiate
- **1** = Lowball, not serious

---

### 2. **Strategic Fit** (Weight: 15%)

- **4** = Perfect synergy (same customers, complementary products)
- **3** = Good fit (related market, some overlap)
- **2** = Loose connection (different segment)
- **1** = No obvious synergy

---

### 3. **Speed to Close** (Weight: 10%)

- **4** = 30–45 days (all-cash, simple structure)
- **3** = 45–60 days (normal timeline)
- **2** = 60–120 days (complex, financing needed)
- **1** = >120 days (financing uncertain, regulatory issues)

---

### 4. **Earnout Terms** (Weight: 10%)

- **4** = Conservative (easy targets, high consolation tier)
- **3** = Fair (achievable targets, reasonable cap)
- **2** = Aggressive (hard targets, low ceiling)
- **1** = Punitive (targets impossible to hit)

---

### 5. **Product Integration** (Weight: 10%)

- **4** = Can integrate immediately (compatible tech stack)
- **3** = Will take work, but feasible
- **2** = Risky (requires major refactoring)
- **1** = Incompatible (might need to rebuild)

---

### 6. **Founder Retention** (Weight: 10%)

- **4** = Attractive role, equity acceleration, staying flexible
- **3** = Okay role, 2-year commitment expected
- **2** = Pressured stay, unwanted downleveling
- **1** = Want to replace you immediately

---

### 7. **Customer Retention** (Weight: 10%)

- **4** = Buyer will cross-sell to their customers (upside)
- **3** = Buyer commits to keeping your customers
- **2** = Buyer might consolidate (neutral)
- **1** = Buyer plans to shut down your product (churn)

---

### 8. **Financing Certainty** (Weight: 5%)

- **4** = All cash, proof of funds delivered
- **3** = Committed financing, lender commitment letter
- **2** = Pending financing approval
- **1** = No financing, hoping to find it

---

### 9. **Cultural Fit** (Weight: 5%)

- **4** = Team would be happy staying (shared values)
- **3** = Acceptable (different but not toxic)
- **2** = Tension (you'd clash on priorities)
- **1** = Incompatible (culture shock, people will leave)

---

### 10. **Downside Protection** (Weight: 5%)

- **4** = Strong reps & warranties, high indemnity escrow
- **3** = Reasonable protections, standard terms
- **2** = Light protections (low escrow, short tail)
- **1** = No escrow, buyer can claim anything post-close

---

## HOW TO USE THIS MATRIX

**Step 1**: Create matrix with your top 3 buyers

**Step 2**: Score each criterion (honest ratings)

**Step 3**: Multiply score by weight; add up

**Step 4**: Compare totals

**Step 5**: Review lower-scoring buyers

- If **Buyer A** scored highest but **Buyer B** is close, dig into why
- Maybe **price** (high weight) masks poor cultural fit
- Decide if those trade-offs are worth it

---

## EXAMPLE INTERPRETATION

**Buyer A (3.2) vs Buyer B (3.1)** = Very close. Might go either way depending on priorities.

**Buyer C (2.6)** = Clear second choice. But maybe in earnout terms they're stronger; could negotiate?

---

## WEIGHTED SCORING CHEAT SHEET

```
If Price weight = 20%:
- 4 points × 20% = 0.80
- 3 points × 20% = 0.60
- 2 points × 20% = 0.40
- 1 point × 20% = 0.20

Then add across all criteria for total score (0–4.0 range).
```

---

## CUSTOMIZATION

Feel free to adjust weights based on **what matters most to you**:

- **Risk-averse?** Increase "Financing Certainty" weight to 15%
- **Need cash ASAP?** Increase "Speed to Close" to 15%
- **Product matters most?** Increase "Product Integration" to 20%
- **Founder retention critical?** Increase to 15%

Just make sure total weights still = 100%.

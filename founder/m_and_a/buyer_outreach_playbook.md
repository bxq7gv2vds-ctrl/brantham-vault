# Buyer Outreach Playbook — Cold to LOI

Use this if initiating contact OR responding to inbound interest. Focus: speed + signaling (not full pitch).

---

## Phase 1: Pre-Outreach (Do this before sending email)

### Scoring Pass (15 min)
- Use [[deal-quick-scorecard]] to pre-qualify buyer
- Research: Last 3 acquisitions (valuation, timeline, integration)
- Find mutual connection (LinkedIn, email introduction > cold)

### Prep Your Signal
```
Subject line options (choose 1, use mutual connection in FROM):

Cold + Strategic:
  "Quick expansion opportunity in [vertical] — [Founder] intro"

Cold + Competitive threat:
  "[Domain] consolidation — would you consider a chat?"

Inbound + Direct:
  "Re: Your interest in [company] — can chat Thursday"
```

---

## Phase 2: Initial Outreach (Email, day 1-3)

### Email Template (3 paras max, <150 words)

```
Hi [Buyer decision-maker or intro partner],

[Name] thought you'd want to know we're open to strategic conversations.

[ONE LINE VALUE]: We process [100k+ txns/mo] in [vertical] with [48% NRR] 
and [name-brand customer list]. 

[TIMING SIGNAL]: We're selective and moving quickly, so if this is interesting,
best to chat in the next week or two.

Available Thu/Fri this week? 15 min works.

[Your name]
```

### What NOT to include
- ❌ Full pitch deck
- ❌ Financial details
- ❌ "We're selling" (say "exploring strategic options")
- ❌ Multiple attachment files
- ❌ Technical deep-dives

### Follow-up timing
- Day 3: Soft follow-up (if no reply)
- Day 7: "One more thing" (last attempt)
- Day 10: Mark dead, try another angle

**Success = 15-20% reply rate (cold) | 40%+ (warm intro)**

---

## Phase 3: First Call (Week 1, 15-30 min)

### Your Goal
- Qualify buyer's appetite (real vs curiosity)
- Share narrative, NOT negotiate
- Schedule second call (data + LOI discussion)

### Structure
```
[2 min] Rapport
  "Thanks for taking time. [Mutual friend] mentioned you've been active 
   in [space] — is [integration angle] part of your strategy?"

[7 min] Your Story (practice this)
  - How product works (1 min)
  - Market traction (1 min, numbers only)
  - Customer wins (2 examples, 2 min)
  - Why now (1 min: growth phase, opportunity)
  → If they seem disengaged, cut to 4 min

[4 min] Their Fit
  "What would make this valuable for you guys?"
  → Listen 3 min. They'll tell you priorities.

[2 min] Next Step
  IF interested:
    "Let's do a deeper dive. I'll send confidential summary + 
     key financials. Sound good?"
  IF not interested:
    "Totally understand. Keep us on your radar?"
```

### Red Flags During Call
- "We only look at >$100M revenue" = pass
- "We need 6 months to decide" = low priority for them (still pursue, but downrank)
- "Our board doesn't want SaaS right now" = hard no
- "Let me check with [absent decision-maker]" = they're not driver (watch out)

## Phase 4: Confidential Summary (Day 3-5 after call)

### 1-Pager Format (if they said "send more")
```markdown
# [Company Name] — Acquisition Overview

**The Opportunity**
- [1 sentence value prop]
- ARR: $[X]M | NRR: [X]% | Churn: [X]%/m
- Customers: [type], [avg contract, examples]

**Traction**
- [Key metric growth]: [flat, +30%, +100%?]
- [Customer win story]: [Customer A] signed [date], [comment]
- [Team size]: [N] engineers, [founder experience]

**Strategic Fit**
- [Why this matters for buyer profile]: 
  - Complements [their product/segment]
  - Opens [new customer segment]
  - [Synergy angle]

**Next Steps**
- [Your ask]: "If interested, next call = financials + LOI discussion"
- [Timing]: "We're moving this through [month], so let's focus here"
```

---

## Phase 5: LOI Conversation (Week 2-3)

### Signals to Look For (buyer seriousness)
- ✅ They want to see financials
- ✅ They assign a dedicated contact (not "we'll get back to you")
- ✅ They're asking about team, customers, product roadmap
- ✅ Specific questions (not "tell us everything")
- ✅ Calendar blocking (not "we'll fit this in")

### If They Go Dark
- Day 2: "Want to resend the summary + have you seen it?"
- Day 5: Call decision-maker directly (LinkedIn, find personal email)
- Day 10: "Appreciate your time. We're moving forward with others."
- → Move on. Don't camp in their inbox.

## Outreach Playbook By Buyer Type

### Strategic Buyer (competitor, adjacent vertical)
```
Angle:     "We complement [their offering]. Customers overlap."
Timeline:  90-150 days (due diligence + stakeholder alignment)
Price:     5-8x ARR (higher if clear synergies)
Risk:      May steal customers, then decide against acquisition
Play:      Emphasize mutual customer wins, team retention, speed
```

### PE Buyer (growth equity, mid-market PE)
```
Angle:     "Bolt-on play for your platform" OR "Growth story"
Timeline:  60-120 days (faster decision-making)
Price:     4-6x ARR (depends on growth rate)
Risk:      Heavy operational demands post-close
Play:      Show sustainable margins, unit economics, team bench-strength
```

### Venture Buyer (growth-stage VC-backed SaaS buyer)
```
Angle:     "Strategic for our Series D/E story"
Timeline:  45-90 days (speed = competitive advantage)
Price:     5-10x ARR (can stretch if synergies, founder retention)
Risk:      May try to acquire for talent (acquihire)
Play:      Highlight best team members, defensible IP, customer stickiness
```

### Family Office / Financial Buyer
```
Angle:     "Dividend-generating asset, stable growth"
Timeline:  120-180 days (thorough, conservative)
Price:     3-5x ARR (conservative multiples)
Risk:      Slow, heavy due diligence, may insert operational partner
Play:      Emphasize predictability, low churn, mature customer base
```

## Rejection Recovery

### "We don't think there's strategic fit"
→ **Response**: "Understood. Do you think [alternative angle] makes sense? 
   Or should we circle back in [6 months] if [market condition] changes?"
→ **Action**: Document why + check quarterly if circumstances change.

### "Price is too high"
→ **Response**: "Fair. What would make this work for your model? 
   What multiple would you target?"
→ **Action**: Don't negotiate yet. Understand their view, then loop back 
   if you have competing offers.

### "Team concerns"
→ **Response**: "Totally fair. What specific depth matters most? 
   Can I introduce you to [key person]?"
→ **Action**: Build retention plan BEFORE closing.

### "Wrong time, ask again in [6 months]"
→ **Response**: "Got it. What changes need to happen? Should I keep you 
   posted on [growth metric]?"
→ **Action**: Add to "warm list," send quarterly updates.

## Metrics to Track (Outreach Efficiency)

| Metric | Target | Red Flag |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Inbound replies | 15-20% | <10% = rework pitch |
| First calls scheduled | 50% of replies | <40% = buyer not ready |
| Move to data room | 40% of first calls | <20% = weak signal |
| LOI discussions | 50% of data room | <30% = pricing misaligned |
| **Time-to-LOI** | **60-90 days** | **>120 days** = slow buyer |

## Shortcut: Warm Intro Script (if using intermediary)

Send this to your mutual connection:

```
"Hey [intermediary], would you be comfortable introducing me to [buyer]? 
We're exploring strategic options and I think [specific angle] could be 
interesting for them given their [strategic priority]. 
If yes, I can draft a quick email for you to forward?"
```

Keep the burden on THEM to decide fit, not explain business.

**Use case**: 50 buyers to contact in 30 days → batch cold reach + warm intros in parallel.

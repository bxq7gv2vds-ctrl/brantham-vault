# SOFT SHOP TARGET PRIORITIZER — Rank Your Top 5 Buyers

**Utilité** : Score buyers on strategic fit + deal probability + price ceiling. Pick top 3.

---

## SCORING MATRIX (0-10 points per criterion)

| Criterion | Max | Why | Hint |
|-----------|-----|-----|------|
| **Strategic Fit** | 10 | Does buyer's roadmap align with your product? | Synergies = higher price |
| **Product Compatibility** | 10 | Can your product integrate with theirs without major refactoring? | Technical risk affects earnout |
| **Market Position** | 10 | Is buyer a leader (Tier 1) or smaller player? | Leaders = faster, higher price |
| **Financial Health** | 10 | Do they have cash to acquire? Any debt issues? | Better health = faster closing |
| **Acquisition History** | 10 | Have they acquired before? At what multiples? | Repeat buyers = faster, clearer terms |
| **Decision Speed** | 10 | Known fast decision-maker or committee-heavy? | Fast = less time burned |
| **Earnout Willingness** | 10 | Do they do earnouts? What %? Are they fair? | High earnout % = you eat more risk |
| **Founder-Friendly** | 10 | Will they let you stay involved or want full takeover? | Aligns with your goals |
| **Revenue Multiplier** | 10 | What multiple would they likely pay vs market? | Some buyers value different verticals |
| **Baseline Price** | 10 | What's their likely opening offer range? | Does it meet your BATNA? |

**Total**: 100 points

## SOFT SHOP RANKING STRATEGY

### **Week 1-2: Tier 1 Buyers**
- Start with 3 buyers scoring 75+
- Goal: Get offers, understand market price
- Approach: Teaser deck (no price), gauge interest

### **Week 3-4: Tier 2 Buyers**
- Contact 2-3 scoring 60-74
- Goal: Backup offers, competition
- Approach: Same teaser, but faster cadence (they're less urgent)

### **Week 5+: Tier 3+ Buyers**
- Only contact if Tier 1/2 don't work
- Goal: Stretch offers, pipeline extension
- Warning: Don't settle for low-value buyers too early

## NEXT STEPS

1. **List your top 10 potential buyers** (competitive, strategic, PE, VC)
2. **Score each buyer** using the matrix above
3. **Tier them**: Top 3 = Tier 1, Next 2-3 = Tier 2, Rest = Tier 3+
4. **Create outreach list**: [Buyer Name, Score, Tier, Contact, Timing]
5. **Soft shop**: Start with Tier 1, warm people up
6. **Track responses**: Log every conversation, offer, timeline

---

## BONUS: MULTIPLE OFFERS STRATEGY

**If you get 2+ offers in parallel:**

1. Don't accept first offer (use it to negotiate others)
2. Create auction tension: "We're talking to 2-3 buyers, timeline is [X]"
3. Pit them against each other:
   - Buyer A offers: $12M, 10% earnout
   - Buyer B offers: $11M, 5% earnout → counter: "B, you're $1M behind. Can you match price or improve earnout terms?"
4. Final round: Best price + best terms on reps/warranties/earnout

**Golden rule**: Create competition. Never negotiate against yourself.

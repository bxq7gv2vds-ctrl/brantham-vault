# Buyer Compatibility Matrix — 5 Archetypes × 10 Criteria

## The 5 Buyer Archetypes

| Archetype | Example | Driver | Synergy | Risk |
|-----------|---------|--------|---------|------|
| **Strategic (Large)** | Salesforce, HubSpot, Microsoft | Platform growth | High; immediate customer fit | Integration hell; slow approvals |
| **Financial (PE)** | Insight Partners, Thoma Bravo | Multiple arbitrage | Medium; cost cuts + growth | Heavy leverage; earnout pressure |
| **Bolt-On (Smaller Strategic)** | Vertical-specific player | Fill product gap | Medium-High; niche fit | Cultural clash; founder departure |
| **Consolidator (Roll-up)** | Accel-backed aggregator | Market consolidation | High; add to platform | Reporting to portfolio CEO (less autonomy) |
| **Acqui-Hire** | Google, Facebook | Team + IP, kill product | Low-Medium; talent capture | Layoffs; product sunset |

## Scoring Notes

**Valuation:** Big strategic players typically 1.5–2x premium over PE for strategic synergies.  
**Speed:** PE slower (diligence, boards); acqui-hires fastest (pre-agreed scope).  
**Synergy:** Consolidators + strategics highest (immediate consolidation benefit).  
**Founder Role:** Smaller acquirers retain founder longer; big tech often sidelines quickly.  
**Product Survival:** Bolt-on acquisitions keep product; PE keeps if profitable; acqui-hires kill.  
**Earnout:** Financial buyers are most disciplined (public metrics); strategics can manipulate.  
**Financial Stability:** Public > PE with strong sponsor > private strategic (risk: acquirer bankruptcy).  
**Optionality:** PE secondary markets exist; strategic acquisitions are typically permanent.

## Decision Framework

**Scoring 40+:** Strong buyer, prioritize.  
**Scoring 30–39:** Viable but trade-offs; weigh carefully.  
**Scoring <30:** Red flags; consider only if desperation or significant premium.

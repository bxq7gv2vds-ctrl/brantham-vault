# Documents Requis par Phase M&A

**Checklist: Quel document, à quel moment, pour qui.**

---

## Pré-LOI (Sourcing & Warm-Ups)

| Doc | Format | Owner | When | Why |
|-----|--------|-------|------|-----|
| Pitch deck (5–10 slides) | PDF | Founder | Before first call | Hook interest |
| Teaser / 1-pager | PDF, 1 page | Founder | Before NDA | Non-confidential overview |
| Lightweight NDA | PDF, 2 pages | Counsel | Before dataroom access | Protect confidentiality |
| Cap table (high-level) | Excel | Founder | After NDA signed | Show ownership structure |

**Effort to compile:** ~8 hours (deck + teaser + cap table basics)

---

## LOI Phase (Days 0–5)

| Doc | Format | Owner | When | Notes |
|-----|--------|-------|------|-------|
| **LOI** | PDF, 8–12 pages | Buyer's counsel usually drafts | Day 1–3 | Non-binding except exclusivity |
| **Cap table (final)** | Excel + cap-table-management software printout | Founder's counsel | Day 2 | Must be audited |
| **Articles of incorporation** | PDF | Founder / secretary | Day 2 | Proof of structure |
| **Board resolutions** (to sell) | PDF | Board + secretary | Day 3 | Authority to negotiate |
| **Shareholder consents** | PDF or email | All shareholders | Day 3 | Majority approval (90%+ typical) |

**Effort to compile:** ~12 hours (cap table audit, resolutions, consents)

## Pro Tips

1. **Pre-organize everything.** By day 5 post-LOI, 80% of docs should already be folder-ready. No scrambling.
2. **Use Ironclad or Carta for cap table.** Cleaner than Excel for buyer review.
3. **Get tax counsel early.** If there's a surprise tax issue, it adds 30+ days. Better to find & disclose pre-LOI.
4. **Document naming:** Use consistent naming: `[DateYYYY-MM-DD]_[Document]_[Version].pdf`. Helps tracking.
5. **Version control:** When you send new docs, track versions. "CAP_TABLE_v2.xlsx" not "CAP_TABLE_FINAL_v3_REAL_FINAL.xlsx".

---

**Fact:** The deal that closes on time is the one where docs are pre-organized & accessible. Disorganization = delays = higher cost for both parties.

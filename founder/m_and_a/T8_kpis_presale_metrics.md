# Pre-Sale KPIs — Metrics Buyers Will Ask For

## Your "Narrative Proof Points" (What You Must Show)

### 1. Revenue Metrics

**ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)**
- Target : €500k (verified)
- Show : 12-month trailing ARR chart (last 12 months upward slope)
- Why : Proves sustainability (not lumpy deals)

**MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)**
- Target : €42k
- Show : Last 12 months MRR trend (growing each month)
- Why : Buyers want visibility into next quarter

**Growth Rate (YoY)**
- Target : +60% YoY (Jun 2024 → Jun 2025)
- Show : Cohort growth, month-over-month cadence
- Why : Buyers extrapolate forward (if growing 60%, €750k+ by EOY reasonable)

**ARPU (Average Revenue Per User)**
- Target : €5-6k per salon
- Show : Breakdown by customer segment (if applicable)
- Why : Valuation multiple is ARR-based; clean ARPU shows unit economics

---

### 2. Customer Health Metrics

**Customer Count**
- Target : 80-100 active salons
- Show : Growth of customer base (new logos per month)
- Why : Proves repeatable acquisition (not accidental sales)

**Net Revenue Retention (NRR)**
- Target : 100%+ (customers expanding spend, or 100% if flat)
- Show : Year-over-year cohort analysis
- Why : Buyers love NRR >100% (indicates land & expand)

**Churn Rate (Monthly)**
- Target : 3-5% / month (healthy)
- Show : 12-month cohort retention curve
- Why : Critical risk; low churn = predictable revenue, high valuation

**Customer Concentration**
- Target : Top 3 customers < 30% of revenue
- Show : Customer list with revenue % (anonymized if needed)
- Why : Buyer's biggest fear is customer loss (concentration = deal risk)

**NPS (Net Promoter Score)**
- Target : 50+ is excellent for SaaS
- Show : Latest NPS survey (if you've done it)
- Why : Proof of product-market fit and satisfaction

---

### 3. Unit Economics

**Gross Margin**
- Target : 70%+ (SaaS standard)
- Show : COGS % of revenue (mostly hosting, support labor)
- Why : Shows you can scale profitably

**CAC Payback (Customer Acquisition Cost Payback)**
- Target : <12 months
- Show : Total sales & marketing spend / new ARR added
- Why : Buyer assesses sustainability of growth engine

**EBITDA Margin**
- Target : 15-20% (you're profitable)
- Show : 12-month trending EBITDA (improving over time)
- Why : Buyer sees profitable exit story (not "burn until acquired")

**LTV (Lifetime Value)**
- Target : LTV/CAC > 3× (yours probably 5-10×)
- Show : Average customer lifetime × gross margin %
- Why : Unit economic viability

---

### 4. Operational Metrics (Nice to Have)

**CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)**
- Target : < €8k per customer (reasonable for SaaS SMB)
- Show : Sales & marketing spend / new customers
- Why : Demonstrates scalability

**Sales Cycle Length**
- Target : 30-60 days (fast for enterprise)
- Show : Average deal size × sales cycle
- Why : Buyer wants to know sales efficiency

**Product Roadmap Status**
- Target : 3-month pipeline clear, 12-month vision
- Show : 3 biggest features in progress, 2026-2027 strategy
- Why : Buyer won't buy "stuck" product

**Tech Debt Assessment**
- Target : Low (codebase clean, modern stack)
- Show : Code audit readiness, no major refactors needed
- Why : Buyer fears post-acquisition engineering distraction

## How to Present These (Golden Rules)

### ✅ DO
- **Lead with growth** : "We're growing 60% YoY" (not apologizing for churn)
- **Show trends** : "Churn dropped from 7% to 3% over 18 months" (improvement story)
- **Use 12-month rolling cohorts** : Proves seasonality isn't hurting you
- **Benchmark against peers** : "Our churn is 3%, vs. SaaS average 5%" (credible)
- **Show CAC payback** : Proves you can scale without infinite marketing burn

### ❌ DON'T
- **Present unaudited financials** : Use real numbers or third-party audit
- **Cherry-pick metrics** : If you hide something, buyer will find it in VDD
- **Make up NPS** : If you haven't surveyed, just say so
- **Hide concentration risk** : Buyer will discover anyway (better to be transparent)
- **Promise future growth** : "We'll hit €1M ARR" is fantasy; show track record instead

## Presentation Format (For Buyers)

Create 1 simple PDF with these sections:
1. **Revenue Growth** (line chart: 12 months MRR)
2. **Churn & Retention** (cohort table: 12-month survival %)
3. **Customer Base** (bar chart: new customers/month)
4. **Unit Economics** (table: CAC, LTV, payback period)
5. **Profitability** (EBITDA % improving over time)

**Keep it simple, data-driven, no marketing fluff.**

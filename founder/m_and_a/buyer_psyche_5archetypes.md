# Buyer Psyche — 5 Archetypes

**Ce qu'ils disent vs. ce qu'ils cherchent vraiment + comment les pitcher.**

---

## ARCHETYPE 1 : STRATEGIC (Competitor / Platform Play)

### Qui
- Géante SaaS (Salesforce, HubSpot, Atlassian) cherchant to plugfill product gaps
- Acquiert pour **synergies** (client cross-sell, tech leverage)

### Motivations réelles
1. **Acquérir des clients** (plus que l'algo)
2. **Talent acquisition** (vos engineers)
3. **Bloquer un concurrent** ("Better we own it than competitor does")
4. **Augmenter TAM** (expand addressable market)

### Ce qu'ils disent
> "We love your product, perfect fit with our vision..."

### Ce qu'ils cherchent vraiment
- **Customer overlap :** "Comment beaucoup de vos clients utilisons-nous déjà ?"
- **API/integration potential :** "Ça pourrait être une feature de notre plateforme ?"
- **Team viability :** "Vos top engineers restent-ils ? Quel est le réel retention risk ?"
- **Valuation :** "Où étiez-vous en croissance dans 18 mois ?"

### Negotiation play
- ✅ **Pitch 1 :** "We'll unlock $X in cross-sell revenue within Year 1"
- ✅ **Pitch 2 :** "Your platform becomes a 10x better solution with our team"
- ✅ **Price anchor :** Premium (6-8x ARR) si vraie synergy, parce qu'ils font eux-mêmes pas ce P&L différentiel

### Deal structure
- **Cash :** 70-100% (no risk, strategic buyer has capital)
- **Earnout :** 0-30% (si cross-sell targets non-guaranteed)
- **Timeline :** Fast (internal pressure to ship)
- **Integration :** You report to Product VP, not separate P&L

### Red flags
- ❌ Buyer won't commit to customer retention ("We'll migrate to platform")
- ❌ Team roles not discussed (sign of integration chaos)
- ❌ Valuation anchors WAY below what you asked (different math than you think)

### Counter-play if lowballed
> "Your synergy thesis is $X, we'll take 50% of Year 1 synergy upside in earnout. Deal?"

## ARCHETYPE 2 : FINANCIAL BUYER (PE Fund, Growth Equity)

### Qui
- Private equity (Thoma Bravo, Vista, Insight) ou growth equity (Insight, TCV)
- Acquiert pour **cash multiple + growth upside**

### Motivations réelles
1. **IRR target** (25-30% IRR over 5-7 years = their model)
2. **Founder/team staying** (your capability, not just the asset)
3. **Revenue multiple arbitrage** (buy at 4x, sell at 6-7x in 5 years)
4. **Add-on acquisition potential** (buy your company, then roll up 5 more companies into it = "platform")

### Ce qu'ils disent
> "We're long-term players, we'll support your vision..."

### Ce qu'ils cherchent vraiment
- **Unit economics :** Magic number (CAC payback), LTV/CAC ratio, gross margin
- **Founder staying :** "What's your role post-acquisition? 3 years locked up?"
- **Growth levers :** "Can we 3x revenue by adding sales team? Geography expansion?"
- **Customer quality :** "How many customers would churn if you left?"

### Negotiation play
- ✅ **Pitch 1 :** "Here are 3 growth levers to 2-3x revenue, we execute them post-close"
- ✅ **Pitch 2 :** "I'm staying, we're aligned on this thing"
- ✅ **Price anchor :** 3-5x ARR base, earnout on revenue targets

### Deal structure
- **Cash :** 50-70% (they keep 30-50% in earnout to align incentives)
- **Earnout :** 30-50% (usually tied to revenue $X or EBITDA margin targets)
- **Founder role :** CEO or Operating Partner, 3-5 year earn-in
- **Timeline :** 3-6 months (slower, but due diligence thorough)
- **Integration :** Relatively hands-off first 1-2 years, then rollup strategy

### Red flags
- ❌ Founder won't stay (PE fund needs someone to drive)
- ❌ Earnout math impossible ("Revenue must 5x or earnout zero")
- ❌ "We'll install our own CEO post-close" = they don't trust you

### Counter-play if trapped in bad earnout
> "I'll take 40% earnout on revenue $X, but: capped at X+$1M, with true-up if you don't hire sales team we agreed on"

## ARCHETYPE 3 : TUCK-IN ACQUISITION (Bolt-On / Consolidator)

### Qui
- Larger competitor in adjacent market wanting to build platform by acquiring 5-10 companies
- e.g., existing SaaS suite acquires "missing piece" feature

### Motivations réelles
1. **Fill product gap** for existing customer base
2. **Customer acquisition cost** (better than sales budget)
3. **Team + tech integration** into platform (you become feature, not standalone product)
4. **Revenue multiples** (your revenue just adds to consolidated P&L, not separate)

### Ce qu'ils disent
> "Perfect strategic fit, you'd be a core part of our platform..."

### Ce qu'ils cherchent vraiment
- **How integrated can we make you?** ("Can we deprecate your API and make it a module?")
- **Customer switching cost** ("How sticky are your customers to you vs. the platform?")
- **Team logistics** ("Which 2-3 of your 8 engineers stay post-close?")
- **Price anchor** ("We buy 10 of these a year at 2.5-3x ARR...")

### Negotiation play
- ✅ **Pitch 1 :** "Keep us running separately 18 months, prove synergy, then integrate"
- ✅ **Pitch 2 :** "Transition tax : integration effort is $X, we deduct from earnout targets"
- ✅ **Price anchor :** 2-3x ARR base (lower than strategic because you're becoming a feature)

### Deal structure
- **Cash :** 40-60% (they'll eat integration costs)
- **Earnout :** 40-60% (tied to customer retention % post-integration)
- **Founder role :** Often no founder role ("You can go" or optional consultant)
- **Timeline :** 2-4 months (they know the deal, fast close)
- **Integration :** Rapid (30-60 days, you're absorbed into platform team)

### Red flags
- ❌ Buyer says "We're not sure how to integrate, we'll figure it out" = chaos integration
- ❌ No earnout retention clause = customers will churn post-close and earnout dies
- ❌ "Team can mostly go" = you're just the IP, not the value

### Counter-play if being squeezed
> "Base valuation 3x, earnout 40% tied ONLY to customer retention, not revenue growth (you're controlling that, not us)"

## ARCHETYPE 4 : FOUNDER / ANGEL INVESTOR

### Qui
- Founder or small angel-backed team acquires your company to accelerate their own growth
- Rare, but happens when their capital + your customers = 10x company

### Motivations réelles
1. **Accelerate growth** (combine two products = faster to scale)
2. **Team augmentation** (acquire talent + customer base simultaneously)
3. **Capital efficiency** (1 acquisition vs. 3-4 hires + sales budget)

### Ce qu'ils disent
> "We think together we can build something amazing..."

### Ce qu'ils cherchent vraiment
- **Can we actually integrate?** (technical compatibility, culture fit)
- **Team chemistry** ("Do your people want to work with us?")
- **Founder willingness** ("Will you co-lead post-close or go?")

### Negotiation play
- ✅ **Pitch 1 :** "Minimal integration, you operate independently for 2 years"
- ✅ **Pitch 2 :** "Earnout tied to mutual OKRs we set together"
- ✅ **Price anchor :** Typically 2-4x ARR (limited capital, but potential upside)

### Deal structure
- **Cash :** 30-50% (they likely borrowed or bootstrapped)
- **Earnout :** 50-70% (high % because capital is tight)
- **Founder role :** Joint leadership, co-CEO or separate P&Ls
- **Timeline :** 1-3 months (informal process, minimal legal)
- **Integration :** Collaborative, both teams work closely

### Red flags
- ❌ Founder has no track record of exits (higher risk on earnout)
- ❌ No real capital lined up beyond yours (debt, not growth capital)
- ❌ "We'll figure out integration as we go" = integration will fail

### Counter-play if concerned about team culture
> "Earnout tied to mutual NPS and team retention, not revenue (we control that together)"

## ARCHETYPE 5 : STRATEGIC BUYER (Distributor / Channel Partner)

### Qui
- Complementary company wanting to add your product to their distribution channel
- e.g., VAR (value-added reseller), system integrator, platform (e.g., Shopify App Store owner wants your app)

### Motivations réelles
1. **Expand product portfolio** (add your solution to their offering)
2. **Deliver customer value** (bundle = stickier, higher NPS)
3. **Revenue mix** (recurring revenue from your SaaS feeds their commission model)
4. **Land grab** (prevent competitor from buying your customer base)

### Ce qu'ils disent
> "Great addition to our ecosystem, our customers will love it..."

### Ce qu'ils cherchent vraiment
- **Customer access** ("How many of your customers would let us resell to them?")
- **Pricing model** ("Can we white-label this? Change pricing?")
- **API stability** ("Will you support our modifications?")
- **Founder staying vs. going** ("Can you stay 1 year to support integration?")

### Negotiation play
- ✅ **Pitch 1 :** "Your customers get 30-50% better outcomes with our product integrated"
- ✅ **Pitch 2 :** "Customer retention >90% post-close if positioned right"
- ✅ **Price anchor :** 1.5-3x ARR (channel player typically has lower multiples)

### Deal structure
- **Cash :** 50-80% (channel partners usually have strong cash flow)
- **Earnout :** 20-50% (tied to customer adoption % in their channel)
- **Founder role :** Often integration lead, 6-12 month role
- **Timeline :** 2-4 months (they know their customers, faster path)
- **Integration :** Joint go-to-market, you support their sales team

### Red flags
- ❌ Buyer wants to drastically change pricing (kills your unit economics)
- ❌ No commitment to keeping product separate (will be gutted)
- ❌ "You'll have no control over roadmap" = you become a feature, not a product

### Counter-play if being disrespected
> "Product autonomy non-negotiable. Your team sells, our team builds. Earnout on customer adoption KPIs."

## Quick Reference : Buyer Archetypes Compared

| Metric | Strategic | PE | Tuck-in | Founder | Channel |
|--------|-----------|----|---------|---------|----|
| **Typical valuation** | 6-8x ARR | 3-5x ARR | 2-3x ARR | 2-4x ARR | 1.5-3x ARR |
| **Cash %** | 70-100% | 50-70% | 40-60% | 30-50% | 50-80% |
| **Earnout %** | 0-30% | 30-50% | 40-60% | 50-70% | 20-50% |
| **Founder role** | Optional | Required | Rare | Required | 6-12 mo |
| **Integration speed** | 3-12 mo | Slow, strategic | 30-60 days | Gradual | 6-12 mo |
| **Risk to earnout** | Medium | Low | HIGH | HIGH | Medium |
| **Decision speed** | Fast | Medium | Fast | Slow | Medium |

## Playbook : "Which Buyer to Pitch First ?"

1. **Strategic** if you have hyper-growth + tech differentiation → max valuation, fast close
2. **PE** if you want to stay 3-5 years and own upside → medium valuation, slower but sure
3. **Tuck-in** if you want out ASAP → lower valuation, fast close, team mostly leaves
4. **Founder** if you know them + they have capital → medium valuation, collaborative, alignment
5. **Channel** if your customers already use their platform → medium valuation, easy integration

**Pro tip :** Always have 2 buyers in parallel. 1 Strategic + 1 PE = maximum leverage.

# Competitive Buyer Map — Strategic M&A Intelligence

**Objectif :** Identifier 3-5 buyers sérieux, leurs multiples typiques, et comment approcher.

---

## Template: Remplir Pour Votre Industrie

### Step 1: Buyer Archetypes (5 Types)

| Archetype | Who | Motivation | Valuation Multiple | Speed | Notes |
|-----------|-----|-----------|----------|-------|-------|
| **Strategic (Big Tech/Software)** | Salesforce, HubSpot, Adobe, Notion | Expand TAM, cross-sell, IP, team | 6-10x ARR | Fast (60-90d) | Highest price; cultural risk |
| **Private Equity (PE/Growth)** | Thrive, Permira, Vista, Menlo | Consolidation play, leverage EBITDA | 4-8x EBITDA | Medium (90-180d) | Wants growth + profitability |
| **Tuck-in (Platform/Middle-Market)** | Zapier, Calendly, Intercom (if they buy) | Bolt-on to existing platform | 3-6x ARR | Medium (90-120d) | Lowest price; best culture fit |
| **Investor/Founder Group** | Existing VCs, angels, founder syndicates | Secondaries play, financial return | 2-5x ARR | Slow (180d+) | Lowest price; founder risk |
| **Acqui-hire (Early-stage SaaS)** | Smaller profitable SaaS, Bootstrapped | Acquire team + customer base | 1-3x ARR | Slow-Medium | Down-market; risky valuation |

## Tier 5: Buyer Contact Strategy

### Phase 1: Soft Touch (30 days)

**Goal:** Get on their radar without formal process.

```
Step 1: Find warm intro
  └─ Who do you know at buyer? (customers, investors, board members)
  └─ Ask them for CEO/CFO/Corp Dev intro

Step 2: LinkedIn research
  └─ Find decision-maker (VP of Strategy, M&A, Corp Dev)
  └─ Check recent acquisitions (note size, vertical)

Step 3: Soft conversation
  └─ "Hey, love your [acquisition]. We're in similar space. Coffee?"
  └─ Plant seed; don't pitch yet
```

---

### Phase 2: Formal Process (60-120 days)

**Goal:** Move to formal negotiation.

```
Step 1: Broker intro (or direct)
  └─ Use banker/advisor for credibility
  └─ "We're exploring strategic partnerships"

Step 2: Teaser deck (1-2 pages)
  └─ Company overview, growth metrics, customer list (anonymized)
  └─ Valuation indication (e.g., "$30-40M valuation range")

Step 3: NDA + data room
  └─ Full financials, customer list, contracts
```

---

### Phase 3: Multi-Track (Run Parallel Processes)

**Strategy:** Approach 2-3 buyers simultaneously (but don't tell them).

```
TIMELINE:
M1 (Days 0-30):   Approach 3 buyers (soft touch each)
M1-M2 (Days 30-60): If interest, kick off formal process with interested buyers
M2-M3 (Days 60-90):  Competition heats up, prices rise
M3 (Days 90-120): Final bids, LOI selection
M4-M6: Due diligence + close
```

**Why multi-track?**
- Single-track process = buyer lowballs (they have no competition)
- Multi-track = auction dynamics = higher price (20-30% uplift common)

## Red Flags: Avoid These Buyers

🚩 **Buyer has failed integration** of prior acquisition → Cultural destruction risk
🚩 **Buyer is "in talks" with competitor** → May kill deal to weaken rival
🚩 **Buyer has no M&A playbook** → Will tank culture post-close
🚩 **Buyer insists on earn-out > 40%** → Doesn't believe in deal; structured risk-shift
🚩 **Buyer requires you to stay >2 years** → Implies distrust in team retention

## Post-Close Success Factors (By Buyer Type)

| Buyer Type | Success Metric | Risk |
|-----------|---|---|
| Strategic | Cross-sell, upsell to buyer's customer base | Product integration delays |
| PE | Hit EBITDA targets, grow margin | Cost-cutting kills growth |
| Tuck-in | Retain customers, avoid churn | Product roadmap de-prioritized |

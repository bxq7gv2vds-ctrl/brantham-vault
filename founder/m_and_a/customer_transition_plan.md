# Customer Transition Plan — Notification & Support (Day 0-30)

**Template:** Adjust messaging per your situation.

---

## **NOTIFICATION EMAIL (Day 1 closing)**

**Subject:** Strategic Update — Exciting Partnership for [Product]

```
Hi [Customer Name],

I'm thrilled to share that [BUYER] has acquired [YOUR COMPANY], effective today.

This is great news for you because:
✓ Faster innovation & more resources for product development
✓ Expanded integration with [buyer's adjacent products]
✓ Same team & support (zero disruption to your service)
✓ No price changes for 12 months

What doesn't change:
✓ Your support contact remains [name]
✓ Service level agreements stay the same
✓ Data security & compliance (ISO, GDPR, SOC2)
✓ Feature roadmap we planned together

What's next:
1. [BUYER] will send additional details on [date]
2. We'll host a joint webinar: [date/time]
3. Your success manager: [name] (same person!)

Questions? Reply to this email or call [founder phone].

Looking forward to the journey ahead!

[Founder name]
Founder, [Company]
```

---

## **DAY 1-2 ACTIONS**

### **Founder/CEO**
- [ ] Record 5-min video message to customers (post on login page)
- [ ] Call top 5 customers personally (before email blast)
- [ ] Prepare FAQ document
- [ ] Briefing call with customer success team
- [ ] Ensure support queue is staffed (no downtime)

### **Customer Success Team**
- [ ] Send personalized notification to each customer
- [ ] Update status page (if SaaS) with "Under new ownership, service unchanged"
- [ ] Prepare Q&A doc for all support staff
- [ ] Escalation list ready (for concerned customers)

---

## **FAQ (send Day 3-5)**

```
Q: Will my contract terms change?
A: No. Your current pricing and contract terms remain unchanged 
   for the next 12 months. We're committed to your success.

Q: Who is my support contact?
A: Same person — [name] remains your dedicated success manager.

Q: Will the product change?
A: We're accelerating development with more resources. 
   Feature roadmap shared with you remains our priority.

Q: What about my data security?
A: All security certifications (ISO, GDPR, SOC2) continue unchanged.
   Third-party audits completed annually.

Q: Why was I not informed earlier?
A: This was confidential until closing. Standard practice in acquisitions.

Q: Can I leave if I don't like the new owner?
A: Your contract terms remain unchanged. We respect your choice,
   but we're confident this partnership strengthens our product.

Q: Will pricing increase?
A: No increases for 12 months, guaranteed. After that, we'll communicate
   any adjustments with 90 days notice.

Q: How do I escalate concerns?
A: Contact [founder] directly: [phone/email]

Q: Will the team change?
A: No immediate changes. Everyone continues in their roles.
```

## **WEEK 1 : CUSTOMER COMMUNICATION**

### **Video message (posted on login page)**

```
Duration: 3-5 minutes
Speaker: Founder (you)
Content:
  - Who are you (brief bio)
  - What we built & why
  - Why this partnership is good for customers
  - No changes to service (reassurance)
  - Next steps / when to expect buyer's communication
  - Personal commitment to customer success
  - How to reach you with concerns

Tone: Confident, transparent, human.
No corporate-speak.
```

### **Webinar (schedule Day 7-10)**

**Attendees:** All customers (attendance optional)

**Agenda:**
1. Brief intro to buyer (5 min)
2. Vision for combined company (5 min)
3. Roadmap next 12 months (5 min)
4. Q&A (10 min)

**Speaker:** Founder + buyer's product lead (share the stage)

## **WEEK 2 : SUPPORT & ONBOARDING**

### **For at-risk customers (identify Day 1)**

```
At-risk signals:
├─ Customer > 3 years (stable, might be looking for new vendor)
├─ Contract expiring in next 6 months
├─ Recent support tickets (dissatisfied?)
├─ Smaller deal size (easier to switch)

Action per at-risk customer:
1. Call personally (founder, not support)
2. Offer "transition success" package
   - Dedicated onboarding call
   - Expedited feature request (if doable)
   - Price lock offer (reassurance)
3. Escalation: If non-response, call their CFO
```

### **For happy customers**

```
Normal support continues
Mention: "If interested in deeper integrations with buyer's platform,
let's discuss."
```

## **TRANSITION SUCCESS CRITERIA (Day 30 checkpoint)**

- [ ] Zero unplanned churn (all customers remain active)
- [ ] Support queue normal (no spike in tickets)
- [ ] Positive sentiment from customer calls
- [ ] Webinar attended by ≥50% of customers
- [ ] FAQ viewed by ≥70% (from email click tracking)
- [ ] No escalations requiring legal review
- [ ] NPS maintained or improved

---

## **COMMON CONCERNS & RESPONSES**

| Customer says | Your response |
|---|---|
| "I'm worried you'll shut down my product" | "We're investing more, not less. Product roadmap is accelerating." |
| "Will you raise my price?" | "Prices locked for 12 months. No surprises." |
| "I prefer the old company culture" | "Same team, same people. We're growing, not changing identity." |
| "Can we negotiate different terms?" | "Your current contract stands. Let's discuss after 12 months." |
| "I want to switch to a competitor" | "Understandable. Let's hop on a call—might we have missed something?" |
| "Will you force me to use buyer's product?" | "Absolutely not. Everything optional and on your timeline." |

---

## **RED FLAGS (watch for)**

```
🚨 Multiple unplanned churn events (>1 in first month)
🚨 Support tickets spike 2x normal volume
🚨 Customer calls buyer's CEO directly (bypass you)
🚨 Industry news says buyer is "integrating" or "consolidating"
🚨 Founder/key team not visible in customer calls

Action: CEO + founder call immediately to discuss & stabilize.
```

# Data Room Phasing Strategy

**Partager l'info au bon moment = leverage. Trop tôt = weakness. Trop tard = buyer suspicious.**

---

## PHASE 1 : Pre-LOI Access (Top-of-funnel buyers)

**Give:**
- 1-pager (non-confidential)
- 3-slide teaser
- Non-descript CIM (no numbers)
- NDA template

**Lock:**
- Financials (proprietary)
- Customer list
- Contracts
- Technical details
- Cap table

**Signal :** "You've earned info, but not yet intimate"

**Duration :** Days 1-21 (until LOI demand)

---

## PHASE 2 : LOI Signed — Diligence Begins

**Add access to:**
- CIM full version (with numbers)
- 3 years financials
- Customer concentration analysis
- Employee info (org chart, comp)
- Material contracts (NDA'd list)

**Still lock:**
- Individual customer contracts (until Phase 3)
- Employee contracts
- Detailed customer health metrics
- Technical architecture docs

**Signal :** "You're serious, but we're still vetting you"

**Duration :** Days 21-45

---

## PHASE 3 : Management Meetings Passed

**Add access to:**
- Material contracts (full copies)
- Customer health metrics (NPS, churn, expansion)
- Product roadmap (detail)
- Tech architecture & security audit
- Fundraising history (if relevant)

**Still lock:**
- Individual employee information
- Sensitive IP (algorithms, secret sauce)
- Regulatory findings (if adverse)

**Signal :** "We're comfortable with your team, share accordingly"

**Duration :** Days 45-70

---

## PHASE 4 : SPA Draft Shared

**Add access to:**
- Everything except operational/sensitive
- Regulatory docs (if clean) or risk disclosures
- IP assignment agreements
- All employee contracts

**Still lock:**
- Earnout / retention bonus details (keep surprise)
- CEO negotiation notes

**Signal :** "We're closing, last transparency checkpoint"

**Duration :** Days 70-91 (closing)

## Red Flags : What NOT to Share (Ever)

- ❌ Personal financial info (founder personal accounts)
- ❌ Confidential buyer analysis ("If buyer X acquired us, we'd integrate like...")
- ❌ Negotiation notes ("Buyer Y lowballed at...")
- ❌ Emotional content (founder journal, exec emails venting)
- ❌ Unverified allegations (customer complaint emails, litigation threats)

---

## Tactical Moves

### Move 1 : "Slow Release" of Bad News
```
If you have a legal issue or customer concentration problem:

Week 1 (teaser): No mention
Week 2 (LOI): Soft mention ("We have some items in disclosure schedule")
Week 3 (diligence): Full disclosure with fix
```
→ Buyer less shocked, already mentally prepared

### Move 2 : "Quick Release" of Good News
```
If you have a great partnership deal or customer win coming:

Week 1 (teaser): "We have a material partnership in late stages"
Week 2 (LOI): Full details + revenue impact
```
→ Valuation anchors high, buyer excited

### Move 3 : "Customer Reference Control"
```
Pre-LOI: Buyer can't call customers
LOI signed: Buyer can call references (YOUR list of 3 vetted customers)
DD phase: Buyer calls all customers (unvetted)
```
→ You control narrative early, natural revelation later

---

## Data Room Red Flags (Buyer perspective)

If buyer asks for:
- ❌ **"Full customer contracts"** before LOI → Too early, they're fishing
- ❌ **"Employee email access"** → Unusual, red flag about their intent
- ❌ **"Shareholder cap table"** → Standard, ok
- ✅ **"Customer health dashboard"** → Reasonable, they're serious

If you deny reasonable request:
- "Not until LOI" → fine
- "Not at all" → buyer thinks you're hiding (it'll come out)

---

## Pro Tips

1. **Version control your data room** : If you release v1 with typos, v2 corrected, buyer notices → looks sloppy
2. **Redact but name** : Don't hide customer names until LOI, but redact contract terms
3. **Index everything** : Buyer shouldn't have to hunt. Folder 003_LEGAL, item 5, page 12.
4. **Make one person the gatekeeper** : You, not your team. Consistency.
5. **Track access logs** : Who accessed what, when. Helps DD and negotiations later.

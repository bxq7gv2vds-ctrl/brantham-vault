# PLAYBOOK: WALK-AWAY TRIGGERS — WHEN TO SAY NO

**The strongest negotiating position is willingness to walk away. Define your non-negotiables upfront. When a buyer crosses a line, you walk — no exceptions.**

---

## PRINCIPLE: WALKING AWAY IS A STRATEGY

**Walking away is hard.** You'll feel pressure (from advisors, board, investor expectations). Define your walk-away criteria NOW, while clear-headed. Then, if triggered, execute without emotion.

**Why walking away is powerful:**
1. **Credibility:** Buyers know you're serious; won't try to low-ball
2. **Optionality:** You can keep growing independently or talk to better buyers
3. **Patience:** Sellers who walk away often get better offers later (buyer realizes they lost a good deal)

---

## SECTION A: FINANCIAL WALK-AWAY TRIGGERS

### Trigger #1: Total Enterprise Value Below Floor

**Define your floor:** Below this number, you decline immediately.

```
Example:
- Fair value: $[100]M
- Stretch value: $[120–130]M
- FLOOR: $[70]M

If offer < $[70]M, you walk.
Reason: Opportunity cost too high. You can grow to $[150]M in 3 years @ 40% growth.
```

**How to calculate your floor:**
```
Your 3-year projected value = Current ARR × [3-year growth] × [multiple at year 3]

Example:
- Current ARR: $[10]M
- Year 3 ARR (at 35% YoY growth): $[10]M × 1.35^3 = $[24.6]M
- Multiple at Year 3 (maturity): 6x (vs. 8x today)
- Year 3 valuation: $[24.6]M × 6x = $[147.6]M
- Discount to present (3 years, 15% WACC): $[147.6]M / 1.15^3 = $[97]M

So your floor is ~$[97]M. Below that, you're better off growing independently.
```

---

### Trigger #2: Earnout Too High / Unachievable

**Define achievability threshold:**

```
Example:
- Your confidence you'll hit target: 60%
- Walk-away: If earnout is >20% of total value AND confidence < 70%
- Translation: "I'll only take earnout if I'm 70%+ sure I'll hit it"

If buyer insists on $[X]M earnout that you only have 50% confidence in:
→ WALK. The "expected value" is low; not worth the risk.
```

**Earnout red flags (walk if you see):**
- ❌ Targets set by buyer with no seller input (setup for clawback)
- ❌ Earnout >40% of total deal value (too much "faith-based" value)
- ❌ Measurement is vague (no 3rd party auditor; buyer decides)
- ❌ Clawback triggers are broad ("any breach of reps" = buyer can claw back for minor issues)
- ❌ Earnout payments delayed >6 months after measurement (you wait forever)

---

### Trigger #3: Escrow / Indemnification Too Harsh

**Define your comfort level:**

```
Example:
- Max escrow: 20% of purchase price
- Max indemnification cap: $[15]M (15% of deal value)
- Reps survival period: 18 months (not 24–36)
- Per-claim basket: $[10]K (not $[50]K)

If buyer wants:
- 30% escrow (too high) → WALK
- 36-month survival (too long) → WALK
- Unlimited indemnification cap (too risky) → WALK
```

**Why these matter:**
- High escrow = your money locked up for months; risky if company struggles
- Long survival = litigation risk extends for 3 years
- High cap = seller can be sued for millions; no ceiling on liability

## SECTION B: CONTROL / STRATEGIC WALK-AWAY TRIGGERS

### Trigger #4: Product Will Be Shut Down / Killed

**Red flag: Buyer explicitly says they'll kill your product.**

```
Examples of walk-away scenarios:
- Buyer: "Nice product, but we're sunsetting it and migrating customers to ours."
  → Your team has no future; product dies
  → WALK

- Buyer: "We'll keep it for 2 years, then shut it down."
  → Sunset clause limits product future
  → Consider walking (unless you're OK with 2-year runway)

- Buyer: "We'll fold it into our platform."
  → Your product identity lost; employees absorbed; likely layoffs
  → WALK if you care about your product legacy
```

**What to accept:**
- ✅ "We'll keep as separate offering for 3+ years"
- ✅ "We'll maintain a separate brand/team"
- ✅ "Product roadmap will be independent"

---

### Trigger #5: You (Founder/CEO) Get Fired or Demoted

**Red flag: Buyer won't commit to your role.**

```
Examples:
- Buyer: "You'll be VP Product (not CEO) reporting to our COO."
  → You're demoted; loss of autonomy
  → WALK if you want to stay CEO

- Buyer: "We'll keep you for 12 months, then transition."
  → You'll be fired post-earnout period
  → WALK (your earnout is at risk if you're forced out)

- Buyer: "Equity refresh at our discretion."
  → You might get no new options; old options expire
  → WALK if not locked in
```

**What to accept:**
- ✅ "You'll stay CEO for [3–5] years minimum"
- ✅ "You'll get refreshed equity grants vesting over [4] years"
- ✅ "You can't be fired without cause for [24] months"

---

### Trigger #6: Key Team Members Will Be Terminated

**Red flag: Buyer plans layoffs immediately post-close.**

```
Examples:
- Buyer: "We'll need to consolidate sales teams; your 10 salespeople are redundant."
  → Your best talent scattered/fired
  → Product integration fails; customers churn
  → WALK (earnout targets will be missed; no payout)

- Buyer: "We'll keep you and CTO, but the other [X] co-founders need to go."
  → Team conflict; knowledge loss; product suffering
  → WALK

- Buyer: "You'll have a $[500]K retention pool for key people, but we decide who qualifies."
  → Open-ended; buyer controls who stays
  → WALK (ask for $[2]M pool and employee list explicitly)
```

**What to accept:**
- ✅ "$[5]M retention pool for named [20] key employees"
- ✅ "Core team (CEO, CTO, VP Sales) stays through [earnout period]"
- ✅ "Standard severance for any non-retained employees"

---

## SECTION C: PROCESS / TIMING WALK-AWAY TRIGGERS

### Trigger #7: Diligence Taking Too Long / Getting Invasive

**Red flag: Buyer is stalling or overly intrusive.**

```
Examples:
- Timeline: Deal was supposed to close in [90 days]. Now it's [150 days].
  → Stalling; either they're losing interest or using time to negotiate down
  → After [120 days], give them [15-day ultimatum]: "Close by [date] or we walk"

- Invasive: Buyer wants access to [all customer contracts] + [employee personal emails] + [proprietary research]
  → Excessive due diligence
  → WALK if they're harvesting IP without commitment
```

**What to expect:**
- ✅ [60–90 day] diligence timeline
- ✅ Access to financial, technical, customer contracts (normal)
- ❌ Unlimited time / repeated requests for more info (stalling tactic)
- ❌ Requests for proprietary research, source code, customer emails (IP harvesting)

---

### Trigger #8: Buyer Gets Acquired or Changes Leadership

**Red flag: Buyer company changes hands; your deal gets deprioritized.**

```
Examples:
- During your diligence: Buyer gets acquired by a larger company
  → Deal is delayed / repriced / killed
  → NEW buyer might not want you; or negotiates down price
  → WALK; restart process with your top choice now

- During your diligence: Buyer's CEO/CFO leaves; replaced by new leadership
  → New leadership might kill the deal
  → Post-close integration will be different (new management might not honor commitments)
  → WALK; too much uncertainty

- Buyer has layoffs; acquisition team is fired
  → Deal momentum dies
  → WALK; start with a new buyer
```

**What to monitor:**
- Keep pulse on buyer's health (news, board activity, leadership)
- If major change, request renegotiation or walk

---

## SECTION D: PERSONAL / EMOTIONAL WALK-AWAY TRIGGERS

### Trigger #9: You Don't Feel Good About the Buyer

**Red flag: Trust your gut.**

```
Examples:
- Buyer feels slimy in negotiations (shady tactics, lying, pressure)
  → You'll regret this for 3+ years (earnout period)
  → WALK; work with someone you respect

- Buyer treats your team poorly (dismissive, disrespectful, arrogant)
  → Post-close culture clash; team leaves
  → Earnout targets missed
  → WALK

- Buyer is in a different vision than you (wants to pivot product)
  → You'll be fighting them for 3 years
  → WALK; find aligned buyer
```

**Trust your gut. If you don't feel good about the buyer, walk.**

---

### Trigger #10: Your Team Wants to Walk

**Red flag: Co-founders / key employees say "no thanks."**

```
Example:
- CTO says: "I'm not comfortable with this buyer. They'll ruin the product."
  → If you ignore your CTO, they'll quit post-close
  → Earnout will be missed (product suffers without CTO)
  → WALK; listen to your team
```

---

## RED FLAGS THAT SHOULD TRIGGER WALK-AWAY

🚩 **Financial red flags:**
- Offer <$[your floor]M (opportunity cost too high)
- Earnout >40% of total value (too risky)
- Earnout confidence <60% (likely won't hit target)
- Escrow >25% of deal value (capital locked up too long)
- Indemnification has no cap (unlimited liability)

🚩 **Strategic red flags:**
- Buyer plans to shut down your product
- You'll be demoted or fired
- Key team will be fired immediately post-close
- Buyer is in a different strategic direction

🚩 **Process red flags:**
- Diligence delayed beyond [120 days] (stalling)
- Buyer getting acquired (deal at risk)
- Buyer's leadership changes (uncertainty)
- Buyer treats you disrespectfully (trust erosion)

🚩 **Emotional red flags:**
- You don't feel good about the buyer (trust your gut)
- Your co-founders strongly object
- You'd regret this in 5 years (walking away isn't failure; it's strategic)

## THE WALK-AWAY CHECKLIST

**Before you sign LOI, define these in writing:**

| Item | Your Trigger | Buyer's Commitment |
|---|---|---|
| **Minimum Price** | $[X]M | Offer ≥ $[X]M or I walk |
| **Earnout % & Confidence** | <40% of deal, >70% confidence I'll hit it | [Earnout formula agreed] |
| **My Role** | CEO for 3+ years, refreshed equity, no layoffs in first 12m | [Employment agreement attached] |
| **Product Fate** | Standalone for 3+ years minimum | [Written commitment] |
| **Key Team** | [List 10 names] won't be fired in first 12m | [Retention agreements] |
| **Timeline** | Close by [date], not >120 days diligence | [Closing schedule in LOI] |
| **Trust** | I feel good about buyer; they treat me well | [Regular check-ins; walk if soured] |

## THE WALK-AWAY DECISION TREE

```
Is total deal value > your floor?
  NO  → WALK (opportunity cost too high)
  YES → Continue

Is earnout <40% AND your confidence >70%?
  NO  → WALK (too risky)
  YES → Continue

Will you stay CEO with refreshed equity for 3+ years?
  NO  → WALK (you're being demoted)
  YES → Continue

Will your product be kept alive for 3+ years?
  NO  → WALK (your baby dies)
  YES → Continue

Will key team (CTO, VPs) be retained?
  NO  → WALK (team will leave; product suffers)
  YES → Continue

Do you trust the buyer? Do they treat you well?
  NO  → WALK (trust your gut)
  YES → Continue

Does your co-founder / team support this deal?
  NO  → WALK (your team will regret it)
  YES → SIGN LOI

                    ↓
            YOU'RE CONFIDENT
            SIGN & PROCEED
```

## EXECUTING THE WALK-AWAY

**When you decide to walk, do it cleanly:**

1. **Tell them directly:** "We appreciate your offer, but it doesn't align with our values/price/vision. We're going to decline and focus on organic growth."

2. **Don't burn bridges:** "We may revisit this in the future if circumstances change. Thanks for the opportunity."

3. **Stay quiet:** Don't badmouth the buyer or share rejection details publicly.

4. **Move on:** Focus on growing your company. Sometimes, walking away gets you a better offer later (buyer realizes they lost you and increases bid).

## NEXT STEPS

1. **Before you start selling, define your walk-away triggers** (in writing)
2. **Share with co-founder, board, advisors** (alignment)
3. **During negotiations, monitor each trigger** (is buyer crossing lines?)
4. **If trigger hit, walk immediately** (no negotiation; execute cleanly)
5. **Repeat with better buyers** (you'll find aligned partner)

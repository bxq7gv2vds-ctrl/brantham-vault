# 50 BUYER QUESTIONS + FOUNDER SCRIPTS

**Utilité** : Memorize 5-10 per buyer type. Never get caught off-guard.

---

## FINANCIAL QUESTIONS (10)

### Q1: "What's your unit economics?"
**Answer:**
"CAC is $[X], LTV is $[Y], payback period is [Z] months. Our LTV:CAC ratio is [ratio]x, which puts us in the [top quartile/median] of SaaS benchmarks. More importantly, LTV grows each year as customers expand and we improve retention."

### Q2: "Your gross margin is only 60%. How do you scale?"
**Answer:**
"Our gross margin is 60% because we offer [white-glove implementation / premium support] that customers pay for. As we self-service, margin will hit [70%] within 12 months. COGS is already trending down [down from 45% → 40% over 2 years], so we're already optimizing."

### Q3: "Your EBITDA is negative. When will you be profitable?"
**Answer:**
"We're investing heavily in growth now (CAC-payback period is [Z] months, so we recover investment quickly). If we cut growth spending, we'd be EBITDA-positive today. But we're choosing to grow. The real question: is growth ROI good? Yes — we're returning $[Y] per $1 spent on CAC."

### Q4: "How much cash do you have left?"
**Answer:**
"We have $[X] in the bank, which is [6/9/12] months of runway. But that's not our limiting factor. We're cash-flow positive on incremental growth, so we don't need outside capital. This acquisition is about accelerating, not survival."

### Q5: "Your customer acquisition cost has been rising. Why?"
**Answer:**
"We've shifted from cheap channels [organic] to more expensive channels [paid ads, sales team] to hit growth targets. But ROIC on the expensive channels is still [2/3/4x]. As we mature, we'll shift back to lower CAC channels. This is intentional growth strategy, not efficiency loss."

### Q6: "Have you ever done M&A before?"
**Answer:**
"No, this is our first time. That said, I've [have a mentor / advisor / CFO] who has done [X] acquisitions. We're prepared. More importantly, I'm committed to making this successful because [earnout / equity rollover / staying on as CEO] means my interest aligns with yours."

### Q7: "Why do your margins vary so much month-to-month?"
**Answer:**
"[Revenue recognizes quarterly / we have enterprise contracts that bunch / seasonal revenue from [industry]]. That's normal for [our business model]. If you look at trailing-12-month average, it's [X]% and trending [up/stable]."

### Q8: "Your cash flow doesn't match your profit. Why?"
**Answer:**
"We have [$X] in deferred revenue (customer deposits), which is a liability, not profit. We also have [$Y] in AR because we bill [quarterly/in arrears]. So profit ≠ cash. But it's all real and recurring. Deferred revenue is a strength — customer commitment upfront."

### Q9: "What's your customer concentration risk?"
**Answer:**
"Top 3 customers are [X]% of ARR. But each has [multi-year contract / NPS >80 / expansion this year]. More importantly, our new customer acquisition has [diversified/accelerated], so concentration is [falling/stable]. We're also intentionally shifting to SMB segment to reduce concentration."

### Q10: "How much revenue is at-risk if [your biggest customer leaves]?"
**Answer:**
"We'd lose $[X]/month. But unlikely because: (1) They just expanded [contract value / seats]. (2) We're their [critical tool / top 3 vendors]. (3) Switching cost is [high — data/integration/training]. That said, we're not complacent. We check in [weekly/monthly] and have escalation paths."

## PRODUCT QUESTIONS (10)

### Q11: "Your product is too simple. Where's the differentiation?"
**Answer:**
"We're [purpose-built / vertical-focused / deliberately simple]. Our NPS is [72], which beats the [incumbent/average]. Why? Because we solve [specific problem] end-to-end, not trying to be everything to everyone. Simplicity is the differentiation."

### Q12: "How much technical debt do you have?"
**Answer:**
"We have a running list of [X] tech debt items. The biggest is [Y], which we're tackling in [Q3/next quarter]. We also did a codebase refactor [6 months ago] that eliminated [Z] technical risk. Not zero, but it's managed and visible."

### Q13: "Can your product handle 10x load?"
**Answer:**
"We tested it. We can handle [5-10x load] with our current infrastructure. Beyond that, we'd need [refactoring / caching layer / database sharding]. But that's good — we can grow into it without immediate capex."

### Q14: "Your product uses [old technology]. Why not rewrite it?"
**Answer:**
"[Our stack / Python + Django] is mature, battle-tested, and [boring = stable]. Rewriting would take [6-12 months] and delay feature work. We prioritize [feature velocity / customer value] over [tech stack novelty]. If you want to refactor post-close, that's your call — but it has an opportunity cost."

### Q15: "How many bugs are in your backlog?"
**Answer:**
"We track bugs in [Jira / GitHub Issues]. [X] high-priority, [Y] medium, [Z] low-priority. We fix critical bugs within [24 hours], high-priority within [1 week]. Our engineering team has [sprint cadence / bug-fix allocation] that keeps backlog healthy. Happy to show the tooling."

### Q16: "Can you integrate with [platform]?"
**Answer:**
"[Yes / we have it on the roadmap]. Today we integrate with [list 3-5 major platforms]. Integrations follow a [standard API pattern], so new ones take [2-3 weeks] to build. If [platform] is strategic, we can prioritize it."

### Q17: "Your UI is outdated. Cost to modernize?"
**Answer:**
"We've prioritized [backend features / scalability] over [UI polish]. Modernizing UI would cost [X] in dev time. But it's low-hanging fruit — doesn't require architecture changes. Post-close, that could be a [quick win / leverage your design team]."

### Q18: "How do you handle data privacy?"
**Answer:**
"We're [SOC2 Type II certified / GDPR compliant / HIPAA eligible]. We encrypt data [at-rest and in-transit], maintain [audit logs / backup/recovery], and have [DPA / BAA] agreements with customers. We can provide [compliance documentation] for your review."

### Q19: "What's your uptime SLA?"
**Answer:**
"We maintain [99.9% / 99.95%] uptime. Last year, we had [X] minutes of downtime. Post-incident, we [postmortem / implement safeguards]. We're not perfect, but we're reliable. Would you like to see our [status page / incident logs]?"

### Q20: "Can we keep your product separate or must we integrate?"
**Answer:**
"That's your call. [If standalone: We can operate as-is, which reduces risk.] [If integrated: We can integrate via [API / data connector / gradual migration] over [timeline].] Happy with either direction."

## TEAM QUESTIONS (10)

### Q21: "Will your team stay?"
**Answer:**
"Yes. I've already had conversations with [names] and they're excited. We've structured [retention packages / earnout linkage / new roles] that incentivize staying. I expect [95%+] retention in first 12 months."

### Q22: "What if you leave?"
**Answer:**
"I'm committed to [staying for X years / seeing the integration through]. To de-risk: (1) [other founder/COO] can step in. (2) Key processes are [documented / transferable]. (3) [retention packages] keep team stable. But if I leave, earnout adjusts downward — that's built in."

### Q23: "Your founding team has only [X] years of experience."
**Answer:**
"True, but we've [shipped quickly / grown revenue fast / solved hard problems]. We also have [advisors / board members / hires] with [X years] of industry experience who guide us. Fresh perspective + industry expertise = good combination."

### Q24: "Who are your key people?"
**Answer:**
"[Name] is our [CTO / VP Sales / VP Product]. They bring [10 years of experience at Y]. [Name 2] is our [VP People] with [background]. Here are the [org chart / bios]. All have equity and are invested in success."

### Q25: "Will your team join our company culture?"
**Answer:**
"Our culture is [description]. Your culture is [description]. The overlap is [alignment on values / customer obsession / execution]. We expect some integration friction, but it's manageable. We're excited to join your team."

### Q26: "How much of this company is dependent on you?"
**Answer:**
"Good question. We've deliberately [cross-trained / documented / decentralized] decision-making. [CTO] can ship product without me. [VP Sales] can close deals. Revenue is not dependent on me having meetings. If I left tomorrow, business would [keep going]. That's intentional."

### Q27: "Has anyone left the company?"
**Answer:**
"[Yes / No]. [If yes: We've had [X] departures over [Y years], which is [below industry average / normal churn / expected turnover]. Reasons: [list]. We've backfilled well.] [If no: We've retained everyone, which speaks to culture and execution.]"

### Q28: "Are there any employee lawsuits pending?"
**Answer:**
"No. We maintain [good relations / legal compliance / HR best practices]. All employees are [properly classified / paid minimum wage / offered benefits]. We've never had a wage claim or discrimination suit."

### Q29: "Who owns the IP?"
**Answer:**
"All IP is assigned to the company via [IP assignment agreements with all founders/engineers]. We have [no disputes / clear ownership] of code, design, and product. We've also [audited for open source / cleared any external dependencies]."

### Q30: "Can your team work with our team?"
**Answer:**
"Yes. We've [worked with outside vendors / collaborated with partners / managed distributed teams] before. We're comfortable with [Slack / Jira / GitHub / your tools]. We're team players and excited to work together."

## TRACTION QUESTIONS (10)

### Q31: "Why is your churn [X]%?"
**Answer:**
"It's [industry-normal / below average / something we're addressing]. Root causes: [list]. We're [improving retention via X / investing in product / reducing churn in segment Y]. Trend: [down / flat / improving]."

### Q32: "How sticky is your product?"
**Answer:**
"Sticky = [switching cost / data lock-in / integration depth]. We have [daily active users / integration with Y systems / customer data that's hard to move]. NPS is [X], which indicates strong product-market fit. Customers expand [Z]% year-over-year."

### Q33: "What's your net revenue retention?"
**Answer:**
"We're at [X]% NRR, which means [for every $1 of revenue from year 1 customers, we retain $X and expand to $Y]. This indicates [strong expansion / good retention / market demand]. Benchmark: [SaaS median is 90%]."

### Q34: "Your biggest customer has a [problem]. Is it at-risk?"
**Answer:**
"We're aware of [the issue]. We've [assigned an exec sponsor / started a project / escalated]. Odds of churn: [low because X / moderate because Y / we're willing to discuss pricing/product]. We're not complacent."

### Q35: "How do customers acquire you?"
**Answer:**
"Channels: [Organic 30%, Paid 40%, Partner 20%, Direct Sales 10%]. Our best-ROI channel is [X], which we're doubling down on. Worst-ROI is [Y], which we're cutting. Customer acquisition is [diversified / improving / optimizing]."

### Q36: "What's your sales cycle?"
**Answer:**
"[X days] on average. [Small customers: Y days / Enterprise: Z days]. It's [consistent / trending down / reasonable for our segment]. No drawn-out negotiations or deal-specific customization."

### Q37: "Can you grow this business faster?"
**Answer:**
"Yes. We're currently [growth-rate]%. We could [hire more sales / increase marketing / expand into new segments] to hit [target]%. We're not doing it today because [constraints: capital / founder time / product-readiness]. You can help us scale."

### Q38: "Do you have a sales process?"
**Answer:**
"Yes. [Demo → Trial → Negotiation → Close]. Average deal: [value], [timeline]. Sales is [repeatable / documented / optimized]. It's not dependent on founders closing deals — [sales team] closes [X]% of deals."

### Q39: "How much will you churn post-close?"
**Answer:**
"Best case: [5% / no churn]. Our churn is driven by [customer fit / product improvements]. Post-close, we'll [improve product / maintain support / expand team], which should [reduce churn / improve retention]. We're not planning customer losses."

### Q40: "Are you losing customers to competitors?"
**Answer:**
"Minimally. Our win/loss analysis shows we lose to [Competitor X] when [reason], but it's [rare / improving]. We win against competitors because [differentiation / price / support]. We're not facing competitive pressure from [Competitor Y]."

## STRATEGIC QUESTIONS (10)

### Q41: "Why are you selling now?"
**Answer:**
"We're [profitable / growing fast / product-market fit]. We're selling because: (1) [Your platform expands our TAM / you have [customer base / integrations] that supercharge us]. (2) [Founder wants to focus on product, not operations]. (3) [We can accomplish [X vision] faster together]. It's strategic, not desperate."

### Q42: "What does success look like post-close?"
**Answer:**
"Success = [specific metrics]. In Year 1: [ARR → $Y, churn → X%, NPS → Z]. In Year 2-3: [scaled to $XM ARR / integrated into your platform / top 3 in category]. This is achievable because [we have roadmap / product works / team executes]."

### Q43: "Is your market growing?"
**Answer:**
"Yes. [TAM is $X, growing Y% annually]. [Specific trend: vertical SaaS is growing faster than traditional SaaS]. We're in a [tailwind / good moment] and riding the wave."

### Q44: "Are you a one-product company?"
**Answer:**
"Today, yes. But the platform can expand to [adjacent products / verticals / use cases]. We have [roadmap / ideas / customer requests] that could drive [Z% additional revenue]. We're not myopic."

### Q45: "What's the moat?"
**Answer:**
"Our moat is: (1) [Network effects / data / switching cost / customer relationships]. (2) [We've solved a [specific problem] that competitors haven't]. (3) [We have [X] years of customer trust / integration depth]. It's defensible, not unbreakable."

### Q46: "Could someone build this in-house?"
**Answer:**
"Technically, yes. Cost: [time + dev + training] = [$X]. That's why we exist — we've already paid that cost. Your buyer would need to [opportunity cost / opportunity risk / delay]. This is [faster / cheaper / lower-risk] to acquire us."

### Q47: "Why shouldn't we build instead of buy?"
**Answer:**
"Build pros: [internal control]. Build cons: [opportunity cost of engineers = $X, timeline = 18-24m, risk of failure]. Buy pros: [speed, proven team, revenue immediately]. Buy cons: [integration work]. For most companies, buy is [better ROI / faster]. For you specifically, I think buy is [strategic because X]."

### Q48: "Can we retain flexibility to unwind this if it doesn't work?"
**Answer:**
"Yes. [Stock sale = you own it fully]. [If things go wrong, you're not stuck]. [Earnout structure lets us prove value before you pay]. [We're confident, but mutual incentives protect both sides]."

### Q49: "What if the market softens?"
**Answer:**
"We've [stress-tested our model / planned for downturn scenarios]. Our business is [recession-resistant because X]. Even in downturn, we'd [maintain profitability / grow slowly]. We're not betting on [hockey stick growth that requires perfect conditions]."

### Q50: "Is there anything we should be concerned about?"
**Answer:**
"Honest answer: [If you're asking, you're thinking about real risks]. Here are the [risks I see]: (1) [Integration complexity because X] — mitigated by [plan Y]. (2) [Customer concentration] — mitigated by [diversification plan]. (3) [Earnout targets] — mitigated by [conservative forecast]. Transparency first."

## HOW TO USE

1. **Memorize 10 questions** that are most likely for your buyer type:
   - Strategic buyer? Learn Q1-5, Q31-37, Q41-45
   - PE buyer? Learn Q1-10, Q21-30, Q38-42
   - Venture buyer? Learn Q11-20, Q46-50

2. **Before calls, rehearse answers**. Record yourself. Listen back.

3. **In real conversations:**
   - Listen fully (don't interrupt)
   - Answer directly (don't ramble)
   - If you don't know: "Great question. I'll get back to you [by tomorrow] with the data"

4. **Red flag: You can't answer your own question.** That means you haven't done diligence. Fix it before soft shop.

---

## BONUS: THE HONEST ANSWER

For every question above, there's a **corporate answer** (positive spin) and an **honest answer** (acknowledging reality).

**Best practice:** Lead with honest answer. Shows confidence.

Example:
- **Corporate**: "Our churn is [X]%, which is [justified]."
- **Honest**: "Our churn is [X]%, which is [higher than we'd like]. We're tackling it by [solution]. It's our biggest risk, but we're optimistic."

Buyers respect honesty more than spin.

# TEASER DECK — 2-PAGE OUTLINE + COPY

**Purpose:** Hook buyer in 2 pages (before they sign NDA)  
**Format:** 1 page visual + 1 page text  
**Time to create:** 3-4 hours  
**Distribution:** PDF (no watermark, no branding initially)  

---

### Option A: Growth Chart (Most common)

```

```

## PAGE 2: TEXT + KEY METRICS

### Section 1: HEADLINE (1 sentence)

**Template:**
```
"[Company] is a leading [category] platform helping [customer type] 
to [primary benefit]. We've achieved $XM ARR with 102% NDR and <3% churn."
```

**Real example:**
```
"Alliance Coiffure is a SaaS appointment management platform for 
independent salons. We've hit $3.5M ARR with 102% net retention and 
strong product-market fit across 12 European markets."
```

---

### Section 2: THE ASK (2-3 lines)

**Template:**
```
"We're exploring strategic partnerships with [buyer type] interested in 
[synergy]. Potential value includes [1–3 bullet points].

This teaser is confidential and prepared for evaluation purposes only."
```

**Real example:**
```
"We're exploring a potential partnership with hair-care SaaS platforms, 
franchise networks, or PE buyers focused on SMB vertical SaaS.

Strategic synergies:
• Expanded customer base (we reach 8K+ independent salons)
• Standalone product retained (keep your brand)
• Earnout upside (you capture our growth)"
```

---

### Section 3: QUICK METRICS (4-6 bullets)

| Metric | Value | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Revenue** | $3.5M ARR | Profitable, predictable |
| **Gross Margin** | 78% | Healthy unit economics |
| **Churn** | <3% | Sticky product |
| **NDR** | 102% | Growing within existing customers |
| **Customer Count** | 1,200 | Scale but not too large to integrate |
| **Growth Rate** | 75% YoY | Decelerating but solid |

---

### Section 4: TEAM (1-2 bullets)

```
• Founder: [Your background] (built [relevant company] before)
• Team: 25 people (7 eng, 8 customer success, 5 sales/ops)
```

**Why this format:** Keeps it brief. Buyer doesn't need bios yet.

---

### Section 5: MARKET/POSITIONING (3-4 bullets)

**Template:**
```
• Market: [Total addressable market $XM growing YZ% CAGR]
• Competitors: [2–3 competitors + how you're differentiated]
• Customers: [Describe your ideal customer + example logos]
• Geographies: [Where you operate + expansion plans]
```

**Real example:**
```
• Market: SMB vertical SaaS ($150B TAM, sub-segment $8B+)
• Competitors: TimeTrade (appointment booking), Square (payments) 
  → We're the Salesforce for salon operations
• Customers: Independent salons (80%), boutique chains (20%)
• Geographies: 12 European markets (focused on France/UK/DE)
```

---

### Section 6: TRACTION (Investment highlights)

**Template:**
```
• Founded: [Year]
• Raised: $[Total] ([Investor names if notable])
• Last round: [Date, valuation, investors]
• Profitability: [Status — "cash-flow positive since Q3 2023"]
```

**Real example:**
```
• Founded: 2019
• Raised: $4.2M (Seed + Series A from [VCs])
• Last valuation: $25M (2024)
• Profitability: Positive EBITDA since Q4 2023
```

---

### Section 7: CLOSING (2 sentences + CTA)

**Template:**
```
"We're evaluating potential acquirers and investors who can accelerate 
our growth trajectory while preserving product and team culture.

If this is interesting, please let's have a conversation. This teaser 
is confidential and prepared for evaluation purposes."
```

## VISUAL DESIGN TIPS (If you're designing the PDF)

### Font & Layout
- **Font:** Helvetica or Sans-serif (professional)
- **Color:** 1-2 brand colors max (don't be rainbow)
- **Page 1:** 80% white space, 20% content (visual impact)
- **Page 2:** Readable text, bullet points (not paragraphs)

### What NOT to do
- ❌ Don't include valuation (save for NDA)
- ❌ Don't name specific customers without their permission
- ❌ Don't include financials beyond headline metrics
- ❌ Don't add water marks ("Confidential," "For [Buyer Name] Only")
- ❌ Don't make it look like a sales pitch (buyer knows what this is)

### Image/Charts
- Use Figma or Canva (professional-looking)
- Growth chart should show 3-year trajectory with clear numbers
- Don't make it cartoonish or overly designed

## VARIATIONS BY BUYER TYPE

### For Strategic Acquirer
```
Focus on: Market expansion + customer reach + retained product

"Alliance Coiffure gives you:
• 1,200+ salon customers (beachhead for your salon suite)
• $3.5M ARR + 75% growth (adds to your SaaS revenue)
• Standalone product (keep the brand, accelerate with your distribution)
• Team of 25 builders (core engineering stays)"
```

### For PE Buyer
```
Focus on: Profitability + multiple arbitrage + growth levers

"EBITDA Profile:
• Revenue: $3.5M ARR | Gross margin: 78% | EBITDA margin: 22%
• Valuation: $25M (7x multiple) → opportunity for multiple expansion
• Growth levers: Sales + European expansion (we have $2M cash runway)
• Multiple by 2026: 10x possible (post-acquisition investment)"
```

### For Acqui-Hire
```
Focus on: Team + engineering capability (not product)

"Our core tech team (7 engineers, 2-year average tenure):
• Background: [Specific founders' past companies]
• Capabilities: [Product type] at scale
• Retention: [Detail post-deal role + equity/bonus structure]"
```

## DISTRIBUTION STRATEGY

**Who gets it:**
1. CEO/COO of target buyer (directly)
2. CEO → CFO (if CEO warm intro)
3. Business development lead (if existing relationship)

**How to send:**
```
"I thought you might find this interesting given your focus on [market].
Happy to have a quick conversation if it resonates.

[Teaser as PDF attachment]"
```

**What to measure:**
- Open rate (if email tracked)
- Response rate (expect 10-15% if warm intro)
- Meeting rate (expect 3-5 meetings per 20 teasers sent)

---

## COMMON MISTAKES

| Mistake | Why it's bad | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| **Teaser is too detailed** | Buyer already knows market, wants price | Keep it 2 pages. Save details for NDA. |
| **Valuation is mentioned** | Anchors negotiation before LOI | Never in teaser. Say "valuation TBD" |
| **Product demo included** | Buyers want financials, not features | Teaser is business doc, not marketing |
| **Too salesy** | Reads like marketing material | Use facts, not adjectives |
| **Missing "The Ask"** | Buyer doesn't know if you're selling | Open with "We're exploring strategic partnerships" |

---

## FOLLOW-UP CADENCE

After sending teaser:

```
Day 1: Send teaser
Day 3: "Did you get a chance to look?" (if no response)
Day 7: "Happy to answer any questions" (personal note)
Day 14: Move to next buyer (they're not interested) OR
        "Let's schedule a call if you want to learn more"
```

## FINAL CHECKLIST

Before sending teaser:

- ✅ Spellcheck (no typos)
- ✅ No confidential info (don't leak customer data)
- ✅ Footnote: "Prepared for evaluation purposes. Confidential."
- ✅ Your contact info on page 2 (email + phone)
- ✅ No watermark / personalization yet (send the same to many)
- ✅ PDF is under 5MB
- ✅ Lawyer reviewed (especially financials/claims)

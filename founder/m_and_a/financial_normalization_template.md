# Financial Normalization — EBITDA Adjustments Template\n\n**Objectif :** Modèle pour présenter \"adjusted EBITDA\" / \"normalized earnings\" à acquéreur (vs. GAAP P&L).\n\n---\n\n## GAAP vs. ADJUSTED EBITDA\n\n**Why Normalize?** Acquéreur regarde (Revenue × Multiple) - mais aussi EBITDA margins. Better margins = higher valuation.\n\n**Reality:** Startups have one-time costs (legal, audit, recruiting) that don't repeat post-acquisition.\n\n---\n\n## TEMPLATE — 3-YEAR NORMALIZED FINANCIALS\n\n```\nFINANCIAL SUMMARY — [COMPANY_NAME]\n(All figures in $000s)\n\n                           2024 ACTUAL    2025 YTD    2026 FORWARD\n─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────\nREVENUE                       2,100        1,200         5,200\nCost of Revenue                420          240         1,040\n─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────\nGROSS PROFIT                  1,680          960         4,160\nGross Margin %                 80%           80%           80%\n\nOPERATING EXPENSES:\n  Sales & Marketing             800          500         1,500\n  Research & Dev                300          180           600\n  General & Admin               250          150           400\n  Facilities/Other              100           60           200\n─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────\nTOTAL OPEX                    1,450          890         2,700\n\nEBITDA (GAAP)                  230           70         1,460\nEBITDA Margin %                11%           6%            28%\n```\n\n---\n\n## ADD-BACK ADJUSTMENTS (Where Normalization Happens)\n\n### Category A: Stock-Based Compensation (SBC)\n- **Definition:** Founder equity, employee options/RSUs\n- **Why add back:** Post-acquisition, buyer will use cash salary (no SBC)\n- **2024 SBC:** $[X]k (Founders took below-market salary; options instead)\n- **Add-back:** +$[X]k → Normalized EBITDA +$[X]k\n\n### Category B: One-Time Professional Fees\n- **Definition:** M&A legal, audit, accounting setup, recruiting\n- **Examples:**\n  - M&A legal: $[100]k (happens once)\n  - Audit setup (SOC2, Series A prep): $[50]k (one-time)\n  - Executive recruiting: $[75]k (one-time)\n  - Severance (let go 1 person): $[30]k (non-recurring)\n- **Add-back:** +$[255]k total\n\n### Category C: Related-Party / Affiliate Transactions\n- **Definition:** Company paid related party for services\n- **Examples:**\n  - Rent from Founder's personal property: $[10]k/year (market rate: $[8]k)\n  - Insurance broker consulting (Founder's brother): $[20]k (market: $[15]k)\n- **Add-back:** +$[5]k (premium over market)\n\n### Category D: Discontinued / Non-Recurring Items\n- **Definition:** Revenue or cost that won't repeat\n- **Examples:**\n  - Warranty claim settlement: $[50]k (once-in-5-years event)\n  - Lawsuit settlement: $[25]k (resolved, won't recur)\n  - Bad debt write-off: $[15]k (credit quality improving)\n  - Discontinued product line revenue loss: -$[200]k (removed, not buyer's concern)\n- **Add-back:** +$[90]k\n\n### Category E: Restructuring / Reorganization Costs\n- **Definition:** Costs to streamline before acquisition\n- **Examples:**\n  - Sales team reorg: $[40]k (severance)\n  - Office consolidation: $[30]k (moving costs)\n  - System migration: $[25]k (one-time)\n- **Add-back:** +$[95]k\n\n---\n\n## NORMALIZED EBITDA CALCULATION\n\n```\nGAAP EBITDA (2024)                                    $230k\n\nAdd-backs:\n  Stock-Based Compensation                          +$150k\n  M&A Legal / Professional Fees                     +$255k\n  One-Time Severance & Restructuring                 +$95k\n  Affiliate Transaction Premiums                      +$5k\n  Warranty / Litigation Settlements                  +$90k\n  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────\n  Total Add-backs                                    +$595k\n\nLess: Benefits Not Recurring Post-Acquisition\n  Grant/founder option forfeiture (positive cash):   -$50k\n  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────\n\nNORMALIZED EBITDA (2024)                            $775k\nNormalized EBITDA Margin %                           36.9%\n```\n\n**Translation:** Instead of 11% margin (GAAP), normalized is 37% (post-add-backs).\n\n---\n\n## RED FLAGS IN NORMALIZATION (What Acquirer Questions)\n\n❌ **\"You're adding back $600k? That's [X]% of your revenue. Seems aggressive.\"**\n\n**Response:**\n- Be conservative: only add back legitimate one-time items\n- Show evidence (receipts, invoices for legal, severance letters, etc.)\n- Offer to segment: \"Here's conservative add-backs ($X) vs. aggressive ($Y)\"\n- Buyer will use conservative number anyway; better to frame it\n\n❌ **\"You took massive salary as founder—can't add back SBC.\"**\n\n**Response:**\n- \"Yes, we under-paid salary to preserve cash. At market founder salary = $[300]k.\n  We only took $[150]k. Adjustment = $[150]k—not excessive.\"\n- Show proof: founder salary benchmarks (16z, Radford Survey, etc.)\n\n❌ **\"You're normalizing away $300k of true costs; those don't disappear post-acquisition.\"**\n\n**Response:**\n- \"Agree. We're showing two scenarios:\n  1. Conservative EBITDA (minimal add-backs) = $[X]M\n  2. Normalized EBITDA (legitimate one-time items) = $[Y]M\n  You pick which to use for valuation.\"\n\n---\n\n## THREE SCENARIO ANALYSIS (Conservative / Base / Aggressive)\n\n| Scenario | Add-backs | EBITDA 2024 | Multiple | Valuation | Notes |\n|----------|-----------|---|---|---|---|\n| **Conservative** | SBC only ($150k) | $380k | 10× | $3.8M | Buyer uses this |
| **Base Case** | Add-backs ($595k) | $775k | 12× | $9.3M | Fair / realistic |\n| **Aggressive** | Add-backs + benefits ($645k) | $825k | 14× | $11.5M | Seller asks for |\n\n**Negotiation:** Buyer picks conservative, seller asks aggressive, settle on base.\n\n---\n\n## FORWARD-LOOKING PROJECTIONS\n\n**Why Provide 2-Year Forecast?** Buyer wants to see margin improvement trajectory.\n\n```\n continued investment)\n❌ **Add back sales/marketing as one-time** (buyer may have different budget)\n❌ **Add back loss on discontinued product** (buyer doesn't care—show ongoing business)\n❌ **Restate revenue retroactively** (\"we actually recognized this in prior year\") — too suspicious\n\n---\n\n## AUDIT TRAIL (PROOF FOR EACH ADD-BACK)\n\n| Add-back Item | Amount | Proof Document | Status SG&A habits?\" (normalizing away problems)\n3. \"Will adjusted EBITDA hold post-integration?\" (margin compression risk)\n\n**Your job:** Make normalization feel like \"adjusting for one-time items,\" not \"cooking the books.\"\n\n---\n\nCréé : 2026-06-13 | À inclure en Exhibit au teaser / pitch deck\n"

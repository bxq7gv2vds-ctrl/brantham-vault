# Concession Map — What to Give, What to Hold

Fill this out BEFORE negotiations start. It's your playbook for conceding strategically.

## Your Concession Hierarchy

| Item | Category | "Would Die for This" | Willing to Trade? | Fallback Position | Equivalent Value | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Price** | Financial | YES | NO | 20–30% earnout OK | $X | Non-negotiable unless earnout sweetens deal |

## Strategic Concession Order

**Round 1 (Opening Stance):**
- Hold: Price, earnout triggers, non-compete scope
- Signal willingness: Legal reps (offer R&W insurance), data access (phased), timeline

**Round 2 (After Initial Pushback):**
- Concede: Indemnity cap (move to 18–24 months), basket (move to 1% from 0.5%), timeline (move to 2yr from 3yr earnout)
- Still hold: Price (maybe move 3% max), earnout KPIs (hard block)

**Round 3 (Final Push):**
- Concede: Non-compete scope (narrow to US-only vs global), founder salary (add $50k bonus), stay bonus (add pool)
- Still hold: Price ± 5%, earnout mechanics (trigger definitions are sacred)

## Leverage Points (When Buyer Pushes)

| If Buyer Says | Your Response (Concession Offered) |
|---|---|
| "Price is too high" | "I'll accept 20% in earnout tied to [specific KPI]. What's your opening offer?" |
| "We need 3-year warranty" | "I'll do 2 years capped at 18mo × EBITDA if you buy R&W insurance at your cost." |
| "Indemnity basket must be 1%" | "Acceptable if indemnity cap is capped at 12mo × EBITDA (not 18mo)." |
| "Non-compete must be 3 years, global" | "1 year, global OR 3 years, US only. Which?" |
| "We need you to stay 2 years" | "12 months at current salary + 12mo acceleration of any equity I'm getting + 6mo notice." |
| "Earnout at 50% revenue growth" | "25% growth is our realistic target. Earnout is 10% of total purchase price, not more." |

## Dealbreakers (Don't Budge)

- [ ] Customer concentration > 25% exposed
- [ ] Indemnity capped at < 6 months × EBITDA (you're liable forever on reps)
- [ ] Earnout KPIs are buyer-controlled (e.g., they slash marketing, reduce revenue)
- [ ] Non-compete + non-solicitation > 2 years for founders
- [ ] Closing conditions too vague (opens renegotiation risk)
- [ ] No R&W insurance (buyer passing all legal risk to you)

## Tactics

**Bundling**: Don't concede one thing standalone. Bundle: "I'll move the earnout timeline to 18 months IF you cap indemnity at 12 months."

**Anchoring**: Open with what you want, not what you'll settle for. Don't lead with your "real" position.

**Trading**: "I'll move the price 5% down if you move the cap down to 12 months × EBITDA."

# CIM — Confidential Information Memorandum

**Use this for serious buyers (post-LOI, pre-DD). Length: 15-20 pages. Format: PDF + deck.**

---

## Structure (Steal this outline)

```
1. Executive Summary (1 page)
2. Market Opportunity (2 pages)
3. The Product (2 pages)
4. Traction (2 pages)
5. Business Model (1 page)
6. Go-to-Market (1 page)
7. Team (1 page)
8. Financial Projections (2 pages)
9. Competitive Advantages (1 page)
10. Use of Proceeds / Next Phase (1 page)
```

---

   Defensibility: [Switching cost / data moat]

  ✗ "Great team" (everyone says this)

  ✓ "Only vendor in category that solves [specific problem]"

## Production Checklist

- [ ] Data is current (within 2 weeks of sending)
- [ ] No typos, consistent formatting
- [ ] All [brackets] filled in with actual numbers
- [ ] No jargon without explanation
- [ ] Customer names anonymized if required by contract
- [ ] Financial numbers match your cap table / board deck
- [ ] Spelling: Company names, people names, titles
- [ ] Visuals: Every page has 1-2 diagrams or data tables (avoid wall of text)
- [ ] PDF optimized for file size (<10 MB)
- [ ] Confidentiality notice on every page

---

## The Golden Rule

**One page = one idea. If it takes 2 pages, you don't understand it well enough.**

Pick the 10 most compelling facts about your company. Let those drive the CIM.

**Use case**: Buyer says "send more detail" after first call → send this within 48 hours.

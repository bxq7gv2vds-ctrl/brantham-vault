# Objection Handler: "Your Tech Is Dated"

**When you hear it:**
- Buyer's CTO: "Architecture seems legacy. Rewrite cost?"
- Buyer's M&A: "Why not modernize before exit?"
- Buyer's diligence team: "Tech debt looks high. De-risk us."

---

## **Bad Response** ❌

"No, it's actually modern. We use [Framework]. Totally scalable."

*Why it fails:* You're defensive. Buyer's engineer knows tech debt exists in every company. You lost credibility.

---

## **Good Response** ✅

**Step 1: Acknowledge the real constraint**

"You're right—we have [X%] of our infrastructure on [Legacy tech]. Here's why:

1. **Customer lock-in** — Our biggest customers have integrated into our APIs. Rewriting would break their integrations and cause churn.
2. **Capital constraints** — Spending $[Z]k/year on tech modernization would've come from growth budget. We optimized for revenue, not architecture.
3. **Technical productivity** — Our current stack lets us ship [X] features/year. Team is highly productive in it."

**Step 2: Flip to buyer's incentive**

"Here's the upside for you:

- **Fast payoff:** You can modernize incrementally (not rewrite) using our current infrastructure as a foundation.
- **de-risks earnout:** We don't burn capital on tech debt. Earnout margin is predictable.
- **Retains customers:** No migration = no churn risk."

**Step 3: Show the plan**

"Post-close, we'd recommend:
1. Year 1: Migrate [Component A] to [Modern Framework] (isolated, low-risk, $[Cost]k)
2. Year 2: [Component B]
3. By Year 3: [Goal state]

Cost + timeline: [Your roadmap]"

**Step 4: Quantify the risk**

"If we tried a full rewrite pre-sale, we'd risk:
- Losing [X]% of our engineering velocity (6 months)
- Potential customer churn (rewrite = feature freeze)
- Blowing $[Z]k on infrastructure work

Instead, you acquire a revenue-generating business and modernize on your schedule."

---

## **Script Template**

```
Buyer: "Tech looks dated. How much to rewrite?"

You: "Fair point. We've optimized for customer stickiness, not architecture. 
Here's what we'd need to rewrite: [List 2–3 key components].

But rewriting now = 6 months of tech debt payoff instead of revenue.

What we recommend: Acquire the revenue engine, then modernize [Component A] 
in Year 1 (isolated, lower risk, ~$[Cost]k). That's smarter capital allocation.

We can walk through the Year 1 modernization roadmap with your CTO if helpful?"
```

---

## **Red Flags to Avoid**

🚩 Don't say: "It's actually fine." (You sound uninformed)
🚩 Don't offer: "We'll rewrite as part of the deal." (You'll over-promise, under-deliver)
🚩 Don't blame: "Our investors forced us to ship, not architect." (Sounds disorganized)

---

## **Preemptive Strike (Use Before Objection)**

If you know tech debt is an issue, **bring it up first:**

"Our tech is production-ready and battle-tested with [X] customers. 
There are [2–3] areas we'd modernize given capital:
1. [Component] — would improve developer productivity
2. [Component] — would reduce infrastructure costs

We built the business optimizing for customer outcomes. 
Post-acquisition, you can allocate capex to modernization on your timeline."

This shows confidence + transparency.

---

## **When This Response Doesn't Work**

If buyer's CTO genuinely thinks a full rewrite is needed:

**You ask:**
"If we modernized [Component A] post-close, would that address your concerns? 
Or are you seeing a specific risk that a rewrite would mitigate?"

**They say:**
"No, the whole thing is brittle. Too risky to build on."

**Then:**
You have a real problem. Either:
1. Negotiate a technology holdback/escrow (buyer retains $[X] for tech debt payoff)
2. Reduce purchase price (account for modernization cost as buyer's liability)
3. Walk (buyer is wrong about the magnitude, or your tech is worse than you thought)

---

## **Tying to Valuation**

If buyer pushes tech debt hard:

"A full rewrite would cost ~$[Cost]. That's ~[X]% of your current offer. 
Would you prefer to:
1. Adjust the valuation down by that amount, or
2. Proceed at current price and we'll do phased modernization?"

This forces buyer to put a real number on it.

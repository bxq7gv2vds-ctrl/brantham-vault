# Deal Mechanics 101 — Price, Earnout, Reps, Indemnity

**Tâches 22-24 combined: Everything you need to understand deal structure.**

---

## PRICE BREAKDOWN (Tâche 22)

**Deal: $10M upfront + $2M earnout + $500K retention bonus**

| Component | Amount | When Paid | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Upfront cash** | $10M | At close (day 1) | Secured |
| **Earnout** | $2M | Over 24 months if revenue hits $[target] | Tied to performance |
| **Retention bonus** | $500K | At close (day 1) | Secured (incentive to stay) |
| **Hold-back / Escrow** | $1.5M (15% of upfront) | Held for 12 months, released if no claims | Security for buyer |
| **Working capital adjustment** | +/- $X | At close | True-up of debt/cash |
| **Total deal value** | **$13.5M** | — | — |

**Real cash at close:** $10M - $1.5M (escrow) + $0.5M (bonus) = **$9M** + earnout potential

---

## EARNOUT CALCULATION (Most Important!)

**Goal:** Tie your payout to metrics you control (revenue), not metrics buyer controls.

**Good earnout structure:**
```
Year 1 (Months 1-12):
  If MRR ≥ $[target] → earn $0.5M
  
Year 2 (Months 13-24):
  If MRR ≥ $[target] → earn $1.5M
```

**Bad earnout structure:**
```
"Earn $2M if buyer's integration succeeds"
(You can't control buyer's execution)
```

**Proration rule (fight for this):**
```
If you hit 90% of target, earn 90% of earnout.
(Not all-or-nothing)
```

## REPRESENTATIONS & WARRANTIES (Tâche 23)

**What you're saying is true about your company.**

| Rep | What You Say | Risk If False |
|---|---|---|
| **Revenue Rep** | "Last 12 months revenue = $[X]" | Indemnity claim if inflated |
| **Customer Rep** | "Top customers are [List], not leaving" | Indemnity if they churn |
| **Compliance Rep** | "We're GDPR / SOC 2 compliant" | Indemnity if we're not |
| **IP Rep** | "All IP is clear title, no third-party claims" | Indemnity if lawsuit comes post-close |
| **Litigation Rep** | "No active lawsuits against company" | Indemnity if hidden lawsuit surfaces |

**Golden rule:** Only rep things you know are true. Carve out unknowns.

**Example caveat:**
> "We rep revenue is $2M, except for [Customer X's pending refund]."

**Survival period:** Reps are "live" for 12-18 months post-close. After that, buyer can't claim indemnity.

## INDEMNIFICATION (Tâche 24 — Most Critical!)

**If your rep is false, buyer can claim money back.**

| Rule | You Want | Buyer Wants | Compromise |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Indemnity Cap** | Unlimited (bad) | 15% deal value (good) | 12-15% of $10M upfront = $1.2-1.5M max |
| **Basket** | $0 (bad) | $50K (good) | $25K (claims below this = ignored) |
| **Survival** | 18 months (good) | 3 years (bad) | 18 months for general reps; 3 years for tax/IP |
| **Escrow-Funded** | No (bad) | Yes (good) | Yes — first claims paid from escrow, then your pocket |

**Real example:**
- Escrow: $1.5M
- Indemnity cap: $1.5M (covered by escrow)
- Customer churns post-close, buyer claims $100K loss
- Claim paid from escrow; you lose $100K of your $1.5M hold-back
- Remaining escrow: $1.4M returned to you in 12 months

## WORKING CAPITAL ADJUSTMENT (True-Up)

**Goal:** Buyer pays for debt you had at close.

**Mechanism:**
```
At close, you owed: $500K debt (buyer pays you this back)
You had: $200K cash (buyer deducts from purchase price)

Net adjustment: +$500K - $200K = +$300K bonus for you
(Or negative, depending on debt/cash position)
```

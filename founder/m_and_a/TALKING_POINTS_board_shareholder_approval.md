# Board/Shareholder Approval Talking Points

Use this to get board approval for the M&A deal.

## Slide 1: The Ask (1 min)

> **We're seeking board approval to proceed with acquisition by [Buyer] at $[X] valuation.**
>
> This unlocks immediate liquidity for shareholders and positions [Company] for [strategic outcome: market leadership, global scale, profitability].

---

## Slide 2: Market Context & Timing (1.5 min)

### The Why Now

> We identified this opportunity because:
> 1. **Market consolidation:** [Buyer] has acquired [X competitors]; they control [% market share]
> 2. **Our growth plateau:** We've hit [describe growth slowdown: saturation, competition, capex wall, founder burnout]
> 3. **[Buyer]'s appetite:** They're actively acquiring in our space and can pay strategic multiples
> 4. **Timing window:** This offer window may not last [because: their board may change, market conditions shift, competing bids expire]

---

## Slide 3: Deal Overview (2 min)

| Item | Details |
|---|---|
| **Buyer** | [Name], [stage: public/PE/strategic], $[revenue], market leader in [category] |
| **Valuation** | $[X] (acquisition price) |
| **Implied Multiple** | [X]x EBITDA (vs [comparable multiples]) |
| **Composition** | $[Y] cash + $[Z] earnout (over [years] if [conditions]) |
| **Implied IRR** | [If VC-backed] [IRR]% for investors from initial investment |
| **Timeline** | LOI → DD (8-12 weeks) → Close (4-6 weeks) = [total 4-6 months] |

---

## Slide 4: Why This Buyer? (2 min)

> [Buyer] is a strategic choice because:
>
> **Market Synergies:**
> - They have [X] customers in [segment]; our product will be useful to [% of them]
> - Cross-selling opportunity: our [product] into their [customer base] = [revenue uplift estimate: $X–Y]
> - [Buyer]'s [distribution / technology / team] accelerates our [roadmap goal]
>
> **Financial Synergies:**
> - Cost of goods (COGS) reduction: [They own hosting / payment processing], saving [cost estimate: $X/yr]
> - Sales efficiency: [They have enterprise sales team], eliminating our need to hire [X headcount]
> - Operating margin improvement: [From X% to Y%] within [timeline]
>
> **Strategic Fit:**
> - We share vision for [market outcome]
> - They've invested in [product category]; our IP extends their capability
> - No cultural mismatch (I've met their leadership; they respect [your product/team/vision])

---

## Slide 5: Alternative Scenarios (1.5 min)

### What If We Don't Sell?

| Path | Upside | Downside | Probability |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Stay independent** | $[X–Y] in 3 years (if we achieve targets) | Requires [capex/hires/time], dilutes existing shareholders in next round, founder burnout | 30% |
| **Raise Series [X]** | Maintain control, extend runway | Dilution of [%], valuation likely < current offer | 40% (but terms uncertain) |
| **Seller financing** | [Similar to acquisition] | Customer concentration risk, no buyer support, stuck | 10% |
| **Competitor acquires us** | Potentially higher valuation | Less strategic fit, less supportive of [product/team] | 20% |

**Board analysis:** "Selling now to [Buyer] locks in value and avoids execution risk of independent path."

---

## Slide 6: Management Continuity (1 min)

> **Post-Close Structure:**
> - [Founder]: [Title] through [date], reporting to [Buyer's lead]. Compensation: $[X] + [equity/earn-out details]
> - [CTO]: [Title] with [Buyer's engineering lead]
> - [Sales lead]: Integrates into [Buyer]'s sales organization
>
> [Buyer] has committed to:
> - Keeping core team intact (no forced departures)
> - Honoring [any employment contracts / equity terms]
> - Maintaining product roadmap for [X quarters]

## Slide 8: Deal Risks & Mitigants (2 min)

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| **Customer churn post-close** | Lose [% ARR] if [Top 3 customers] leave | We've secured change-of-control waivers for [customer names]; [Buyer] CSM plan in place |
| **Earnout miss** | Founder loses $[Z]; board unhappy | Earnout tied to [realistic metrics: revenue, not margin]. We've hit [metric] for [X years] |
| **Integration failure** | Team departure, product stagnation | [Buyer] has [X companies] successfully integrated; 100-day playbook in place |
| **Financing contingency** | Deal falls through | [Buyer] is [PE / public], fully funded; no financing condition; only board approval needed |

## Slide 10: Board Motion (Final Slide)

> **Motion:** [Board] approves the proposed acquisition of [Company] by [Buyer] at $[X] valuation, on substantially the same terms outlined above.
>
> **Conditions:** Approval is conditional on:
> 1. Board approval of final LOI terms (to be presented at next board meeting)
> 2. No Material Adverse Change before close
> 3. [Founder] maintaining CEO role through close

**Voting:** ☐ Aye ☐ Nay ☐ Abstain

## Talking Points for Tough Questions

### "Why didn't you shop this more?"

> We did [quietly reached out to X potential buyers over X months]. [Buyer] emerged as the best fit because [specific reason: strategic synergy, cultural fit, terms]. We can explore other buyers [through LOI nonexclusivity / after close if earnout beats], but [Buyer] is our primary option.

### "Is this a forced sale? Are you okay with the valuation?"

> I'm genuinely excited about this. [I've met their team / I believe in their product / I see the synergies]. The valuation is [fair based on comps / above our internal targets / achieves our goals]. I've committed to [stay X months / take earnout / buy equity] to demonstrate confidence.

### "What if [competitor] comes in with a higher offer?"

> The LOI will have a [go-shop clause / X-day shop period] allowing us to solicit competing bids. If a superior offer emerges, we can pursue it. But [Buyer]'s terms are [already competitive / give us the most certainty].

### "What about [customer concentration / churn risk / tech debt]?"

> We've [identified the risk / built mitigation into the deal]. [Buyer] is taking [those risks / earnout protection]. The earnout is structured to [reward us if we hit targets / protect [Buyer] if churn is worse than expected].

### "Why is so much of the valuation in earnout?"

> Earnout protects both of us. It aligns incentives [I'm only fully paid if the business performs]. And it's reasonable [because we've hit our targets consistently]. But I've pushed to [minimize earnout / shorten the period / loosen the KPIs].

## Red Flags to Address (If Board Raises Them)

| Red Flag | Your Response |
|---|---|
| "You haven't talked to any other buyers" | We have / we will in exclusive negotiation / [Buyer] is best fit |
| "[Buyer]'s valuation is a 3x step-down from last funding round" | [Market has changed / we need liquidity / this is still a strong return] |
| "Why do you trust the earnout will happen?" | We've [structured it conservatively / bought earnout insurance / made it buyer-controlled] |
| "I'm worried about cultural integration" | [I'll stay X months / we've met their team / they've invested post-acquisition] |
| "This seems rushed" | We've been evaluating for [X months / quietly], and timing is right because [X] |

---

**Pro Tip: Get your lawyer and M&A advisor to review these talking points before the board meeting. They'll catch any legal/financial statements that need nuance.**

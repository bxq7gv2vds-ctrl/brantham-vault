# Buyer Scoring Matrix — Fit & Risk Assessment

**Rate each potential buyer on 10 dimensions. Total score 0–100. Target: >70 to move to LOI.**

---

## Scoring Template

For each buyer, assign 0–10 per dimension:

| Dimension | Score (0–10) | Notes |
|-----------|--------------|-------|
| **Strategic Fit** | — | Does their platform/strategy need you? |
| **Financial Health** | — | Can they actually close this deal? |
| **Cultural Alignment** | — | Team compatibility; founders' philosophy alignment |
| **Timeline Clarity** | — | Realistic? Or vague (bad sign)? |
| **Integration Plan** | — | Do they have a *real* post-close plan? |
| **Earnout Likelihood** | — | Can the earnout targets realistically be hit? |
| **Customer Retention Risk** | — | Will your customers stay post-close? |
| **Founder Role** | — | Will they honor your role / give you autonomy? |
| **Price Momentum** | — | Are they serious or fishing for info? |
| **Reference Checks** | — | What do former founders say about them? |
| | **TOTAL** | **/100** |

---

## Scoring Guide

### Strategic Fit (0–10)

- **10:** Direct vertical consolidation. Your product = their next feature. Clear synergy.
- **8:** Adjacent vertical. Some overlap, significant cross-sell.
- **6:** Horizontal acquihire. They want the team, product is bonus.
- **3:** Conglomerate "we buy software companies." No real synergy.
- **0:** Completely orthogonal acquisition (desperation buy).

**Red flag:** If they can't articulate a specific strategic reason for the acquisition in their first call.

---

### Financial Health (0–10)

- **10:** IPO-public or late-stage VC (Series D+, $100M+ ARR). Proven acquisition track record.
- **8:** Growth-stage (Series B/C, $10–100M ARR). Multiple acquisitions, clear M&A budget.
- **6:** Early-stage (Series A, $1–10M ARR). Funded, capable of integrating.
- **3:** Well-funded but in pivot mode. Might run out of money.
- **0:** Burning cash, layoffs announced, or underfunded.

**Due diligence:** Ask them directly: "What's your M&A budget for this year?" Listen for hesitation.

---

### Cultural Alignment (0–10)

- **10:** Founder-friendly. You know former founders they acquired. Story aligns with yours.
- **8:** Data-driven, startup experience. CEO was founder once. Respects autonomy.
- **6:** Professional, professional. No red flags. Neutral vibe.
- **3:** Corporatey. Risk-averse. Lots of sign-offs required.
- **0:** Adversarial. Penny-pinching. Micro-manages.

**Test:** Do a 1-hour call with their Head of Product or CEO, not just BD. How do *they* talk about former acquisitions?

---

### Timeline Clarity (0–10)

- **10:** "We want to close in 90 days. Legal team is ready. Here's the process." Specific dates.
- **8:** "90–120 days. Typical for us." Predictable.
- **6:** "It depends on due diligence. Probably 4–6 months."
- **3:** "Vague. Might be quick, might be slow."
- **0:** "Let's keep talking. No timeline yet." = They're still researching.

**Yellow flag:** If they won't commit to a timeline by call 2, they're not serious.

---

### Integration Plan (0–10)

- **10:** They've acquired similar companies. Product roadmap is clear (6–12m forward). Knows which of your features to keep/deprecate.
- **8:** "We'll [specific action]: merge your [feature] into our platform in [timeframe]."
- **6:** General plan, but unclear execution.
- **3:** "We're still figuring it out." No plan.
- **0:** "We haven't really thought about it." = Red flag.

**Drill down:** Ask them: "Which of our features do you want to keep? Deprecate? Why?"

---

### Earnout Likelihood (0–10)

- **10:** Earnout targets are 80% achievable under buyer ownership. (e.g., "hit $XM ARR" = realistic given past performance).
- **8:** Targets stretch but achievable. (e.g., "grow 50% YoY" when you're already at 30%).
- **6:** Targets are a 50/50 bet.
- **3:** Targets require external miracles (e.g., "grow 300% YoY").
- **0:** Targets seem designed to fail.

**Math check:** If earnout = 30% of deal value and you have 40% chance to hit targets, expected value of earnout drops 40%. Account for this in your walk-away price.

---

### Customer Retention Risk (0–10)

- **10:** Customers *want* this buyer. You've told them; they're excited.
- **8:** Customers neutral. Assume 95% retention post-close.
- **6:** Some customer concern. Risk of 10–15% churn post-close.
- **3:** Significant customer concern. Risk of 25%+ churn.
- **0:** Customers will likely leave. (Competitor acquisition, bad reputation, etc.)

**Test:** Run an NPS survey asking: "If we joined [buyer], how likely to stay?" >8/10 = safe.

---

### Founder Role (0–10)

- **10:** "You run the product. Full autonomy. Direct report to CEO."
- **8:** "You advise on product. We listen. You have veto on [certain decisions]."
- **6:** "You consult for first 6 months. Then we take over."
- **3:** "You're a PM now. Report to our product team."
- **0:** "Please leave at close. We'll hire someone new."

**Red flag:** If they won't commit to your role in LOI, they don't respect you. Pass.

---

### Price Momentum (0–10)

- **10:** They've made a concrete offer (LOI draft ready). Price is above your minimum.
- **8:** They've given you a range that feels generous. Asking for more detail suggests they're serious.
- **6:** "We think $X sounds reasonable." Willing to negotiate.
- **3:** "Let's see what the numbers say." = Fishing for info.
- **0:** "That price is too high. What's your BATNA?" = Negotiating against you, not with you.

**Telltale:** If they ask "What do you think you're worth?" before they've done any diligence, they're not ready.

---

### Reference Checks (0–10)

- **10:** Former founders rave: "Best acquisition ever. Got [specific outcome]."
- **8:** Former founders positive. "They delivered on their promises. Fair people."
- **6:** Former founders neutral. "Mixed. Good parts, bad parts."
- **3:** Former founders guarded. "It was fine. Complicated."
- **0:** Former founders say "Avoid. Terrible experience."

**How to run this:** Buyers will give you 2–3 references. Email them directly (not through buyer). Ask:
- "Did they honor your post-close role?"
- "Was the integration smooth?"
- "Any surprises (bad or good)?"
- "If you could do it again, would you?"

## Decision Rules

- **Score > 75:** Move to LOI. Buyer is genuine + compatible.
- **Score 60–75:** Keep warm. Explore simultaneously with top candidates.
- **Score < 60:** Polite decline or very low on priority list.

---

## How to Use This

1. **After first call with buyer:** Score them. Record notes.
2. **After 2nd call (deeper dive):** Re-score. Adjust based on new info.
3. **Before LOI:** Final score. If <70, consider walking.
4. **Parallel track:** Score 3–5 buyers. Prioritize top 2.

**Secret:** The best deals are where you *don't* need the earnout to be happy. If buyer scores high on strategic fit + cultural alignment + timeline + role, the earnout is just gravy. If you're betting on the earnout to make the deal work, it's a bad deal.

---

## Red Flags to Downgrade Score Instantly

🚩 **Buyer suddenly vague on timeline** (was clear, now "we'll see") → they hit internal resistance  
🚩 **Buyer asks you to sign additional NDAs** after first one → they're negotiating against you  
🚩 **Buyer pivots their integration strategy** mid-process → they're still figuring it out  
🚩 **Former founder references dodge questions** → something happened  
🚩 **Buyer misses 2 meetings in a row** → you're not a priority  

---

**Final note:** Scoring is *subjective*. The score is a *conversation starter*, not gospel. Use it to force critical thinking, not to automate a bad decision.

## Scoring Guide

### 1. Valuation (0-10)
```
10 = 8x+ ARR, you're thrilled
8-9 = 6-7x ARR, fair for your growth
6-7 = 4-5x ARR, acceptable
5 = 3x ARR, lean but doable
<5 = <3x ARR, insulting (kill)
```

### 2. Earnout Structure (0-10)
```
10 = 100% cash, zero earnout
8-9 = 75%+ cash, <25% earnout
6-7 = 50-75% cash, earnout reasonable & achievable
5 = 40-60% split, targets possible but tight
<5 = >50% earnout, targets impossible (kill)
```

### 3. Founder Role / Clarity (0-10)
```
10 = CEO, 3-year commitment, full authority
8-9 = CTO / Operating Partner, clear scope
6-7 = Defined role, some independence
5 = Vague "advisor" role, unclear scope
<5 = No role offered / forced departure (kill)
```

### 4. Integration Risk (0-10)
```
10 = Standalone operation, minimal integration
8-9 = Light integration, your team stays together
6-7 = Some integration, but team leadership preserved
5 = Major integration, team dispersed
<5 = Complete absorption, no autonomy (reconsider)
```

### 5. Buyer Track Record (0-10)
```
10 = Buyer has exited 5+ acquisitions, all founders happy
8-9 = Buyer has 3+ exits, good rep, founders stayed
6-7 = 1-2 exits, mixed rep, some churn
5 = First-time buyer, no track record
<5 = Buyer has reputation for killing companies (kill)
```

### 6. Customer Retention (0-10)
```
10 = Customers explicitly said "we'll stay" (letters)
8-9 = Customer concentration low, high trust
6-7 = Most customers likely stay, some risk
5 = Medium churn risk (5-10% expected)
<5 = High churn risk (>10% expected) — earnout dies
```

### 7. Team Fit (0-10)
```
10 = Your team excited, buyer culture aligns, zero departure risk
8-9 = Most team happy, 1-2 may leave
6-7 = Team neutral, some will leave (expected)
5 = Team concerned, retention bonuses needed
<5 = Team revolt, key people leaving (deal risk)
```

### 8. Speed / Timeline (0-10)
```
10 = Buyer says "we can close in 4-6 weeks"
8-9 = 6-8 weeks realistic
6-7 = 8-12 weeks, normal deal pace
5 = 3+ months, slow moving
<5 = 6+ months, buyer non-serious (walk)
```

## Decision Rules

```
65-80   → ✅ ACCEPT (strong deal)
50-64   → 🟡 NEGOTIATE (work the terms, then decide)
40-49   → ❌ KILL or walk away (too risky)
<40     → ❌ KILL immediately (bad deal)
```

---

## Negotiation Priorities (If 50-64)

```
Rank the scores, improve lowest 3:

Example:
- Earnout (5) → Push for 60% cash, 40% earnout
- Founder Role (6) → Demand CEO title, not advisor
- Team Fit (5) → Request 2-day offsite to build trust

Target: Get to 60+ before LOI.
```

---

## Red Flag Overrides

Even if scorecard is 70+, KILL if:
- Buyer asks for exclusivity without LOI
- Buyer wants renegotiate significantly between LOI and SPA
- Customer concentration >50% and buyer won't commit retention
- Key person role not defined by week 2 of DD
- Buyer discovers legal issue and blames you

---

## Bottom Line

**Before you sign LOI:**
1. Score the deal (honest, not wishful)
2. If <50 → kill
3. If 50-64 → identify 3 fixes, negotiate
4. If 65+ → move to LOI with confidence
5. If any red flag override → walk

This is the biggest decision of your company. Don't rush.

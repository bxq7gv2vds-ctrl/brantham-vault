# Tax Liability Estimation Template

**Critical:** Calculate your tax bill before signing anything. This shapes walk-away price.

## Capital Gains Calculation

| Item | Value |
|------|-------|
| **Purchase Price (enterprise value)** | $[X]k |
| **Less: Liabilities assumed by buyer** | –$[Y]k |
| **= Equity Value (what you pocket)** | $[Z]k |
| **Less: Your cost basis in company** | –$[Basis]k |
| **= Capital Gain** | $[Gain]k |
| **Capital gains tax rate (US, long-term)** | × 20% (federal) |
| **Federal capital gains tax** | **$[Fed]k** |
| **State capital gains tax** (if applicable) | + $[State]k |
| **Medicare surtax (3.8% if net investment income > $200k)** | + $[Surtax]k |
| **= Total Capital Gains Tax** | **$[Total]k** |

## Adjustments (US-Specific)

| Scenario | Tax Impact | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| **Founder has stock options** | + [Ordinary tax on exercise gains] | If exercised pre-sale, treat as ordinary income (37–39%) |
| **Stock is ISO (incentive stock options)** | – [Lower tax if 2-year hold] | If exercise > 2 years ago, treat as LTCG (20%) |
| **Earnout payments** | Spread over years | Each year's earnout = separate capital gain (may lower tax rate if spread across lower-income years) |
| **Equity rollover** | – [Tax-deferred] | If you roll equity into buyer's subsidiary, defer some taxes |
| **Section 1202 exclusion** (if qualified small biz) | – [Up to $10M exclusion] | Get CPA opinion on this; not automatic |

## What You Actually Net

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Enterprise Value (buyer's offer) | $[X]k |
| – Repay debt | –$[Debt]k |
| – Repay preferred investors | –$[Pref]k |
| – Pay employee bonuses (if incentivized) | –$[Bonuses]k |
| – Pay M&A advisors (1–2% of deal value) | –$[Advisors]k |
| – Pay lawyer / accountant | –$[Legal]k |

| – **Capital gains tax** | –$[Tax]k |

**Example:** $10M purchase → $8M net after taxes + fees.

## Strategies to Reduce Tax Burden

| Strategy | Mechanics | Tax Savings | Risk |
|----------|-----------|---|---|
| **Earnout (spread over 2–3 years)** | Structure deal as $7M cash + $3M earnout | –$100–200k (lower tax brackets) | Miss earnout = realize loss |
| **Equity rollover** | Keep 10–20% equity in buyer post-close | Defer capital gains on rollover amount | Equity vests over 4 years; future tax liability |
| **Stock + cash combo** | Receive buyer's stock instead of all cash | Defer gains until you sell buyer's stock | Buyer stock volatility = tax risk |
| **Installment sale** | Receive purchase price over 2–3 years | Spread capital gain across multiple years | Buyer default risk |
| **Section 1202 exclusion** | Qualify small business stock exemption | Up to $10M capital gains exempt | Must meet 5-year holding period + passive investment test |

## Pre-Close Checklist

- [ ] File prior 3 years' tax returns (needed for audit)
- [ ] Gather cost basis documentation (founding stock purchase, exercises, investments)
- [ ] Identify any related-party transactions (inter-company loans, management fees) that IRS might challenge
- [ ] Get CPA/tax lawyer opinion on Section 1202 eligibility
- [ ] Estimate state vs. federal tax exposure (impacts walkaway price)
- [ ] Calculate earnout vs. cash trade-off tax consequences
- [ ] Run 3 scenarios (pessimistic/base/optimistic earnout scenarios) to know tax under each

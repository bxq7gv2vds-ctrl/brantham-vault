# Rejection Emails — Keeping Doors Open

**Situation**: Buyer made offer, but you're turning it down (choosing another buyer, staying independent, or terms aren't right).

**Goal**: Say no gracefully so they might want to re-engage later, or work with you on something else.

---

## TEMPLATE 1: "We're Going in Another Direction"

```
Subject: [Company] — update on our exploratory process

Hi [Buyer Name],

I wanted to give you a heads-up before you hear it elsewhere.

After exploring our options over the past [X weeks], we've decided to 
[accept an offer from another buyer / continue as independent / pursue 
a different partnership structure].

This wasn't an easy decision. Your [acquisition thesis / team fit / vision 
for the product] really appealed to us, and we deeply appreciated the time 
your team invested.

Why did we go another direction?

[Choose one of the following, depending on truth:]
- The other buyer offered [specific, concrete advantage: e.g., "faster integration 
  timeline", "better earnout structure", "larger distribution platform"]
- We realized we're not quite ready [e.g., "we wanted to hit our Series B targets 
  first to optimize valuation"]
- We got excited about [partnership / strategic investor] route instead

I want to emphasize: **this is not a reflection on you or your offer**. You 
were professional and fair throughout. It's just that [specific reason] was 
the tiebreaker.

**Here's what I'd like to stay true:**

If circumstances change — either for us (acquisition becomes appetizing again) 
or for you (you want to partner on [adjacent opportunity]) — I'd love to revisit 
this conversation.

No need to respond if you're busy, but I wanted you to hear it directly from me.

Best,
[Founder]
```

## TEMPLATE 2: "Terms Weren't Aligned"

```
Subject: [Company] — thank you, and a door stays open

Hi [Buyer Name],

I wanted to reach out personally to say thank you for the time and effort you 
invested in exploring this transaction with us.

We had productive conversations, and I genuinely respected your team's approach 
and strategic vision. That said, we ultimately concluded that the deal terms 
weren't aligned with where we are as a business.

Specifically:
- [Term 1]: We felt [valuation / earnout structure / employment terms] needed 
  to reflect [our growth trajectory / market comparables / founder retention goals]
- [Term 2]: [Another misalignment]

I know negotiations are about give-and-take, and we could have found middle ground. 
But at this moment, [we decided to stay independent / chose a different path], 
because [reason].

**Going forward**: I'd like to keep the door open between us. Here's what might 
make sense:

- [If you raised capital]: We reopen this conversation in [6–12 months] once 
  we've hit [milestone: Series B, $[X]M ARR, etc.]
- [If market shifts]: If competitive threats emerge or customer demand accelerates, 
  acquisition becomes more attractive
- [Alternative]: Could we explore a [partnership / integration / OEM agreement] 
  instead of full acquisition?

I'll check in with you next [month / quarter]. In the meantime, if you want to 
grab coffee or catch up, just let me know.

Best,
[Founder]
```

## TEMPLATE 3: "Better Offer Came In" (Honesty)

```
Subject: [Company] update — another offer came in

Hi [Buyer Name],

I wanted to reach out directly rather than have you hear through the grapevine.

We received another offer for [Company] that we've decided to pursue. The 
acquirer is [Competitor / Strategic buyer / PE firm], and their offer is:

[Optional transparency]:
- Higher valuation: $[X]M vs. your $[Y]M
- Better earnout structure: [describe]
- Faster integration: [describe]

I genuinely appreciated your offer and the way your team approached this. 
You were [professional / collaborative / flexible], and that meant a lot.

This is just how dealmaking sometimes works — sometimes there are competing 
interests, and one works out better for us at this moment.

I do hope we can stay in touch. [Potential future]:
- If this deal falls through, you'll be one of my first calls
- Once we integrate, maybe there are partnership opportunities
- [Specific idea, if relevant]

Thanks again for your time and professionalism.

Best,
[Founder]
```

## TEMPLATE 4: "Asking for Feedback" (If You Want to Improve)

```
Subject: Quick question on your offer for [Company]

Hi [Buyer Name],

We decided to go in a different direction with our acquisition process, but 
before we close the door completely, I wanted to ask your perspective on something.

**Specifically**: What would have made your offer more competitive for us?

Was it:
- [ ] Price/valuation
- [ ] Earnout structure (too aggressive / too low ceiling)
- [ ] Integration approach (we wanted to stay independent longer)
- [ ] Strategic fit (we found a buyer with better synergies)
- [ ] Something else?

I'm asking partly for curiosity, and partly because there's a small chance 
we might revisit this in [6–12 months]. Knowing what would have worked for us 
would be helpful context.

No pressure to respond — I know you're probably moving on to other opportunities.

Best,
[Founder]
```

## TEMPLATE 5: "Door Stays Open, But We're Moving Forward"

```
Subject: [Company] — partnership opportunity?

Hi [Buyer Name],

We've decided to pursue an independent path for [Company] rather than accept 
an acquisition at this time. That said, I don't think this is the end of our 
relationship.

Here's what I'm imagining for the future:

[Choose 1–2]:
- **Partnership**: Could your platform benefit from integrating [our product / 
  our tech]? We could collaborate without a full acquisition.
- **Customer referrals**: If your customers have [pain point we solve], could 
  we work together to serve them?
- **Tuck-in acquisition in 18–24 months**: Once we hit [milestone], we might 
  be acquisition-ready on terms that make sense for both of us.

I'd love to stay in touch on a quarterly basis — even if it's just coffee / 
Zoom to catch up on what each of us is building.

Would that interest you?

Best,
[Founder]
```

## ANTI-PATTERNS (What NOT to Do)

❌ **"You don't understand our vision"** — condescending; burns bridges  
❌ **"Your offer was insulting"** — will make them defensive; closes door  
❌ **"We're getting a better price"** — unnecessary detail; they'll resent you  
❌ **"You moved too slow"** — blame shifts to them; don't do it  
❌ **Radio silence** — unprofessional; they deserve a real note

---

## KEY PRINCIPLES

✓ **Be direct** — don't dance around it  
✓ **Be grateful** — they invested time; acknowledge it  
✓ **Be honest** — give a real reason (not "circumstances changed")  
✓ **Keep it short** — 3–4 paragraphs max  
✓ **Leave door open** — "let's stay in touch", "revisit in X months", "partnership idea"  
✓ **Sign it personally** — email feels more authentic than impersonal  

---

## TIMING

**Send rejection email ASAP** — don't leave them hanging. The longer you wait:
- The more they feel disrespected (they invested months, you owe them clarity)
- The more awkward it feels
- The less likely they'll want to work with you later

Send within **24 hours** of deciding to go another direction.

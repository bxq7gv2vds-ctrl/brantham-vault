# Timing M&A + Buyer Strategy + Types (condensed)

---

## A. Quand vendre? (Growth stage matrix)

| Stage | ARR | Growth | Approach | Valuation |
|-------|-----|--------|----------|-----------|
| **Pre-PMF** | <$1M | Flat | Build, don't sell | 1-2x ARR (if at all) |
| **Early** | $1-5M | >50% YoY | Build OR acquire talent | 3-5x ARR |
| **Growth** | $5-30M | 30-50% | Optimal window | 6-10x ARR ⭐ |
| **Scale** | $30-100M | 20-30% | Still good | 8-12x ARR |
| **Mature** | $100M+ | <20% | SaaS, less attractive | 10-15x EBITDA |

**Sweet spot: $5-30M ARR, 30-50% growth** = buyers most aggressive.

---

## B. Timing signals (sell NOW vs wait)

### Sell NOW if

✅ Growth accelerating (30% → 50% YoY)
✅ Competitive threat (bigger player launching)
✅ Market hot (other SaaS exit at high multiples)
✅ Founder burnout (you're exhausted)
✅ Capital efficient (don't need $50M series C)

### Wait if

⏳ ARR declining (fix first)
⏳ Churn increasing (product issue)
⏳ Key team unstable (hire first)
⏳ Market downturn (wait 6-12 months for recovery)
⏳ Can reach profitability in 12 months (more leverage)

## D. Strategic vs Financial buyers (comparison)

| Dimension | Strategic | Financial (PE) |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|
| **Goal** | Synergies (revenue, cost) | Cash-flow + 5y exit |
| **Price** | 6-12x ARR (higher) | 4-6x ARR (lower) |
| **Timeline** | 6-8 months LOI-close | 8-12 months (more DD) |
| **Founder stay** | Often required | Optional |
| **Product** | Likely integrated | Kept separate |
| **Earn-out** | 10-20% at risk | 20-30% at risk |
| **Antitrust** | Possible delay | Unlikely |

**Choose strategic if**: You want high price, excited about buyer.  
**Choose PE if**: You want autonomy, prefer cash, buyer is solid.

## E. Types of strategic buyers

### Type 1: Category leader (best buyer)

Example: HubSpot buying sales tools

- **Pros** : High price, clear synergies, stable post-deal
- **Cons** : Integration likely, product killed
- **Multiple** : 7-12x

### Type 2: Horizontal expansion (good buyer)

Example: Slack buying Glitch (dev tools)

- **Pros** : Adjacent market, expands TAM, less integration risk
- **Cons** : Cross-sell pressure, culture integration
- **Multiple** : 6-10x

### Type 3: Acquiring talent (okay buyer)

Example: Apple acquiring small AI team

- **Pros** : Less product integration (shut down later)
- **Cons** : Lower price, product killed post-deal
- **Multiple** : 3-5x

### Type 4: PE / Growth equity (solid buyer)

Example: Insight Partners, Summit acquiring SaaS

- **Pros** : Patient capital, let you scale, professional
- **Cons** : Lower valuation, 5-year exit pressure
- **Multiple** : 5-8x

## F. Auction vs exclusive (process)

### Auction (recommended if >1 buyer)

```
Month 1: Send to 20 buyers (teasers)
Month 1-2: NDA + full deck to 10
Month 2: Management presentations (5 LOIs expected)
Month 3: Negotiate LOIs (pick best 2-3)
Month 4-5: Diligence & negotiation

Result: Competition = 30%+ higher price
Risk: Someone leaks before you want
Timeline: Longer (3-4 months)
```

### Exclusive (if only 1 serious buyer)

```
Month 1: NDA + deep conversations
Month 2: LOI drafted (negotiate hard)
Month 3: Diligence (thorough)
Month 4: Close

Result: Faster, cleaner, but lower price
Risk: Buyer knows no competition
Timeline: Shorter (2-3 months)
```

**Pro move** : Start exclusive, if stuck after 4 weeks, flip to auction.

## H. Multiple breakdowns (what changes valuation?)

```
$6M ARR SaaS = baseline 6x = $36M

Boosts valuation:
+ NRR 120% = +1x multiple (now 7x = $42M)
+ CAC payback 7m = +0.5x (now 7.5x = $45M)
+ Enterprise customers = +0.5x (now 8x = $48M)
+ Team stays 2y = +0.5x (now 8.5x = $51M)

Cuts valuation:
- Churn trend bad = -1x (now 5x = $30M)
- Tech debt high = -0.5x (now 5.5x = $33M)
- Competition intense = -0.5x (now 5.5x = $33M)
- Founder leaves = -1x (now 5x = $30M)

Moral: NRR, churn, team = biggest drivers of multiple.
```

## I. Competitive bidding playbook

### Week 1: Teasers to 15+ buyers

```
Subject: Opportunity

Hi {Buyer},

We're exploring strategic options. Thought you'd be interested:
- $8M ARR, 35% growth
- NRR 118%
- 40 Fortune 500 customers
- Synergy potential: $5M revenue lift

Interested? Would love to chat.
```

**Expected response rate** : 30-40% (5-6 interested buyers)

### Week 2: NDA + management presentation to 5

After NDA signed:
- 30-min call with your CEO + their exec
- Pitch deck + 1-pager
- "What questions do you have?"

**Expected outcome** : 2-3 LOIs by week 4

### Week 3: LOI negotiation

Each LOI specifies:
- Price (or range)
- Earn-out %
- Timeline
- Key conditions

**Strategy** : Show all LOIs to each buyer.
- "We have LOIs at $50M and $48M. Your offer?"
- Drives price up 10-20%

## J. Deal process timeline (realistic)

```
Week 1-2: Teasers + NDA
Week 2-4: Initial conversations (5 buyers interested)
Week 4-6: Presentation + LOI negotiation
Week 6-8: Exclusive with 1-2 best buyers
Week 8-10: Diligence (data room, tech audit, customer calls)
Week 10-12: Negotiation cleanup + SPA draft
Week 12-14: Legal review + final negotiation
Week 14: Closing!

Total: 14 weeks (3.5 months) = realistic
```

## K. Red flags on buyer (walk away if)

🚩 Buyer asks for exclusive before NDA (trap)
🚩 Buyer wants to wait 6+ months to close (stalking)
🚩 Buyer's due diligence is lazy (doesn't care)
🚩 Buyer's price is lowest of all bidders (not competitive)
🚩 Buyer has history of killing products (research them)

## L. Final pricing strategy

```
1. Determine market multiple (research comps, similar SaaS exits)

2. Compute valuation range
   Conservative: 5x ARR = $30M
   Base: 6.5x ARR = $39M
   Optimistic: 8x ARR = $48M

3. Start high (anchor)
   Ask for: 8.5x ARR = $51M
   
4. Expect to land at base (6-7x ARR)
   Negotiate to: $42-45M

5. Add earn-out for flex
   "$40M cash + $5M earn-out (2y, NRR >110%)"
   Total "potential": $45M (if you hit targets)
```

**Psychology** : Anchor high, negotiate down, but seem happy to land at base.

# Employee Retention & Communications Plan

Use this timeline to minimize flight risk post-announcement.

## Pre-Close Preparation (Month Before Close)

**Identify Key Retention Risks:**
- [ ] Map all employees by retention criticality (tier 1: irreplaceable, tier 2: important, tier 3: replaceable)
- [ ] Note any active job searches, recent promotion requests, or external recruiter contacts
- [ ] Identify equity holders or beneficiaries (biggest upside from deal)
- [ ] List anyone with expiring contracts or upcoming raises

**Prepare Retention Packages:**
- [ ] Design "stay bonus" for Tier 1 (12–24 months of base salary, sometimes 50% equity acceleration)
- [ ] Prepare role/title confirmations for key managers (reassure they're not demoted)
- [ ] Communicate equity acceleration to optionholders (e.g., "your options fully vest on close")
- [ ] Document buyer commitments to product roadmap, team size (reassure against layoffs)

**Draft Communications:**
- [ ] All-hands announcement email (see template below)
- [ ] 1-on-1 talking points for managers (what to say to direct reports)
- [ ] FAQ for employees (address salary, benefits, severance, new team, integration timeline)
- [ ] Optional: recorded video message from founder (humanizes the news)

---

## Announcement Day (All-Hands First)

**Timing:** Do this BEFORE employees hear rumors or see press. Saturday AM or Friday PM sometimes used to contain weekend gossip.

**Sequence:**
1. **All-hands meeting** (15–20 min, founder + buyer rep if possible)
2. **1-on-1s by managers** (immediately after, next 2 hours)
3. **Email to all staff** (minutes after all-hands; sets official tone)
4. **Press release** (if public; after employee comms; next hour)

---

## All-Hands Script (Word-for-Word)

> "Everyone, thanks for joining. I have exciting news to share.
>
> **We've been acquired by [Buyer Name].**
>
> First, the big picture: This is about accelerating our mission. [Buyer] shares our vision for [product/market], and they bring resources, market access, and expertise we couldn't build alone.
>
> **What this means for you:**
>
> - **Your job is safe.** [Buyer] is investing in [list: product, team, roadmap]. We're not cutting headcount.
> - **Your salary and benefits don't change.** You'll be employed by [Buyer] starting [date], at the same compensation.
> - **Your equity has value.** [If options] Your vested options are accelerating [X%] on close, and vesting continues. [If restricted stock] Your RSUs unlock immediately.
> - **You'll have new leaders.** Meet [Buyer's Head of Product / Eng Lead]. They'll share details on structure post-close.
>
> **What happens next:**
> - We close the deal in [X weeks]
> - There's NO immediate layoff or team restructure
> - I'll stay [through the transition / X months], then move to [new role]
> - [Buyer] has a 100-day integration plan; your manager will share details
>
> **Questions?** We'll do 1-on-1s this afternoon. Your manager has scheduling details. I'm also available after to chat informally.
>
> We're excited. Let's go build this."

## 1-on-1 Talking Points for Managers

**Use this script for each direct report (30 min each):**

**Goal:** Reassure, explain what's changing (title/reporting/salary?), ask about concerns, address equity if applicable.

```
[Open with personal tone, not corporate]

"Hey [Name], thanks for meeting. I wanted to talk through the acquisition and what it means for you specifically.

**The good news:**
- Your role stays the same. You're still reporting to me (or [new manager] from [Buyer]).
- Your salary doesn't change.
- [If equity] Your options are accelerating [X%]. You'll get a check for vested equity when we close.

**What's changing:**
- You'll have [Buyer's team] on [process]. We'll be using their [tools/process] for [function].
- Your manager is now [Name] from [Buyer] (if applicable). They'll reach out this week to meet.
- The product roadmap now includes [Buyer's priorities], but we're keeping [what you're working on].

**My commitment to you:**
- I'm staying [X months] to ensure continuity
- I'm fighting to keep this team together
- You'll have a seat at the table in integration planning

**Any concerns?**
[Listen. If they ask about severance, cuts, or new team, address directly.]

If they're flight-risk (top talent considering leaving):
> "I want to be direct: you're critical to this working. [Buyer] knows that. Is there anything we can do to make you excited about this transition? New opportunity, equity acceleration, [role change], anything?"
```

## FAQ for Employees

**Q: Will I be laid off?**
A: No. [Buyer] has committed to keeping the team. There are no planned layoffs.

**Q: What happens to my salary/benefits?**
A: Your salary stays the same. Your benefits continue with [Buyer], which are comparable or better than our current plan. Benefits start on [date].

**Q: What about my options/RSUs?**
A: Your vested options are [accelerated X% / exercisable on close]. You'll get details in a separate communication. Your exercise window extends to [X days] after close.

**Q: Who's my new manager?**
A: You'll report to [Name] from [Buyer]. You'll meet this week.

**Q: Will the product change?**
A: We'll prioritize [Buyer]'s roadmap, but we're keeping our vision. You'll see details in the 100-day integration plan.

**Q: When do I start working for [Buyer]?**
A: You'll transition to [Buyer] payroll on [date]. Day-to-day doesn't change.

**Q: What if I want to leave?**
A: You're free to go. No severance unless specified in your offer letter. If you're considering it, let's talk.

**Q: How do I get my equity payout?**
A: [Your cap table vendor] will distribute proceeds [X days] post-close. You'll get a separate packet with details.

## Retention Bonus Structure (If Using)

**Typical for Tier 1 (critical hires):**

```
Stay Bonus = (Base Salary × 1.0) + (Vesting Acceleration × 50%)

Example:
  Base salary: $150,000/year
  Vested options at close: $200,000
  
  Stay bonus = $150,000 + ($200,000 × 0.5) = $250,000
  Paid out: $125,000 at 12-month mark, $125,000 at 24-month mark
  
  Condition: Must still be employed on payout date (clawback if they quit before 12 months)
```

**Negotiation:** Keep stay bonus reasonable (1–2x annual salary). If you overshoot, you'll burn cash and set bad precedent.

## Post-Announcement Cadence

| Timing | Action | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Day 1 | All-hands + 1-on-1s + email | Founder + managers |
| Day 2 | Manager follow-ups with worried employees | Managers |
| Day 3 | Optional: all-hands Q&A (video or written) | Founder + [Buyer's lead] |
| Week 1 | [Buyer's HR team] hosts benefits intro call | [Buyer] HR |
| Week 2 | [Buyer] sends equity/options confirmation letters | [Buyer] HR |
| Week 4 (pre-close) | Town halls on integration, stay bonuses finalized | Founder + [Buyer] |
| Close date | Celebrate with team | Founder |

---

## Red Flags to Avoid

| Mistake | Consequence | Prevention |
|---|---|---|
| Leaking news before all-hands | Rumors spiral, people panic | Email all-hands invite Friday, execute Monday AM |
| Managers say different things | Conflicting info confuses team | Distribute talking points 1 hour before all-hands |
| Not mentioning equity payout clearly | Staff thinks they're getting nothing | Explicit: "You're getting $X on close" |
| Saying "no changes for 6 months" | They expect autonomy, then buyer reorganizes at month 3 | Set realistic expectations ("100-day integration plan") |
| Founder stays absent after news | Team feels abandoned | Founder in office next 3 days, available for chats |

# RFI Buyer — Request for Information

Template standard. Le buyer posera ces questions. Préparez les réponses AVANT outreach.

---

## A. Financial & Business

1. **Revenue historique (3 ans)** — breakdown par customer type / segment / geography
2. **Gross margin par ligne** — COGS, allocation OpEx (support, hosting, etc.)
3. **Normalized EBITDA** — add-backs (stock, owner perks, one-time costs)
4. **CAC & LTV** — cohort analysis, payback period
5. **Churn mensuellement** — net retention, expansion revenue
6. **Cash position & burn rate** — runway si pas d'exit
7. **Debt outstanding** — terms, covenants, liens
8. **ARR concentration** — top 10 customers % revenue
9. **Pricing model changes** — historique, raison des pivots
10. **Budget 2026-2027** — forecast, assumes

---

## B. Legal & Compliance

11. **Contracts clés signés** — customer master agreements, vendor contracts, employment offers (redacted)
12. **Litigations en cours ou passées** — settlements, claims, IP disputes
13. **IP portfolio** — patents, trademarks, copyrights owned / licensed
14. **Security & privacy** — SOC2 status, GDPR compliance, breaches history
15. **Regulatory licenses** — permits needed for ops (fintech, health, etc.)

---

## C. Customers & Retention

16. **Customer base** — count, by ACV tier, acquisition channels
17. **Top 20 customers** — name, ACV, contract terms, renewal dates, relationships
18. **SLAs en place** — uptime commitment, support response times
19. **NPS / satisfaction scores** — trends, verbatim feedback
20. **Customer advisory board** — who, feedback, churn risk

---

## D. Product & Technology

21. **Architecture overview** — cloud provider, DB, languages, dependencies
22. **Tech debt inventory** — known issues, scalability limits, security gaps
23. **Roadmap 24 mois** — planned features, investments, headcount needs
24. **Infrastructure costs** — monthly cloud spend, margins if scaled
25. **Third-party integrations** — critical dependencies, vendor risk

---

## E. Team & Culture

26. **Org chart** — roles, tenures, key person risks
27. **Retention agreements post-close** — who stays, incentives, cliffs
28. **Key person insurance** — coverage, beneficiary
29. **Employee IP assignment** — all code / IP assigned to company ?
30. **Equity structure** — options issued, vesting, cap table

---

## How to Use This

### Pre-Outreach (Week 1–2)
1. Compile answers to Q1–10 (financial, revenue story)
2. Draft Q11–15 (legal summary — may need lawyer)
3. Gather Q16–20 (customer data)

### During LOI (Week 3–6)
- Seller provides RFI package + **data room folder structure**
- Buyer sends Q21–30 + request for specific documents
- Respond in <5 business days per question category

### During DD (Week 6–16)
- Deep dives on red-flag questions (ask advisor)
- Buyer verifies with external resources (customer calls, tech audit)

---

## Document Checklist to Attach

- [ ] 3 years P&L (actual)
- [ ] ARR cohort analysis (CAC / LTV)
- [ ] Customer list (redacted: company, ARR, health, churn risk)
- [ ] Org chart + biographies (key roles)
- [ ] Material contracts (sample NDA, master agreement)
- [ ] IP assignment confirmation (all team IP assigned to co.)
- [ ] SOC2 / security audit report (if exists)
- [ ] Product roadmap (12–24 month outlook)

# Pitch Deck (Strategic Buyers) + Targeting

## A. Pitch Deck Structure (25-30 slides)

### Slides 1-5: Hook
1. **Cover** : Company name, tagline, logo
2. **The problem** : 1 sentence + stat (95% of X struggle with Y)
3. **Market size** : TAM ($ billions), TAM/SAM/SOM
4. **Our solution** : What we do (1 sentence)
5. **Why now** : Timing insight (trend, regulation, shift)

### Slides 6-12: Product & traction
6. **Product demo** : 1-2 screenshots + use case
7. **Key features** : Top 3, differentiated vs competitors
8. **Competitive positioning** : 2x2 matrix (us vs main competitors)
9. **Customer testimonial** : 1 quote + logo + result
10. **Unit economics** : CAC, LTV, payback, margin
11. **Customer journey** : Acquisition → expansion → retention
12. **Why customers love us** : NPS quote, retention stat

### Slides 13-18: Business
13. **Revenue model** : Pricing tiers, subscription, expansion
14. **Historical financials** : 3-year P&L (ARR, MRR, EBITDA)
15. **Growth trajectory** : ARR growth rate (chart 3 years)
16. **Cohort analysis** : Retention & expansion per customer vintage
17. **Key metrics** : NRR, churn, CAC payback, burn rate (if pre-profit)
18. **Profitability roadmap** : When cash-flow positive?

### Slides 19-23: Team & execution
19. **Founder/CEO** : 1-liner background (why credible?)
20. **Exec team** : CTO, VP Sales, CFO (1 line each)
21. **Advisory board** : 3-4 industry heavy-hitters
22. **Company culture** : Team size, how we hire, values
23. **Why we'll win** : 3 unfair advantages

### Slides 24-26: Close
24. **Why now** : Market window, competitive pressure
25. **The ask** : "We're exploring strategic partnerships" (don't say "selling")
26. **Contact** : Your name, email, board seat teaser

## B. Strategic vs. Financial Buyers

### Strategic buyer profile

**Who** : Larger SaaS platform (e.g., Salesforce, HubSpot, Slack) wanting to acquire:
- Adjacent product to expand TAM
- Customer base to cross-sell to
- Technical talent
- Market position

**What they care about**:
- ✅ NRR >110% (proof of expansion)
- ✅ CAC payback <12 months (efficient)
- ✅ Key customers (Fortune 500 logos?)
- ✅ Synergies (revenue lift post-M&A)
- ✅ Team stickiness (will they stay?)

**What they dislike**:
- ❌ Churn trend upward
- ❌ Product roadmap conflicts (we already have this)
- ❌ Outdated tech stack
- ❌ Key person dependencies

**Price expectation**: 6-12x ARR multiple (high end if NRR>120%, founders stay)

### Financial buyer profile (PE/VC)

**Who** : Private equity firm wanting:
- Cash-flowing business (path to profitability clear)
- Exit in 5-7 years (IPO or secondary sale)
- Stable revenue + growth

**What they care about**:
- ✅ Unit economics (CAC, LTV, margin improving)
- ✅ Recurring revenue (not one-time projects)
- ✅ Management team depth (can run without founder?)
- ✅ EBITDA (or clear path to it)
- ✅ Market expansion opportunity (can we 3x ARR in 5 years?)

**What they dislike**:
- ❌ Pre-revenue or pre-product-market-fit
- ❌ Tech debt killing margins
- ❌ Founder-dependent (leaves = company fails)

**Price expectation**: 4-6x ARR multiple (lower, but more certainty on close)

## D. Targeting by industry (examples)

- **Strategic buyers** : Marketo (acquired by Adobe), HubSpot, Salesforce, Drift, Intercom

- **Strategic** : Stripe, Square, Plaid, PayPal, Wise

- **PE** : Insight, Vista, Summit Partners
- **Multiple baseline** : 5-7x ARR

## E. Outreach sequence (30 days)

### Week 1: Teaser
```
Subject: Brief opportunity — {Your Company} + {Their Company}

Hi {Buyer contact},

Thought you'd find this interesting. {Your Company} is exploring 
strategic options (partnership, acquisition), and {Their Company} 
came to mind given the synergies on [customer base / product roadmap / 
geographic expansion].

Quick question: is this something you'd want to learn more about? 
If so, happy to send a brief overview under NDA.

Best,
{Your name}
```

### Week 2-3: NDA + deck
- Send NDA (legal review)
- Send 1-page exec summary
- Brief demo call (15 min)

### Week 4: Deep dive
- Management presentation
- Financial model walkthrough
- Customer reference calls

## F. Data room checklist (quick version)

**Folder structure** :
```
/01_EXECUTIVE
  - Executive summary (1-pager)
  - Company overview (3-pager)
  - Pitch deck (final)
  
/02_FINANCIALS
  - P&L (3 years monthly detail)
  - Balance sheet, cashflow
  - Cap table (who owns %)
  - Customer cohort analysis
  
/03_LEGAL
  - Articles of incorporation
  - Board resolutions (last 12m)
  - Customer contracts (redacted)
  - Vendor contracts (key ones)
  - IP assignment agreements
  
/04_COMMERCIAL
  - Customer list (with ARR, contract end)
  - NPS survey results
  - Market research / competitive analysis
  - Case studies (3-5 biggest customers)
  
/05_TECHNICAL
  - Architecture overview
  - Security audit (if completed)
  - Roadmap (next 12 months)
  - Development team bios
  
/06_HR
  - Org chart
  - Key employee bios
  - Option pool / equity plans
  - Retention agreements (if any)
```

Total: ~80-100 files. Organized by folder. Updated weekly during process.

## G. Red flags for buyers (manage proactively)

| Red flag | How to address |
|----------|----------------|
| Revenue concentrated (top customer >30%) | Show diversification plan / customer mix improvements |
| Churn trend upward | Share remediation plan (pricing, product fixes) |
| Tech debt high | Show technical roadmap (modernization plan) |
| Key person dependency | Highlight exec team, succession plan |
| Legal/compliance issues | Be transparent; show remediation timeline |

---

## Bottom line

- **Strategic buyers** value growth (NRR), synergies (revenue multiplier), team (retention)
- **Financial buyers** value cash flow, unit economics, management depth
- **Target 5-10 strategic, 3-5 PE firms** (parallel process = better prices)
- **Pitch deck ≠ selling memo** (deck is pretty, memo is detailed forensics)

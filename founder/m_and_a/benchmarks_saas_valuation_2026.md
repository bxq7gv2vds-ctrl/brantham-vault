## Multiple by Growth Rate (Rule of 40)

**Rule of 40:** Growth% + EBITDA% should = 40+

| Growth Rate | EBITDA Margin | Multiple (ARR) | Buyer Type |
|-------------|----------------|---|---|
| **80%+ YoY** | Negative OK | 8-12x | Late-stage VC, Strategic |
| **50-80%** | -20% to +10% | 6-10x | Growth PE, Strategic |
| **30-50%** | 0-20% | 4-7x | Strategic, PE |
| **15-30%** | 20-40% | 2.5-5x | PE, Financial |
| **<15%** | >40% | 1.5-3x | PE, Acquirer (roll-up) |

## How Buyers Actually Think

### Strategic Buyer
> "This adds $X revenue + saves Y in CAC. I'll pay Z if it's <3 year payback on synergies."

- Multiple driven by **synergy** value, not ARR alone
- Usually pays **4-8x** (higher than financial buyers)
- May pay more for defensibility

### Growth PE
> "If we hire sales team & expand internationally, this does 3x revenue in 3 years."

- Multiple: **3-5x**, low multiples
- Driven by **EBITDA**, margin expansion
- Earn-out heavy (60% of upside)

### VC/Growth Buyer
> "Market growing 50% YoY, this is gaining share, IP is defensible."

- Multiple: **5-10x**, premium for growth
- Often **stock-heavy** (founder alignment)
- Less about current margins, more about TAM

## Red Flags That Lower Multiple

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Customer concentration >30% | -1.5x |
| Churn >10%/month | -2x |
| Founder-dependent | -1x to -2x |
| Declining growth | -1x to -3x |
| Tech debt / outdated stack | -0.5x |
| Regulatory risk | -1x to -2x |

---

## Premium Factors (Raise Multiple)

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Net Retention >120% | +1.5x |
| <3%/month churn | +1x |
| Diversified customers (top 10 = <50%) | +0.5x |
| Product-led growth (free users → paid) | +1x |
| Defensible IP/patents | +1x |
| Strategic moat (switching cost $50k+) | +1.5x |

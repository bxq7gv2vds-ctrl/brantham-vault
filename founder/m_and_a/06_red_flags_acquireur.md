# Red Flags — Acquéreur Problématique

## **SIGNAUX CRITIQUES (Walk immediately)**

### 🚨 Financière
- [ ] **Dettes élevées** : Ratio dette/EBITDA > 4x (risque de faillite buyer)
- [ ] **Credit rating** : Downgrade récent (moody's, S&P)
- [ ] **Actif sale rumor** : "Ils vont vendre division X" → Attention
- [ ] **Restatement financière** : Si buyer a dû corriger ses numbers
- [ ] **Covenant violations** : Si debt covenants are being breached

**Check** : Écris "Buyer Name + debt + financials" dans Google, lis articles presse

---

### 🚨 M&A Track Record
- [ ] **Acquisitions échouées** : Divisées/soldues dans 3 ans (2+ cas)
- [ ] **Founder departures** : 80%+ of founders left <2 years post-close
- [ ] **Killing product** : Achète produit, le shutdown 6 mois après (acquisition de trop)
- [ ] **Cultural disaster** : LinkedIn avis "Worst acquisition ever"

**Check** : Linkedin founders, Glassdoor reviews, Crunchbase M&A history

---

### 🚨 Comportement en Négociation
- [ ] **Price anchor très bas** : "We usually pay 2x ARR, non-negotiable" → BS
- [ ] **Refuse NDA** : Ou demande NDA unilatérale (they copy your idea)
- [ ] **No M&A lawyer** : "On va se passer de legal, on fait ça simplement"
- [ ] **Demand secrets before LOI** : "Tous les contrats clients" (theft prep)
- [ ] **Refuse to sign LOI** : "On veut vrai diligence avant commit" (not serious)

**Red flag pattern** : If 3+ of these, they're either idiots or stealing your business

---

### 🚨 Vibes / Human Signals
- [ ] **Dismissive of team** : "We'll replace your engineers" (culture clash 100%)
- [ ] **Unclear decision maker** : "Le CEO va approuver mais on sait pas quand"
- [ ] **Interest in only one metric** : "ARR is all we care about" (missing risk)
- [ ] **Pushy timeline** : "Decide by Friday or deal's off" (artificial urgency)
- [ ] **No vision articulated** : Can't explain why they want you (panic buy)

**Red flag pattern** : If you wouldn't work here, don't sell to them

---

## **SIGNAUX JAUNES (Negotiate Carefully)**

### ⚠️ Structurelle
- [ ] **Earnout très long** : 3-4 ans (you're stuck)
  - Solution : Max 2 ans, descending payout schedule
- [ ] **Non-compete très large** : "Monde entier, 5 ans, tous secteurs"
  - Solution : "France, 2 ans, secteur coiffure seulement"
- [ ] **Reps & warranties excessive** : "Vous garantissez zéro bugs"
  - Solution : Standard W&I insurance coverage
- [ ] **Price locked at LOI** : "No adjustment for churn discovery"
  - Solution : "Price adjustment clause for material facts"

---

### ⚠️ Comportement
- [ ] **Slow decision-making** : 45+ days to reply to anything
  - Red flag level : High (deal will take 6 months)
  - Response : Set hard deadlines ("Besoin réponse jeudi ou on move on")
- [ ] **Moving goalposts** : "Actually, we need your customer list" (not mentioned in LOI)
  - Response : "That's not in LOI scope, discuss with your lawyer"
- [ ] **Key person unavailable** : CFO on paternity leave, deal stalls
  - Response : "We need decision maker in room, otherwise pausing"
- [ ] **Cultural incompatibility signals** : "Your remote policy is weird" or "Why so many women ?" 
  - Response : Walk if they're this disrespectful

---

### ⚠️ Financière (But Manageable)
- [ ] **Squeezed margins** : "We want to cut your costs by 40%" (integration hell)
  - Solution : Detailed integration plan, earnout tied to team stability
- [ ] **Buyer pivot risk** : "Might focus on different market next year"
  - Solution : "Non-compete can't restrict if you pivot; we need clarity on keep-together clause"
- [ ] **Parent company instability** : Buyer is subsidiary of struggling parent
  - Solution : Get cash upfront (not deferred), shorter earnout

## **DUE DILIGENCE ON BUYER (Do This)**

### Reference Checks (3 founders bought by this buyer)
```
Interview template:
1. "When did you close ? When got paid final ?"
2. "Did they hit earnout targets fairly ?"
3. "Did they keep you as promised ?"
4. "What surprised you (good/bad) ?"
5. "Would you do it again ?"
```

### Financial Check
```
- Check latest 10-K (SEC, if US public)
- Read earnings calls (search YouTube)
- Check credit rating (Moody's, S&P)
- Google "[Buyer Name] layoffs" or "[Buyer Name] acquisition fails"
```

### Culture Check
```
- Read 50 Glassdoor reviews (filter "acquisition" comments)
- Linkedin: Search "worked at [buyer]", read 20 profiles → how many founders left ?
- Crunchbase: [Buyer Name] + acquisitions → 5-year track record
```

## **Buyers to Avoid (By Profile)**

| Profile | Why | Signal |
|---------|-----|--------|
| **Burning cash, no revenue** | Will fold in 18 months | Raised $50M, lost $20M/year |
| **Serial acquirers (>10/year)** | Integration chaos, you're token deal | Can't name 3 success stories |
| **Trying to fix broken board** | You're their savior play | "This acquisition will turn us around" |
| **Founder left founder, not replaced** | Culture is dead | "We're looking for new CEO" |
| **Industry disruption imminent** | Your niche evaporates | "Self-driving will kill taxi" (analogy) |
| **Acquisition of opportunity (not strategy)** | They'll kill you | Can't articulate why they want you |

## **Exit Interview Template (If Walking)**

**Email subject** : "Appreciate the interest, not the right fit"

```
Hi [CEO],

Nous avons apprécié les discussions. Après réflexion, nous croyons 
qu'une acquisition n'est pas le bon chemin pour [company] en ce moment.

Nous restants ouverts si les circonstances changent, mais on va 
explorer d'autres options pour la croissance.

Best regards,
[Your name]
```

**Don't say** : "Your offer is too low" or "You're disorganized" (keep door open)

---

## **The Walk-Away Trigger**

**You should walk if:**
- Buyer score (from earlier canvas) falls below 5/10
- OR they violate 3+ red flags from the "Critical" section
- OR your gut says "I don't trust this person" (≥80% accuracy)

**You should walk within 48h if:**
- They ask illegal things (tax fraud, misrepresenting data)
- They demand IP before LOI (theft)
- They're actively hostile (personal attacks)

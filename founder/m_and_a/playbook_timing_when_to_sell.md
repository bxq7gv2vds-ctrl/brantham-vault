# PLAYBOOK: TIMING — WHEN TO SELL VS. WHEN TO WAIT

**The most important M&A decision isn't *who* to sell to, it's *when* to sell. This playbook helps you identify the optimal timing window.**

---

## FRAMEWORK: THE 4 DIMENSIONS OF TIMING

### Dimension 1: PRODUCT MATURITY (Must Have)

| Stage | Description | Ready to Sell? | Why/Why Not |
|-------|---|---|---|
| **Concept** | Product MVP; <50 customers | ❌ No | Too early; unproven |
| **Product-Market Fit** | 100+ customers; NPS >50; NRR >110% | ✅ Yes | Buyer sees traction; valuations good |
| **Scale** | 500+ customers; $[5–50]M ARR; efficient CAC | ✅✅ Yes | Peak valuation; proven model |
| **Mature** | >1000 customers; $[50]M+ ARR; profitability | ⚠️ Caution | Could worth more if you keep going; PE interested |

**Decision:** You need at least **product-market fit** to command a decent multiple. Selling too early leaves valuation on the table.

---

### Dimension 2: MARKET WINDOW (Opportunity)

**Market windows close. Watch for:**

#### 📈 **BUY SIGNALS — Market is HOT**
- Sector getting acquired (consolidation wave)
- Competitors raising at high valuations → multiples rising
- SPACs or IPO window opening for your vertical
- Strategic buyer (e.g., Salesforce) actively acquiring in your space
- PE dry powder abundant; deal activity up YoY

**Verdict:** Window is OPEN. Consider selling within **6–12 months**.

#### 📉 **SELL SIGNALS — Market is COOL**
- Competitors doing acqui-hires (not premium valuations)
- Venture capital drying up; down rounds happening
- Your vertical slowing (customer churn up, growth rates down)
- Macro conditions: recession, tech layoffs, inflation
- Buyer consolidation slowing; acquisitions postponed

**Verdict:** Window is CLOSING. Consider waiting **12–24 months** for recovery or accelerating growth.

---

### Dimension 3: YOUR FINANCIAL METRICS (Readiness)

**Track these 5 metrics. Selling is optimal when ALL are trending well:**

| Metric | Healthy Range | Weak | Your Status |
|--------|---|---|---|
| **Revenue Growth (YoY)** | >30% | <20% | [Check] |
| **Gross Margin** | >70% | <50% | [Check] |
| **NRR (Net Revenue Retention)** | >120% | <100% | [Check] |
| **CAC Payback** | <12 months | >24 months | [Check] |
| **Path to Profitability** | Clear (12–24m) | Unclear | [Check] |

**Decision Rule:**
- **All 5 healthy:** Sell NOW. Multiples will be highest.
- **3–4 healthy:** Wait 6 months, improve the weak metrics, then sell.
- **<3 healthy:** Wait 12–24 months. Buyers will discount heavily otherwise.

---

### Dimension 4: YOUR PERSONAL READINESS (Life Stage)

**This is non-financial. Be honest:**

| Question | If YES | If NO |
|---|---|---|
| Do you need liquidity? (savings, debt, family situation) | Sell sooner | Can wait |
| Are you burned out? | Sell | Stay |
| Is the co-founder relationship strained? | Sell | Stay |
| Do you want to start something new? | Sell | Stay |
| Are you worried about competition? | Sell | Wait and compete |
| Do you have a 5-10 year vision for growth? | Consider waiting | Sell now |

**Decision:** If >3 YES, you're psychologically ready to sell.

## SCENARIO PLANNING: WHEN TO SELL

### SCENARIO A: Strong Growth, Hot Market

**Your metrics:**
- ARR: $[20]M, growing 50% YoY
- NRR: 125%
- Gross margin: 75%
- Market: SaaS consolidation wave, buyers active

**Recommendation:** **SELL WITHIN 6 MONTHS**

**Rationale:**
- You're at peak valuation (growth + margins = multiples highest)
- Market window won't stay open; consolidation waves are finite (~12–18 months)
- Buyer enthusiasm is maximum; pricing power is yours

**Actions:**
1. Week 1–2: Build buyer list (top 5 strategic + 2 PE firms)
2. Week 3–6: Warm intros via advisors; send 1-pagers
3. Week 7–10: LOI from top 2–3 buyers
4. Week 11–16: Negotiate, choose winner
5. Month 4–6: Diligence and close

---

### SCENARIO B: Decent Growth, Neutral Market

**Your metrics:**
- ARR: $[10]M, growing 25% YoY
- NRR: 110%
- Gross margin: 65% (path to 75% in 18 months)
- Market: Normal activity; no hot sector dynamics

**Recommendation:** **SELL IN 12 MONTHS (after improving metrics)**

**Rationale:**
- Current valuation: 5–7x ARR (decent, not premium)
- If you improve to 7x (growth 35%+, NRR 125%, margin 75%) = +40% upside
- 18-month effort worth it; market conditions neutral so no urgency

**Actions:**
1. Month 0–6: Product improvements (reduce churn, expand use cases)
2. Month 6–12: Sales acceleration (CAC stays efficient, ACV grows)
3. Month 12: Begin buyer outreach (sellers process takes 4–6 months)
4. Month 16–18: Close

---

### SCENARIO C: Slowing Growth, Market Cooling

**Your metrics:**
- ARR: $[15]M, growing 15% YoY (declining from 35% two years ago)
- NRR: 100% (declining; churn rising)
- Gross margin: 60%
- Market: Macro headwinds; buyer activity slowing

**Recommendation:** **WAIT 18–24 MONTHS (focus on product/growth)**

**Rationale:**
- Current valuation: 3–4x ARR (low; buyers see deceleration)
- If you fix churn (improve NRR to 120%) + accelerate growth = +50% upside
- Market conditions don't favor sellers right now; wait for recovery

**Actions:**
1. Month 0–6: Root cause analysis on churn; product fixes
2. Month 6–12: New GTM strategy (expand market, target new segments)
3. Month 12–18: Prove growth reacceleration (2 quarters of 30%+ growth)
4. Month 18+: Resume buyer conversations

## SEASONAL TIMING (When Within the Year?)

**Best quarters for acquisitions:**

| Quarter | Activity | Why | Recommendation |
|---------|----------|-----|---|
| **Q1** | Moderate | Buyers setting budgets | Good time to start process |
| **Q2** | Moderate | Holiday vacations start | Can get slower |
| **Q3** | PEAK | Deal season; budgets set | Optimal |
| **Q4** | Low | Year-end, tax considerations | Avoid (closes often slip to Jan) |

**Recommendation:** Start selling process in Q1–Q2; target close by Q3.

---

## RED FLAGS: SIGNS YOU SHOULD NOT SELL RIGHT NOW

🚩 **Growth deceleration:** Revenue growth down YoY → wait for reacceleration  
🚩 **Churn inflection:** NRR declining for >2 quarters → buyer will discount heavily  
🚩 **Key person leaving:** Expect 20–30% valuation hit → wait for stability  
🚩 **Market downturn:** Recession/major macro shock → multiples will collapse; wait for recovery  
🚩 **Unproven unit economics:** CAC payback >24m or customer LTV unclear → wait until dialed in  
🚩 **You're not ready:** Diligence materials disorganized; cap table unclear → wait 3 months to prep  

---

## GREEN FLAGS: SIGNS YOU SHOULD SELL NOW

✅ **Growth accelerating:** QoQ revenue growth trending up  
✅ **Churn improving:** NRR rising; product-market fit deepening  
✅ **Hot market:** Sector consolidating; peers getting acquired at high multiples  
✅ **Competitive threat:** A well-funded competitor emerging; acquire them while you can, or sell while you're winning  
✅ **Founder readiness:** You're excited to move on; team is strong; vision is complete  
✅ **Window closing:** Market conditions won't improve in next 12 months; sell now

## VALUATION IMPACT: TIMING MATTERS

**Same company, different timing = wildly different valuations:**

| Timing | Revenue | Growth | Margin | Multiple | Valuation |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **12 months ago** (before pivot) | $[8]M | 20% | 50% | 4x | $[32]M |
| **Today** (current) | $[12]M | 50% | 70% | 8x | $[96]M |
| **12 months from now** (if continued growth) | $[20]M | 40% | 75% | 7x | $[140]M |

**Takeaway:** Waiting 12 months to improve metrics → +45% valuation (from $96M to $140M). But waiting during a market downturn → -50% valuation ($96M to $48M).

## DECISION CHECKLIST: SHOULD YOU SELL NOW?

Answer YES to at least **6 of 8:**

- [ ] Product-market fit proven (NPS >50, NRR >110%)
- [ ] Revenue growth >25% YoY
- [ ] Market conditions favorable (sector consolidation or hot VCs)
- [ ] Buyer appetite confirmed (inbound inquiries or warm advisor intros)
- [ ] You're ready personally (excited or burned out)
- [ ] Path to profitability clear (<24 months to breakeven)
- [ ] Financial metrics stable or improving
- [ ] No existential competitive threat on horizon

**Scoring:**
- **6–8 YES:** Sell within 6 months. Timing is RIGHT.
- **4–5 YES:** Sell in 12–18 months. Improve weak areas first.
- **<4 YES:** Wait 18–24+ months. Not ready yet.

## FINAL RECOMMENDATION FRAMEWORK

| Condition | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| All metrics strong + market hot + founder ready | **SELL NOW** | Start in 2 weeks |
| All metrics strong + market neutral + founder hesitant | **SELL in 6–12 months** | Take time; optimize |
| Some metrics weak + market hot | **SELL SOON (4–6 months)** | Capitalize on window; fix later |
| Some metrics weak + market cool | **WAIT 12–24 months** | Fix metrics; market will improve |
| Metrics declining + market declining | **WAIT 24+ months or restructure** | Risky timing; focus on survival |

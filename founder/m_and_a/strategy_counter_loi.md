# Counter-LOI Strategy — What To Fight, What To Concede

**Buyer sends LOI. You have 48 hours to accept or counter. Use this playbook.**

---

## COUNTER FORMULA

**For every ask you make, concede on one item they care about.**

**Example:**
- You ask: "Earnout vests only if revenue > $[X] (not buyer's subjective KPIs)"
- You concede: "We'll agree to 15% hold-back instead of 20%"

---

## TIER 1: FIGHT FOR THESE (Red Lines)

| Item | Your Position | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Price** | Don't move > 15% below anchor | Sets tone; everything else flows from price |
| **Earnout Metrics** | Revenue / ARR only (not subjective KPIs) | You control revenue; buyer controls subjective |
| **Earnout Duration** | Max 24 months (not 36) | Founder burnout after 24m |
| **Founder Stay** | 24 month commitment, not 36 | Can't stay longer; burnout risk |
| **Retention Bonus** | Cash at close, not escrowed | Earned upfront for staying |
| **Indemnity Cap** | 12-15% of deal value (not unlimited) | Protects you from unforeseen issues |
| **Rep/Warranty Survival** | 18 months max (not perpetual) | Limits exposure to old issues |
| **Change of Control Consents** | Buyer responsible for getting them | Not your job to negotiate with customers |
| **Non-Compete Scope** | Direct competitors only (not entire market) | You need to work after this |

---

## TIER 2: CONCEDE ON THESE (Trade Fodder)

| Item | Buyer Usually Wants | Your Concession |
|---|---|---|
| **Hold-back / Escrow** | 20% | Agree to 15% |
| **Closing Timeline** | 8 weeks | Accept 10-12 weeks |
| **Buyer DD Scope** | All files + calls | Agree to customer calls but limit to top 10 |
| **Transition Services** | 12 months | Offer 9 months |
| **Founder's Role Title** | "VP Product" | Accept "Head of Product" (same, lower level) |
| **Data Room Access** | Full, unlimited | Phased access (financials week 1, tech week 2) |
| **Board Seat Post-Close** | Yes | Offer "advisory board" instead |

---

## TIER 3: LET THEM WIN (Meaningless to You)

| Item | Buyer Wants | You Agree |
|---|---|---|
| **Non-Solicitation** | 24 months | Fine (you don't want to hire their people) |
| **Confidentiality** | 5 years | Sure (facts are published anyway) |
| **Press Release Approval** | Buyer has final say | No skin in game for you |
| **IP Indemnity Carve-Out** | "Except open-source" | Fair; you're doing audit anyway |

---

## COUNTER-OFFER TEMPLATE

**Buyer sends LOI with:**
- Price: $15M upfront
- Structure: 10M + $5M earnout (24m)
- Earnout: Revenue > $[target]
- Hold-back: 20%
- Stay: 36 months

**Your counter (2 pages):**

```
COUNTER-OFFER: [Company Name] Acquisition

PRICE ADJUSTMENT
- Buyer's: $15M upfront
- Our counter: $16M upfront (justify: market comps at 8.5x, growth, margins)
- Rationale: Small increase reflects [strategic value / team quality]

EARNOUT STRUCTURE
- Buyer's: 24-month earnout, revenue-based
- Our counter: $4M (not $5M) earnout, 24-month, revenue-based
- Rationale: 24m is founder's max commitment; earnout ties to revenue (we control)

HOLD-BACK / ESCROW
- Buyer's: 20%
- Our counter: 15%
- Rationale: Clean financial records; indemnity risk is low

FOUNDER STAY / ROLE
- Buyer's: 36 months + VP Product title
- Our counter: 24 months + Head of Product title
- Rationale: 24m is market standard; we'll be fully integrated by then

EARNOUT VESTING
- Buyer's: Full earnout if revenue hits target
- Our counter: Same, but if revenue misses by < 10%, earnout prorated (not zero)
- Rationale: Fair sharing of integration risk

INDEMNIFICATION
- Buyer's: Unlimited indemnity, survive 2 years
- Our counter: Capped at 15% deal value, survival 18 months
- Rationale: Standard for SaaS acquisition

---

ACCEPTANCE:
Contingent on: [list 3-5 items]
Timeline: We can close by [date]
Next: Schedule call with [buyer contact] to discuss
```

---

## NEGOTIATION SCRIPT

**Buyer:** "We need 36-month founder commitment."

**You:** "I appreciate that. 24 months is my max; that's when I've typically moved on. That said, let's structure it so earnout completion motivates me through 24m. And if we hit the revenue targets, we're integrated by month 18 anyway."

**Buyer:** "Can't go above 20% hold-back."

**You:** "Fair on hold-back. But here's how we de-risk you: [list 3 controls]. And we'll indemnify you for the 20% if issues emerge in first 90 days. That's the real insurance."

---

## RED LINE NEGOTIATION (Walk-Away Scenario)

If buyer won't move on **any** Tier 1 item:

**You:** "I appreciate the offer, but these terms don't work for me or my team. [Earnout is fully buyer-controlled / 36 months is too long / price is too far below market]. Let's pause and come back when we can find alignment. I'm open to other buyer conversations while you think about it."

**Then:** Walk away. Most buyers will re-approach in 1-2 weeks with better terms.

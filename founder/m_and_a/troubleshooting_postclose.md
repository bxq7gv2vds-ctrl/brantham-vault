# Post-Close Troubleshooting — When Things Go Wrong

Use this if issues arise first 30-90 days. Escalate immediately for red flags.

---

## SCENARIO 1: Key Person Resignation

**The Problem**: Your CTO / VP Sales leaves within days of close.

**Why it happens:**
- Integration anxiety, unsure about role
- Buyer pressure (different culture)
- Better offer elsewhere
- Remote work vs. in-office surprise

**Your response (within 24 hours):**

1. **Understand**: Call them directly. "What went wrong? What would make you stay?"
2. **Options**:
   - Bump retention bonus ($[X]K additional)
   - Change reporting line (report to you, not buyer)
   - Flexible work (remote vs. office)
   - Role expansion (more autonomy)
   - Accelerate equity vesting (6 months instead of 12)

3. **Escalate to buyer CEO**: "We may lose [person]. Here's what we need to fix."

**Prevention next time**: Retention plan locked BEFORE signing.

---

## SCENARIO 2: Customer Threatens to Leave

**The Problem**: Your top customer (10%+ of revenue) says they're canceling.

**Why it happens:**
- Doesn't trust buyer
- Afraid of service degradation
- Competitive offer from elsewhere
- Pricing concerns post-close

**Your response (same day):**

1. **Call customer directly**: "I heard you're unhappy. Let's talk."
2. **Listening phase**: Don't pitch. Understand the real issue.
3. **Options**:
   - Price concession (if financially OK)
   - Enhanced support / SLA (buyer commits resources)
   - Product roadmap commitment (new feature they need)
   - Multi-year contract lock-in (security)
4. **Escalate**: If customer is serious, buyer CEO + your CEO call together.

**Prevention**: Customer calls scheduled pre-close, relationships cemented.

---

## SCENARIO 3: Buyer Cuts Budget (Kills Your Roadmap)

**The Problem**: Buyer announces cost-cutting, your product team headcount getting slashed.

**Why it happens:**
- Unexpected financial pressure (market downturn)
- Synergies identified that kill your team
- Buyer's board mandate ("reduce burn by 20%")

**Your response:**

1. **Ask questions first**: "What's the business case? What specifically is changing?"
2. **Quantify impact**: "If we lose [X engineers], here's what breaks:"
   - Product roadmap delays → Earnout risk
   - Customer churn accelerates → Revenue impact
   - Integration timeline slips → More costs
3. **Propose alternatives**:
   - Defer non-critical hires (keep core team)
   - Optimize other functions first (reduce G&A)
   - Link cuts to revenue targets (if we hit them, we keep headcount)
4. **Escalate**: If cuts threaten earnout, escalate to buyer CEO.

**Prevention**: Headcount plan locked in LOI.

## SCENARIO 4: Integration is Slower Than Planned

**The Problem**: Product merge was supposed to be 90 days. It's day 100, still not done.

**Why it happens:**
- Code quality issues emerged during review
- Infra complexity underestimated
- Buyer's tech team distracted (other priorities)
- Architecture decisions are harder than expected

**Your response:**

1. **Root cause analysis**: "Why is it slower? What's the blocker?"
2. **Options**:
   - Parallel run longer (accept 120-day timeline)
   - Phase the merge (do MVP first, full merge later)
   - Add resources (both sides contribute more engineers)
   - Reduce scope (ship core features only, deprecate edge cases)
3. **Impact assessment**: "If delayed to day 120, how does this affect earnout targets?"
4. **Adjust timeline**: Update integration roadmap, reset expectations.

**Prevention**: Realistic timelines with 20% buffer built in.

---

## SCENARIO 5: Earnout Target Looks Unreachable

**The Problem**: Day 60 data shows you're tracking 20% behind earnout target.

**Why it happens:**
- Sales pipeline weaker than projected
- Customer churn higher than forecasted
- Buyer's integration choices killed momentum
- Market downturn affects customer budgets

**Your response:**

1. **Understand the gap**: "Are we missing numbers or is projection wrong?"
2. **Options**:
   - Adjust target downward (renegotiate)
   - Extend timeline (earn over 3 years instead of 2)
   - Change metric (tie to something you can control)
   - Accelerate new initiatives (fix root cause)
3. **Don't panic**: Sometimes slow start, fast finish (Q3 picks up).
4. **Escalate**: If gap > 20%, talk to buyer CFO about adjustment.

**Prevention**: Conservative projections, built-in floor payment.

## SCENARIO 6: Escrow Claim Surprise

**The Problem**: Day 180, buyer claims you breached reps, files for $[X]M escrow claim.

**Why it happens:**
- You discovered liability post-close (tax issue, data breach, etc.)
- Buyer claims revenue was misstated
- Product IP infringement claim emerged

**Your response:**

1. **Get legal counsel**: Call your M&A lawyer IMMEDIATELY.
2. **Understand the claim**: "Exactly what are you claiming? Here's our defense."
3. **Gather evidence**: "Here's why this claim is invalid or overstated."
4. **Negotiate**: "Let's narrow this to actual damages, not speculative."
5. **Resolve**: Most claims settle at 50-75% of claimed amount via escrow.

**Prevention**: 
- Disclose all known issues pre-close
- Reps audit pre-signing
- Cap = escrow (don't pay beyond holdback)
- 12-month survival (claim window, then released)

## SCENARIO 7: Cultural Clash (Team Morale Tank)

**The Problem**: Your team is unhappy. Buyer's culture different. People feel squeezed out.

**Why it happens:**
- Different work style (rigid vs. autonomous)
- Different values (profit-driven vs. mission-driven)
- Buyer's processes feel bureaucratic
- Salary / benefits changes

**Your response:**

1. **Pulse survey**: Anonymous feedback. "What's wrong?"
2. **Address top issues**:
   - Communication style: Have buyer + your team align
   - Decision-making: Clarify who decides what
   - Flexibility: Negotiate remote work, schedule flexibility
   - Values: Joint culture rituals (celebrate wins together)
3. **Skip-level lunches**: Buyer exec + your team (informal, builds trust).
4. **Quick wins**: Ship product improvements, celebrate together.

**Prevention**: Joint culture workshop pre-close, explicit integration rituals.

## SCENARIO 8: Buyer's Strategic Priorities Shift

**The Problem**: Buyer says "Actually, we're pausing your product integration. New priority is [different area]."

**Why it happens:**
- New CEO / board member with different vision
- Market shift (competitor move)
- Acquisition of another company (distraction)
- Financial pressure (focus on core business)

**Your response:**

1. **Understand timeline**: "How long is the pause? What triggers restart?"
2. **Ask about earnout**: "Does this affect our earnout targets? How do we adjust?"
3. **Propose alternatives**:
   - Keep your team autonomous (you keep running product)
   - Parallel path (you work independently for 12 months)
   - Earnout adjusted for market conditions
4. **Escalate**: If threatens deal thesis, founder + buyer CEO conversation.

**Prevention**: Product roadmap locked for Year 1 (separate agreement).

---

## SCENARIO 9: Founder Role Ambiguity

**The Problem**: You're not sure who you report to, what decisions you own, what authority you have.

**Why it happens:**
- Integration plan changed post-close
- Buyer's org structure unclear
- No clear job description document
- Different expectations

**Your response:**

1. **Get clarity**: Write down your understanding of role:
   - Title, reporting line
   - Decisions you own (hiring, product, budget)
   - Decisions you consult on
   - Decisions that are buyer-only
2. **Align with buyer**: "Is this what you expect?"
3. **Document**: Get agreement in writing (email confirmation).
4. **Weekly 1:1s**: With your manager (buyer exec) to sync.

**Prevention**: Role documentation pre-close, weekly check-ins.

## SCENARIO 10: Data / Security Incident Post-Close

**The Problem**: You discover (or customer reports) a security breach or data loss.

**Why it happens:**
- Migration data corrupted
- Backup failed during integration
- Security vulnerability in legacy code
- Vendor misconfiguration

**Your response (IMMEDIATE):**

1. **Contain**: Stop the issue, isolate affected systems.
2. **Assess**: How many customers affected? What data? Severity?
3. **Notify**: Buyer CEO, legal counsel, customers (if required).
4. **Remediate**: Fix the issue, prevent recurrence.
5. **Document**: Timeline, root cause, fix, prevention.

**Escalation**: This is a red flag. Expect legal consequences.

**Prevention**: Security audit pre-close, SOC2 certification if possible, disaster recovery tested.

## RED FLAGS: When to Call Your Lawyer

Immediately call M&A lawyer if:

- [ ] Key person resignation + can't retain
- [ ] Earnout target looks >20% off track (might lose millions)
- [ ] Buyer sues you over reps (escrow claim)
- [ ] Data breach or security incident
- [ ] Major customer (>10%) threatens to leave
- [ ] Buyer breaches acquisition agreement terms
- [ ] Regulatory investigation / subpoena issued
- [ ] Fraud discovered in due diligence (yours or buyer's)

**Cost of calling lawyer**: $[X]K now
**Cost of NOT calling lawyer**: $[1-10]M later

Call them.

## Escalation Path

**For any issue:**

1. **Day 0**: Direct conversation with buyer counterpart
2. **Day 1**: If unresolved, escalate to buyer department head
3. **Day 2-3**: Escalate to buyer CEO if still unresolved
4. **Day 3**: Get legal counsel involved

**Time is money.** Don't wait weeks to escalate.

## Quick Decision Rules

| Issue | Severity | Action |
|-------|----------|--------|
| One person leaves (non-critical) | Low | Backfill, move on |
| Key CTO leaves | Critical | Escalate to buyer CEO same day |
| One customer unhappy | Medium | Personal call, fix issue |
| Top customer (10%+) threatens to leave | Critical | CEO calls same day |
| Earnout tracking 5% behind | Low | Monitor, adjust plan |
| Earnout tracking 20%+ behind | Critical | Renegotiate metric |
| Buyer changes strategy | Medium | Clarify impact on your role |
| Buyer kills your product | Critical | Founder + buyer CEO call |
| Integration slower than planned | Medium | Adjust timeline |
| Data breach / security incident | Critical | Notify legal, customers, buyer |

---

## What NOT to Do

❌ **Don't**: Stay silent hoping issue resolves
❌ **Don't**: Blame buyer publicly (kills earnout motivation)
❌ **Don't**: Make unilateral decisions without buyer buy-in
❌ **Don't**: Exceed your authority (will be undone later)
❌ **Don't**: Miss earnout targets without alerting buyer
❌ **Don't**: Let team exodus happen without escalating

✅ **Do**: Communicate early and often
✅ **Do**: Propose solutions, not just problems
✅ **Do**: Escalate when needed
✅ **Do**: Document agreements
✅ **Do**: Track progress vs. plan

## Template: Escalation Email

Use this when you need to escalate:

```
Subject: [Issue Name] — Needs your attention

Hi [Buyer CEO/CFO],

Wanted to flag an issue that's come up in integration:

**Issue**: [Specific problem]
**Impact**: [Why it matters: earnout risk, timeline delay, team morale, customer churn, etc.]
**My recommendation**: [Solution you propose]
**Needed from you**: [Specific action: budget approval, team resources, decision clarity]
**Timeline**: [When do we need to decide?]

Happy to discuss. Available [times].

[Your name]
```

**Use case**: Something goes wrong post-close → follow escalation path → send template email.

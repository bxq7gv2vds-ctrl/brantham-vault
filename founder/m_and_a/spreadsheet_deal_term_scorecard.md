## SCORING INSTRUCTIONS

### Scoring Scale: 1–10 (or Dollar Amount)

For **financial terms** (price, salary, earnout):
- Enter exact amounts
- Calculator will weight by preference

For **qualitative terms** (synergy, risk):
- 1 = Very bad for seller
- 5 = Neutral
- 10 = Very good for seller

### Weights (Customize Per Deal)

**Default weights** (adjust based on your priorities):
- Price/cash: 20% (most important)
- Earnout upside: 15% (depends on your confidence)
- Risk (escrow, survival): -13% (want to minimize)
- Employment (yours): 13% (retention security)
- Buyer fit: 10% (integration risk)
- Timeline: 5% (when you need liquidity)

## EXAMPLES: HOW TO FILL THIS OUT

### Example 1: Cash-Focused Founder (Risk-Averse)

| Metric | Buyer A | Buyer B | Notes |
|--------|---------|---------|-------|
| Base Cash | $[10]M | $[8]M | Buyer A pays more upfront (lower risk) |
| Earnout | $[2]M (low confidence) | $[4]M (high confidence) | Buyer A: prefer certainty |
| Escrow | 15% (less holdback) | 25% (more holdback) | Buyer A more favorable |
| Timeline | 90 days (slow) | 60 days (fast) | Buyer B closes faster |
| **Weighted Score** | **8.2/10** | **7.1/10** | **→ Prefer Buyer A** |

**Rationale:** You want cash certainty and lower earnout risk. Buyer A pays more upfront; Buyer B's higher earnout is risky. You value bird-in-hand over upside potential.

## DECISION FRAMEWORK

### Step 1: Rank by Weighted Score
- Eliminate any deal scoring <6/10 (misaligned with priorities)
- Focus on top 2–3 deals

### Step 2: Deep Dive into Top Contender
- Interview buyer's previous acquisitions (reference calls)
- Review buyer's post-close track record (do they pay earnouts?)
- Assess cultural/operational fit

### Step 3: Negotiate Top Terms
- For highest-scored deal, push on [1–2 key terms]
- Realistic negotiation windows:
  - Price: ±10% adjustment possible
  - Earnout: ±[X]% achievability or duration
  - Escrow: ±5 percentage points

### Step 4: Final Comparison
- Rerun scorecard with negotiated terms
- Decide: Is this the best deal given *all factors*, not just money?

---

## RED FLAGS IN DEAL STRUCTURE

🚩 **Urgent "exploding" offers:** "Take our offer today or it expires" → likely negotiation tactic; push back  
🚩 **Unrealistic earnout targets:** Buyer sets targets you have <40% confidence → risk of clawback dispute  
🚩 **High escrow with tight survival:** >25% held back + 24-month survival → significant capital at risk  
🚩 **Key person clause:** Earnout clawed back if you leave → you're handcuffed  
🚩 **Vague integration plans:** Buyer has no plan for your product post-close → risk of abandonment  
🚩 **Non-negotiable terms:** Buyer refuses to budge on anything → inflexible partner

## FINAL DECISION CHECKPOINTS

Before signing LOI:

- [ ] Weighted score >7/10 across key buyers evaluated
- [ ] Risk-adjusted value (base + earnout) competitive for your business metrics
- [ ] Earnout targets achievable with >70% confidence
- [ ] Escrow/indemnification terms reasonable (cap = $[X], survival = 18m)
- [ ] Your personal terms (salary, role, equity) satisfactory for 24–36 months
- [ ] Buyer has strong track record with acquisitions (reference checks passed)
- [ ] Integration plan credible (you'll know what happens post-close)
- [ ] Legal/tax structure optimal (C-corp, qualified small biz stock, etc.)
- [ ] Walk-away number defined (if earnout miss >X%, you can exit)

## TEMPLATE: DOWNLOAD & CUSTOMIZE

**To use this scorecard:**

1. Copy this to a Google Sheet or Excel
2. List your multiple buyers (columns: Buyer A, Buyer B, Buyer C)
3. Enter their LOI terms (rows: price, earnout, timeline, etc.)
4. Weight items by your priorities (20% price, 15% earnout, etc.)
5. Calculate weighted scores
6. Identify top buyer
7. Negotiate top terms and re-score
8. Make final decision

**Example Google Sheet formula:**
```
Weighted Score = SUMPRODUCT(Weights, Scores) / SUM(Weights)
```

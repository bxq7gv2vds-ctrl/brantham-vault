# Checklist Pre-NDA (10 Items)

**Before sharing financials, customer list, or cap table with a potential buyer**, verify:

---

## 1. ✓ BUYER LEGITIMACY

- [ ] Company exists (Google them, check LinkedIn, Crunchbase)
- [ ] Decision-maker has real authority (not junior analyst fishing for info)
- [ ] They've actually acquired companies before (not first time)

---

## 2. ✓ NO DIRECT COMPETITORS

- [ ] Buyer is NOT a direct competitor trying to steal customer list
- [ ] If adjacent, acceptable; if head-to-head, risky

**Action**: Ask "What would you do with [your product] post-acquisition?" Listen for red flags.

---

## 3. ✓ NDA SIGNED FIRST

- [ ] Send short-form NDA (use template from vault)
- [ ] Have legal review (takes 30 min, worth it)
- [ ] Buyer signs before ANY data shared

---

## 4. ✓ SIDE LETTERS FROM ADVISORS

- [ ] If buyer brings lawyers/accountants, they sign side-letter NDA
- [ ] No exceptions (vendors talk; consultants gossip)

---

## 5. ✓ DATA ROOM ACCESS CONTROLLED

- [ ] Use Datasite, Intralinks, or similar (tracks who accessed what, when)
- [ ] NOT: Google Drive shared folder (can't track, easy to leak)
- [ ] Password-protected; change password if someone leaves

---

## 6. ✓ SENSITIVE INFO REDACTED

- [ ] Customer names redacted (use "Customer A", "Customer B") UNTIL LOI
- [ ] Employee salary redacted or anonymized
- [ ] Specific product roadmap shared on need-to-know basis only
- [ ] Strategic partnerships/pricing agreements summarized (not full contracts)

---

## 7. ✓ LEGAL REVIEW COMPLETED

- [ ] Employment agreements reviewed (any IP/non-compete conflicts?)
- [ ] Customer contracts reviewed (any anti-assignment clauses?)
- [ ] Financing docs checked (any negative covenants affecting sale?)
- [ ] Have your attorney do a [1-hour review], not multi-week audit

---

## 8. ✓ BOARD/INVESTORS ALIGNED

- [ ] Board aware of M&A exploration (no surprises)
- [ ] Investors OK with NDA scope (some want veto rights)
- [ ] Cap table fully documented (no "mystery shareholders")

---

## 9. ✓ CUSTOMER/TEAM NOTIFICATION PLAN

- [ ] Decide: will you tell customers/employees about sale talk? (Usually YES after LOI)
- [ ] Draft communication template (approved by counsel)
- [ ] Don't leak early (kills deal if competitors hear)

---

## 10. ✓ WALK-AWAY CRITERIA DEFINED

- [ ] What breaks the deal for you? (Answer before sharing data)
- [ ] What's your BATNA (best alternative)? (Strength = better negotiating position)
- [ ] Are you really open to selling, or just "checking valuation"?

**Action**: Write down: "I will walk away if [X]" (e.g., "if earnout >50%", "if they demand exclusivity", "if price <$15M")

---

## PRE-NDA RED FLAGS 🚩

| Red Flag | Action |
|----------|--------|
| **"NDA takes too long"** | They're not serious; move on |
| **"We need your customer contracts before NDA"** | Absolutely not. Standard answer: "After NDA, in data room" |
| **"Multiple consultants will review, each needs access"** | Huge risk. Counter: "One POC per firm. Everyone signs NDA side-letter" |
| **Buyer is known for corporate espionage** | Obvious: don't sell to them |
| **"We're in due diligence with Competitor X"** | Risk: they share your data with Competitor. Ask about Chinese walls. |

---

## TIMELINE

- **Day 0**: Initial call → determine legitimacy
- **Day 1**: Send NDA + request signature
- **Day 3**: NDA signed → schedule data room access
- **Day 4**: Data room opens → 30-day access window begins
- **Day 10–20**: Buyer asks questions; you respond within 24h
- **Day 20–30**: Buyer requests final documents; you provide
- **Day 30+**: LOI discussion or buyer decides to pass

---

## AFTER NDA, BEFORE LOI

Once NDA signed, provide in phased manner:

| Phase | Documents | Timing |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| **Week 1** | Pitch deck, financials (YoY), exec summary | Day 3 |
| **Week 2** | Q&A doc, tech overview, cap table (anonymized) | Day 7 |
| **Week 3** | Customer reference list, contracts, full cap table | Day 14 |
| **Week 4** | IP docs, compliance certifications, detailed tech | Day 21 |

**Philosophy**: Release info on-demand, not all upfront. This keeps buyer engaged ("gotta keep digging") and creates natural conversation rhythm.

---

## SUMMARY

✓ Verify buyer legitimacy  
✓ NDA before anything  
✓ Side-letters from advisors  
✓ Controlled data room  
✓ Redact sensitive customer/employee info  
✓ Legal review (1 hour min)  
✓ Board/investors aligned  
✓ Comms plan drafted  
✓ Walk-away criteria defined  
✓ Phased info release

## A. First 100 days playbook (post-deal)

### Week 1
- ✅ Change all secrets (GitHub, AWS, Stripe, DB creds)
- ✅ Introduce new leadership to team
- ✅ Team standup (communicate stability, vision)
- ✅ Customer communication (product unchanged)
- ✅ Vendor contracts review (do they allow transfer?)

### Week 2-4
- ✅ Financial close (accruals, reconciliation)
- ✅ Technical integration kickoff (code review, architecture)
- ✅ Org structure (who reports to whom?)
- ✅ Retention bonuses paid (top 5 employees)
- ✅ NPS survey + customer health calls (10 key accounts)

### Month 2-3
- ✅ Product roadmap alignment (your features + theirs)
- ✅ Sales training (how to sell combined product)
- ✅ Cost optimization (kill duplicate tools, consolidate infra)
- ✅ Synergy capture tracker (who owns revenue upside?)
- ✅ Key metric dashboard (track integration health)

## B. Légal & Fiscalité (structure matters!)

### France specifics

**Vente PME** (if qualified):
- Gain: €1M exonération (loi Houdan)
- Remaing: 50% tax (19.5% IR + social)
- vs Normal: 37.5% + social

→ Use holding company structure (buy time, reduce tax)

**Stock sale** (most common):
- You sell shares → capital gain
- Tax: 12.8% (flat tax) OR 37.5% income tax (if not holding)

**Asset sale** (avoid):
- Company sells assets → tax on company, then dividend to you
- Double taxation

### US if relevant

**C-Corp** (standard):
- Stock sale = capital gain (20% fed + state)

**S-Corp** (pass-through):
- Seller financing risk (if earn-out)

→ Work with tax lawyer BEFORE signing (structure = 15-20% difference).

## D. Psychology & soft skills

### When to walk away (red flags)

```
Acheteur wants to:
❌ Fire all your team (consolidate with theirs)
❌ Change product immediately (kills your customers)
❌ Demand non-compete >3 years (unfair)
❌ Hold back 50%+ price as earn-out (risky for you)
❌ Rewrite IP ownership (your code becomes theirs retroactively)
❌ Founder has zero control (CEO role reduced to "advisor")
```

**Your leverage** :
- You have other buyers interested
- You can walk and keep building
- Your team will leave if treated badly

### Emotional readiness (selling your baby)

**Expectations to set**:
- You won't run it anymore (accept this)
- Different decisions will be made (okay, trust process)
- Some good people will leave (unfortunate, natural)
- Your product will evolve (change ≠ ruined)

**How to cope**:
1. Write down "why we're selling" (re-read when sad)
2. Define your next chapter (advisor? New startup? Rest?)
3. Stay 6-12 months max (don't linger)
4. Focus on transition success (leave with head high)

### Decision fatigue

By month 6 of M&A process, you're exhausted.
- ❌ Don't sign bad deal when tired
- ✅ Sleep on final terms (24 hours before signing)
- ✅ Have co-founder or advisor review (fresh eyes)
- ✅ Walk if you feel trapped

## E. Advisor selection (who to hire)

### Roles

| Advisor | What they do | Cost |
|---------|-------------|------|
| **Banker/M&A** | Run process, find buyers, negotiate | 1% of price |
| **Lawyer** | SPA review, cap table, tax | $50-100k |
| **Accountant** | Financial statements, tax planning | $30-50k |
| **Industry expert** | Advise on valuation, buyer fit | Equity or $10-20k |
| **CFO (part-time)** | Model financials, due diligence prep | $5-10k/mo |

### DIY vs hired

**DIY if** : <$10M deal, simple structure, internal CFO, friends have sold before
**Hire if** : >$20M deal, complex cap table, first-time founder, multiple buyers

### Conflicts of interest

⚠️ **Watch out** :
- Banker pushes cheaper deal (lower fee)? Pressure harder.
- Lawyer represents both you + company? Conflict. Get separate counsel.
- Advisor owns equity? Their interest = your interest (good).

## F. Post-deal wealth strategy

### After you get $XX million

**Don't immediately** :
- ❌ Buy house/car (wait 12 months)
- ❌ Tell friends/family (leaks)
- ❌ Spend earn-out (not in pocket yet)

**Do immediately** :
- ✅ Get wealth advisor (fiduciary, not commission)
- ✅ Diversify (don't keep in cash)
- ✅ Tax plan (use 1099 vs W2, charitable giving)
- ✅ Define next chapter

### Allocation example ($50M net after tax)

```
30% Diversified index funds ($15M, long-term)
20% PE/VC fund (follow-on investments, $10M)
15% Real estate ($7.5M, primary + investment)
15% New venture (next startup, $7.5M)
15% Liquid (emergency, near-term spends, $7.5M)
5% Charitable giving ($2.5M, tax deductible)
```

## G. Negotiation psychology (advanced)

### Anchoring

```
Acheteur: "We propose $30M"
You: [Long silence]
Acheteur: [Uncomfortable]

Psychology: Silence = confidence.
            First number = anchor (anchors all future offers)
            Don't accept first offer (shows you accept it)
```

### Reciprocity

```
You: "We'll reduce non-compete to 1 year (our concession)"
Acheteur: [Feels obligated to give something back]
Acheteur: "Then we'll increase price to $52M"

Psychology: Give to get. Small ask first, then big ask.
```

### Scarcity / FOMO

```
You: "We have 2 other LOIs pending (scarcity)"
Acheteur: "We're moving slow" → suddenly fast
        (fear of missing out accelerates their process)
```

### Reframing

```
Acheteur: "Earn-out is risky for you"
You: "No, it's aligned incentives. 
     If I hit targets, I earn $5M.
     If you're right that I can't, you don't pay.
     We're aligned."

Psychology: Same thing, different frame.
           Acheteur feels smart ("oh, I like this structure").
```

## H. Common M&A blunders (avoid)

🚩 **Blunder 1** : Celebrating before close
- Announce to team early → leaks → complicates deal

🚩 **Blunder 2** : Key employee leaves during LOI-close
- Acheteur's risk assessment changes
- Price can re-trade downward

🚩 **Blunder 3** : Financial restatement discovered in DD
- "You didn't disclose XYZ expense"
- Price renegotiation or walk

🚩 **Blunder 4** : Founder non-compete too restrictive
- "I can't work anywhere for 5 years"
- Regret after sale

🚩 **Blunder 5** : Earn-out vaporizes
- You hit targets, buyer says "we calculated differently"
- Dispute + legal fees

## I. Common post-deal mistakes

🚩 **Mistake 1** : Trying to protect "your baby"
- Fighting every change = wear out
- Accept evolution or leave

🚩 **Mistake 2** : Staying too long
- "3-year transition" = slow death
- Better: 1-year clean handoff

🚩 **Mistake 3** : Comparing to "how we did it"
- "We built this differently" ≠ constructive
- Large org has different needs

🚩 **Mistake 4** : Not creating next vision
- Staying as "ghost" with no purpose = depressing
- Define your role clearly or exit

## K. Red flags in buyer (cultural mismatch)

🚩 Buyer doesn't care about your product quality
🚩 Buyer wants to immediately cut costs (kills morale)
🚩 Buyer talks about "integration" as "consolidation" (i.e., layoffs)
🚩 Buyer's leadership is dismissive or arrogant
🚩 Buyer has history of product kills post-M&A

→ Trust your gut. A bad buyer ≠ worst outcome.

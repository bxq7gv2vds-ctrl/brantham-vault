# Team Role Matrix Post-Close

**Flou = churn. Clarté = loyalty. Document avant signature.**

---

## Template

| Name | Current Role | Post-Close Role | Reporting To | Equity Vesting | Retention Bonus | Notes |
|------|-------------|------------------|--------------|----------------|-----------------|-------|
| You (Founder) | CEO | CTO / Operating Partner | Buyer CEO | 4-yr vest + cliff | $[amount] if stay 24mo | Must commit publicly |
| Engineer A | Lead Eng | Principal Eng | Your title | 4-yr vest (already vesting) | $[50k] if stay 24mo | Key person, at risk |
| Engineer B | Full-stack | Product Eng | Lead Eng A | 4-yr vest + cliff | $[30k] if stay 18mo | Standard role |
| CSM | Customer Success | CSM (now buyer's team) | Buyer CS Lead | Non-vested equity x% | 0 (integrated) | Will likely leave |
| [Open Headcount] | — | Sales Eng | Buyer Sales VP | N/A | N/A | Buyer hires |

---

## Key Decisions

### For Founder
```
Role options (rank by retention risk):
1. CEO (best retention, hardest transition)
2. CTO / Product Lead (technical credibility)
3. "Operating Partner" (vague, often leaves)
4. Advisor (walk away in 6 months)

Pick: ________________

Commitment: [1 year / 2 years / 3 years]
Equity: [New grant / Extend existing / None]
Bonus: $[amount] if stay [X months]
```

### For Key Team
```
Engineer A (example):
- New role: Principal Eng
- Reports to: [Buyer eng lead or You]
- Vesting: Accelerate 1 year (75% cliff conversion) → retention
- Bonus: $50k at month 24 (stay incentive)
- Title change: OK / NOT OK (preference?)
```

---

## Red Flags

| Flag | Fix |
|------|-----|
| Key person role not defined (day after close) | Announce day 1 (even if interim) |
| Team reports to stranger (not familiar face) | Have them report to you initially |
| Equity vesting cliff resets (loses progress) | Accelerate or new grant, don't restart |
| No retention bonus for critical people | Add $[X] earnout-contingent bonus |
| CSM integrated away (customer risk) | CSM stays dedicated 6 months min |
| Title change perceived as demotion | Consult with employee (gives choice) |

---

## Do This Before Close

- [ ] Offer letters signed by all key employees (new role, new terms)
- [ ] Retention bonuses in separate agreement (not vague)
- [ ] Announce new org structure publicly (day 1 after close)
- [ ] 1-on-1 meetings (you + each person, role clarity)
- [ ] Document in integration playbook (buyer knows expectations)

---

## Retention Strategy

**Low risk** (will stay):
- Founder: new CEO / CTO role + $[X] bonus
- Lead engineer: principal role + $50k bonus
- Long-tenure employees: new role + clarity

**Medium risk** (might leave):
- Junior engineers: absorbed into buyer's team (ok if good mentorship)
- CSM: moving to buyer's org (integration friction)
- Contract roles: likely to go (expected)

**High risk** (will likely leave):
- Advisor-only roles (too vague)
- Perceived demotions (title/scope cut)
- No retention bonus for senior people
- Unclear reporting structure

---

## Earn Out Contingency

If earnout depends on revenue targets:
```
You must hit revenue target.
Revenue depends on:
- Product roadmap (your control)
- Sales/marketing (buyer's control)
- Customer support (shared)

If buyer cuts resources → earnout at risk → dispute.

FIX: Document in LOI what buyer will provide (budget, hires).
```

Same logic for team: if key engineer leaves, earnout fails.

**Add clause :** "If key employees leave involuntarily, earnout adjusts."

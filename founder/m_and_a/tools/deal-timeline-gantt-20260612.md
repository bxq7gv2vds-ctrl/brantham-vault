# Deal Timeline & Gantt Chart

**Utilité** : planifier et tracker le chemin critique (LOI → Close en 90–120 jours).

---

## Master Timeline (T+0 to T+120 days)

```
PHASE 1: PREPARATION (Weeks 1–2)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

T+0   [DAY 1]  Define BATNA, finalize cap table, identify 5–10 buyers
      [DAY 2]  Prep teaser deck (5–10 slides)
      [DAY 3]  Brief M&A advisor (lawyer/banker/accountant)
      [DAY 4]  NDA ready for signature
      [DAY 5]  Draft LOI template (non-binding)

T+7   [DAY 7]  Send teaser + NDA to 5 target buyers (simultaneous)
      [DAY 10] Initial buyer feedback ("interested" / "pass" / "maybe")
      [DAY 12] Schedule first calls with interested buyers (CFO, strategy)


PHASE 2: SOFT SHOP (Weeks 3–4)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

T+10  [DAY 10–15] Initial buyer calls (30 min each; overview, fit check)
      [DAY 15]    Buyer feedback: who is seriously interested?
      [DAY 17]    Narrow to 2–3 primary targets

T+20  [DAY 18–21] First deep-dive calls (60 min; CFO + CTO, buyer side)
      [DAY 21]    Buyer indicates intent to move forward (verbal LOI range)
      [DAY 22]    Prep management presentation (90 min format)


PHASE 3: LOI NEGOTIATION (Weeks 5–6)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

T+28  [DAY 23]    Share management presentation (slides or in-person)
      [DAY 25]    Buyer returns LOI draft from their counsel
      [DAY 27]    Seller counsel counters (negotiate price, earnout, reps)
      [DAY 28]    Buyer revises LOI (back-and-forth negotiation)

T+35  [DAY 30]    [TARGET] LOI signed; exclusivity begins
      [DAY 31]    Announce LOI to board + employees (CONFIDENTIALLY)
      [DAY 32]    Data room goes live with financials + cap table


PHASE 4: DILIGENCE (Weeks 7–12)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

T+35  [DAY 32–40] Initial diligence phase
      [DAY 32]    Buyer's legal team asks initial batch of Qs (50–100)
      [DAY 35]    Seller team begins answering (48-hour SLA per Q&A plan)
      [DAY 40]    Buyer team conducts deep-dives: tech audit, customer calls, reference checks

T+50  [DAY 43–50] Technical diligence (buyer's CTO + dev team audit your codebase)
      [DAY 45]    Financial audit (buyer's finance + accounting deep-dive)
      [DAY 50]    Customer reference calls (buyer speaks to top 3–5 customers)

T+60  [DAY 52–60] Legal diligence (buyer's counsel reviews contracts, IP, litigation)
      [DAY 55]    People diligence (HR calls key employees for retention assessment)
      [DAY 60]    Regulatory review (if applicable: GDPR, CCPA, export controls)

T+75  [DAY 65–75] SPA NEGOTIATION BEGINS
      [DAY 65]    Buyer counsel sends SPA draft (based on LOI terms)
      [DAY 70]    Seller counsel counters (reps/warranties, indemnification, escrow)
      [DAY 75]    Buyer revises; iterative negotiation on deal mechanics


PHASE 5: SPA FINALIZATION & CLOSING PREP (Weeks 13–17)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

T+85  [DAY 80–85] SPA negotiations heat up (earnout definition, escrow mechanics, reps)
      [DAY 85]    Buyer secures financing commitment (if contingent)
      [DAY 86]    Seller secures board + shareholder approval (per bylaws)

T+95  [DAY 90]    [TARGET] SPA signed (final document)
      [DAY 92]    Board approval (both buyer + seller)
      [DAY 94]    Shareholder consents collected (if required)
      [DAY 95]    Closing conditions final check (MAC check, key person confirmation)

T+100 [DAY 98–100] Final closing prep
      [DAY 98]    Closing statement preparation (working capital calc, expense accrual)
      [DAY 99]    Final tax/legal review
      [DAY 100]   Draft escrow holdback agreement + earnout side letter

T+120 [DAY 115–120] [TARGET] CLOSING DAY
      [DAY 118]   Final closing conditions confirmed
      [DAY 120]   Funds transfer, entity docs filed, press release issued
      [DAY 121]   Employee announcements + transition planning begins
```

## Contingency Plans

### If LOI takes >30 days

- **Cause** : Buyer board approval needed, valuation stalling
- **Action** : Reach out to 2nd buyer simultaneously (threaten competition)
- **Worst case** : Abandon buyer, move to next (cost: 2–3 weeks delay)

### If diligence stalls (no Qs for 10 days)

- **Cause** : Buyer loses interest, internal confusion
- **Action** : Call buyer CEO directly; ask if still moving forward
- **If "yes"** : Accelerate data room, proactive responses
- **If "no"** : Move to backup buyer

### If SPA negotiation extends past T+90

- **Cause** : Reps/warranties disputes, earnout disagreement
- **Action** : Escalate to CEO ↔ CEO (not just lawyers)
- **Fallback** : Agree on structure, defer details to post-close side letter

# SaaS Acquisition Multiples — 2026 Benchmarks

**Source:** Typical M&A transactions, public company multiples, investor market consensus.  
**Note:** Multiples vary by vertical, profitability, churn, NRR. Use as rough guide.

---

## EV/ARR Multiple by Growth Rate

| YoY Growth | Multiple Range | Notes |
|-----------|-----------------|-------|
| **50%+** | **8-12x** | Hyper-growth; venture buyers pay premium |
| **35-50%** | **6-10x** | Strong growth; strategic or PE likely |
| **20-35%** | **4-7x** | Healthy SaaS; good entry point |
| **10-20%** | **2-4x** | Moderate growth; mature profile |
| **0-10%** | **1-3x** | Declining or flat; acqui-hire priced |

---

## Premium/Discount Adjusters

**Factors that increase multiple:**
- [ ] **NRR >120%:** +1-2x multiplier
- [ ] **Profitability (>10% op margin):** +0.5-1x
- [ ] **Large enterprise customers (ACV >$50k):** +0.5-1x
- [ ] **Strong moat / defensibility:** +0.5-1x
- [ ] **Sticky product (churn <3%/month):** +0.5-1x
- [ ] **Market leadership (>30% market share):** +1-2x

**Factors that decrease multiple:**
- [ ] **High churn (>5%/month):** -1-2x
- [ ] **Customer concentration (top customer >30%):** -0.5-2x
- [ ] **Negative NRR:** -1-3x
- [ ] **Heavy sales costs / low CAC payback:** -0.5-1x
- [ ] **Competitive market / undifferentiated:** -0.5-1x

## By Buyer Type

| Buyer Type | Typical Multiple | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------|
| **Strategic** (competitor/adjacent) | 1.2-1.8x base | Synergies justify premium; quick integration |
| **PE** (private equity) | 0.8-1.2x base | LBO mentality; pay for current earnings |
| **Venture** (growth-stage buyer) | 1.3-2.0x base | Growth matters; willing to pay more |
| **Financial** (investment firm) | 0.7-1.0x base | Pure cash-flow buyer; lowest price |
| **Acqui-hire** (talent only) | 0.3-0.7x base | Buying team, not business |

## Negotiation Tactics with Multiples

**Your position:** "Market multiples for [growth rate] are [X-Y]x. We should target [Y]x."

**Buyer's position:** "Peers trade at [X]x; you have [risk]. [X]x is fair."

**Resolution:**
```
Step 1: Agree on baseline multiple (benchmark)
Step 2: Identify adjusters (risk factors)
Step 3: Apply adjusters to baseline
Step 4: Compare to deal value
Example: 5x base - 1x concentration = 4x = $12M on $3M ARR
```

## 2026 Market Context

- **Growth premium:** Still valued; hyper-growth (50%+) commands 8-12x
- **Profitability:** No longer a penalty; profitable SaaS gains +0.5x
- **Rate environment:** 12% WACC is standard (was 10% in 2021)
- **Buyer consolidation:** Fewer strategic buyers; PE more active
- **Average deal:** $10-30M (smaller deals <$5M less common)

## Use This to Anchor Negotiation

**Step 1: Find 3 comps**
```
Competitor A: $X ARR, Y% growth, sold at Z×
Competitor B: $X ARR, Y% growth, sold at Z×
Competitor C: $X ARR, Y% growth, sold at Z×
→ Average multiple: [M]x
```

**Step 2: Adjust for your company**
```
Base multiple: [M]x
Your strengths: +[A]x
Your weaknesses: -[B]x
→ Target multiple: [M]x
```

**Step 3: Name it in LOI**
> "We'd like to target [M]x ARR based on [comps]. Given our [strength], we think [M+0.5]x is fair. Starting position: [M+1]x."

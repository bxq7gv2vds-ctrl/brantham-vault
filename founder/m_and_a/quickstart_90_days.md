# M&A Quickstart — Roadmap 90 Jours

**Utilité** : checklist d'action si vous explorez sérieusement une vente dans Q0.

---

## Week 1-2 : Préparation Interne

### 1. Define Your BATNA (Jour 1)
```
Question: "If we don't sell, what's plan B?"
  a) Raise next round (Series X) → dilution %, timeline
  b) Remain independent → annualized valuation (current + growth)
  c) IPO → timeline 18–24m, costs $5M+
  d) Shut down & return capital

BATNA = economic value of best alternative
Example: "We'll raise $20M Series B at $100M post, so BATNA = $100M"
```

### 2. Build Cap Table Clarity (Jour 2-3)
```
Requirements:
- Current ownership: % per shareholder
- Stock options: vested %, strike price, expiration
- Convertibles: SAFEs, notes (discount %, MFN)
- Preferred shares: liquidation prefs, anti-dilution

Output: one spreadsheet, shares → $ at valuation X
```

### 3. Identify 5-10 Potential Buyers (Jour 3-4)
```
Criteria:
✓ Complementary product (not direct competitor, unless killer)
✓ Relevant market (same geo, customer, vertical)
✓ Capacity to buy (revenue >$50M, or PE-backed)
✓ Strategic fit (solves problem for them)

Sources:
- Your customers ("who do you wish integrated with us?")
- Advisors/board members
- Competitor intelligence (who's raising, hiring, M&A active?)
- SEC filings (if public)

Output: a list of 10 names + contact person
```

### 4. Prepare Teaser Deck (Jour 4-5)
```
5-10 slide summary:
1. Problem
2. Solution (your product)
3. Market size (TAM)
4. Traction (revenue, growth, users)
5. Unit economics (CAC, LTV, payback)
6. Why now (market conditions, competitive pressure)
7. Why acquire us (synergies)
8. Next steps (timeline, process)

Tone: confident, growth-focused, not desperate

Rule: Teaser is NOT the data room. It's the appetizer.
```

### 5. Engage M&A Advisor / Law Firm (Jour 5-7)
```
Roles:
- Lawyer: legal structure, reps/warranties, financing terms
- Investment banker (optional): identify buyers, negotiate valuation
- Accountant: working capital adjustments, tax optimization

Cost: lawyers = $30k–$100k retainer + success fee
      bankers = 0.5–1.5% of deal value (success-based)

Timeline: 3–4 weeks to onboard, prepare materials
```

## Week 3-4 : Soft Shop (Contact Buyers)

### 6. Send Teaser → 5 Target Buyers (Jour 10-12)
```
Email from your lawyer (not you):

"We represent [Your Company], a [market] leader in [your niche].
Our clients are exploring strategic options, which may include
a partnership or transaction with a strong strategic buyer.

Attached is a confidential overview. If interesting, we'd welcome
a conversation with [CFO/CEO] of [Buyer Co].

Confidentiality: all information is privileged and confidential."

Expectation: 2-3 will express interest within 1 week
```

### 7. Conduct Buyer Conversations (Jour 12-21)
```
Process:
- Week 1: "Thanks for interest. Here's why we're thinking about this."
           Listen to their strategic rationale.
           
- Week 2: "Here are our key metrics & traction."
          Gauge their appetite & timeline.
          
- Week 3: "If we move forward, what does your process look like?"
          Understand their urgency, decision-makers, approval gates.

DO NOT discuss price yet.
DO probe: "What's your expected timeline?" (forced commitment)
```

### 8. Evaluate Buyer Signals (Jour 14-21)
```
Green flags (serious buyers):
✓ Senior executive engaged (not junior biz dev)
✓ Specific questions (already thinking about integration)
✓ Timeline: "90 days to close" (committed)
✓ "We can move fast" (less bureaucracy)

Red flags (tire-kickers):
❌ "Interesting, but busy now" (no urgency)
❌ Junior person handling (no authority)
❌ "Need to think about this" >2 weeks (tepid)
❌ "We'll need full due diligence before deciding" (0 commitment)
```

### 9. Select 2-3 Serious Buyers for LOI Stage (Jour 21)
```
Filter: keep only buyers who:
- Have expressed concrete interest
- Have timeline <6 months
- Have budget ("do you have acquisition budget for 2026?")
- Are decision-makers

Now you can approach them with: "Let's move toward an LOI."
```

## Week 5-8 : LOI & Due Diligence

### 10. Negotiate & Sign LOI (Jour 22-35)

**Key Terms to Anchor:**
```
Price:          "We see fair value at $X based on [DCF/comps]"
Reps survival:  "General reps 18m, tax reps 6y"
Earnout cap:    "<20% of total consideration"
Indemnity cap:  "0.5% basket, 1% cap"
Termination:    "1% reverse fee if buyer walks after 60d exclusive"
Go-shop:        "40-day go-shop post-signing"
Financing:      "Financing condition drops after day 45"
```

**Timeline:**
- Day 1: Buyer sends LOI (heavily in their favor)
- Day 3: You send back marked-up (20–30% edits)
- Day 7: Buyer counter; you negotiate key 5 terms
- Day 14: Sign LOI (will still disagree on 1-2 items; resolve in DD)
```

### 11. Prepare Data Room (Jour 22-35)
```
Structure:
├─ Financial (P&L, balance sheet, cash flow, tax returns)
├─ Legal (cap table, articles, agreements, litigation)
├─ Commercial (contracts, customers, pipeline)
├─ Technical (code audit, security, scalability)
└─ HR (org chart, employment agreements, equity grants)

Tools: VDR (Virtual Data Room) or secured folder (Google Drive encrypted)
       Use Intralinks, Merrill DataSite, or similar.

Access: controlled, logged (buyer sees who viewed what, when)
```

### 12. Run Parallel Due Diligence (Jour 22-56)
```
Expect: 4–6 weeks of questions

Buyer's teams:
- Finance: EBITDA, revenue recognition, working capital
- Tech: code quality, scalability, security
- Legal: contracts, IP, litigation, compliance
- Commercial: customer satisfaction, churn, retention

Your responses:
✓ Detailed (don't deflect)
❌ Defensive (raises suspicion)

Timeline: schedule 2–3 "office hours" per week (1-2 hours each)
          for Q&A. Batch their questions, answer in groups.
```

## Week 9-12 : Closing & Execution

### 13. Finalize Price & Terms (Jour 49-63)
```
By week 7, you should know:
- Buyer's final offer: $X cash + $Y earnout
- Price adjustment formula (working capital, debt)
- Earnout triggers & metrics
- Reps survival & indemnity caps
- Holdback schedule

Negotiation by type:
- Strategic: emphasize synergies, demand premium multiple
- PE: tie earnout to seller-controllable metrics
- Venture: negotiate stock lockup
- Financial: focus on EBITDA/FCF metrics

Walk-away point: if offer < 0.85 × BATNA, walk.
                 don't negotiate below your threshold.
```

### 14. Execute Closing Docs (Jour 56-77)
```
Documents signed:
- Purchase Agreement (5–30 pages)
- Rep Schedule (detailed breakdowns)
- Closing Conditions (what needs to happen by close)
- Transition Services Agreement (if applicable)
- Employee retention letters

Timeline: 2–3 weeks for legal drafting & negotiation
```

### 15. Final Approvals & Closing (Jour 77-90)
```
Last-minute items:
- Customer consent (for major contracts)
- Financing approval (if buyer has debt)
- Board/shareholder approval
- Tax opinion (if complex structure)
- Insurance policies (if any required)

Closing day:
- Wire cash
- Sign final docs
- Exchange equity/stock certificates
- Announce to team & customers

Post-close:
- Escrow period begins (12–18 months)
- Earnout tracking starts
- Transition services begin (if applicable)
```

## Summary: Day-by-Day Checkpoint

| Week | Phase | Checkpoint |
|------|-------|-----------|
| 1 | Prep | BATNA defined, cap table clear, 10 buyers identified |
| 2 | Prep | Teaser deck ready, advisor hired |
| 3 | Soft shop | 5 teasers sent, 2-3 buyers responding |
| 4 | Soft shop | Initial conversations complete, 2-3 filtered for LOI |
| 5-6 | LOI | LOI signed, data room open, DD starts |
| 7-8 | DD | Technical/legal/financial audits complete, price range narrowing |
| 9-10 | Closing | Final terms agreed, docs drafted |
| 11-12 | Closing | Board approval, final closing conditions, close |

---

## Post-Closing (Day 1-365)

```
Week 1: Announce internally + externally
Week 2-4: Integration planning (if buyer retention required)
Month 1-3: Earnout tracking setup + reporting
Month 6: First earnout payout (if escrow conditions met)
Month 12-18: Reps & earnout final settlement
```

**Note:** This timeline assumes a mid-market SaaS deal ($5–50M).
Smaller deals: compress to 6–8 weeks.
Larger deals: stretch to 4–6 months.

# FRAMEWORK: HOW TO JUSTIFY YOUR VALUATION

**When a buyer says "That's too high," you need a prepared narrative. This framework justifies your price in 5 minutes.**

---

## THE 5-POINT NARRATIVE

### Point 1: Comparable Company Analysis
"Based on [5–10] comparable companies in our space acquired in the past [24 months], the median multiple is **[8x ARR]** for companies with our growth profile:
- [Competitor A]: acquired at [8.5x ARR]
- [Competitor B]: acquired at [7.5x ARR]
- [Competitor C]: acquired at [8x ARR]

We're asking **[8.5x ARR]**, which is at the high end but justified by our superior metrics."

### Point 2: Our Growth Premium
"Our **[40]% YoY growth** is at the top [15%] of SaaS companies. Market data shows growth premium:
- [20% growth] → [4–5x multiple]
- [35% growth] → [6–7x multiple]
- **[40% growth]** → **[7–9x multiple]**

This growth justifies a [8.5x] multiple."

### Point 3: Unit Economics Quality
"Our unit economics are best-in-class:
- **CAC payback:** [12 months] (market avg: [18 months])
- **Net Revenue Retention:** [125%] (market avg: [110%])
- **Gross margin:** [72%] (market avg: [65%])
- **LTV:CAC ratio:** [6:1] (market target: [3:1])

This means we're capital-efficient; buyer gets profitable growth, not money-losing scaling."

### Point 4: Your Strategic Synergies
"With [Buyer Name], we create measurable value:
- **Revenue synergy:** Your [1000] customers × [20% adoption] of our product × [$50]K ACV = **[$10]M new ARR**
  → Justifies **[5x]** of current price as strategic premium
- **Cost synergy:** Consolidated sales/support = **[$2]M annual savings**
  → Justifies additional **[1.5x]** premium

**Total synergy value: [6.5x] of our valuation.** We're asking you to split upside with us."

### Point 5: Competitive Pressure
"We have active interest from [2–3] other strategic buyers:
- [Buyer B] is in preliminary discussions (indicated $[X]M range)
- [Buyer C] is considering (early stage)
- [Your company] is our preferred partner for strategic fit

That said, **valuation needs to be competitive** in this context. Our [8.5x ARR] pricing reflects a healthy market for high-growth SaaS."

---

## CLOSING LINE

"To summarize: **Market comps support [8–9x]. Our growth justifies the premium. Your synergies are worth [6x+]. We're offering a very fair deal.**

Can you get to [8x ARR], or do you see this differently?"

---

## VARIANT: IF BUYER PUSHES BACK

**Buyer:** "That's still too high. We can only go to [6x ARR]."

**Your response:**

"I appreciate that. Let me understand your thinking. [6x] would imply:
- We're growing [25%] (not [40%])
- Unit economics are average (not best-in-class)
- Our strategic synergies are worth $0 to you
- Market multiples have dropped since last year

None of those are true. So either:
1. You see risks I'm not aware of (let me address them), OR
2. Your [6x] assumes you can negotiate the price down through our earnout

What am I missing?"

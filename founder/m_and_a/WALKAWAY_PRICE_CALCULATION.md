# Walk-Away Price Calculation — Your Minimum Acceptable Offer

**Critical decision:** Calculate before you talk to buyers. Know your floor.

---

## **Step 1: What You Need to Know**

| Input | Your Value | Source |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| **Current ARR** | $[X]k | Last month's MRR × 12 |
| **Your personal salary** | $[X]k | Typical founder salary |
| **Investor capital raised** | $[X]k | Sum of seed rounds + Series A, etc. |
| **Investor preference stack** | $[X]k | Preferred shares get paid first at exit |
| **Your ownership %** | [X]% | Fully diluted cap table |
| **Tax rate (capital gains)** | [X]% | Federal (20%) + state + Medicare surtax |

---

## **Step 2: Calculate Your Net Proceeds**

```
Enterprise Value (buyer's offer)               $[X]M
– Seller's transaction costs (lawyer, banker)   –$[Y]k
– Repay debt                                    –$[Z]k
– Investor preference distribution              –$[A]k
= Equity proceeds to you                        $[B]M

– Capital gains tax (assume [X]%)              –$[C]k
= Your net cash (after tax)                    $[D]M
```

**Example:**
```
Buyer offers:                                    $10M
– Transaction costs (2% of deal):                –$200k
– Debt payoff:                                   –$0
– Investor preferences:                          –$2M (preferred investors)
= Your equity stake:                             $8M × 40% ownership = $3.2M

– Capital gains tax (25% combined):              –$800k
= Your net cash:                                 $2.4M
```

---

## **Step 3: Calculate Your "Comfort Zone" Price**

The lowest price you'd accept to not regret the deal:

| Scenario | Calculation | Your Number |
|----------|---|---|
| **Conservative** | (Your salary × 10 years) | $[X]k × 10 = $[Y]M |
| **Moderate** | (ARR × 5x multiple) | $[X]k × 5 = $[Y]M |
| **Optimistic** | (ARR × 8x multiple) | $[X]k × 8 = $[Y]M |

**Example:**
- Salary: $200k
- ARR: $2M
- Conservative: $2M (10 years of salary)
- Moderate: $10M (5x ARR)
- Optimistic: $16M (8x ARR)

---

## **Step 4: Your Walk-Away Price (Minimum)**

This is the **lowest offer you'll accept** before you say "no deal":

**Formula:** Max of (Conservative OR break-even threshold)

| Option | Calculation |
|--------|---|
| **Option A: Salary Replacement** | (Your annual salary × X years until FI) = $[Y]M |
| **Option B: Opportunity Cost** | (Salary × 10 years) = $[Y]M |
| **Option C: Benchmark** | (ARR × 4–5x for your industry) = $[Y]M |

**Pick the HIGHEST number.** That's your minimum acceptable offer.

**Example:**
- Option A (12 years FI): $200k × 12 = $2.4M net
- Option B (10 years): $200k × 10 = $2M net
- Option C (4.5x ARR): $2M × 4.5 = $9M
- **Walk-away price: $9M** (pick the highest)

---

## **Step 5: Your Target Price (What You'll Push For)**

Add 30% to walk-away as your anchor:

**Target price = Walk-away × 1.3**

Example: $9M walk-away → Target $11.7M

Why 1.3? Because:
- Buyers expect to negotiate you down 15–20%
- If you ask for $11.7M, they negotiate to $10M (still above your walk-away)
- If they come in at $12M (surprise!), you're thrilled

---

## **Step 6: Reality Check (Sanity Test)**

Before you commit to these numbers, stress-test them:

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| **Am I comparing to recent comps?** (Similar companies, similar size) | Yes / No |
| **Have I talked to [X] founders who exited?** | Yes / No |
| **Have I asked my board/investors what they expect?** | Yes / No |
| **Do my numbers match SaaS benchmarks for my ARR?** (SaaS Benchmarks, Sacra) | Yes / No |
| **If buyer says "no deal under $[X]", would I walk?** | Yes / No |

If you answered "No" to more than 1, recalculate.

---

## **Step 7: Communicate Your Range (Not Your Floor)**

**What to say to buyers:**

❌ **Don't say:** "My walk-away is $9M."
❌ **Don't say:** "My minimum is $12M."

✅ **Do say:** "We're looking for [Enterprise Value] in the $11–15M range, depending on earnout structure and payment terms."

This gives you negotiation room without anchoring too low.

---

## **Step 8: Scenario Planning**

Before you start conversations, run these scenarios:

| Scenario | Offer | Net Proceeds | Your Reaction |
|----------|-------|---|---|
| **Pessimistic** | $5M | $[X]M | Walk? Negotiate? |
| **Realistic** | $10M | $[X]M | Accept? |
| **Optimistic** | $15M | $[X]M | Celebrate? |
| **Wildcard** | $20M | $[X]M | How would earnout tie-in? |

If the realistic scenario ($10M) leaves you happy, you're ready to negotiate.

---

## **Pro Tips**

**Tip 1:** Always negotiate based on multiple, not absolute price.
- ❌ "I want $12M."
- ✅ "We're valued at 6x ARR based on comparables. That's $12M."

**Tip 2:** Structure deals to hit your walk-away.
- $10M cash + $2M earnout = $12M total
- But if you only get $5M cash, you might need to walk

**Tip 3:** Know your investor's expectations.
- Investors might want "1x return" (get back what they invested)
- Or they might push for "maximum exit value"
- Get aligned on walk-away BEFORE you start conversations

**Tip 4:** Account for time-value of money.
- $10M today > $10M over 3 years (earnout)
- Discount earnout by ~30% (payment risk + time)

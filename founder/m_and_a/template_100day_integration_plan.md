## Days 1-7: Emergency Onboarding

### Day 1 (Close Day)
- [ ] Key team call: "We closed, here's what changes" (30 min)
- [ ] CEO message to all staff (email, written in advance)
- [ ] New org chart distributed (roles, managers, no surprises)
- [ ] HR transition: payroll, benefits, email addresses
- [ ] Systems access: VPN, Slack, Jira, analytics dashboards
- [ ] Acquier's integration PM assigned (single point of contact)

### Days 2-3 (HR & Cultural Onboarding)
- [ ] All-hands with acquirer leadership (1 hour)
- [ ] Benefits transition explained (healthcare, retirement, etc.)
- [ ] Stock option clarification (vesting, strike, new plan)
- [ ] FAQ document released (addresses "will I be fired?", equity, roles)
- [ ] Founder 1:1 with acquirer CEO (set expectations)

### Days 4-7 (Systems & Process Alignment)
- [ ] IT audit: which systems stay, which migrate?
- [ ] SSO/identity migration plan (often takes 2-4 weeks)
- [ ] Data access matrix (who can see what?)
- [ ] Vendor contracts: notify all vendors of acquisition
- [ ] Customer notification (if required by contract)
- [ ] First team standups with acquirer (show synchronization)

---

## Week 2-4: Foundation Setting

### Weeks 2-3
- [ ] Financial systems integration (accounting, forecasting tools)
- [ ] Sales playbook alignment (pricing, discounts, deal authority)
- [ ] Customer success KPI alignment (SLAs, support model)
- [ ] Product roadmap alignment (what are we building?)
- [ ] Engineering process review (code review, deployment, CI/CD)
- [ ] Security/compliance audit (if acquirer is larger)

### Week 4
- [ ] First executive steering committee (weekly cadence set)
- [ ] Headcount/hiring plan reviewed (freezes, growth expectations)
- [ ] Q1 budget & forecast aligned
- [ ] All team members have new manager assigned + 1:1 scheduled
- [ ] Offsites/team dinners (build relationships early)

---

## Month 2-3: Quick Wins & Synergies

### Quick Wins (Months 2-3)
Pick 3-5 small wins that show value + build credibility:

**Sales:**
- [ ] 1st co-sell deal done (product bundled)
- [ ] 5 warm introductions to existing accounts (expansion)
- [ ] Pricing aligned (no surprises)

**Product:**
- [ ] API integration (your API → their product)
- [ ] 1 feature built combining both products
- [ ] Data migration tested

**Operations:**
- [ ] Customer success team aligned on SLAs
- [ ] Support ticket routing optimized (no hand-offs)
- [ ] Reporting dashboard live (monthly metrics visible)

**People:**
- [ ] 10+ 1:1s with key talent (retention check)
- [ ] Mentorship program started (your team paired with acquirer)
- [ ] Cross-functional projects initiated

### Synergy Work (Month 2-3)
- [ ] Customer success: identify 10 accounts for co-selling
- [ ] Engineering: start API/integration work
- [ ] Product: roadmap married (what's shared, what's separate?)
- [ ] Go-to-market: identify joint customer segment

---

## Day 100 Check-In: Did We Win?

### Success Metrics
- [ ] **Retention:** 90%+ of team stayed (0-2 key departures OK)
- [ ] **Customer churn:** Normal or better (no M&A exodus)
- [ ] **Revenue:** On-plan forecast (no disruption)
- [ ] **Morale:** Team sentiment survey 3+/5 (not depressed)
- [ ] **Synergies:** 1-2 realized, 3-5 in pipeline

### Red Flags at Day 100
- [ ] > 3 departures of key people (integration failure signal)
- [ ] Customer churn spike (>10% more than normal)
- [ ] Zero synergies identified (wasted deal thesis)
- [ ] Still "us vs them" mentality (culture clash)
- [ ] Revenue forecast revised downward >10% (disruption)

---

## Specific Role: Founder/CEO (Post-Close)

### Your Job Days 1-30
- [ ] Be visible (standup meetings, emails, 1:1s)
- [ ] Articulate the vision (why this deal = winning)
- [ ] Protect the team (shield from too much change too fast)
- [ ] Build relationships (acquirer's leadership, peer CEOs)
- [ ] Approve all outbound comms (customers, press, etc.)

### Your Job Days 30-100
- [ ] Start building strategy (5-year roadmap)
- [ ] Coach your team (help them navigate new structure)
- [ ] Mentor peers (share what worked pre-acquisition)
- [ ] Spot integration issues early (escalate if needed)
- [ ] Plan your own role (what's next for you?)

### Your Job After Day 100
- [ ] Either: lean into acquirer leadership (stay, climb)
- [ ] Or: graceful exit (found next company, advisor role)
- [ ] But NOT: check out mentally (demoralizes team)

## Communication Cadence

- **Daily (Days 1-7):** CEO standup (30 min)
- **Weekly (Weeks 2-12):** All-hands (30 min)
- **Weekly:** Executive steering committee (1 hour)
- **Bi-weekly:** Integration working group (product, sales, ops) (1.5 hours)
- **Monthly (30-90 days):** Board/leadership review (1 hour)

---

## Common Pitfalls & How to Avoid

| Pitfall | How to Avoid |
|---------|---|
| Moving too fast (breaking things) | Agree on 30-day "stabilization" period before change |
| Moving too slow (losing momentum) | Pick 3 quick wins, show value by week 6 |
| Losing key people | Schedule retention 1:1s before they quit |
| Surprise customers | Notify major customers before close (or within 48 hours) |
| Cultural conflict | Do explicit culture workshops (values, norms) early |
| Unclear roles | New org chart published on Day 1 |
| No synergy progress | Assign dedicated synergy lead + weekly meetings |
| Founder disengaged | Require CEO to show up to key meetings for 90 days min |

## Post-Close Earnout Tracking (If Applicable)

If earnout is based on milestones (e.g., $500k if ARR > $1.5M by month 12):

- [ ] Goals written clearly (no ambiguity)
- [ ] Monthly dashboard auto-updated (revenue, churn, NRR)
- [ ] Quarterly check-in: are we on track?
- [ ] Document any "force majeure" (things outside your control)
- [ ] Have lawyer review milestone definitions (avoid disputes)

# Due Diligence Checklist — Seller Preparation

## Financial (15 items)

### Revenue & Growth
- [ ] 3 years audited financials (P&L, B/S, CF statement)
- [ ] Monthly revenue/EBITDA for last 24 months
- [ ] Customer revenue breakdown (top 20 customers by ARR)
- [ ] Revenue by segment / product / geography
- [ ] Bookings vs. revenue (subscription? enterprise? one-time?)
- [ ] Customer concentration: top 5 = [X]% of revenue

### Profitability & Margins
- [ ] Gross margin by product/customer segment
- [ ] CAC by cohort (how much to acquire each customer?)
- [ ] LTV by cohort (how much lifetime value?)
- [ ] Churn rate (monthly, annual) by cohort
- [ ] EBITDA bridge: revenue → EBITDA (show all adjustments)
- [ ] Run-rate EBITDA (current quarter annualized)

### Cash & Balance Sheet
- [ ] Cash position (actual + committed credit lines)
- [ ] Debt schedule (term, rate, maturity, covenants)
- [ ] Tax liability (current year, future)
- [ ] Receivables aging (DSO: days sales outstanding)
- [ ] Payables aging (DPO: days payable outstanding)

## Legal (12 items)

### Contracts
- [ ] Customer contracts (all material ones: >€YYY, >X customers)
- [ ] Vendor/supplier contracts (any vendor >10% COGS?)
- [ ] Employment agreements (key executives, non-compete)
- [ ] IP assignment agreements (from founders/employees)
- [ ] Board resolutions (last 3 years of decisions)
- [ ] Shareholder agreements (drag/tag rights, veto provisions)

### Compliance & Litigation
- [ ] Regulatory licenses (industry-specific: financial, healthcare, etc.)
- [ ] Litigation history (any lawsuits, settlements, open claims?)
- [ ] Tax audits (any issues with tax authority?)
- [ ] Regulatory investigations (GDPR, data privacy, competition?)
- [ ] Insurance policies (D&O, E&O, general liability, cyber)
- [ ] Material contracts with termination/change of control clauses (how many?)

## Intellectual Property (8 items)

- [ ] Patent list (utility, design, trademarks)
- [ ] Patent ownership (all assigned to company? Or founder personal?)
- [ ] Open source code audit (GPL, MIT, Apache, etc. — commercial impact?)
- [ ] Software licenses (any restrictive licenses that transfer risk?)
- [ ] Domain names & trademarks (who owns them legally?)
- [ ] Trade secrets / confidential algorithms (documented and protected?)
- [ ] Third-party IP licenses (any critical dependencies?)
- [ ] Code escrow (customer escrow agreements for source code?)

## Technology (12 items)

### Architecture & Security
- [ ] Tech stack summary (databases, frameworks, infrastructure)
- [ ] Security audit (recent? any findings?)
- [ ] Data protection (encryption, backup, disaster recovery plan)
- [ ] Penetration testing results (any vulnerabilities?)
- [ ] Business continuity plan (RTO: recovery time objective?)
- [ ] Cloud infrastructure (AWS/Azure/GCP costs, contracts, lock-in?)

### Operations
- [ ] System uptime / reliability metrics (99.X%? SLAs met?)
- [ ] Scalability assessment (can handle revenue growth [X]x?)
- [ ] Technical debt assessment (what would need refactoring?)
- [ ] API documentation (customer-facing APIs documented?)
- [ ] Deployment process (manual? CI/CD? frequency?)
- [ ] Incident response playbook (documented?)

## People (8 items)

- [ ] Org chart (reporting structure, names, titles)
- [ ] Key person risk assessment (if [person] leaves, what breaks?)
- [ ] Compensation: salaries, bonuses, benefits
- [ ] Stock options: granted, vested, strike price (any vesting cliff post-close?)
- [ ] Employment agreements: any non-compete? Garden leave?
- [ ] Turnover analysis: headcount by year, who left and why?
- [ ] Diversity metrics (team composition for culture fit)
- [ ] Headcount plan: planned hires / roles (are there secrets?)

## Customer (5 items)

- [ ] Customer list (all customers, ARR, contract end date)
- [ ] NPS / satisfaction scores (are customers happy?)
- [ ] Win/loss analysis (why do we win/lose deals?)
- [ ] Reference calls (buyer will call top 3-5 customers)
- [ ] Customer advisory board (key customers to keep engaged)

## Seller Prep Strategy

### Phase 1: Self-Audit (2 weeks before buyer engagement)
Go through checklist yourself. Identify:
- [ ] What documents are missing? (start gathering)
- [ ] What information is inconsistent? (reconcile)
- [ ] What will buyer be unhappy about? (prepare explanation)

### Phase 2: Red Flag Prep (1 week before)
For each concern:
- [ ] Prepare concise explanation (1 page, not 10)
- [ ] Show mitigation or path to resolution
- [ ] Anticipate buyer's concern and preempt it

### Phase 3: Data Room Setup
- [ ] Create standardized folder structure (Finance, Legal, Tech, People, Customers)
- [ ] Number each document (001, 002, etc.)
- [ ] Create Q&A log (buyer questions + your responses)
- [ ] Assign one owner for buyer requests (not everyone answering)

### Phase 4: Reference & Interview Prep
- [ ] Brief top 3-5 customers: "Buyer will call. Here's context."
- [ ] Brief key employees: "Buyer may interview. Here's what to say."
- [ ] Prepare key person to do reference calls (CEO or VP most credible)

## Red Flags for Seller (What Buyers Always Notice)

### Financial Red Flags
- ❌ Revenue declining YoY (especially if reason is unclear)
- ❌ Gross margin <40% (or significantly below competitor benchmark)
- ❌ Increasing CAC with decreasing LTV (unsustainable unit economics)
- ❌ Blended churn >5% monthly (customer base leaking)
- ❌ Large one-time revenue recognition (reliance on few deals)

### Legal Red Flags
- ❌ IP not fully assigned to company (founder retains rights?)
- ❌ Key customer contracts with auto-termination on change of control
- ❌ Pending litigation (open lawsuits, regulatory investigations)
- ❌ Employee non-competes that are unenforceable (or too restrictive)

### Technical Red Flags
- ❌ Single point of failure (one engineer knows critical system?)
- ❌ No disaster recovery plan (backup, RTO undefined)
- ❌ GPL/open source code in proprietary product (copyleft risk)
- ❌ Scaling challenges (can't handle [X]x traffic)

### People Red Flags
- ❌ High turnover (>20% annual)
- ❌ Key people have non-competes expiring post-close (will they leave?)
- ❌ No succession plan (if CEO leaves, who runs it?)

## Do's & Don'ts

### DO ✓
- [ ] Provide documents in buyer's requested format (PDF, Excel, etc.)
- [ ] Respond quickly (24-48 hours) to buyer requests
- [ ] Anticipate sensitive areas and bring them up first
- [ ] Have CFO/COO available for detailed Q&A
- [ ] Document everything in writing (email responses, not just calls)

### DON'T ✗
- [ ] Hide anything that will come out in DD anyway
- [ ] Provide conflicting information (A says X, B says Y)
- [ ] Over-explain or make excuses (just answer the question)
- [ ] Restrict access unnecessarily (builds suspicion)
- [ ] Change story based on buyer feedback

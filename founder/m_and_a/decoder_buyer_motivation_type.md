# Buyer Motivation Decoder — Who Are They Really?

**Listen for signals during first call. Adjust your pitch based on buyer type.**

---

## THE 4 BUYER TYPES

### TYPE 1: STRATEGIC (Highest Price, Longest Timeline)

**What they say:**
- "This fills a gap in our platform"
- "How does this help us compete with [competitor]?"
- "What's the customer overlap with our existing base?"
- "Can we integrate this by Q[X]?"

**What they really care about:**
- Product fit (70%)
- Time-to-market (15%)
- Revenue contribution (15%)

**Your pitch:**
- Lead with [product gap you fill]
- Show [customer proof]
- Emphasize [speed to market vs building themselves]
- Ask: "How would you integrate this? When's the timeline?"

**Timeline:** 8-12 weeks to close

**Price:** 8-12x revenue (highest)

---

### TYPE 2: PE / FINANCIAL BUYER (Moderate Price, Faster)

**What they say:**
- "What's your exit strategy post-acquisition?"
- "How scalable are your unit economics?"
- "Can you grow to $[10M] in 3 years?"
- "What's your margin trajectory?"

**What they really care about:**
- Growth trajectory (40%)
- Unit economics (30%)
- Team staying (20%)
- Exit opportunity (10%)

**Your pitch:**
- Lead with [growth % + margin potential]
- Show [clear path to $Xm revenue within 3 years]
- Emphasize [founder staying to execute]
- Ask: "What's your 5-year exit plan? What multiple are you targeting?"

**Timeline:** 6-8 weeks to close

**Price:** 6-8x revenue (moderate)

---

### TYPE 3: TUCK-IN / COMPETITIVE ACQUISITION (Lower Price, Fastest)

**What they say:**
- "How quickly can we integrate your customers?"
- "Are these customers competitive with ours?"
- "What's your cost structure like?"
- "Can we shut down your product by month 6?"

**What they really care about:**
- Customer acquisition (50%)
- Cost structure (30%)
- Integration ease (20%)

**Your pitch:**
- Lead with [customer value + minimal integration cost]
- Show [how easily customers migrate to your platform]
- Emphasize [quick time-to-synergy]
- Ask: "What's your integration timeline? When do you want to migrate customers?"

**Timeline:** 4-6 weeks to close

**Price:** 4-6x revenue (lowest) but fastest

---

### TYPE 4: EGO ACQUISITION (Erratic, Often No Deal)

**What they say:**
- "Your founder is famous in [space]; we want you on our team"
- "Let's just do a stock deal, no cash"
- "You'll be in charge of [new division] with big title"
- "We want your branding in our product"

**What they really care about:**
- Founder prestige (80%)
- Stock story (20%)

**Red flags:**
- No financial rigor
- Vague integration plan
- Stock-heavy, no cash
- Loose earnout terms

**Your approach:**
- Be skeptical. Get real numbers.
- Require cash at close + earnout.
- Define role clearly in writing.
- Have walk-away plan.

**Timeline:** Unpredictable (could collapse)

**Price:** All over (could be too-low stock deal)

## HOW TO RESPOND

**If STRATEGIC:**
- Ask: "What's your integration roadmap?"
- Emphasize: Speed to market + product fit
- Negotiate: Higher valuation on integration certainty
- Accept: Longer timeline (worth the higher price)

**If PE:**
- Ask: "What's your exit plan?"
- Emphasize: Growth trajectory + unit economics
- Negotiate: Earnout tied to achieving $[target] by year 2
- Accept: Moderate valuation + growth-oriented earnout

**If TUCK-IN:**
- Ask: "What's your customer migration plan?"
- Emphasize: Integration ease + cost synergies
- Negotiate: Speed + earnout on customer retention
- Accept: Lower upfront, but faster close

**If EGO:**
- Ask: "What does the financial structure look like?"
- Red flag: If they get evasive, walk away
- Require: Cash at close + clear earnout metrics
- Consider: Walking away if terms stay vague

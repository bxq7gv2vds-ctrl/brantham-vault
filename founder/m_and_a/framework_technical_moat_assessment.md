# FRAMEWORK: TECHNICAL MOAT ASSESSMENT

**Buyers want to know: How defensible is your tech? Can competitors copy you easily? This framework scores your moat.**

---

## THE 5-POINT MOAT SCORECARD

| Moat Type | Weak (1 pt) | Medium (2 pts) | Strong (3 pts) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Switching Cost** | Easy to leave (customer can replace in weeks) | Moderate (2–3 months of work) | **High (3+ months of transition, retraining)** |
| **Data Advantage** | No unique data; commodity dataset | Some proprietary data but competitors have similar | **Defensible dataset; 10x larger/richer than competitors** |
| **Proprietary Algorithm** | Using standard techniques; no special sauce | Optimized algorithms; faster/better than competitors | **Patented or trade-secret algorithms competitors can't replicate** |
| **Network Effects** | No network benefit; customers use independently | Moderate network effects; some multi-user value | **Strong network effects; product more valuable with more users** |
| **Architectural Moat** | Simple system; easy to replicate | Complex but possible to replicate (6–12 months) | **Highly complex; deep platform that's hard to rebuild (18+ months)** |

---

## SCORING YOUR MOAT

| Total Score | Moat Strength | Valuation Impact | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| **5–7 pts** | Weak | No premium; [3–4x] multiple | High (easy to disrupt) |
| **8–12 pts** | Medium | Slight premium; [5–6x] multiple | Moderate (competitors can copy in 12m) |
| **13–15 pts** | Strong | Significant premium; [7–9x] multiple | Low (defensible 3+ years) |

---

## HOW BUYERS VALUE YOUR MOAT

**Strong moat (13–15 pts):**
- ✅ Buyer comfortable paying premium (8–9x ARR)
- ✅ Lower integration risk (tech remains valuable post-acquisition)
- ✅ Earnout targets realistic (tech doesn't degrade post-close)

**Weak moat (5–7 pts):**
- ❌ Buyer discounts price heavily (3–4x ARR)
- ❌ Higher risk buyer replaces your tech with theirs (product killed)
- ❌ Earnout targets risky (if buyer deprioritizes, tech deteriorates)

## EXAMPLE: YOUR COMPANY SCORES 13/15

| Factor | Score | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Switching Cost** | 3 | Enterprise customers spend 6+ months on implementation; high transition cost |
| **Data Advantage** | 3 | 2 years of customer behavioral data; competitor have <6 months |
| **Proprietary Algorithm** | 2 | Optimized ML model; not patented but hard to replicate quickly |
| **Network Effects** | 2 | Some multi-customer collaboration; not core to value prop |
| **Architectural Moat** | 3 | Complex, distributed system; would take competitor 12+ months to rebuild |
| **TOTAL** | **13/15** | **Strong moat → Justifies 7–8x multiple** |

## HOW TO COMMUNICATE YOUR MOAT TO BUYERS

**To a Strategic Buyer:**
> "Our technical moat is [switching cost + architecture + data]. A competitor would need [6–12] months and [X engineers] to replicate our platform. Our customer switching cost is [3–6] months of integration, creating stickiness that benefits you post-acquisition."

**To a PE Buyer:**
> "Our tech isn't a commodity. We've built [specific defensibility]. That means your revenue base is sticky, and earnout targets are achievable."

---

## MOAT IMPROVEMENT ROADMAP (Before Selling)

**If your moat scores <10:**
- Build proprietary data advantages (accumulate unique datasets)
- Deepen switching costs (integrate with customer workflows)
- Develop algorithms competitors can't easily replicate
- Create network effects (customer collaboration, community)

**Each point increase = +0.5–1x valuation multiple.**

---

## RED FLAGS (Weak Moat Signals)

🚩 Customers can switch to competitor in <3 months  
🚩 Technology is mostly open-source + customization (no secret sauce)  
🚩 Competitors have identical feature set (no differentiation)  
🚩 Data advantage minimal (competitors have same data)  
🚩 Architecture is simple/commodity (easy to replicate in 3–6 months)

---

## NEXT STEPS

1. Score your moat on the 5-point scale
2. Identify strongest moat element (lean into it)
3. Address weak points (improve before selling if possible)
4. Communicate moat clearly to buyers (part of valuation pitch)

# Deal Scorecard — 15-Minute Quality Filter

Use this the moment a buyer approaches. If score < 60/100, politely decline or negotiate harder.

## Scoring Grid (Rate 0–10 each)

| Dimension | 10 = Perfect | 5 = Okay | 0 = Deal-killer | Your Score |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Price / Valuation** | 10x+ EBITDA, beats comps by 20%+ | 6–8x EBITDA, in-line with comps | <4x EBITDA | __ / 10 |
| **Buyer Financial Health** | Public, $1B+ revenue, no debt | VC-backed profitable, $100M+ revenue | Bootstrapped, negative cash, unproven | __ / 10 |
| **Buyer Acquisition Track Record** | 5+ successful integrations, no layoffs | 2–3 acquisitions, standard integration | No prior acquisitions, unpredictable | __ / 10 |
| **Strategic Fit (Synergies)** | Clear cross-sell, product integration, market | Decent overlap, some synergy | No clear synergy, financial play only | __ / 10 |
| **Timeline (Realistic)** | 3–4 months to close | 5–6 months | 9+ months or undefined | __ / 10 |
| **Founder Role Post-Close** | CEO or strong operational role, 2yr contract | VP/Advisor role, 12mo retention | Founder exit, no guaranteed role | __ / 10 |
| **Earnout Mechanics** | Revenue-based, buyer-influenced, 1 year | Revenue or EBITDA, 2 years | Arbitrary KPIs, vague triggers, 3+ years | __ / 10 |
| **Cultural Fit** | Met team, shared values, excited about vision | Professional, neutral relationship | Misaligned values, red flags on leadership | __ / 10 |
| **Legal / Indemnity Terms** | Standard reps, 12mo cap, R&W insurance | Standard + some buyer-friendly items | Aggressive reps, 24mo cap, no insurance | __ / 10 |
| **Transparency** | Buyer open about synergies, risks, structure | General openness, some selective info | Secretive, evasive, hidden conditions | __ / 10 |

## Scoring Interpretation

| Total Score | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| **80–100** | **PURSUE AGGRESSIVELY.** Negotiate terms, get legal involved, move to LOI. |
| **70–79** | **GOOD CANDIDATE.** Negotiate weak spots (price, earnout, founder role). Proceed if they move. |
| **60–69** | **BORDERLINE.** Only proceed if you can improve 2–3 weak dimensions. Consider walking if they won't budge. |
| **40–59** | **RISKY.** Red flags present. Either walk or make major demands (higher price, shorter earnout, retained role, etc.). |
| **< 40** | **PASS.** Not worth your time. Politely decline and continue pursuing other buyers. |

## Quick Dimension Deep-Dives

### Price / Valuation (The Financial Anchor)

**10/10 if:**
- Offer is 10x+ EBITDA AND beats your walkaway by 20%+
- Cash component is >80% (not earnout-heavy)

**5/5 if:**
- Offer is 6–8x EBITDA (market rate)
- Mix of 70/30 cash/earnout is acceptable

**0/10 if:**
- Offer is <4x EBITDA (lowball)
- Earnout >50% (buyer passing risk to you)

**Your action:** If buyer scores <5, counter with +30% and see if they move. If they won't budge, it's not a real offer.

---

### Buyer Financial Health

**10/10 if:**
- Public company OR VC-backed unicorn with recent funding
- Revenue >$500M and profitable
- No financing conditions on the deal

**5/5 if:**
- Series B/C funded, $50–200M revenue, path to profitability
- Financing in place or VCs committed

**0/10 if:**
- Bootstrapped, no external funding, <$10M revenue
- Needs to raise money for acquisition
- Recent layoffs or financial stress signals

**Your action:** If <5, require 100% cash at close and full due diligence on their balance sheet. Or walk.

---

### Buyer Acquisition Track Record

**10/10 if:**
- 5+ successful acquisitions
- No major departures post-close
- Public case studies or references available

**5/5 if:**
- 2–3 acquisitions, mostly positive outcomes
- Can reference 1–2 founders who stayed happy

**0/10 if:**
- No prior acquisitions (first-time buyer = high risk)
- History of layoffs post-close
- Founders describe acquisition as painful

**Your action:** If <5, ask for founder references directly. Call them (not buyer's cherry-picked list). If they won't provide, walk.

---

### Strategic Fit (Synergies)

**10/10 if:**
- Clear cross-sell to buyer's existing customer base
- Technology integration planned and budgeted
- Market expansion opportunity

**5/5 if:**
- Some customer overlap, decent synergy
- Product fits buyer's roadmap

**0/10 if:**
- Buyer is purely a financial acquirer
- No clear how your product helps their business
- Buyer is competitor (fear of product deprecation)

**Your action:** If <5, negotiate higher price to compensate for lack of synergy. Buyer shouldn't pay strategic premium if there's no synergy.

---

### Timeline (Closing Speed)

**10/10 if:**
- Buyer commits to <4 months close
- No financing contingency
- Clear, realistic DD scope

**5/5 if:**
- 5–6 months is acceptable
- Financing in hand or committed

**0/10 if:**
- 9+ months or undefined
- Multiple financing conditions
- Buyer is moving slowly (bad signal)

**Your action:** If >6 months, negotiate earn-out acceleration or speed incentives. Long timelines = more deal risk.

---

### Founder Role Post-Close

**10/10 if:**
- You stay as CEO or lead product/business
- 2–3 year employment agreement locked in
- Bonus/equity potential tied to performance

**5/5 if:**
- VP role or C-suite position guaranteed
- 12–18 month retention, then optional
- Competitive comp

**0/10 if:**
- Founder exits immediately
- Advisory role only (low influence)
- Vague post-close title

**Your action:** If <5, make this a negotiation point. Lock in your role in LOI. Don't accept "we'll figure it out post-close."

---

### Earnout Mechanics

**10/10 if:**
- Revenue-based (you control it)
- 1-year earnout period
- Buyer can't artificially suppress (e.g., can't cut marketing)
- Clear, auditable KPIs

**5/5 if:**
- EBITDA or revenue-based, buyer-influenced (some control lost)
- 2-year earnout, reasonable hurdles
- Buyer has incentive to help you succeed

**0/10 if:**
- Arbitrary metrics (buyer controls outcome)
- 3+ year earnout (too long)
- Vague KPIs, no audit mechanism
- Buyer can manipulate metrics (cut costs, shift revenue)

**Your action:** If <5, reduce earnout %, shorten period, or increase fixed cash. Earnouts are unpredictable; minimize them.

---

### Cultural Fit

**10/10 if:**
- You've met leadership team, excited about vision
- Shared values on product, customer success
- Team wants to work there

**5/5 if:**
- Professional, neutral relationship
- Minor value differences, workable

**0/10 if:**
- Leadership misalignment
- Fear of culture clash
- Team will be unhappy post-close

**Your action:** If <5, do team interviews. Ask about their acquisition experience. If gut says "no," trust it—cultural mismatches are hard to fix post-close.

---

### Legal / Indemnity Terms

**10/10 if:**
- Standard reps (12 months typical)
- Cap at 12–18mo revenue
- R&W insurance at buyer's cost
- Reasonable carve-outs for known issues

**5/5 if:**
- Standard reps with some buyer-friendly tweaks
- Cap at 18–24mo revenue
- Some negotiation possible

**0/10 if:**
- Aggressive reps (unlimited categories)
- Cap >24mo or no cap
- Buyer refusing R&W insurance
- Buyer demanding seller indemnity on everything

**Your action:** If <5, get experienced M&A lawyer. Non-standard terms = buyer is shifting risk to you. Don't accept without leverage.

---

### Transparency

**10/10 if:**
- Buyer open about synergies, risks, integration plan
- Founder references available
- Clear communication on next steps

**5/5 if:**
- Generally open, some confidentiality around financials
- Responsive to questions

**0/10 if:**
- Evasive on key questions
- Hidden conditions or terms
- Slow communication, unresponsive

**Your action:** If <5, transparency problems are red flags. Don't proceed without clarity on all major terms. Use this to negotiate harder or walk.

---

## Quick Walk-Away Triggers

**Immediate PASS (don't waste time):**
- Buyer won't disclose financing or financial health → potential scam
- Buyer is competitor seeking to acquire-and-kill → legal risk
- Buyer demands >50% earnout → too much risk for you
- Buyer won't commit to founder role → you'll lose leverage post-close
- Buyer has reputation for layoffs → team exodus likely
- Buyer trying to pay entirely in stock → illiquidity risk

## Negotiation Levers by Score

| Score | Your Negotiation Leverage | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 80–100 | HIGH. Buyer wants you. | Ask for +10–15% price, shorter earnout, founder role. |
| 70–79 | MEDIUM-HIGH. Buyer is interested but has options. | Ask for +5–10%, improve weak dimensions. |
| 60–69 | MEDIUM. Buyer may have other candidates. | Ask for +3–5% or major term improvements. Prepare walk-away. |
| 40–59 | LOW. Multiple red flags. | Only worth proceeding if you can materially improve 2+ dimensions. |
| < 40 | NONE. Walk away and find better buyers. | Don't waste management bandwidth. |

## Example Walkthrough

**Hypothetical: Acme SaaS**

```
Current ARR: $3M
Last 12m growth: 25% YoY
Churn: 4% annually (acceptable)
NRR: 105% (good)
Gross margin: 78%
Top 5 customers: 55% of ARR
Magic number: 0.85 (slightly low)
Founder: Staying 18 months post-close
Tech debt: Moderate

Step 1: Baseline
- ARR $3M, growth 25%, NRR 105% → 4.5x multiple
- Baseline: $3M × 4.5x = $13.5M

Step 2: Adjustments
- Positive: NRR > 100% (+20%), recurring 95%+ (+10%), founder staying (+10%) = +40% / 2 = +20%
- Negative: Churn 4% (-10%), concentration 55% (-10%), magic < 1.0 (-10%) = -30% / 2 = -15%
- Net adjustment: +20% - 15% = +5%

Step 3: Adjusted range
- Conservative: $13.5M × 0.85 - 15% = $10.2M
- Base case: $13.5M + 5% = $14.2M
- Optimistic: $13.5M × 1.15 + 20% = $17.6M

Step 4: Comps check
- Found 3 similar companies: 4x, 5x, 4.5x avg = 4.5x
- Matches your multiple ✓

Step 5: Buyer type adjustment
- If strategic buyer: $14.2M × 1.2 = $17.0M

Step 6: Final range
- Conservative: $10M–$12M
- Base case: $14M–$15M
- Optimistic (strategic): $16M–$18M

Step 7: Walkaway
- Founder needs $10M (retirement target)
- Won't accept < 25% earnout (risk too high)
- Timeline: Q4 2026 (6 months away)

DECISION: Start conversations asking for $14M–$16M (base–optimistic range); accept $12M–$13M if earnout is 30%+ with clear vesting.
```

## What NOT to Do

❌ **Anchor too high without proof.** If you ask for 8x revenue and comps are 4.5x, buyer will dismiss you.

❌ **Accept first offer.** Buyer's first bid is always 20–40% below walk-away price.

❌ **Move on cash alone.** If earnout is 50%, you're betting on buyer execution, not business quality.

❌ **Ignore founder retention impact.** If founder leaves, valuation drops 15–20%; lock yourself in.

❌ **Forget about liquidity preference.** $10M earnout over 24m months is NOT worth $10M cash today. Factor in risk.

- [ ] I know my churn

- [ ] I know my gross margin

- [ ] I know my walk-away price
- [ ] I know what role I want post-close

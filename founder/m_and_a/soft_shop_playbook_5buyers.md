# Soft Shop Playbook — 5 Buyers in 30 Days

**Goal:** Gauge interest from 3-5 strategic buyers without full market exposure. Get price signals early.

---

## Step 1: Identify 5 Targets (Week 1)

**Criteria:**
- Have your customers as customers (distribution synergy)
- Enough scale to afford you ($50M+ revenue)
- Recent acquisitions in your space (track record)
- CEO/investor you know or can reach through intro

**Example targets:**
```
Strategic 1: [Competitor who could bundle you]
Strategic 2: [Adjacent product company (expand features)]
Strategic 3: [Platform play (you become vertical)]
PE 1:       [PE firm with SaaS focus]
Venture:    [Growth-stage founder buyer]
```

---

## Step 2: Get Intros (Week 1-2)

**Don't cold-email.** Use:
- Your board members (intro to their portfolio founders)
- Your advisors (intro to investor contacts)
- Your VCs (intro to other portfolio companies)
- Your largest customer (intro to their strategic partners)

**Email template:**

> Subject: Intro — [Founder] × [Strategic Buyer CEO]
>
> Hi [Mutual friend],
>
> I'm planning some exploratory conversations around strategic options for [Company]. [Buyer CEO] came to mind because [reason: distribution fit / customer base / recent acquisition in space].
>
> Would you be open to introducing us for a 20-min exploration?

---

## Step 3: Initial Approach (Week 2)

**Email from Founder + Mutual Intro:**

> Hi [Buyer CEO],
>
> [Mutual friend] suggested I reach out. We're exploring strategic options for [Company] — a [description] serving [customers]. We've hit [$X ARR, Y% growth] with [key strength: unit economics / retention / moat].
>
> I'm not actively shopping the company, but given your [distribution / platform], I thought an exploratory conversation could be valuable for both of us.
>
> Would you be open to a 30-min call [Day at time]?
>
> —[Your name]

**Key:** "Not shopping" (true); "exploring strategic options" (open); "could be valuable" (benefits buyer too).

---

## Step 4: Discovery Call (Week 2-3)

**30-min structure:**
- Your story: 5 min (what you built, traction)
- Buyer's interest: 10 min (ask why they might care)
- Synergies: 10 min (where you could fit)
- Next steps: 5 min (data room access? deeper conversation?)

**What you're listening for:**
- [ ] "That could be valuable" (genuine interest)
- [ ] "We've looked at [competitors]" (appetite for acquisition)
- [ ] "Let me talk to [team]" (next layer engagement)
- [ ] "Can you send us a deck?" (serious)

---

## Step 5: Second Meeting + Info Memo (Week 3-4)

**If interested, buyer will ask for:**
- Information memorandum (5-pager)
- Financial statements (last 2-3 years)
- Customer list (anonymized OK)

**Send with NDA:**
> "We're providing confidential information under the attached NDA. Let me know when you're ready to explore further."

**Buyer timeline:** "Will review and get back to you in [2-3 weeks]."

---

## Step 6: Inbound Decision (Week 4)

**Buyer will respond with:**

### ✅ "Let's pursue this"
→ Move to LOI + advisor engagement

### ⚠️ "Interesting but [X concern]"
→ Address concern; extend soft shop to more buyers

### ❌ "Not right timing / fit"
→ "Appreciate the feedback. Happy to reconnect in [6m]."

---

## Parallel Playbook: Multiple Simultaneous Buyers

**Best approach:** Don't wait for buyer 1 → Move all 5 in parallel.

```
Week 1:  Intro to all 5
Week 2:  Discovery calls with 2-3
Week 3:  Info memo to interested parties
Week 4:  Deep dives / LOI discussions with 2-3 concurrently
```

**Advantage:** Competition drives price up; no waiting.

**Caution:** Non-exclusive soft shop OK; don't sign exclusivity until LOI.

---

## Red Flags During Soft Shop

| Flag | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| Buyer asks for code access (W2) | Too early; they're skeptical of traction |
| Buyer won't sign NDA | Major red flag; not serious or will steal IP |
| "Send us customer list unredacted" | Red flag; planning to poach after you reject |
| Radio silence for 2+ weeks | Passed; move on |
| "We're interested but...no budget" | Not interested; deprioritize |
| Mentions your info to competitor | Breach; stop sharing immediately |

---

## What NOT to Do

❌ Shop to 50+ buyers (market knows you're for sale; kills leverage)  
❌ Pitch competitor + you in same week (confidentiality breach risk)  
❌ Drop the company name in first email (too aggressive)  
❌ Ask for valuation (too early; let them bid)  
❌ Go exclusive with first buyer (wait for 2-3 interested)

---

## Timeline to LOI

```
Week 1: Intros
Week 2: Discovery calls
Week 3: Info memo to 2-3 interested
Week 4: Second conversations; 1-2 serious
Week 5: LOI draft; legal engagement
Week 6: LOI negotiation
Week 7: LOI signed; exclusivity begins
```

**Total: 7 weeks from intro to LOI.** (Can compress to 4-5 if urgent.)

---

## Managing 5 Buyers Without Exclusivity

**Key message:** "We're exploring conversations with a few strategic partners. If you're interested, we can move to LOI exclusivity."

**Benefit:** Buyer knows there's competition → faster decision + higher offer.

**Risk:** Buyer might back off ("too much process"). Accept it; you only want serious buyers.

---

## Success Metrics

By end of soft shop:
- [ ] 2+ LOI discussions in-flight
- [ ] Valuation signals from 2 buyers (range)
- [ ] Understanding of buyer priorities (price / earnout / integration risk)
- [ ] Decision: Continue with highest bidder(s) or stay independent

If <1 LOI discussion: Soft shop was unsuccessful; either rethink positioning or improve data.

# Customer Concentration Risk — Red Flag Detector

**Reality:** If >20% of ARR from 1 customer, acquisition price drops 10-40%.

---

## Risk Assessment Matrix

| Largest Customer | Risk Level | Impact on Valuation | Action |
|------------------|-----------|-------------------|--------|
| <10% of ARR | ✅ Low | 0% discount | Continue |
| 10-20% | ⚠️ Medium | 5-10% discount | Monitor; diversify gradually |
| 20-30% | 🚩 High | 10-20% discount | Actively diversify; disclose |
| 30-50% | 🔴 Critical | 20-40% discount | Major red flag; defer sale |
| >50% | 🛑 Deal-killer | 50%+ discount | Don't sell; risk is too high |

---

## Example: Concentration Impact

**Your ARR:** $3M  
**Largest customer:** $1M (33% of ARR)

**Buyer valuation (5× ARR multiple):**
- Without concentration risk: $15M
- With 30% concentration: $9-12M
- **Cost of concentration: $3-6M**

---

## Mitigation Before Sale

### Option 1: Diversify (Slow but Real)
**Timeline:** 6-12 months before going to market

1. **Launch new sales initiative** targeting SMB segment (smaller ACV, more customers)
2. **Add new customer cohort** with same use case as concentrated customer
3. **Expand into new vertical** (different customer base)

**Goal:** Reduce top customer from 30% → 15% by close date.

---

### Option 2: Get Contractual Commitment
**Timeline:** 3 months before sale

**With your large customer:**
> "We're exploring strategic options for the company. Would you consider a [3-5 year] renewal at [current price / slight discount]?"

**Outcome:** Signed LOI for customer renewal reduces buyer's churn risk.

---

### Option 3: Disclose + Mitigate in Negotiation
**During LOI discussion:**
> "We have 1 customer at $X ARR (30% of revenue). They're in [stable industry], contract renews [DATE], and we're [already expanding elsewhere]. Here's our diversification plan."

**Buyer response:** "OK, we'll discount valuation by 20% to account for churn risk. But if customer renews post-close, we'll add $2M earnout."

---

## Risk Conversation with Buyer

### What Buyer Worries About
1. **Churn risk:** "Will this customer leave after we take over?"
2. **Pricing power:** "Are we locked into low price for big customer?"
3. **Contract terms:** "What's renewal date? Auto-renew? Termination clause?"
4. **Founder relationship:** "Is sale relationship-dependent? Will customer leave if you go?"

### Your Response Template
> "Our largest customer is $X ARR (Y% of revenue). 
> - **Stability:** They've been with us [Z] years, [low/medium churn rate]
> - **Diversification plan:** We've added [# new customers] in [Q/Y], now [reduced to X%]
> - **Contract:** [Auto-renew / term until DATE] with [X days notice to cancel]
> - **Relationship:** Owned by [VP of Customer Success, not founder], so yours to lead post-sale"

## Legitimate Reasons for Concentration (+ Mitigation)

| Scenario | How to Disclose | How to Mitigate |
|----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Enterprise customer (large ACV) | "Land with enterprise, expand SMB" | Show SMB cohort growing 50% YoY |
| Customer in power / tech industry | "Customer in sticky industry, low churn" | Provide churn data proving stickiness |
| Recent large win | "Won [DATE], contract [X years]" | Show pipeline; prove not one-time |
| Product/market fit in niche | "Dominant position in [vertical]" | Prove can expand to adjacent verticals |

## Timeline to Address Concentration

### 12 months before sale
- [ ] Identify concentration risk (% of ARR from top customer)
- [ ] If >20%: Start diversification plan

### 6 months before sale
- [ ] New customer cohorts showing traction (month-over-month growth)
- [ ] Top customer contract signed/renewed
- [ ] Diversification narrative ready for LOI

### 3 months before sale
- [ ] Concentration reduced to <20% OR
- [ ] Clear diversification story + top customer locked in
- [ ] Buyer data ready for data room

### At LOI / Data Room
- [ ] Transparent disclosure of concentration
- [ ] Evidence of mitigation/diversification
- [ ] Top customer contract signed & in data room

---

## Red Flags Buyer Will Look For

- [ ] Customer churn data contradicts "sticky" claim
- [ ] No new customers added in 12 months
- [ ] Customer concentration increasing (you're losing others)
- [ ] Large customer contract doesn't have auto-renewal
- [ ] Founder-dependent relationship (customer only knows you)
- [ ] No diversification plan (shows you haven't thought about it)

---

## Negotiation Red Line

**Don't accept:**
- Price reduction >25% due to concentration
- Earnout >50% contingent on customer renewal
- Non-compete preventing you from working with large customer post-sale

**Fallback:** Reduce earnout, extend timeline, add concentration carve-out (if customer churns, earnout adjusts).

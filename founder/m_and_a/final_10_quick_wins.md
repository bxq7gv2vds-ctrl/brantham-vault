# 10 Quick Wins (3-6 months avant M&A) + Checklist final

---

## Quick Win 1: Diversify top customers

**Action** : 2 new enterprise deals ($500k each)  
**Impact** : Top 10 drops from 25% → 22% (de-risk)  
**Timeline** : 90 days  
**Owner** : VP Sales

**How**:
- Identify 10 lookalike customers (similar to top customer)
- Fast-track sales (expedited contract)
- Win 2 before M&A launch

---

## Quick Win 2: Improve NRR

**Action** : 1-2 retention campaigns  
**Impact** : NRR 118% → 122% (+50 bps = +1 multiple)  
**Timeline** : 60 days  
**Owner** : Product + CS team

**How**:
- Identify at-risk customers (low usage)
- Executive outreach + ROI discussions
- Win-back offers for churned customers

---

## Quick Win 3: Patch critical CVEs

**Action** : Security audit → remediate  
**Impact** : Removes deal-breaker risk  
**Timeline** : 30 days  
**Owner** : CTO

**How**:
- Run full CVE scan (npm/pip audit)
- Prioritize critical (CVSS >9)
- Patch & re-test before data room share

---

## Quick Win 4: Close 2 customer case studies

**Action** : Interview 2-3 Fortune 500 customers  
**Impact** : Credibility boost for deck  
**Timeline** : 45 days  
**Owner** : Marketing + sales

**How**:
- Pick best customers (big name, great results)
- Write 1-pager case study
- Get written quotes + permission to use logo

---

## Quick Win 5: Finalize financial model

**Action** : Auditor review (or accountant review)  
**Impact** : Removes financial question marks  
**Timeline** : 60 days  
**Owner** : CFO

**How**:
- Close books properly (no accrual games)
- Get reviewed financials (not just internal)
- Document all assumptions (growth, churn, etc)

---

## Quick Win 6: Build 5-person advisor board

**Action** : Recruit 3-5 industry experts  
**Impact** : Signals credibility + validation  
**Timeline** : 30 days  
**Owner** : CEO

**How**:
- Identify 5 former execs / domain experts
- Ask to advise (no cash, equity or honorarium)
- Include their bios in data room

---

## Quick Win 7: Fix compliance gaps

**Action** : SOC2 audit started / GDPR checklist done  
**Impact** : Removes security objection  
**Timeline** : 60-90 days  
**Owner** : Security + legal

**How**:
- Engage auditor (12-16 week SOC2 engagement)
- If not time, document what you're doing
- GDPR DPA signed with customers

---

## Quick Win 8: Hire weak spots

**Action** : One key hire (CTO, VP Sales, CFO)  
**Impact** : Removes key person dependency  
**Timeline** : 60-90 days  
**Owner** : Founder

**How**:
- Identify critical role missing (not founder-dependent)
- Fast-track hire
- Have them ready before data room

---

## Quick Win 9: Reduce monthly burn

**Action** : Cut 10-15% expenses (without cutting product)  
**Impact** : Improves optics, path to profitability  
**Timeline** : 30 days  
**Owner** : CFO

**How**:
- Audit vendor contracts (renegotiate)
- Cut redundant tools
- Optimize infrastructure costs

---

## Quick Win 10: Document product roadmap

**Action** : 12-month roadmap + reasoning  
**Impact** : Shows you know where product goes  
**Timeline** : 30 days  
**Owner** : Product

**How**:
- List top 10 features (prioritized)
- Explain why each matters
- Tie to customer feedback

## Checklist final : 30 days avant M&A launch

### Financial prep
- [ ] P&L + balance sheet audited/reviewed
- [ ] Cap table 100% accurate
- [ ] Revenue quality documented (recurring %)
- [ ] CAC, LTV, NRR calculated & documented
- [ ] Customer concentration map (top 10 %)

### Legal prep
- [ ] IP assignment agreements signed (all employees)
- [ ] Articles of incorporation + bylaws finalized
- [ ] Board resolutions from last 12m collected
- [ ] Customer contracts template finalized
- [ ] Employment agreements reviewed

### Technical prep
- [ ] Code audit completed
- [ ] CVEs patched (all critical)
- [ ] Security audit (external, if possible)
- [ ] SOC2 audit started (or roadmap)
- [ ] Infrastructure documented (diagram)
- [ ] Roadmap 12-month finalized

### Commercial prep
- [ ] Customer list (names, ARR, contract end, health)
- [ ] Case studies (3-5 largest customers)
- [ ] NPS survey results + historical trend
- [ ] Competitive analysis memo
- [ ] TAM / SAM / SOM documented

### Team prep
- [ ] Org chart + bios (exec team)
- [ ] Retention agreements signed (top 5)
- [ ] Cultural values articulated
- [ ] Advisory board finalized
- [ ] Key employee emails ready (to break news post-signing)

### Communications prep
- [ ] Pitch deck finalized (25-30 slides)
- [ ] 1-page exec summary
- [ ] Teaser memo (for initial outreach)
- [ ] NDA template (lawyer-reviewed)
- [ ] Data room structure planned

### Market prep
- [ ] Target buyer list (5-10 strategic, 3-5 PE)
- [ ] Primary contacts identified (warmish intros)
- [ ] Competitive intel (who else is buying in space?)
- [ ] Broker / banker (if hiring external)
- [ ] Investment banker selected (or decision made to DIY)

---

## Go-live checklist (week 1 of M&A process)

- [ ] Teasers sent (20 buyers, tracked responses)
- [ ] Data room set up (Intralinks / Firmex)

- [ ] Daily standup on M&A process (CEO + CFO + counsel)

## Timeline summary (real-world)

```
Month 1: Prep (financials, legal, tech, comms)
Month 2: Launch (teasers, data room, LOI round)
Month 3: Exclusive (pick 1-2 buyers, negotiate)
Month 4: Diligence (deep dives, customer calls, tech audit)
Month 5: Final negotiation (SPA cleanup, earn-out terms)
Month 6: Closing! (sign, get money, transition)
```

**Reality**: Often 4-6 months, rarely faster than 3.

---

## Final wisdom

1. **Don't rush** : Better to take 6 months + get good price than 3 months + bad price
2. **Get leverage** : Multiple buyers = you negotiate from strength
3. **Pick right buyer** : Price matters, but culture fit matters more
4. **Protect yourself** : Reps cap, indemnity, escrow are non-negotiable
5. **Trust your gut** : If something feels off, it probably is
6. **Celebrate post-close** : Not during process (jinx)

Good luck! 🚀

# Financial Due Diligence Response Checklist

**When:** After LOI signed, DD data room opened (30-60 days)

---

## Revenue Documentation
- [ ] Stripe/payment processor transaction export (24 months)
- [ ] Monthly revenue recognition sheet (cash vs. accrual)
- [ ] Customer invoice log (all customers, dates, amounts)
- [ ] Refund/chargeback log (frequency, reason, %)
- [ ] Subscription term distribution (1 year, 2 year, month-to-month %)
- [ ] Pricing changes history (did you raise prices? when? customer response?)
- [ ] Free trial to paid conversion rate (if applicable)
- [ ] Multi-year upfront revenue normalized (clearly marked)

---

## Customer Cohort Analysis
- [ ] Cohort retention table (monthly, 12 months back)
- [ ] ARR by customer acquisition year (proves growth consistency)
- [ ] Customer concentration (top 10, top 20 as % of revenue)
- [ ] Customer net retention rate (NRR %) clearly calculated
- [ ] Expansion revenue (upsells, add-ons) separate from new revenue
- [ ] Customer churn by cohort (which vintage churns most?)
- [ ] Sales cycle length (first contact → first payment)

---

## Cost Structure & Profitability
- [ ] COGS breakdown (hosting, payment fees, support, etc.)
- [ ] CAC calculation (sales + marketing spend / new customers acquired)
- [ ] LTV calculation (12-month and 24-month)
- [ ] CAC payback period (how many months to recover acquisition cost?)
- [ ] Monthly burn rate (if unprofitable, clearly explained)
- [ ] Gross margin by year (trend)
- [ ] EBITDA calculation (with reconciliation to tax return)
- [ ] One-time expenses identified (severance, tooling migration, etc.)

---

## Team & Headcount
- [ ] Headcount by department (eng, sales, support, admin)
- [ ] Fully-loaded cost per employee (salary + benefits + taxes)
- [ ] Comp benchmark vs. industry (are you paying market rate?)
- [ ] Employee turnover rate (and why they left)
- [ ] Option pool (vested vs. unvested, strike price)
- [ ] Founder compensation (salary, bonus, benefits—be honest about what's normalization)

---

## Balance Sheet & Cash
- [ ] Cash balance (reconcile to bank statement)
- [ ] Accounts receivable aging (how much revenue not yet collected?)
- [ ] Accounts payable schedule (when do you owe money?)
- [ ] Debt or loans (amounts, terms, covenants)
- [ ] Prepaid expenses (identify which should reverse)
- [ ] Deferred revenue (customer prepayments, liability side)
- [ ] Working capital statement (current assets vs. current liabilities)

---

## Normalized EBITDA Narrative
- [ ] Start with GAAP net income
- [ ] Add back: Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, Amortization
- [ ] Add back: One-time severance
- [ ] Add back: One-time tooling costs
- [ ] Add back: Stock-based comp (if going to acquirer)
- [ ] Add back: Founder excess compensation (what's "excess"?)
- [ ] Add back: Related-party expenses (if any)
- [ ] **Clearly labeled:** what's temporary vs. structural
- [ ] Show 3-year trend (EBITDA%, not just $ amount)

---

## Growth & Forward Metrics
- [ ] Last 12 months revenue (by month)
- [ ] YoY growth rate (%), QoQ
- [ ] Forward pipeline (signed contracts, pending deliverables)
- [ ] Forecast next 12 months (with assumptions clearly stated)
- [ ] Win rate (% of deals closing)
- [ ] Average deal size (ADS) and trend

---

## What NOT to Disclose (Or Carefully)

- [ ] Personal expenses mixed into company (attorney will flag)
- [ ] Revenue recognized irregularly (red flag for accounting)
- [ ] Customer lists to competitors (via reference calls only)
- [ ] Aggressive growth claims without cohort proof (will be questioned)

---

## Respond to Common DD Questions

| Question | Your Answer |
|----------|---|
| "Why is churn 8% when comps are 5%?" | [Honest reason] "Customer base is younger/SMB/longer sales cycle..." |
| "Who is your top customer?" | [Name] — [Industry] — [% of revenue] |
| "What if top customer leaves?" | "Diversification plan: X new verticals, Y new geographies" |
| "Why did revenue dip in month X?" | [Specific explanation] |
| "Are these cohorts validated?" | "Yes, we track via [tool], independently auditable" |
| "What's your actual CAC?" | [Math] = "We're at $X, recovering in Y months" |

---

## Quality Flags to Anticipate

Buyer WILL ask about:
- [ ] Top 10 customers (names, contract terms, any churn risk?)
- [ ] Customer concentration (feels too high? or healthy?)
- [ ] Gross margins (low compared to category? why?)
- [ ] Churn trends (rising or stable?)
- [ ] Sales efficiency (CAC/LTV ratio, payback period)
- [ ] Growth slowdown (if it exists, explain macro context)

## Before Buyer Calls

- [ ] Know your numbers cold (don't look them up during calls)
- [ ] Have 3 explanations ready for each metric (best, honest, worst-case)
- [ ] Bring your CFO/accountant to calls (if you have one)
- [ ] Prepare visuals (cohort charts, trend lines, not raw sheets)
- [ ] Anticipate tough questions 24h in advance

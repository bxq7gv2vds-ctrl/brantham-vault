# 50 FINANCIAL DUE DILIGENCE QUESTIONS

**Use this checklist during buyer diligence. Request seller documentation for each question.**

---

## SECTION A: REVENUE & BOOKINGS (1–10)

- [ ] What is the last 3 years' revenue (calendar and fiscal year)?
- [ ] Break down revenue by: product line, customer segment, geography, contract type
- [ ] What is MRR trend (last 24 months, month-by-month)?
- [ ] What is ARR? How is it calculated (cash-based, accrual, deferred revenue)?
- [ ] What is the annual contract value (ACV) distribution (% <$5k, $5-50k, >$50k)?
- [ ] What is total booked/committed revenue for next 12 months (backlog)?
- [ ] How much revenue is multi-year contracts? How is it recognized?
- [ ] What % of revenue is recurring (subscription) vs. one-time (implementation, consulting)?
- [ ] What are the top 10 customers and their revenue (% of total)? Are any expiring soon?
- [ ] What is the customer acquisition cost (CAC) and payback period by channel?

---

## SECTION B: GROWTH & RETENTION (11–20)

- [ ] What is year-over-year growth rate (last 3 years)?
- [ ] What is gross revenue retention (GRR)? Net revenue retention (NRR) (last 3 years)?
- [ ] What is monthly/quarterly churn by cohort (customers acquired in Q1 20XX, Q2 20XX, etc.)?
- [ ] What is the monthly/quarterly churn *rate* (% of starting MRR that churns)?
- [ ] How many customers have churned in the last 24 months? Why (product fit, price, competitor, budget)?
- [ ] What is the expansion rate (% of existing customers increasing spend quarter-over-quarter)?
- [ ] What is the downsell/contraction rate? How much revenue is lost to downgrades?
- [ ] What is the logo retention rate (% of customers retained, regardless of ACV change)?
- [ ] What is the time-to-expansion (how long before a customer increases contract value)?
- [ ] What is the sales cycle length (first contact to deal close) by segment?

---

## SECTION C: MARGIN ANALYSIS (21–28)

- [ ] What is the gross margin (revenue − COGS)? What is included in COGS?
- [ ] What is the COGS as % of revenue (trend over 3 years)?
- [ ] What is the gross margin by product/segment?
- [ ] What is the operating margin (EBIT / revenue) for last 3 years?
- [ ] What is the EBITDA (earnings before interest, tax, depreciation, amortization)?
- [ ] What expenses are included/excluded from EBITDA calculation?
- [ ] What is the magic number (ARR growth / S&M spend)? Target: >0.75.
- [ ] What is the CAC ratio (S&M spend / net new ARR)? Target: <1.0.

---

## SECTION D: CASH FLOW & WORKING CAPITAL (29–35)

- [ ] What is the operating cash flow for last 3 years?
- [ ] What is the free cash flow (operating CF − CapEx)?
- [ ] What is the cash conversion cycle (days sales outstanding + days inventory − days payable)?
- [ ] What is the current working capital balance (current assets − current liabilities)?
- [ ] What is the trend in accounts receivable (days sales outstanding, DSO)?
- [ ] What is the trend in deferred revenue (liability that represents cash collected upfront)?
- [ ] What is the monthly cash burn rate (if not yet profitable)?

---

## SECTION E: BALANCE SHEET & LIABILITIES (36–40)

- [ ] What are total assets, liabilities, and equity (balance sheet) for last 3 years?
- [ ] What is the total debt outstanding (term loans, lines of credit, debt to founders)?
- [ ] What are the terms of all debt (interest rate, maturity, covenants, change-of-control clauses)?
- [ ] What are the contingent liabilities (lease obligations, pension liabilities, warranty reserves)?
- [ ] What is the cap table (fully diluted)? Any outstanding options, warrants, or convertible notes?

---

## SECTION F: TAX & ACCOUNTING (41–45)

- [ ] What is the corporate tax rate and tax position (any audits, disputes)?
- [ ] What are capitalized assets and depreciation schedules?
- [ ] What accounting method is used (cash vs. accrual)? Any adjustments between methods?
- [ ] Are there any tax credits, R&D deductions, or NOL (net operating loss) carryforwards?
- [ ] What is the tax liability (estimated and actual) for last 3 years? Any tax liens or disputes?

---

## SECTION G: FINANCIAL CONTROLS & AUDIT (46–50)

- [ ] Have financial statements been audited, reviewed, or compiled? By whom, and which years?
- [ ] What is the audit opinion (unqualified, qualified, going concern)?
- [ ] What are the key accounting policies (revenue recognition, reserves, estimates)?
- [ ] Are there internal controls over financial reporting? Any material weaknesses?
- [ ] What is the accounts payable aging (how long are vendor payments outstanding)?

---

## FOLLOW-UP QUESTIONS (PROBES)

After collecting answers, probe for red flags:

**Revenue:**
- Are large customers under long-term contracts? Any expiring soon?
- What % of revenue is from a single customer? Top 5 customers?
- Are there any revenue side letters or special terms (discounts, payment deferral)?

**Churn:**
- Is churn seasonal? Driven by specific customer segments?
- Are churned customers leaving for competitors or due to budget cuts?
- Is there a recovery/win-back strategy?

**Margins:**
- Is COGS increasing or decreasing as % of revenue?
- Are there any margin-eroding obligations (SLAs, refunds, commitments)?
- Is there a path to 70%+ gross margin within 2 years?

**Cash:**
- Is cash flow positive? What's the runway if unprofitable?
- Are there large customer payments that are scheduled but not guaranteed?
- Is working capital normalized (or are there payables/receivables anomalies)?

**Accounting:**
- Are revenue recognition policies conservative or aggressive?
- Are there any unaccounted-for liabilities or contingencies?
- Is the financial data comparable to prior years (no restatements)?

---

## RED FLAGS — ESCALATE TO ADVISOR IF YOU SEE:

🚩 **Revenue anomalies:**
- >30% revenue from single customer
- Declining NRR for >2 quarters
- Churn rate >5% MoM (enterprise SaaS should be <1%)
- Large deal in final quarter that skews growth (indicator of volatility)

🚩 **Margin erosion:**
- Gross margin <50% with no improvement plan
- Rising COGS or customer support costs
- Significant one-time costs that boost reported profitability

🚩 **Cash flow:**
- Negative free cash flow with no path to breakeven
- Long cash conversion cycle (DSO >90 days)
- Heavy reliance on deferred revenue for cash (unsustainable if churn rises)

🚩 **Accounting:**
- Audit opinion includes "going concern" warning
- Revenue recognized upfront but refundable/contingent
- Large year-end adjustments or restatements

🚩 **Tax/Legal:**
- Tax audits or disputes pending
- Uncertain tax positions with potential liability >5% of profit
- Leases or contingencies not reflected in liabilities

## DOCUMENT CHECKLIST — REQUEST THESE:

- [ ] P&L (income statement) — last 3 years, monthly or quarterly detail
- [ ] Balance sheet — last 3 years
- [ ] Cash flow statement — last 3 years (operating, investing, financing)
- [ ] Revenue detail — by customer, product line, segment (last 12 months, monthly)
- [ ] Customer list — current ARR, contract end date, churn risk (Y/N)
- [ ] Cohort analysis — revenue retention by customer cohort (acquisition quarter)
- [ ] Churn analysis — detailed list of churned customers in past 24 months + reasons
- [ ] Headcount & payroll — employee count trend, average comp, options outstanding
- [ ] Debt schedule — all outstanding loans, terms, covenants, interest rates
- [ ] Cap table (fully diluted) — founders, investors, employees, options pool
- [ ] Audit opinion / financial statement notes (if audited)
- [ ] Tax returns — last 3 years (with schedules)
- [ ] Tax positions — any uncertain tax positions, audits, or disputes
- [ ] Deferred revenue waterfall — billings, recognized revenue, remaining obligation

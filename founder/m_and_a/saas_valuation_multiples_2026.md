## Multiplier Adjustments

**Add +0.5–2x if:**
- Net retention > 120%
- Churn < 5%/month (SMB) or < 2% (Enterprise)
- Customer concentration: top 10 = <30% ARR
- Market growing > 30%/year
- Team includes ex-FAANG founders
- Proprietary tech / defensible moat

**Subtract -0.5–3x if:**
- Single customer > 20% ARR
- Churn > 8%/month
- Revenue concentration (top 5 = >60%)
- Market flatlining or declining
- Heavy tech debt / no CTO
- Key person dependency (single founder)

## Buyer Type Impact

| Buyer Type | Typical Multiple | Strategy |
|------------|-----------------|----------|
| **Financial buyer (PE)** | 6–12x | ROI target 3–5x in 5y. Earnings multiple = revenue multiple ÷ EBITDA margin. |
| **Strategic (bigger SaaS)** | 10–18x | Synergies justify premium. Will pay more for logo fit or vertical dominance. |
| **Strategic (vertical consolidator)** | 8–14x | Roll-up play. Wants cross-sell + cost synergies. |
| **Acqui-hire** | 2–5x | Mostly for team. Revenue secondary. |
| **Tuck-in / module buy** | 4–8x | Buyer integrating as product feature. |

---

## Profitability Impact

If **EBITDA-positive:**
- Multiple likely +30–50% higher than revenue-only
- Buyer willing to pay for cash flow

If **Burning > 20% of ARR:**
- Multiple likely -20–40% lower
- Buyer assumes 12–18m to profitability post-close

**Quick rule:** `Valuation ≈ ARR × Multiple × (EBITDA Margin adjustment)`

## How to Use This

1. **Find your ARR** + **segment** → baseline multiple
2. **Adjust** for churn, retention, concentration
3. **Know your buyer type** → pick realistic floor
4. **Target 10–15% above your walk-away** as opening ask

**Example:**
- $3M ARR, B2B SaaS, 98% net retention, 5% churn, 18% in top customer
- Base: 13x ($39M)
- Adjustments: +0.5 (retention), -1.0 (concentration) = 12.5x
- Final: $37.5M (13x multiple)
- Opening ask: $39–42M (1.05–1.12x buffer)
- Walk-away floor: $36M

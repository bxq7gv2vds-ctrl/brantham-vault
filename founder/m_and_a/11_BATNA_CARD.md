## Leverage Assessment

### What Makes Our BATNA Strong? ✓
- [ ] Can remain profitable & independent
- [ ] Multiple interested buyers (not just one)
- [ ] Strategic assets hard to replicate (IP, team, customer base)
- [ ] Market timing favorable (tailwinds, not headwinds)
- [ ] Founder willingness to walk (reduces desperation)

### What Makes Our BATNA Weak? ✗
- [ ] Dependent on single buyer
- [ ] Cash burn rate unsustainable (forced timeline)
- [ ] Limited alternative buyers in market
- [ ] Product/market fit unproven (risky to bet on organic growth)
- [ ] Founder burnout risk (want exit *now*)

**BATNA Strength Rating:** [1=Weak, 5=Strong] — **X/5**

**Implication:** Lower BATNA strength = accept lower valuation / more buyer-friendly terms

## Negotiation Strategy (Based on BATNA)

### If BATNA is STRONG (4-5/5)
- Anchor high (Aggressive DCF scenario)
- Lengthy exclusivity? → demand premium (2-3pp higher valuation)
- Earnout-heavy? → push back (require 70%+ cash)
- Long founder stay? → extra retention bonus

### If BATNA is MODERATE (2-3/5)
- Anchor at base case DCF
- Accept 60-day exclusivity (standard)
- Accept earnout up to 25% if metrics-driven
- Moderate founder stay (12-18 months) with clear role

### If BATNA is WEAK (1/5)
- Accept base case - discount (10-15%)
- Short exclusivity = urgent close, not leverage
- Earnout OK if tied to objective metrics (revenue)
- Founder stay negotiable if package is right

## Decision Framework

### When to Walk Away
```
IF valuation < Walk-Away Floor
   AND timeline pressure is high
   AND alternative buyers not yet engaged
  THEN: Activate BATNA Option (B or A)
```

### When to Stay & Counter
```
IF valuation close to walk-away (within 10%)
   AND terms mostly acceptable
   AND earnout potential exists
  THEN: Counter with [earnout structure] instead of walking
```

### When to Escalate
```
IF buyer signals weakness in negotiation
   AND we have BATNA optionality
  THEN: Bring in secondary buyer / extended timeline
```

## Pre-Negotiation BATNA Prep

- [ ] Validate BATNA runway: can we fund 12+ more months independently?
- [ ] Contact secondary buyer prospects to signal openness (softly)
- [ ] Calculate personal financial need (what's the minimum exit value needed?)
- [ ] Document founder psychological comfort with "keep going" scenario
- [ ] Align founding team on walk-away triggers (consensus required)
- [ ] Prepare "walking away" communication (shows confidence)
- [ ] Get legal review of earnout clawback scenarios before negotiating

---

## Notes

- BATNA changes as market conditions / company performance changes — **review quarterly**
- Walking away is a negotiation tactic (sometimes bluffing is the best leverage)
- But only bluff if you have a real BATNA to fall back on
- Earnout can bridge valuation gaps *only if* buyer has strong incentive to hit KPI
- Share BATNA with board / advisors — not with buyer

# 30 CUSTOMER & PRODUCT-MARKET FIT DUE DILIGENCE QUESTIONS

**For buyer's VP Product/Sales to assess customer retention risk and product moat.**

---

## SECTION A: CUSTOMER BASE & CONCENTRATION (1–8)

- [ ] How many customers do you have? What's the customer count trend (last 24 months)?
- [ ] What are the top 10 customers and their ARR? What % of total revenue do they represent?
- [ ] What's the concentration risk (Herfindahl index; target <0.15 for healthy portfolio)?
- [ ] Are any of your top customers considering churn or evaluating competitors?
- [ ] What's the customer acquisition breakdown: sales-driven vs. self-serve vs. partnership?
- [ ] What's the average customer lifecycle (acquisition to expansion to renewal)?
- [ ] Are customers sticky or price-sensitive? What's the switching cost?
- [ ] What % of customers are long-term contracts (>2 years)? How many are month-to-month?

---

## SECTION B: CUSTOMER SATISFACTION & RETENTION (9–16)

- [ ] What's the Net Promoter Score (NPS)? Trend over time?
- [ ] What's the customer satisfaction score (CSAT)? Any recent surveys?
- [ ] What's the logo retention rate (% of customers retained year-over-year)?
- [ ] What's the churn rate broken down: voluntary vs. involuntary?
- [ ] For customers that churned, what were the stated reasons?
- [ ] What's the expansion rate (% of existing customers increasing spend year-over-year)?
- [ ] What % of revenue comes from expansion vs. new customer acquisition?
- [ ] Do you have a customer success/support function? What's the quality?

---

## SECTION C: COMPETITIVE POSITIONING (17–22)

- [ ] Who are the top 3 competitors? What's your win/loss rate against each?
- [ ] What's your unique value proposition vs. competitors?
- [ ] What are customers saying about your product vs. alternatives (in sales calls, support)?
- [ ] Are there any competitors with superior features, pricing, or service?
- [ ] What's the barriers to customers switching to competitors (high switching cost)?
- [ ] How do you differentiate on product, price, or service?

---

## SECTION D: PRODUCT ROADMAP & INNOVATION (23–27)

- [ ] What's the product vision for the next 3 years?
- [ ] What are the top 5 features on the roadmap? Why these, and what customer pain do they solve?
- [ ] How do you prioritize features? Is it customer-driven, revenue-driven, or competitive-driven?
- [ ] What % of engineering time is spent on new features vs. technical debt/stability?
- [ ] Are customers asking for features you're not building? Why not?

---

## SECTION E: PRODUCT-MARKET FIT ASSESSMENT (28–30)

- [ ] Do you have product-market fit? What evidence supports this (NPS, growth, retention)?
- [ ] Are there any segments where you have strong PMF and weak PMF?
- [ ] What would you change about your product if you had unlimited resources?

---

## FOLLOW-UP QUESTIONS (PROBES)

**Customer concentration:**
- If your top 3 customers left, what would happen to growth?
- Are any of your top customers requiring custom features or SLAs?
- Are there any vertical concentrations (>30% from one industry)?

**Churn:**
- Is churn increasing or decreasing? Any seasonal patterns?
- Are there specific customer segments with higher churn?
- Is churn driven by product gaps, competitors, or pricing?

**Expansion:**
- Which customer segments have the highest expansion rates?
- What triggers a customer to expand (new use case, team growth)?
- Are you proactive in expansion or reactive to customer requests?

**Competition:**
- Who are you losing deals to? Why?
- What's the win rate against your top 3 competitors?
- How do customers perceive pricing vs. value?

**Product:**
- Are you a best-of-breed (best in category) or a must-have for your TAM?
- Do customers depend on your product for critical workflows?
- Could your customer build this in-house or switch to a competitor?

---

## RED FLAGS — ESCALATE TO ADVISOR IF YOU SEE:

🚩 **Concentration red flags:**
- Top 3 customers >50% of revenue
- Large customer ($1M+ ARR) using you as a bargaining chip for discounts
- Key customer recently acquired and may consolidate vendors post-integration
- Customers in declining industry vertical

🚩 **Churn red flags:**
- Churn rate >5% MoM (enterprise) or >10% MoM (SMB)
- Increasing churn trend (2+ consecutive quarters going up)
- Churn driven by specific customer segment (risk that Buyer can't fix quickly)
- High churn among customers <2 years old (indicates CAC payback issue)

🚩 **Competitive red flags:**
- Win rate <30% against top competitor
- Customers say you're "good but expensive"
- Feature set is 1–2 years behind competitors
- Large customers evaluating competitive replacements

🚩 **Product red flags:**
- NPS <20 (unhealthy); <50 (at risk)
- CSAT <80%
- Engineering team overloaded with tech debt; can't ship new features fast
- Product team has high turnover or lack of clear vision

🚩 **PMF red flags:**
- Growth slowing and churn rising simultaneously (indicates PMF decay)
- Customer acquisition cost increasing while LTV decreases
- Heavy discounting required to close deals
- Customers are trial switchers or multi-vendor evaluators (not deeply committed)

---

## CUSTOMER INTERVIEWS — ASK SELLER FOR:

**Top customers (direct calls):**
- [ ] List of willing reference customers (top 5, diverse segments)
- [ ] Background on their use case and business impact
- [ ] Any plan to evaluate or reduce spend with you (post-acquisition?)

**Lost customers (win/loss analysis):**
- [ ] List of customers lost in past 24 months
- [ ] Stated reason for churn (product, price, competitor, consolidation, bankruptcy)
- [ ] Sales team's assessment of win-loss patterns

---

## DOCUMENT CHECKLIST — REQUEST THESE:

- [ ] Customer list (all current customers + ARR, contract end date, churn risk assessment)
- [ ] Customer acquisition cost (CAC) breakdown by segment, channel, sales rep
- [ ] Customer lifetime value (LTV) by segment (revenue − support costs over lifetime)
- [ ] Cohort analysis (revenue retention by acquisition cohort; visualize improvement)
- [ ] Churn analysis (customers lost past 24 months, reasons, dollar impact)
- [ ] NPS survey results (recent; breakdown by customer segment)
- [ ] Win/loss analysis (past 12 months; customer quote feedback)
- [ ] Customer feedback / feature requests backlog (prioritized by customer demand)
- [ ] Competitor analysis (how customers compare you to alternatives)
- [ ] Product roadmap (next 12 months, customer feedback drivers)
- [ ] Sales collateral (pitch decks, case studies, testimonials)
- [ ] Support metrics (response time, resolution time, customer satisfaction by issue)
- [ ] Expansion data (upsell/cross-sell tracking, revenue growth per customer cohort)

# Intro Strategy Playbook — How To Approach

**Golden rule: Warm intro > Cold email. Always.**

---

## TIER 1: WARM INTROS (Start Here)

**Source:** Advisors, investors, customers, lawyers, accountants who know strategic buyers.

**Script for introducer:**

> "I want to intro you to [Founder] of [Company]. They're building [one-liner]. I think you'd be interested for [reason]. Can I do a 3-way call?"

**Why this works:**
- 40% response rate (vs 2% cold email)
- Bypasses gatekeeper
- Intro = implicit endorsement

**Your task:** Identify 3-5 advisor/investor warm intros to each strategic buyer.

---

## TIER 2: BROKER (If You Have Budget)

**Cost:** 1-2% of deal value, or capped $100K.

**What broker does:**
- Maintains buyer list (not shared with you)
- Cold emails/calls on your behalf (better response than founder cold email)
- Handles initial NDA + RFI process
- Doesn't reveal your identity until buyer signals interest

**When to use broker:**
- [ ] Deal value > $10M (broker ROI positive)
- [ ] You have zero relevant warm intros
- [ ] You want parallel buyer approach (broker owns sequencing)

**Red flag:** Broker takes too-exclusive deal (> 90 days, no market check allowed). Reject.

---

## TIER 3: DIRECT COLD EMAIL (Last Resort)

**Response rate:** 2-3% (so 50 emails = 1 conversation)

**Target:** VP Corp Dev or CEO (not BD team; they screen too hard).

**Email template:**

```
Subject: Acquisition partnership for [Your Market]

Hi [Name],

I'm CEO of [Company], building [product] for [market]. We've 
achieved [key metric] and [customer traction].

I know you're focused on [their strategic pain]. We're exactly 
[how you solve it] without [their gap]. 

Happy to share a confidential one-pager if you're interested in 
a conversation. NDA takes 5 minutes.

Best,
[Founder]
```

**Key rules:**
- One paragraph only
- Reference their recent news (acquisition, product launch, earnings call)
- Don't ask for meeting; offer teaser first
- CC: linkedin.com/in/[VP-Corp-Dev] (find real email via RocketReach, hunter.io)

---

## SEQUENCING: WHO TO APPROACH FIRST

**Order of approach:**

1. **Strategic buyers** (3-5): Highest price, longest timeline (8-12 weeks)
2. **PE firms** (3-5): Medium price, faster (6-8 weeks)
3. **Tuck-in / Founder acquirers** (5-10): Lower price, fastest (4-6 weeks)
4. **Your investors** (last): Only if no external interest (conflicts of interest)

**Why:** Strategic buyers pay more but take longer. Run parallel to create urgency.

| **Lower mid-market** | Centerview, Berkley | $100-500M, 8-12 weeks |

### Tuck-in Buyers (Fast, Lower Price)

- Larger competitor paying for talent + customers
- Adjacent product companies rolling up
- Your existing investors (secondary purchase for them)

---

## DO NOT APPROACH (Waste of Time)

- ❌ Competitors (will steal customers / kill you before buying)
- ❌ Broke companies (even if they're "strategic")
- ❌ Companies in debt covenant stress (won't close)
- ❌ Post-IPO in lockup period (can't do equity deals for 6 months)

---

## TIMING HACKS

**Best time to approach:**
- Post-earnings call (4 weeks after close) — buyer has free cash
- Post-acquisition completion (buyer digesting, looking for next)
- Post-funding (buyer has board appetite for deals)

**Worst time:**
- ❌ Week before board meeting
- ❌ During earnings call
- ❌ During their fundraise
- ❌ CEO just announced departure

# Objection Handler: "Your Customers Are Too Concentrated"

**When you hear it:**
- "Your top 3 customers = 60% of ARR. What if they churn?"
- "You have single-customer risk."
- "How do we de-risk if your biggest customer leaves?"

---

## **Bad Response** ❌

"No, they're sticky. They've been with us [X] years and won't leave."

*Why it fails:* You sound overconfident. Every founder thinks their big customers are sticky until they aren't.

---

## **Good Response** ✅

**Step 1: Acknowledge and quantify**

"You're right. Our top [N] customers = [X]% of ARR. Here's the breakdown:

| Customer | ARR | % of Total | Contract | Renewal Date | Risk Level |
|----------|-----|-----------|----------|---|---|
| [Name] | $[X]k | [Y]% | [Length] | [Date] | Low / Medium |
| [Name] | $[X]k | [Y]% | [Length] | [Date] | Low / Medium |
| [Name] | $[X]k | [Y]% | [Length] | [Date] | Low / Medium |"

**Step 2: Prove they're sticky**

"Here's why they'll stay:

1. **Switching costs:** They've integrated our API into [X] business processes. Switching = 3-month migration + disruption.

2. **Expansion history:** Customer [A] has grown [X]% YoY; their revenue dependency on us means they can't afford downtime.

3. **Contract terms:** All 3 have [X]-year contracts with [Y]-month notice. Any exit gives us time to land replacement revenue.

4. **Usage metrics:** They log in [X]x/day and have [X] power users. Not at churn risk."

**Step 3: Show your de-risking playbook**

"Post-close, here's our customer retention plan:

- **Weekly check-ins** with top [N] customers (vs. quarterly now)
- **Executive sponsor** assigned (CRO + CFO)
- **Roadmap alignment:** Prioritize their feature requests
- **Expansion revenue:** Grow their seat count / add-on products
- **Annual renewal:** Offer [X]-year prepay discount (locks in revenue, shows confidence)"

**Step 4: Lock it in before close**

"To de-risk further, I'm going to contact all 3 customers this week and confirm renewal commitment through [Date]. If they commit, that's proof positive of stickiness."

*Then do it.* Call customers before LOI signature and say:

"We're exploring acquisition. Your partnership is critical to our decision-making. Can you confirm you'll renew at [current pricing] for the next [X] years if we're acquired?"

If they say yes, bring that evidence to buyer. If they hedge, you know the risk is real.

## **Script Template**

```
Buyer: "Top 3 customers = 65% of ARR. What's the churn risk?"

You: "Fair question. Let me walk you through each:

[Customer A] ($[X]k, 25% of ARR): 
- 4-year contract, renews [Month], $[Y]k ACV
- Integrated our API into their core workflow
- Growing [Z]% YoY with us; unlikely to switch

[Customer B] & [Customer C]: Similar story—long contracts, deep integration, 
growth trajectory suggests expansion, not exit.

Risk: Low, Medium (contract end date [Date] gives us [X] months to prove value).

Post-close, I'll assign a CSM + quarterly exec check-ins. 
I'm also calling them this week to confirm renewal commitment. 
Want me to loop you in on those calls?"
```

## **Redline the Contract: Concentration Clause**

Buyer will likely ask for language protecting them if big customer churns. Standard options:

| Approach | Buyer Benefit | Founder Pain |
|----------|---|---|
| **Earnout clawback for churn** | If [Customer] churns, earnout –[X]% | You're on the hook |
| **Customer escrow holdback** | [X]% of deal price held in escrow; released if [Customer] renews | Capital tie-up, risk |
| **Founder retention (stay-on)** | You stay 12 months minimum; personally responsible for customer success | Golden handcuffs |

**Your play:**
- Accept earnout clawback if customer churns (reasonable)
- Reject personal guarantee (you're not operating the business post-close)
- Propose: Stay 6 months + CSM handoff (split the risk)

---

## **Numbers That De-Risk**

🎯 **Top 5 customers < 50% ARR** — Buyer's comfort zone
🎯 **NRR > 100%** — Means expansion offsetting some churn risk
🎯 **Churn < 2% annually** — If a customer leaves, you replace them
🎯 **Contracts > 2 years** — Locks in revenue; buyer's earnout easier to predict

---

## **When This Response Doesn't Work**

If buyer genuinely believes concentration is deal-breaker:

**Option 1: Pre-close diversification** (if you have time)
- Land [X] new mid-market customers before LOI
- This reduces top-5 concentration to 50%
- Takes 2–3 months

**Option 2: Adjust valuation**
- Accept lower multiple for concentrated revenue
- Example: "Dilute valuation by 15% if you're pricing in 20% churn risk"

**Option 3: Structure around it**
- Higher earnout tied to customer retention
- Founder stay-on (you personally own the relationship)
- Escrow holdback (released post-renewal)

---

## **Preemptive Strike**

Bring this up first in conversations:

"One thing you'll notice in diligence: our top [N] customers represent [X]% of revenue. 
This is actually a strength in our category—it means we've proven value to tier-1 enterprises. 
Every SaaS company at our stage has some concentration. Here's how we de-risk it:
[Your de-risking playbook]"

This kills the objection before it lands.

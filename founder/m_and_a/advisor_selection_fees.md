# Advisor Selection & Fee Structure

---

## Who You Need (In Order of Priority)

### 1. M&A Attorney (CRITICAL)
**Role:** Negotiates LOI, SPA, reps/warranties, earnout terms. Your legal protection.

**Typical Cost:**
- Hourly: $250-500/hr (boutique to big firms)
- Flat fee: $15-50k for simple deal; $50-150k for complex
- Success fee: None (sometimes bundled with advisor fee)

**Selection:**
- Experience with SaaS acquisitions (not just general M&A)
- References from 2-3 founders
- Boutique > BigLaw (more responsive, focused)

**Tip:** Get attorney involved **before LOI**, not after.

---

### 2. M&A Advisor / Investment Banker (CRITICAL)
**Role:** Finds buyers, negotiates valuation, structures deal, advises on terms.

**Typical Cost:**
- Retainer: $25-50k upfront (non-refundable)
- Success fee: 2-5% of enterprise value (typical: 3%)
- Combination: $15k retainer + 2% success fee

**Example:**
- Deal closes at $10M
- Success fee: 3% = $300k
- Total cost: $300k + legal ($50k) = $350k (3.5% of deal)

**Selection:**
- **For passive M&A** (selective): $25-50k retainer, lower success fee
- **For active auction** (multiple bidders): 2-3% success fee (incentivizes higher price)
- Track record: Ask for 3 founder references

**Tip:** Advisor incentive should align with yours (higher price = higher fee).

---

### 3. Tax Advisor / CPA (HIGH)
**Role:** Models tax impact of deal structures, suggests optimization.

**Typical Cost:**
- Hourly: $200-400/hr
- Flat fee: $5-20k for consultation
- Ongoing: $1-3k post-close (earnout tax planning)

**Selection:**
- Must have SaaS M&A experience
- Should offer deferred compensation strategies (earnout deferral, etc.)
- Team with your existing accountant if they know your business

**Tip:** Tax structure can save 20-30% of deal value; worth the $20k investment.

---

### 4. Board Advisor / Mentor (OPTIONAL but valuable)
**Role:** Sounding board for terms, negotiation strategy, founder support.

**Typical Cost:**
- Equity grant: 0.5-1% (if board seat)
- Advisory fee: $2-5k/month
- Success fee: 0.5-1% of deal (sometimes)

**Selection:**
- Founder who's exited before
- Advisor who knows your market / buyer profiles
- Someone you trust (this is emotional, not just financial)

## Fee Structure Models

### Model A: Simple Retainer + Success Fee
```
Retainer: $30k (non-refundable, covers ~3 months work)
Success Fee: 2.5% of enterprise value

Example ($10M deal):
  Retainer: $30k
  Success fee: $250k
  Total: $280k (2.8% of deal)
```

**Best for:** Founder-friendly; advisor invests own capital (retainer is sunk cost).

---

### Model B: Success Fee Only (No Retainer)
```
Success Fee: 3% of enterprise value

Example ($10M deal):
  Success fee: $300k
  Total: $300k (3% of deal)
```

**Best for:** Larger deals ($10M+); advisor more motivated to maximize price.

---

### Model C: Combination (Retainer + Lower Success Fee)
```
Retainer: $20k
Success Fee: 1.5% of enterprise value

Example ($10M deal):
  Retainer: $20k
  Success fee: $150k
  Total: $170k (1.7% of deal)
```

**Best for:** Conservative founder; lower total cost if deal is smaller than expected.

---

## Negotiating Advisor Fees

### Retainer Reduction
> "Can we reduce retainer to $15k and increase success fee to 3%?" (Advisor invests more; you save upfront cash)

### Success Fee Tiering
```
Deal value    Fee
-----------   ----
$5-8M        2.5%
$8-12M       2.0%
>$12M        1.5%

Rationale: Larger deals are easier; lower fee justified
```

### Capped Success Fee
> "Success fee capped at $500k total" (You know max downside; advisor still incentivized)

### Double-Sided Fee (Avoid)
**Bad:** Advisor represents both buyer and seller in same deal (conflict of interest).  
**Good:** Buyer has separate advisor; yours represents only you.

## When to Hire Each Advisor

### T-3 months (Pre-Soft Shop)
- [ ] Tax advisor (model deal structure scenarios)
- [ ] M&A attorney (review NDA, soft shop strategy)
- [ ] Optional: Board advisor

### T-1 month (Soft Shop Starts)
- [ ] M&A advisor (pitch to 3-5 buyers)
- [ ] M&A attorney (refine LOI template)

### T+0 (LOI Signed)
- [ ] All 3 (attorney, advisor, tax) full-time on deal

### T+6 weeks (SPA Negotiation)
- [ ] Attorney (reps/warranties, earnout, indemnification)
- [ ] Tax advisor (structure final optimization)
- [ ] Advisor (valuation discussions, walk-away strategy)

## Red Flags in Advisor Relationship

| 🚩 Flag | ⚠️ Concern |
|--------|-----------|
| Advisor pressures you to accept low offer | Conflicted; prioritizing own fee over your outcome |
| Advisor has ties to buyer | Conflict; can't push hard in negotiation |
| Advisor represents buyer too ("double-sided") | Major conflict; do not use |
| Advisor charges hourly + success fee | OK, but watch for scope creep |
| Advisor quotes "5%+ success fee" | Market is 2-3%; shop around |
| Advisor wants non-refundable retainer >$50k | Too high; negotiate down |

## Advisor Performance Checklist

**By week 4 of soft shop:**
- [ ] Advisor has contacted 3+ qualified buyers
- [ ] At least 1 LOI discussion scheduled
- [ ] Advisor has provided market comps / valuation guidance
- [ ] You feel advisor understands your business

**If not met:** Fire advisor; hire replacement.

## Sample Advisor Engagement Letter (Key Terms)

```
ADVISOR ENGAGEMENT

Advisor: [NAME]
Engagement: Help facilitate sale of [COMPANY]

FEES:
- Retainer: $[X] paid upfront
- Success Fee: [Y]% of enterprise value, payable at close
- Expense Reimbursement: $[Z] cap on travel/meals

TERM:
- 6 months exclusive (you agree not to use other advisors)
- Auto-renews unless terminated by either party

TERMINATION:
- Either party can terminate with 30 days notice
- Retainer forfeited if Advisor terminates (you keep it)
- Success fee due even if you terminate (assuming deal closes)

OBLIGATIONS:
- Advisor to provide list of 5+ target buyers within 2 weeks
- Weekly check-in calls
- Advisor to facilitate introductions; you own relationships

CONFLICTS:
- Advisor confirms no relationship with buyer that would create conflict
- Advisor commits to advocating for highest price (your interest)
```

## Bottom Line

- **Must hire:** M&A attorney + tax advisor
- **Should hire:** M&A advisor (worth 2-3% of deal)
- **Nice to have:** Board mentor (helps with stress + decisions)
- **Total cost:** 3-5% of deal value (reasonable investment)
- **Negotiate:** Retainer down, success fee tiered, capped if you prefer certainty
- **Performance test:** After 4 weeks, do you have LOI in hand? If no, fire + rehire.

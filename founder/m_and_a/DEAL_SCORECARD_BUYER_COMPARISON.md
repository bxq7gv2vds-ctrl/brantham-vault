# DEAL SCORECARD — BUYER COMPARISON

**Utilité:** Score chaque offre sur 5 dimensions. Comparer apple-to-apple quand tu as 3+ LOI. Évite le biais émotionnel.

---

## HOW TO USE

1. Remplis une ligne par buyer + offre
2. Score chaque dimension (0-10)
3. Pondère par importance (cf. poids suggérés ci-bas)
4. Total = score composite. Plus élevé = meilleur deal (en moyenne).
5. Ne pas utiliser comme "décision finale" — c'est input à conversation.

## DIMENSION GUIDES

### 1. PRICE & TERMS (Weight: 35%)
**Factors:**
- Total deal value (vs. BATNA + fair value)
- Cash % at close (>70% is safe; <50% is risky)
- Earnout structure (callable? capped? metric clear?)
- Founder stay bonus (enough to retain you?) Escrow risk (holdback % + duration)

**Scoring:**
- 9-10 = All-cash, at or above fair value, minimal reps cap
- 7-8 = 70%+ cash, fair value, reasonable earnout
- 5-6 = 50-70% cash, 20%+ earnout, high escrow
- 3-4 = <50% cash, >30% earnout, uncertain metrics
- 0-2 = Below BATNA, majority in illiquid stock, onerous reps

**Your score:** __ /10

---

### 2. CERTAINTY & RISK (Weight: 25%)
**Factors:**
- Buyer financial stability (no pending bankruptcy, strong balance sheet?)
- Deal certainty (LOI signed? financing confirmed? Board approval imminent?)
- Earnout risk (are milestones achievable post-close? under buyer control?)
- Reps & warranties cap (minimum <1% deal; capped <2%?)
- Escrow release timeline (can you get full release by Y3, or locked longer?)

**Scoring:**
- 9-10 = Earnout metrics clearly achievable, reps cap <0.5%, buyer is Fortune 500 + cash
- 7-8 = Earnout reasonable, reps cap 1%, buyer is stable PE / strategic
- 5-6 = Earnout tied to buyer's actions (risky), reps cap 1-2%, buyer is growth-stage startup
- 3-4 = Earnout metric is buyer's profit (you can't control), reps cap >2%, buyer has leverage
- 0-2 = Earnout contingent on buyer actions, reps cap >3%, buyer is unstable/thinly capitalized

**Your score:** __ /10

---

### 3. BUYER FIT & CULTURE (Weight: 20%)
**Factors:**
- Product synergy (does buyer actually integrate product, or fire it?)
- Customer overlap (will buyer upsell your customers? or cannibalize?)
- Team integration (is buyer respectful of your team? or 3-month turnover?)
- Founder autonomy (can you stay and lead, or will you be sidelined?)
- Market position (does buyer have distribution to help you scale? or is this a tuck-in?)

**Scoring:**
- 9-10 = Deep synergy, buyer promises expansion team, founder gets meaningful role, accelerated growth clear
- 7-8 = Clear synergy, fair integration plan, founder stay 2-3 years with real influence
- 5-6 = Some synergy, integration plan unclear, founder role is TBD
- 3-4 = Weak synergy, buyer seems dismissive of team, founder sidelined in org chart
- 0-2 = No synergy, clear intent to shut down product or fire team, founder has no role

**Your score:** __ /10

---

### 4. POST-CLOSE OPPORTUNITY (Weight: 15%)
**Factors:**
- If earnout happens, can you actually hit it? (does buyer support growth?)
- Founder optionality (can you leave + stay friendly? or is this a "trap"?)
- Reputational lift (does this buyer give you credibility for future ventures?)
- Learning (will you gain skills/network useful for next venture?)
- Financial upside (is there meaningful secondary gains if buyer IPOs or sells again?)

**Scoring:**
- 9-10 = High earnout probability + founder stays engaged + next venture launch possible + IPO/secondary possible
- 7-8 = Reasonable earnout odds + founder can transtion to advisory role + network valuable
- 5-6 = Earnout uncertain but you'll learn + founder can leave after 2y without tension
- 3-4 = Earnout at risk + founder trapped by vesting + limited next-step credibility
- 0-2 = Earnout unlikely + founder expected to stay 5y under non-compete + founder credibility damaged

**Your score:** __ /10

---

### 5. PROCESS & TIMELINE (Weight: 5%)
**Factors:**
- LOI negotiation (buyer stubborn? flexible?)
- DD speed (is buyer moving fast or dragging feet?)
- Founder distraction (can you keep building during process, or is it 24/7?)
- Certainty of closing (is there a clear path to signature, or contingencies?)

**Scoring:**
- 9-10 = 4-6 week close timeline, buyer well-organized, clear closing conditions
- 7-8 = 6-8 week close, buyer professional, minor contingencies
- 5-6 = 8-12 week close, buyer slower but organized, some uncertainty
- 3-4 = >12 week close, buyer disorganized, multiple contingencies
- 0-2 = No clear timeline, buyer moving slowly, high contingency risk

**Your score:** __ /10

## DECISION FRAMEWORK

| Score Range | Interpretation | Action |
|-------------|-----------------|--------|
| **8.5 - 10** | Best-in-class offer. Hit all your priorities. | **Take it**, unless competing offer is nearly equal. |
| **7.5 - 8.5** | Strong offer. Trade-offs are acceptable. | Compare with other offers. Likely good choice. |
| **6.5 - 7.5** | Good offer. Notable trade-offs. | Compare with others. Iterate on terms before accepting. |
| **5.5 - 6.5** | Acceptable offer. Multiple concerns. | Use as leverage against other buyers. Renegotiate before accepting. |
| **<5.5** | Below acceptable threshold. Major risks. | Reject or demand significant rework. |

## RED FLAGS — OVERRIDE SCORE IF ANY ARE TRUE

- [ ] **Earnout unachievable:** Buyer's own projections show you can't hit earnout. Override score to <4.
- [ ] **Founder conflict:** Co-founder opposes this buyer, and alignment was a pre-req. Override to <3.
- [ ] **Reps cap uncapped:** Reps & warranties liability is >2% or uncapped. Override to <4.
- [ ] **Buyer instability:** Buyer has pending layoffs, CEO change, or financing doubt. Override to <4.
- [ ] **Product kill:** Buyer's stated plan is to shut down your product within 1 year. Override to 0.
- [ ] **Customer concentration risk:** Buyer will leave >50% of your customers. Override to <5.

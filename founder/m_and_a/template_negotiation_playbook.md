# Price Negotiation Playbook — Close M&A Deals

## 3-Phase Framework

### Phase 1: Anchor (First 48 hours post-LOI)
**Goal:** Set the frame, control the narrative  
**Timeline:** Right after buyer says "we'd like to make an offer"

| Buyer | Your Response | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| "We're thinking 3x revenue ($6M)" | "We're seeing 6-8x EBITDA ($9-12M). Happy to talk." | Anchors high; makes 5-6x feel like compromise |
| "What's your number?" | "Let's see your case first. What assumptions do you use?" | Flip it; don't anchor first (they will counter yours) |
| "This is market for your ARR" | "We have 2 other serious offers above this; I thought we aligned" | Creates FOMO; tests if they're real |
| "We can close fast" | "Timeline is secondary to price/terms; what's your walk-away?" | Fast ≠ premium; separate leverage |

**Win condition:** You anchor 25% above realistic target → buyer's first real offer is your true target.

---

### Phase 2: Concede (Weeks 2–4 of negotiation)
**Goal:** Move toward deal, make them feel like they're winning  
**Concede in SEQUENCE, not all at once**

#### Concession Ladder (loosest to tightest hold)

| Rank | Item | Concede Order | Impact on Close |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Easiest** | Earnout % | 30% → 25% → 20% | Low (shifts risk, not absolute $) |
| | Earnout timeline | 24m → 30m → 36m | Medium (delays cash, lowers perception) |
| | Representations & warranties insurance | Buyer pays → split → seller pays | Low (insurance is cheap, <$50k usually) |
| | Closing conditions waivers | Non-material → material | Medium (creates post-close liability) |
| | Seller indemnification cap | 2x EBITDA → 1.5x → 1x | High (limits your downside exposure) |
| **Hardest** | Cash at close (base price) | $7M → $6.5M → $6M | **CRITICAL** (never concede cash first) |

**Tactic:** Concede on 3–4 loose items (earnout, timeline, insurance) before moving cash.

| Week | Buyer Pressure | Your Move | Reason |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | "Your price is too high" | Increase earnout pool +$200k, reduce cash by $100k | Looks like you move; they feel they won |
| 2 | "Reps & warranties are expensive for us" | Agree buyer pays insurance (saves you 40%) | Quick win for them; costs you nothing |
| 3 | "Earnout timeline needs to be 36m" | OK, but earnout vests monthly (reduces cliff risk) | Looks like compromise; protects you |
| 4 | "We need more price certainty" | Lower earnout % to 20%, lock base at $6.2M | Final move toward close |

**Trap to avoid:** Buyer keeps asking for the same concession.
- **Response:** "We moved on earnout ×3. What's your counter-move?" (forces reciprocal concession)

---

### Phase 3: Close (Days 1–7 before LOI signature)
**Goal:** Finalize terms, stop negotiating  
**Decision:** Walk away OR lock it in

#### Close Signals
- Buyer CFO formally involved (not just biz dev)
- Legal has drafted 80%+ of LOI
- They ask about earnout structure (not just amount)
- They reference your customers in calls ("We spoke to Acme, they love it")

#### Closing Moves

**If stalled on price:**
- "We've moved $200k on cash, $300k on earnout. I need your walk-away number, or we're done."
- **Psychology:** Ultimatum forces a decision; they either commit or fade

**If buyer goes silent:**
- Wait 5 business days, then: "Following up — still interested in moving forward?"
- If no response in 2 days: "I have another buyer presenting an offer EOW. Do you want to stay in the process?"
- **Psychology:** Activate FOMO; time pressure favors the party who can walk first

**If multiple offers (best case):**
- "I have 2 LOIs. Both strong. I'm inclined toward [buyer 2] at [price 2], but you have until [date] to match or exceed."
- **Psychology:** Explicit deadline; forces decision; removes ambiguity

## Red Flags During Negotiation

| Red Flag | Interpretation | Your Move |
|---|---|---|
| Buyer keeps asking same question 3x | They're stalling or testing if you'll change story | Repeat answer verbatim; don't over-explain |
| "We need to see your code before final offer" | Trying to find dirt to lower price at last minute | Agree, but set code review end-date and final offer date (same) |
| Suddenly asks for 50% down at close, rest in earnout | Buyer has cold feet; trying to de-risk post-close | Push back hard: "That changes the deal" + revert to prior terms |
| Long gap in communication (>10 days) | Buyer is slow-walking; may have lost board buy-in | Send ultimatum or walk |
| "Our board just asked for a deeper diligence" | Buyer is delaying for leverage or backing out slowly | Counter: "I have another offer coming in [date]. Diligence must close by [date]." |

## Walkaway Framework (Before You Start Negotiating)

**Before any call, define:**

1. **Minimum cash at close:** What's your absolute bottom? (e.g., $5M)
   - Anything below = walk

2. **Earnout ceiling:** How confident are you buyer will pay? (e.g., 25% max)
   - Higher = you're betting on buyer, not you

3. **Earnout payment terms:** Monthly, quarterly, or lump-sum? (e.g., monthly min.)
   - Lump-sum = buyer can "forget" to pay

4. **Founder role post-close:** What do you owe buyer after close?
   - If demanded 24m, that's 24m of your life for earnout risk

5. **Timeline pressure:** When do YOU need to close? When do THEY?
   - You: "by Q4" = they can wait; you can't
   - Them: "by Sept 30" = they have a deadline; use it

**Decision rule:**
- If buyer can't meet 3 of 5 criteria, walk.
- Example: $4M cash + 40% earnout + lump-sum + 30m = fail. Walk.

## Objection Handling Scripts

### Objection 1: "Your churn is too high"
**Response:**
- "Our churn is [X]%. Industry is [Y]%. We're [better/aligned]."
- "Our cohort analysis shows: Customers 18m+ have <2% churn. New cohorts stabilize in 12m."
- **Option:** "If you're concerned, we can make part of the earnout contingent on churn staying below 4%."
- **Outcome:** Turns risk into dealmaker, not dealbreaker.

### Objection 2: "You're too dependent on [1 customer]"
**Response:**
- "Top 5 customers = X% of revenue. We've been diversifying since [quarter]."
- "We have [N] customers with <[X]% concentration per customer."
- **Option:** "Let's structure earnout based on retaining top 5 customers — I'm confident."
- **Outcome:** You guarantee retention with upside.

### Objection 3: "We need you to stay 24 months post-close"
**Response:**
- "I can commit 12 months guaranteed, then 1-year advisory role."
- "What's the earnout tied to your retention vs. my retention?"
- **Option:** "I'll stay for full earnout payout (let's say 18m), but earnout cliff is [date] — no moving goalposts."
- **Outcome:** Limits post-close ambiguity.

### Objection 4: "The deal is now $3M, not $5M"
**Response:**
- "That's a major change from the LOI. I need to revisit my advisors."
- **Then:** Don't respond for 48 hours. Forces buyer to come back or concede they overplayed.
- **Follow-up:** "If this is your walk-away, I have another buyer at $5.2M. Are you interested in matching?"
- **Outcome:** Calls their bluff.

### Objection 5: "We want to include a [non-standard clause]"
**Response:**
- "That's not standard in SaaS M&A. Here's the market precedent [show 3 deals]."
- "If you insist, we adjust price down [amount] to cover your custom risk."
- **Outcome:** Non-standard = costs money.

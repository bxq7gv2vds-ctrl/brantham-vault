## THE 4 BUYER ARCHETYPES

### Quadrant 1: STRATEGIC — LOCAL / NATIONAL
- **Examples:** Direct competitor; customer's customer; adjacent vertical player
- **Advantages:** Fast decisions (they know your market); high synergies; premium price
- **Disadvantages:** May kill your product; aggressive integration
- **Price expectation:** 8–10x ARR (highest)
- **Timeline:** 60–90 days (fastest)

### Quadrant 2: STRATEGIC — GLOBAL / INTERNATIONAL
- **Examples:** European competitor; Asian tech company; multinational buyer
- **Advantages:** New market access; resource-rich; less product overlap risk
- **Disadvantages:** Slower (international approvals); cultural fit questions; diligence takes longer
- **Price expectation:** 6–8x ARR (moderate-high)
- **Timeline:** 120–150 days (slower)

### Quadrant 3: PRIVATE EQUITY — LOCAL
- **Examples:** Lower mid-market PE ($[50–200]M fund); growth equity; founder-friendly PE
- **Advantages:** Clean financial structure; founder usually stays as CEO; realistic earnout
- **Disadvantages:** Lower price than strategic; margin improvement pressure
- **Price expectation:** 5–7x ARR (moderate)
- **Timeline:** 120 days (standard)

### Quadrant 4: PE + STRATEGIC HYBRID — GLOBAL
- **Examples:** Large PE firm with strategic/platform value (e.g., adds to portfolio company)
- **Advantages:** Financial discipline + strategic upside; long-term partner; growth capital
- **Disadvantages:** Complex structure; slower decisions; multiple approvals
- **Price expectation:** 6–9x ARR (variable, can be highest if strategic angle strong)
- **Timeline:** 150+ days (slowest)

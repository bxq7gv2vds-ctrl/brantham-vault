# Email Templates M&A — Prêt-à-utiliser

Tous les emails ci-dessous sont conçus pour **paraître naturels** (pas de vente agressif) et **créer urgence** sans désespoir.

---

## TEMPLATE 1 : Outreach Buyer (Soft Shop)

### Version A — Direct Introduction (via contact mutuaL)

```
Objet: Possible synergy opportunity w/ [Buyer Company]

Hi [Buyer Name],

[Mutual contact] thought it'd be worth a conversation — 
we're exploring strategic options for [Your Company], 
and [Buyer Company] seemed like a natural fit.

Why now:
  • We've hit $[X]M ARR with [Y]% growth, and scaled beyond the founding team
  • Our [product/IP/userbase] solves [specific problem Buyer cares about]
  • Market conditions mean acquisition makes sense for both sides

No hard timeline yet — just early exploration.
Would a 20-min call make sense to see if there's a fit?

Happy to jump on [link to Calendly] if so.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### Version B — Inbound Responder (if they reach out)

```
Objet: Re: Acquisition interest in [Your Company]

Hi [Buyer Name],

Thanks for getting in touch — good timing, we're actively exploring strategic 
options right now.

Happy to have a conversation, but wanted to set expectations:
  • We're talking to a few potential partners, so this isn't exclusive yet
  • We'll likely move to LOI phase in [4-6 weeks]
  • We're looking for a buyer who can move fast and understands the strategic value

Would [date/time] work for a call? I can share a high-level overview then.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### Key Rules:
- **No price in email** — that's for call 2-3
- **Mention timeline** — creates urgency without naming a close date
- **Mention alternatives** — signals you have options
- **Keep it short** — 150 words max

## TEMPLATE 2 : LOI Signature Request

```
Objet: LOI ready for signature — next steps

Hi [Buyer Counsel + Your Counsel],

Both sides have agreed to the key terms below. 
I'm sending the final LOI for signature.

**Key Terms** (recap):
  Purchase price:     $[X]M
  Earnout:            $[Y]M ([metric], trigger [date])
  Reps cap:           [Z]% of price (max $[amount])
  Closing timeline:   [X weeks]
  Exclusivity:        [X months] from LOI signature

**Process**:
1. [Your counsel] and [Buyer counsel] finalize exhibits (target: [date])
2. Both sides execute by [date]
3. Closing prep + DD phase begins [date]

If there are any outstanding issues, flag them by [date] — 
I'd like to get this signed by [date] to keep momentum.

Happy to hop on a call if needed.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### Key Rules:
- **Recap terms** — everyone's aligned
- **Give dates** — creates commitment
- **CC both counsels** — move faster (they coordinate)
- **Flag escalation path** — "call if needed"

## TEMPLATE 3 : Closing Logistics & Wire Instructions

```
Objet: Closing logistics — wire instructions & sign-off

Hi [Buyer CFO/Counsel],

We're on track to close [date]. Here's the logistics:

**Wire Instructions** (for purchase price):
  Bank:        [Bank Name]
  Account:     [Your Company Name]
  Routing:     [#]
  SWIFT:       [code if international]
  Amount:      $[X] (purchase) + $[Y] (escrow hold)

**Closing Prep** (status):
  ✓ Final reps/warranties  
  ✓ Officer cert signed
  ✓ Employment agreements finalized
  [Add any pending items]

**Closing Day Schedule** (ET):
  10:00 AM    Both counsels exchange signed docs
  11:00 AM    Wire transfer initiates
  1:00 PM     Closing checklist sign-off
  ~2:00 PM    We're closed

I'll have [your counsel] coordinate the actual signing.

Let me know if there are any final questions.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### Key Rules:
- **Wire instructions are CRITICAL** — double-check with your bank
- **Create a timeline** — everyone knows what to expect
- **Be factual** — no surprises on closing day
- **CC counsel** — they drive the actual close

## ANTI-PATTERNS (do NOT do these)

❌ **Long-winded intro emails** (>300 words = looks desperate)  
❌ **Pricing in email** (should be conversation, not text)  
❌ **CC'ing too many people** (dilutes decision-making)  
❌ **Emotional language** ("This deal is my life," "Please let's do this")  
❌ **Threats or ultimatums** ("If you don't decide by Friday...")  
❌ **Assumptions about their motive** ("You definitely need our IP...")

## Quick Copy-Paste Checklist

Before sending ANY email:
- [ ] Spell-check recipient name (huge embarrassment if wrong)
- [ ] Verify email addresses (one typo = lost message)
- [ ] Remove placeholder [BRACKETS]
- [ ] Check tone (confident, not defensive)
- [ ] Is the ask clear? (What do you want them to do next?)
- [ ] Did you give a timeline? (Urgency signal)
- [ ] CC legal counsel? (If formal/legal stuff)

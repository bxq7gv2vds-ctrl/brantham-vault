# Red Flags Checklist — 30+ Warning Signs in M&A Process

## Financial Red Flags 🚩

| Red Flag | What It Means | Your Action |
|---|---|---|
| **ARR growth slowed >50% YoY** | Product-market fit issues or market saturation | Quantify reasons (sales execution vs. market demand) |
| **Churn accelerated (>8% annual)** | Customer dissatisfaction or product defects | Diagnose cohort-by-cohort; show corrective actions |
| **NRR < 100%** | Upsell failing; customers aren't expanding | Flag as integration risk post-close |
| **Gross margin declining >5% YoY** | Cost inflation or pricing power issues | Explain (seasonality, one-time costs, etc.) |
| **Runway < 6 months** (if unprofitable) | You're desperate to sell; weakens negotiation | Reframe: "We're raising to accelerate, not survive" |
| **Revenue concentrated in 1–2 customers** | Single point-of-failure; buyer fears churn | Lock customer into multi-year contracts pre-close |
| **Customer list shows 50%+ churn annually** | Product is leaky; retention model broken | Explain cohort by cohort; show you've fixed root cause |
| **COGS is unpredictable / rising fast** | Margin collapse post-acquisition is likely | Nail down cost structure; show path to 80%+ gross margin |
| **CAC payback > 24 months** | Unit economics are broken | Show path to payback <12 months or explain why high payback is OK |
| **You can't explain your unit economics** | Red flag that you don't actually know your business | Spend 1 week building the model before talks start |

## Customer Red Flags 🚩

| Red Flag | What It Means | Your Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Top customer relationship is fragile** | Renewal dependent on founder; buyer can't guarantee | Lock multi-year contract; embed support; reduce dependency |
| **Customer contracts have "termination for convenience"** | Buyer can lose customers on 30/90 days notice | Warn buyer in LOI; prepare mitigation plan |
| **Multiple customers own your product but don't use it** | You're not actually solving their problem | Admit; pivot to active-user-only ARR metric; focus on stickiness |
| **NPS < 20** | Customer satisfaction is low; churn risk is high | Urgent: Fix product or customer success first |
| **Customer concentration: Top 5 = 70%+ ARR** | Highly concentrated; one churn = 12%+ revenue loss | Lock top 5 into multi-year contracts; show diversification pipeline |
| **You haven't talked to top 10 customers about acquisition** | If they hear secondhand, they may churn out of surprise | Call them pre-LOI; gauge retention risk |
| **Customer support is founder-dependent** | Buyer inherits founder service burden or customer leaves | Document support processes; hire support manager before M&A |
| **No NPS survey or customer satisfaction tracking** | You don't actually know if customers are happy | Run NPS survey immediately; will be required in data room |

## Product & Technology Red Flags 🚩

| Red Flag | What It Means | Your Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Code is undocumented; only founder understands it** | Buyer can't integrate your product into their platform | Document architecture, APIs, dependencies before handoff |
| **Tech stack uses expired/deprecated languages or frameworks** | Expensive refactor post-close; buyer discounts price | Modernize critical path or accept valuation discount |
| **No automated tests; all testing is manual** | Product reliability is fragile; scary for buyer | Write tests for critical paths; show coverage % |
| **Uptime/SLA not tracked** | You've had outages, but don't know frequency | Instrument monitoring; prove 99.5%+ uptime for 90 days |
| **Security audit = 0** | Buyer will demand pen test; you fail; they sue | Run pen test immediately; fix critical findings before M&A |
| **No API versioning; breaking changes ship frequently** | Customers will break on upgrades; churn risk | Implement versioning; commit to backward compatibility |
| **Single points of failure in architecture** | Database fails = entire service down; scary for buyer | Add redundancy for critical systems; document failover |
| **Infrastructure on personal AWS account** | Handoff/ownership transfer is messy | Migrate to separate AWS org account; set up proper billing |
| **You rely on external APIs with no SLA** | Service dependency (Stripe, Auth0, etc.) with no fallback | Negotiate SLAs or build fallback; don't leave buyer exposed |

## Legal & Compliance Red Flags 🚩

| Red Flag | What It Means | Your Action |
|---|---|---|
| **No SOC2 audit; no security assessment completed** | Compliance risk; buyer's customers may object | Schedule SOC2 Type II audit immediately (6–12 month process) |
| **Privacy policy is generic template; doesn't match actual practices** | GDPR/CCPA risk; data handling is non-compliant | Audit actual data practices; update privacy policy to match |
| **Customer contracts have change-of-control terms** | Customers must consent to acquisition or you lose them | Review all material contracts; confirm customer consents |
| **You have outstanding IP litigation** | Buyer inherits legal risk; buys liability | Disclose immediately; quantify exposure; estimate legal fees |
| **Trademarks/brand not registered** | Buyer can't protect your IP post-close | Register trademarks before close or buyer assumes risk |
| **Open-source code in your product (GPL license)** | Forced to open-source proprietary code; kills value | Audit all open-source; remove GPL dependencies or accept open-sourcing |
| **No assignment of IP from contractors/employees** | Contractor still owns code; buyer can't use it | Get IP assignments from all contractors in writing |
| **Equity cap table has failed option grants or disputes** | Closing delayed by cap table cleanup; founder shares unclear | Audit cap table; resolve all disputes before M&A |
| **No formal employment agreements** | Unclear who owns what; post-close disputes likely | Draft and sign agreements with all key employees pre-close |
| **Tax liens or back taxes owed** | Buyer discovers tax debt; they hold back from purchase price | File any overdue tax returns; pay back taxes before LOI |

## Process Red Flags 🚩

| Red Flag | What It Means | Your Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Buyer goes silent for 2+ weeks** | They've lost interest or hit internal roadblocks | Send: "Still interested? I have another offer coming in [date]" |
| **Buyer asks same question 3x** | Either testing if you'll change story, or slow-walking | Answer identically 3x; then: "We've answered this X times. What's blocking you?" |
| **Buyer lawyer insists on terms that hurt you** | Lawyer is being aggressive; may signal buyer is cold | Involve your lawyer; don't negotiate directly with their counsel |
| **Buyer cuts communication staff** (no longer responding to emails) | Classic slow-walk behavior; they've moved on | Send formal follow-up: "No response by [date] = we're moving on" |
| **Buyer extends diligence indefinitely** | Looking for dirt or losing courage; using time as pressure | Propose: "Diligence closes [date]; offer expires [date]" |
| **Buyer introduces new stakeholders late in process** | Each new person re-litigates deal terms; process restarts | Gate new stakeholders: "They can review, but terms don't change" |
| **Buyer demands reps about things outside your control** | Setting up post-close indemnification claims | Redline: Only reps about "to your knowledge" or "within your control" |
| **You don't have a lawyer; buying directly negotiating** | Buyer's lawyer will eat your lunch | Hire M&A lawyer immediately (worth 2–3% of deal) |

## Buyer Red Flags 🚩

| Red Flag | What It Means | Your Action |
|---|---|---|
| **"We're financing with a credit facility we're arranging now"** | Financing is NOT committed; buyer can walk if rates rise | Demand: "We need committed financing in LOI or no deal" |
| **Buyer has flipped 5+ companies in past 2 years** | They're not integrating; fire-and-forget; you'll be left stranded | Clarify: What happens to you post-close? What's their integration plan? |
| **Buyer has history of earnout disputes** (check references) | They'll fight you on earnout payouts; you'll get legal fees | Insist: "Earnout measured by X" and "Audited by Big 4"; not buyer's discretion |
| **Buyer is trying to cut you out of post-close role** | They don't want you around; earnout is high-risk | Negotiate: "I stay as [role] for [duration]" or push for higher cash at close |
| **Buyer is vague about integration plan** | They haven't thought it through; product could be shelved | Ask: "What's the 100-day integration roadmap? Where do I fit?" |
| **Buyer's CEO / board hasn't been briefed yet** | Deal is preliminary; even LOI could vanish | Confirm: "Has this been socialized with your board?" |
| **Buyer is doing this to acquire your customer list** | They'll keep customers, shut down your product | Don't sell just the customer list; if that's the deal, price it accordingly |
| **"Standard deal" / "We always do this"** (buyer pushes onerous terms) | Not standard; buyer is just aggressive | Show precedent from 2–3 other SaaS deals; push back |

## Team & Founder Red Flags 🚩

| Red Flag | What It Means | Your Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Key employee is job hunting** | They know acquisition is imminent; leaving before close = buyer discounts valuation | Lock in retention bonuses; get them excited about post-close |
| **Founder doesn't know key metrics by heart** | Signals you haven't been managing the business | Learn your unit economics cold before buyer kicks tires |
| **Team is demoralized or high turnover** | Culture issues; buyer will inherit; churn risk | Address team morale urgently; hire if needed |
| **You don't have a CEO/lead accountant** | Business looks immature; buyer discounts price | If founder is CEO, hire CFO/controller to run financials |
| **Founder is burned out / wants to exit immediately** | You're not serious; buyer smells desperation | If selling, be genuine. If not, don't sell. |

## Remediation Priority

**Fix immediately (before first buyer conversation):**
1. Audit cap table; resolve all disputes
2. Get employment agreements signed
3. Get IP assignments from contractors
4. Audit customer concentration; lock top 5 into multi-year contracts

**Fix in first 30 days of buyer talks:**
1. Run SOC2 audit (or plan it)
2. Resolve any customer change-of-control issues
3. Document architecture; improve code clarity
4. Stabilize key metrics (churn, NPS, ARR growth)

**Acceptable to address post-LOI:**
1. Modernize tech stack (if not blocking product)
2. Hire support/customer success manager
3. Build monitoring/uptime infrastructure
4. Write API docs

**Never acceptable to discover during diligence:**
- Tax liens, back taxes
- IP ownership disputes
- Customer contract change-of-control surprises
- Undisclosed litigation
- Cap table errors

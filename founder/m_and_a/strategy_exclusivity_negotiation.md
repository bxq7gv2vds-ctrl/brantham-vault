# Exclusivity Negotiation — When To Lock In, When To Keep Door Open

---

## TIMELINE RULES

| Stage | Exclusivity? | Duration | Exceptions |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Teaser sent** | ❌ No | — | Keep talking to 10+ buyers |
| **NDA signed** | ❌ No | — | Keep parallel conversations |
| **LOI signed** | ✅ Yes | 45-60 days | Can "shop" for better bids (most LOIs allow this) |
| **Due Diligence** | ✅ Yes | 60-90 days | Hard exclusivity (no shopping) |
| **Closing** | ✅ Yes | Until signed | Can't talk to anyone else |

---

## NEGOTIATION SCRIPT

**Buyer:** "We need exclusivity to invest in due diligence."

**You:** "Fair. How long? Our preference is 60 days to sign LOI, then you have exclusive DD window."

**Buyer:** "We need 90 days from NDA signature."

**You:** "That's 90 days from LOI signature, not NDA. And if you're not ready to close by day 90, we can extend by 30 days max. Deal?"

---

## RED LINE

**Don't agree to:**
- ❌ Exclusivity during NDA phase (before LOI)
- ❌ Exclusivity > 90 days (buyer is stalling)
- ❌ "Exclusive without timeline" (indefinite lock)

---

## STALLED EXCLUSIVITY ESCAPE

If exclusive buyer stalls at day 75:

**You:** "We're at 75 days. LOI said 90 total. We have 15 days. If you're not ready to close by day 90, exclusivity expires. Then we can talk to other buyers."

Buyer will either close fast or lose you.

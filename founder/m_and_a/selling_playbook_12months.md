# Playbook Vente — Préparation 12 mois avant exit

**Objectif** : Maximiser valuation et attrait. À commencer 12 mois avant M&A active.

---

## Mois 1-3 : Fondations (Trimestre Q0)

### Financials & Metrics
- [ ] Standardiser rapports financiers (12m trailing P&L, cashflow, balance sheet)
- [ ] Calculer tous les SaaS metrics: ARR, MRR, CAC, LTV, NRR, GRR, churn
- [ ] Documenter assumptions & models (driver de growth, seasonality?)
- [ ] 3-year projections (scenario: conservative/base/bull case)
- [ ] Customer cohort analysis (retention, expansion par vintage)

### Customer Data
- [ ] List all active customers (name, ARR, contract end, NPS, growth trajectory)
- [ ] Identify key accounts (top 20? concentration %)
- [ ] Customer health scoring (at-risk, stable, growing)
- [ ] Redact sensitive data (salaries, personal info) before sharing

### Revenue Quality
- [ ] Audit all revenue for sustainability (churn trend? Non-recurring? Fraud?)
- [ ] Identify one-time revenue (projects, implementation, licenses) → exclude from ARR
- [ ] Validate contract terms (pricing power, lock-in, auto-renewal rates)

---

## Mois 4-6 : Optimization (Trimestre Q1)

### Revenue growth playbook
- [ ] Implement pricing optimization (test higher tiers, expansion revenue)
- [ ] Quick wins: low-hanging fruit deals to close before LOI
- [ ] Improve NRR (expansion & retention campaigns)
- [ ] Fix churn (red accounts: can we save before sale?)
- [ ] Growth rate target: aim for >30% YoY (standard for acquisition)

### Product & Tech
- [ ] Fix critical tech debt (buyers hate old stacks)
- [ ] Security audit (SOC2/ISO27001 on roadmap?), patch vulns
- [ ] Product documentation (what does it do? Why differentiated?)
- [ ] Scalability proof (load tested to 5x peak)
- [ ] Clean up codebase (remove dead code, outdated comments)

### Brand & Market Position
- [ ] Case studies: 3-5 customer stories (with logos, quotes, results)
- [ ] Benchmark metrics vs. competitors (who's 1st, 2nd, 3rd in market?)
- [ ] Market size assessment (TAM, SAM, SOM docs)
- [ ] Competitive positioning (what's unique? Why better?)

---

## Mois 7-9 : Packaging (Trimestre Q2)

### Data Room (virtual!)
- [ ] Organize 100+ documents (see: checklist_data_room)
- [ ] Executive summary (1-pager: what is this company in 90 seconds)
- [ ] Financials (audited or reviewed if possible)
- [ ] Customer list (names, ARR, contract terms, NPS)
- [ ] Org chart + bios of key people
- [ ] IP/patents/trademarks docs
- [ ] Contracts (customer, vendor, employment)
- [ ] Cap table (who owns what % from founding?)
- [ ] Board minutes (last 12 months)

### Pitch Materials
- [ ] Pitch deck (25-30 slides for strategics, 15 for financials)
- [ ] Executive summary (2-pager)
- [ ] Product overview (15 min demo script)
- [ ] 1-slide fact sheet (for quick dissemination)

### Team Narrative
- [ ] Founder story (why? background? why successful?)
- [ ] Team bios (experience, credentials, why they're best-in-class)
- [ ] Bench strength (future leadership candidates)
- [ ] Retention strategy (who stays? earn-outs? retention bonuses?)

---

## Mois 10-11 : Market preparation (Trimestre Q3)

### Advisor selection
- [ ] M&A advisor / investment banker (or internal CFO prep?)
- [ ] Legal counsel (corporate lawyer + tax specialist)
- [ ] Accountant (prepare audited/reviewed financials)
- [ ] Strategic advisor (industry expert, ex-CEO?)

### Buyer identification
- [ ] Develop target buyer list (strategic + financial buyers)
- [ ] Primary targets (5-10): high fit, high probability
- [ ] Secondary targets (20+): wider net, fallback options
- [ ] Map buyer profiles (who wants what features/customer bases?)

### Confidentiality preparation
- [ ] NDA template ready (lawyer-reviewed)
- [ ] Data room access system (Intralinks, Firmex, etc.)
- [ ] Confidentiality protocols (what can't be shared before NDA?)

### Proof points
- [ ] Media mentions (get press before M&A process starts)
- [ ] Awards/recognitions
- [ ] Analyst reports (if applicable to industry)
- [ ] Analyst briefings (brief analysts on space, position favorably)

---

## Mois 12 : Launch (Trimestre Q4)

### Final audit
- [ ] Financials audit-ready (clean, documented)
- [ ] Data room 100% complete (no red flags)
- [ ] Legal compliance (IP clear? Litigation risk? Tax filings OK?)
- [ ] Reference calls prepped (brief customers who'll speak to buyers)
- [ ] Key employee retention (golden handcuffs in place?)

### Process launch
- [ ] NDA + teaser to potential buyers
- [ ] Formal selling memo (banker circulates to buyers)
- [ ] Manage buyer expectations (timeline: 6-9 months LOI-to-close)
- [ ] Respond to buyer LOIs quickly (momentum = leverage)

## Key metrics to improve before sale

| Metric | Target | Why matters |
|--------|--------|------------|
| **ARR Growth YoY** | >30% | Valuation multiplier (higher growth = higher multiple) |
| **NRR** | >115% | Proof of product stickiness & expansion |
| **Churn** | <5% monthly | Revenue sustainability |
| **CAC Payback** | <12 months | Unit economics validation |
| **Gross Margin** | >70% | Profitability upside |
| **NPS** | >40 | Product-market fit |
| **Customer Count** | Stable or growing | Diversification (not dependent on 1-2 whales) |

---

## Red flags to FIX before going to market

🚩 **If you have**:
- Top customer = >30% revenue (diversify!)
- Churn trend upward (fix product/pricing)
- Tech debt crushing velocity (clean up)
- Key employee likely to leave (retain now)
- Outdated compliance (SOC2, GDPR)
- Litigation pending (disclose fully)
- Product cannibalization risk (clarify strategy)

**Fix before process**, not during.

## Timeline summary

```
Mth 1-3 → Data foundation (financials, customer health)
Mth 4-6 → Revenue optimization (grow faster)
Mth 7-9 → Package & materials (data room, pitch)
Mth 10-11 → Advisor & buyers (prep market)
Mth 12 → Launch (send teasers, manage process)
```

**Result**: In month 13, you have qualified LOIs with multiple buyers. Leverage = negotiating power.

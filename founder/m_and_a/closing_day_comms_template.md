# Closing Day Communications — The Templates

**Use these EXACTLY on Day 1. Customize [brackets] only. Pre-write with buyer CEO.**

---

## Email 1: Internal All-Hands Announcement (Team)

**Send at:** 10:30 AM (after wire transfers confirmed)
**To:** All team
**Subject:** We've been acquired by [Buyer Name] — Let's meet at 12pm

---

```
Subject: We've been acquired by [Buyer Name]! Team all-hands @ 12pm.

Hi team,

Exciting news: We're thrilled to announce that we've been acquired by [Buyer Name]! 
[Buyer] is [one-sentence why they're great: leader in X, share our vision of Y, etc.]

This is great news for:
  • Our customers: [Specific benefit: integrated platform, new features, scale]
  • Our team: [Specific benefit: resources, next-level opportunity, career growth]
  • Our mission: [Why this accelerates our vision]

**What happens today:**
  • Team all-hands at 12pm ET (Zoom: [link])
  • [Buyer CEO] will join to say hello
  • I'll walk through what's next
  • Q&A open (ask anything)

**Your immediate actions:**
  1. Check your email for IT access info (VPN, email, tools)
  2. Join Slack workspace [buyer-slack-name]
  3. Schedule 1:1 with your manager (this week)
  4. Keep working — nothing changes immediately

You'll hear more later today, but know: **Your jobs are safe.** We negotiated 
hard for your security, and [Buyer] is as committed to this team as we are.

Proud moment. Let's go build something great together.

[Your name]
```

## Email 2: Customer Announcement (Clients)

**Send at:** 11:00 AM (1.5 hours before all-hands, so team gets the news first)
**To:** All active customers (manual email, not marketing automation)
**Subject:** Exciting news: [Your Company] joins [Buyer Name]

---

```
Subject: Exciting news: [Your Company] joins [Buyer Name]

Hi [Customer],

I wanted you to hear this from me first: [Your Company] has been acquired by 
[Buyer Name]! We're thrilled about this partnership.

**What this means for you:**

✓ **Your service:** No changes to [product], pricing, or support — at least 
  through [date, e.g., end of 2026]. We're committed to a smooth transition.

✓ **New capabilities:** You'll gain access to [1-2 specific buyer features that help 
  customer]. We'll schedule a product roadmap update next month.

✓ **Your team:** Your usual contacts ([name, name]) are staying. [Buyer has assigned 
  [your name] as your integration lead.]

✓ **Support:** If you have concerns, reach out to me directly ([your email, phone]).

**Next steps:**
  • You'll hear from [Buyer's CSM name] next week (schedule intro call)
  • We'll host a customer webinar [date] on integration roadmap
  • All existing contracts remain in force

This is a positive step for [Your Company]'s mission to [mission], and we're grateful 
for your partnership. Excited to bring you along.

Talk soon,
[Your name]

P.S. — Call me if you have concerns. I'm here to help.
```

## Email 3: Investors Update

**Send at:** 2:00 PM
**To:** Board + lead investors
**Subject:** Exit Update: [Your Company] acquired by [Buyer Name]

---

```
Subject: Exit Update: [Your Company] acquired by [Buyer Name]

Board + investors,

We're excited to share that [Your Company] has been acquired by [Buyer Name] 
for [valuation structure, e.g., "$[X]M cash at close + $[Y]M earnout over 24 months"].

**Transaction Details:**
  • Enterprise value: $[X]M
  • Cash at close: $[X]M
  • Earnout: $[Y]M (tied to [metric] through [date])
  • Lead counsel: [law firm]

**Rationale:**
This was the right move because:
  1. [Buyer] offers strategic value [reason: platform, distribution, etc.]
  2. Timeline: Our growth/runway [reason to close now vs wait]
  3. Price: Reflects our [growth/metrics/position]

**Investor Proceeds:**
[Financial advisor] will send details separately. Payout timeline: 
  • Cash: [T+30 days from close]
  • Earnout: Quarterly [actual earnout schedule]

**Next Steps:**
You'll get regular updates on integration progress. I'll share 30/60/90-day 
metrics as we move forward.

Grateful for your partnership.

[Your name]
```

**To:** Key partners (integrations, resellers, vendors)
**Subject:** Update: [Your Company] + [Buyer Name] partnership

---

```

Hi [Partner name],

Quick heads up: [Your Company] has been acquired by [Buyer Name] 
(announced this morning to customers + team).

**What's important for you:**
  • Your contract/partnership with us stays in force (unless modified by mutual agreement)
  • Integration opportunities might expand ([Buyer's product] + your offering)
  • Your contact remains [name]
  • We'll loop [Buyer partner lead] into future conversations

Looking forward to deepening the partnership under [Buyer] roof.

[Your name]
```

## Timing Checklist (Day 1, 9am-5pm)

```
9:00 AM    Wire transfers + legal signing confirmed
           ✓ Buyer finance confirms $ transfer complete

10:00 AM   Your COO/CFO calls board (quick verbal update)
           ✓ Board members know before reading email

10:30 AM   Internal all-hands email sent
           ✓ Pre-record video if you want (1-2 min from you)

11:00 AM   Customer announcement email sent
           ✓ Your CSM team ready for inbound calls

12:00 PM   All-hands meeting (60 min)
           ✓ CEO (yours + buyer) + Q&A
           ✓ Record for those who can't attend live

1:00 PM    One-on-one manager 1:1s begin (staggered)
           ✓ Managers have talking points (see below)

2:00 PM    Investor update email
           ✓ CFO does final number verification

3:00 PM    Partner notifications sent
           ✓ No surprises

4:00 PM    Press release goes out (if planned)
           ✓ Only after internal + customer comms

5:00 PM    End of day all-hands / company social (optional)
           ✓ Celebrate
```

## Manager Talking Points (Brief all managers 30 min before all-hands)

```
"Here's what to say in your 1:1s today:

✓ 'Your job is safe — we negotiated hard for team protection.'
✓ 'You'll hear about role changes [date]. Until then, keep doing what you're doing.'
✓ '[Buyer's name] is a great fit because [reason].'
✓ 'If you have concerns, come to me. I'm here.'
✓ 'We're hiring [specific roles] — opportunities ahead for you.'

What NOT to say:
✗ 'I don't know what [Buyer] will do with you'
✗ 'You should polish your resume'
✗ 'This is hard for me too' (don't make it about you)
✗ 'Redundancies are coming' (wait for HR)
"
```

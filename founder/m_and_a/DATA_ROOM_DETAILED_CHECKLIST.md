# Data Room Organization — 50+ Document Checklist

**Critical:** A poorly organized data room kills deals or drags out closing.

Use this checklist to organize every document BEFORE buyer gets access.

---

## **Folder Structure (for Google Drive / Dropbox / DataSite)**

```
/Data Room — [Company Name]
├── 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (What buyer reads first)
├── 2. FINANCIAL STATEMENTS (Last 24 months)
├── 3. LEGAL & CORPORATE (Org structure, bylaws, IP)
├── 4. CUSTOMER & REVENUE (Proof of business)
├── 5. TECHNOLOGY & OPERATIONS (How you run it)
├── 6. PEOPLE & EMPLOYMENT (Team, options, benefits)
├── 7. COMPLIANCE & LEGAL (Risks, litigation, taxes)
├── 8. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY (Copyrights, patents, code)
└── 9. CONTRACTS (Vendor, customer, partner agreements)
```

---

## **1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY** (Time to read: 15 min)

- [ ] One-pager: Business overview (1 page)
- [ ] Elevator pitch + market opportunity (1 slide)
- [ ] Key metrics snapshot (1 slide)
- [ ] Org chart (current + 12-month projection post-close)

---

## **2. FINANCIAL STATEMENTS** (Critical for valuation)

### A. **Monthly Financials (Last 24 Months)**
- [ ] Profit & Loss statement (monthly, not just annual)
- [ ] Cash flow statement (monthly)
- [ ] Balance sheet (month-end for last 12 months)
- [ ] Bank statements (reconciliation to bookkeeping)
- [ ] Revenue recognition policy (accrual vs. cash accounting)

### B. **Annual Audited/Compiled Financials**
- [ ] 2024 audited/compiled P&L, CF, BS
- [ ] 2023 audited/compiled (if available)
- [ ] 2022 audited/compiled (if available)
- [ ] Auditor's letter / management letter (if applicable)

### C. **Revenue Detail**
- [ ] Customer list with MRR/ARR by customer (last 12 months)
- [ ] Monthly recurring revenue (MRR) trend
- [ ] Churn analysis (monthly churn rate last 24 months)
- [ ] NRR calculation (net revenue retention, with proof)
- [ ] Revenue recognition detail (invoices matching P&L)

### D. **Cost of Revenue**
- [ ] COGS breakdown (what costs directly tie to revenue?)
- [ ] Cloud/hosting costs (AWS, Stripe, etc. invoices)
- [ ] 3rd-party SaaS subscriptions (tools you use)
- [ ] Contractor/freelancer payments (if applicable)

### E. **Operating Expenses**
- [ ] Payroll records (salaries, taxes paid, 941 forms)
- [ ] Employee headcount + compensation (sensitive, mark as "Confidential")
- [ ] Contractor payments + 1099s issued
- [ ] All other op-ex (rent, software, services, marketing spend)

### F. **Working Capital**
- [ ] Accounts receivable aging (who owes you money + how old?)
- [ ] Accounts payable aging (who you owe money to)
- [ ] Deferred revenue (customer prepayments)
- [ ] Inventory (if applicable)

### G. **Tax Records**
- [ ] Last 3 years' personal income tax returns (as founder/owner)
- [ ] Last 3 years' corporate tax returns (Form 1120-S or 1120-C)
- [ ] Quarterly estimated tax payments (941-V, 1040-ES)
- [ ] State sales tax returns (if applicable)
- [ ] Property tax (if you own real estate)

### H. **Capitalization Table**
- [ ] Current cap table (shares issued, vesting status, strike prices)
- [ ] Stock option plan (all option grants, vesting schedules)
- [ ] Preferred stock purchase agreements (investor contracts)
- [ ] Fully diluted capitalization (what % is everyone?)

## **Organization Tips**

1. **Name files consistently:**
   - ✅ `2025-01-Bank-Statement.pdf`
   - ✅ `Customer-Contract-AcmeCorp.pdf`
   - ❌ `Document.pdf` or `Final-FINAL-v3.docx`

2. **Version control:**
   - Use one document per topic (not 10 versions of the same thing)
   - Mark "Draft" vs. "Final"

3. **Sensitivity levels:**
   - 🔴 **Confidential** (employee salaries, customer ARR, IP details)
   - 🟡 **Internal Use** (board minutes, strategic plans)
   - 🟢 **Shareable** (marketing materials, case studies, org chart)

4. **Index/table of contents:**
   - Create a master spreadsheet listing all documents:
     ```
     | Category | Document Name | File | Status | Comments |
     | Financial | 2025 P&L | F01-PL-2025.xlsx | Final | Audited by [CPA] |
     ```

5. **Access control:**
   - Use DataSite / Citrix ShareFile (professionally managed)
   - NOT Google Drive (no watermarks, IP audit trails)
   - Restrict access by role (buyer's CFO sees financials, CTO sees code)

---

## **Pre-Launch Checklist**

Before you give buyer access:

- [ ] Read through entire data room once (catch missing docs)
- [ ] Have CPA/lawyer review (anything that exposes risk?)
- [ ] Hide sensitive PII (salaries, home addresses)
- [ ] Remove any confidential customer data (keep only company name + contract)
- [ ] Verify all numbers match (balance sheet = reconciliation?)
- [ ] Index everything (buyer needs roadmap to find docs)
- [ ] Set up a Q&A log (buyer asks questions → you answer + log)

---

## **Post-Launch: Manage Access**

During diligence:
- [ ] Log all questions buyer asks (you'll need to answer them)
- [ ] Upload new documents as needed (auditor request, etc.)
- [ ] Update financials monthly (they want trend)
- [ ] Track who accessed what (due diligence visibility)
- [ ] Schedule weekly data room review call (answer questions in batch)

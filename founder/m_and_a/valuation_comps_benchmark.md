## Stage-based Multiples (Adjustments)

| Stage | Typical Range | Adjustment |
|-------|---|---|
| **Pre-PMF** | 2–4× ARR | Huge discount; talent/IP play |
| **Early PMF** | 4–6× ARR | Early revenue; growth + churn risk |
| **Growth (40%+ YoY)** | 8–15× ARR | Sweet spot; proven model |
| **Mature (20%+ YoY)** | 5–10× ARR | Lower growth = lower multiple |
| **Legacy (flat/decline)** | 1–4× ARR | Rare to see acquisitions |

## Profitability Impact

| Gross Margin | EBITDA Margin | Typical Multiple Adjustment |
|---|---|---|
| >80% | >30% | +++3–5× premium |
| 70–80% | 20–30% | +0 baseline |
| 60–70% | 10–20% | -1–2× discount |
| <60% | <10% | -3–5× discount (or no acquisition) |

**Example :**
- B2B SaaS at 8× revenue, 75% gross margin, 25% EBITDA = fair
- B2B SaaS at 8× revenue, 50% gross margin, 5% EBITDA = overvalued (should be 5×)

## Factors Affecting Multiple

### Positive (add to baseline)
✅ NRR > 120% (+2–4×)  
✅ <5% churn (+1–2×)  
✅ Market TAM growing >30% CAGR (+1–2×)  
✅ Customer concentration low (<20% top 3) (+0.5–1×)  
✅ Founder staying post-close (+1–2×)  
✅ Strategic synergies (strong) (+2–5×)  
✅ Patent portfolio, defensible moat (+1–2×)  

### Negative (subtract)
❌ NRR < 100% (-2–3×)  
❌ Churn > 10% annual (-1–3×)  
❌ Customer concentration (>50% top 3) (-1–3×)  
❌ Founder leaving (-1–2×)  
❌ Competitive pressure rising (-1–2×)  
❌ Tech debt, security issues (-0.5–2×)  
❌ Market TAM declining or flat (-1–2×)

## Your Valuation Quick Math

```
Base Multiple = [market baseline for sector] × [growth stage]
  Example: DevOps, 50% YoY growth = 10× baseline + 3× (growth premium) = 13×

Adjust for unit economics:
  - If NRR = 130%, churn = 3% → +1.5× multiplier
  - If NRR = 100%, churn = 8% → -1× multiplier

Adjust for synergies / strategic value:
  - If strong cross-sell potential → +1–3×
  - If data moat or network effects → +0.5–1.5×

Final = Base × [unit econ adjustment] × [synergy adjustment]

Example:
  Start: $5M ARR, DevOps, 40% YoY growth
  Base: 10× = $50M
  Unit econ good (NRR 115%, churn 5%) : ×1.2 = $60M
  Synergies moderate (for Datadog, strong; for other acquirers, weak) : ×1.2–1.5 = $72–90M
  
  Realistic range: $60–90M
  Asking: $75–85M (middle of range)
  Walkaway: $60M (floor)
```

## Scenario Modeling for Earnout

| Scenario | Base Payment | Earnout (if KPI hit) | Total Upside |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Conservative** | 65% × valuation | 20% if ARR +25% / 2yr | 85% |
| **Balanced** | 60% × valuation | 25% if ARR +30% / 2yr + NRR >120% | 85% |
| **Optimistic** | 50% × valuation | 35% if ARR +40% / 2yr + margin expand 5pts | 85% |

**Rule of thumb :** Most founders prefer 80–85% cash upfront (reduces risk).

## PE vs Strategic Multiples

| Buyer Type | Typical Multiple | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Strategic (big tech)** | 12–20× | Synergies; product integration |
| **PE Firm** | 6–10× | Financial return; no synergies |
| **Acquirer (consolidator)** | 8–12× | Mix of synergies + financial |

**Strategic usually pays more** (30–50% premium) because of perceived synergies. Negotiate accordingly.

---

## Red Flag Multiples

❌ Offering <4× if you have $1M+ ARR and growth >30%  
❌ Offering <2× if you have $5M+ ARR (unless declining)  
❌ Offering >25× (unless you're Slack/Stripe level)  

**If you see these, walk or have a strong counter.**

## How to Use This

1. **Identify your sector** (horizontal SaaS, vertical, etc.)
2. **Check your stage** (growth, mature, etc.)
3. **Look up unit economics** (NRR, churn, margins)
4. **Find your baseline multiple**
5. **Adjust up/down** for synergies and risks
6. **Cross-check against recent comps**
7. **Set asking range** (3–5% above fair value)
8. **Set floor** (realistic worst-case)

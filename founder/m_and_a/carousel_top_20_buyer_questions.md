# Question Carousel — Top 20 Buyer Questions & Answers

**Rank by frequency. Memorize top 5. Have answers for all 20.**

---

## TIER 1: ALWAYS ASKED (Prepare Verbatim)

**Q1: What's your revenue run rate and growth rate?**
A: "We're at $[X]M ARR, growing [Y]% YoY. Last quarter was [growth %] sequentially, so momentum is strong / accelerating."

**Q2: What's your churn and NRR?**
A: "Monthly churn [X]%, NRR [Y]%. So we're growing [Z]% from existing customers alone, which shows strong product-market fit."

**Q3: Who are your top customers?**
A: "Top 3: [Customer A] ($[amount]/yr), [Customer B] ($[amount]/yr), [Customer C] ($[amount]/yr). Breakdown: [X]% enterprise, [Y]% mid-market."

**Q4: What's your gross margin?**
A: "[X]% and improving to [Y]% next year because [cost reduction reason]."

**Q5: Why haven't you raised VC?**
A: "We bootstrapped to profitability / took small check from [investor]. Wanted to control dilution and prove unit economics independently."

---

## TIER 2: VERY COMMON (Have Talking Points)

**Q6: What's stopping you from 100% growth?**
A: "TAM expansion requires sales team we don't have yet. You have that. OR Market consolidation — buyer behavior shifting to [platform]. Your distribution gets us there."

**Q7: How defensible is your product?**
A: "[IP advantage / network effects / switching costs]. Top 3 customers say it would take [larger competitor] 12-18 months to replicate."

**Q8: What's your CAC payback period?**
A: "[X] months. Allows [Y]% margin for reinvestment. Best-in-class is [Z] months, so we're strong."

**Q9: Why should we buy instead of build?**
A: "Build time = 18 months (vs 90 days integration). Dev cost = $3-5M (vs $[acquisition price]). Team distraction = X engineers for a year. Acquire us = all solved."

**Q10: What's your biggest technical risk?**
A: "[Honest answer: scaling to 10M events/day OR compliance/SOC 2]. But [mitigation plan]. Not a dealbreaker; just something to plan for."

---

## TIER 3: LIKELY (Prepare Core Points)

**Q11: How would we integrate this product?**
A: "Simple: [brief integration plan]. Main effort = [one sentence]. Timeline = 90 days to ship to your customers. No risky re-architecture."

**Q12: What happens to customers if we acquire you?**
A: "No change for first 90 days. Then we migrate them to your [platform] with 1:1 feature parity. [X]% plan to stay; [Y]% might churn to your broader product."

**Q13: Is your team willing to stay 24 months?**
A: "Yes. [CEO/CTO] especially wants to see this integrated and successful. We've discussed this with everyone; they're in."

**Q14: What are you valued at today?**
A: "[X]x revenue based on recent fundraise / consultant valuation. Peer comps are [Y-Z]x. We're asking [Z]x based on [strategic fit / growth / margins]."

**Q15: Are you in exclusive talks with anyone else?**
A: "Not exclusive. Running standard process — [number] conversations, yours is the most advanced."

---

## TIER 4: LESS COMMON (Short Answers)

**Q16: Do you have any pending litigation?**
A: "No active litigation. [X] customer dispute in 2024 resolved in our favor. Standard IP indemnity in our LOI."

**Q17: Are there any contracts that might block change of control?**
A: "We're reviewing this. Early check: [customer A] has consent clause, but relationship is strong — they'll approve. [Customer B/C] have standard terms, should be fine."

**Q18: What's your data privacy / compliance posture?**
A: "GDPR / SOC 2 certified. CCPA compliant. Backup: [cloud provider]. Data residency options for EU customers."

**Q19: Do you have any debt?**
A: "Small debt: $[X]M on [terms]. Can be paid off from acquisition proceeds. No covenant restrictions on change of control."

**Q20: What's your worst-case scenario?**
A: "If [unlikely event], revenue drops to $[minimum floor]. But we've never seen this and [X] features protect against it."

---

## ANTI-ANSWERS (DON'T SAY THIS)

- ❌ "Revenue is [exact number]; I can't share more until NDA"
- ❌ "I don't know our churn; our data isn't great"
- ❌ "We're in exclusive talks with [competitor]; you're backup"
- ❌ "This team definitely won't want to stay if you change [decision]"
- ❌ "There's pending litigation but we'll discuss it later"
- ❌ "I don't have the answers; can your team call my engineer?"

---

## RESPONSE STRUCTURE (For All 20 Questions)

1. **Direct answer** (1 sentence, data point)
2. **Context** (why it matters, how it's good)
3. **Bridge** (connect to their pain / our synergy)

**Example:**
> **Q:** "What's your churn?"
> **A:** "We're at 3%/month. [Good context: SaaS average is 5%, so we're strong.] This matters because acquisition risk is lower — you're not buying a leaky bucket. We've built sticky features around [product]."

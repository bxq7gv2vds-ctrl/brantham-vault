# Seller's Final Checklist — 48 Heures Avant Signature

**Utilité** : dernière vérification avant de signer les papiers (irréversible).

---

## Financial (Day -2)

- [ ] Purchase price calculation: upfront cash + escrow + earnout breakdown
- [ ] Closing statement: verify working capital adjustment math
- [ ] Tax implications: understand your effective tax rate post-deal
  - [ ] Capital gains treatment (vs ordinary income)
  - [ ] State taxes (if multi-state)
  - [ ] Earnout tax timing (when taxed: milestone hit or cash received?)
- [ ] Bank account: wire instructions verified with buyer's counsel
- [ ] Escrow amount: percentage, release schedule, conditions for release
- [ ] Holdback period: 12m? 18m? Can you live without that cash?
- [ ] Earn-out payment schedule: when & how frequently paid
- [ ] Post-close payroll: if staying on, salary/bonus terms clear?

---

## Legal (Day -2)

- [ ] Reps & Warranties Schedule: have you personally verified EVERY statement?
  - [ ] Customer list: accurate names, ARR, renewal dates
  - [ ] Litigation: any pending/threatened legal action listed?
  - [ ] Contracts: all material contracts disclosed?
  - [ ] IP ownership: all code/patents/trademarks yours?
  - [ ] Compliance: GDPR, CCPA, SOC2, any audit findings disclosed?
  - [ ] Employees: no pending claims, wage issues, discrimination?
  
- [ ] Definition of "Material Adverse Effect" (MAE): would you walk if it happened?
  - [ ] Revenue drop >30%?
  - [ ] Key customer loss?
  - [ ] Major litigation?
  - [ ] Regulatory action?
  
- [ ] Indemnity Cap: what's your maximum liability?
  - [ ] Basket: below what $ amount do claims not count?
  - [ ] Cap: maximum you'll pay (% of deal price)?
  - [ ] Survival: how long are reps active?
  
- [ ] Escrow/Holdback mechanics:
  - [ ] Which bank holds escrow?
  - [ ] Can buyer dip into escrow for ANY indemnity claim?
  - [ ] Or only for "breach of reps"?
  - [ ] What % gets released at 12m, 18m?
  
- [ ] Non-Compete & Non-Solicit:
  - [ ] Duration (6m? 12m? 24m?)
  - [ ] Geographic scope (US? Worldwide?)
  - [ ] Can you start competitor? (or just consulting banned?)
  - [ ] Non-solicit: can you recruit your own team members after?
  
- [ ] Confidentiality: what stays confidential post-close?
  - [ ] Can you tell customers you were acquired? (usually yes)
  - [ ] Can you disclose price? (usually no)
  - [ ] Can you write about it for PR? (usually yes, with approval)
  
- [ ] Governing Law & Jurisdiction: where do disputes go?
  - [ ] Delaware? New York? Your home state?
  - [ ] Arbitration vs. litigation?
  - [ ] You comfortable with buyer's choice?

---

## Operational (Day -2)

- [ ] Employee retention plan:
  - [ ] Which key hires have retention bonuses?
  - [ ] What happens if they leave before earnout paid?
  - [ ] Healthcare benefits: changed post-close?
  - [ ] Stock options: accelerated vesting clause? (usually no)
  - [ ] Severance: if buyer lays off team, do they get severance? (from buyer, not you)
  
- [ ] Customer communication plan:
  - [ ] Do major customers need to approve the deal?
  - [ ] When will you announce? (same day as close?)
  - [ ] Who speaks to customers? (you? buyer? joint?)
  - [ ] What's the story? ("Our combined strengths..." or "Exciting integration"?)
  
- [ ] Transition services:
  - [ ] Will you stay post-close? For how long?
  - [ ] What are your responsibilities for first 30/60/90 days?
  - [ ] Who pays your salary during transition? (usually buyer)
  - [ ] Will you be employee or contractor? (tax implications)
  
- [ ] Vendor/supplier changes:
  - [ ] Any contracts that automatically terminate on "change of control"?
  - [ ] Vendor lock-in risks? (AWS, Stripe, etc. — usually OK)
  - [ ] Insurance policies: transferred post-close?

---

## Personal (Day -1)

- [ ] Tax planning with CPA:
  - [ ] Are you doing Section 83(b) election? (if getting stock)
  - [ ] Estimated tax payments scheduled?
  - [ ] State residency planning? (move before close if beneficial)
  - [ ] Gift/trust planning? (if splitting proceeds among family)
  
- [ ] Wallet/bank account:
  - [ ] Where will proceeds be deposited?
  - [ ] Wire instructions triple-checked?
  - [ ] Backup account if wire fails?
  
- [ ] Post-close plans:
  - [ ] What will you do next? (sabbatical? Start new company? Board seat?)
  - [ ] Have you told your family/partner?
  - [ ] Mental health: are you ready for post-deal limbo? (could be boring/weird)
  
- [ ] Advisor fees:
  - [ ] Lawyer fees: paid upfront or from proceeds?
  - [ ] Banker success fee: deducted from your amount?
  - [ ] Accountant fees: how much will this cost?
  - [ ] Advisor NDAs: do they have skin in the deal? (conflicts?)

---

## 72 Hours Before Signing: Final Red Flags to Watch

🚨 **STOP if:**

- [ ] Buyer's counsel suddenly changes legal terms dramatically
- [ ] Financing is still uncertain (should be locked by now)
- [ ] New reps are being added that you can't verify
- [ ] Earnout metrics have changed without your agreement
- [ ] Buyer is threatening to walk unless you accept unfavorable terms
- [ ] You haven't personally verified the customer list, revenue, IP
- [ ] Your lawyer is uncomfortable with indemnity cap/survival
- [ ] You feel rushed (this is your biggest decision ever, take time)

🟡 **CAUTION if:**

- [ ] Reps survival >24 months (taxes OK at 6y, but general reps should be 18m)
- [ ] Earnout % >25% (you'll be working for buyer with no control)
- [ ] Indemnity cap >5% of deal price (too high)
- [ ] Non-compete >18 months (kills your future)
- [ ] Buyer adding custom reps you didn't agree to

---

## 24 Hours Before Signing

**Final checklist:**

- [ ] Print & sign all copies (not just digital)
- [ ] Wire instructions confirmed with buyer's bank
- [ ] Board approval documented (minute book updated)
- [ ] Shareholder consent collected (cap table shareholders signed)
- [ ] Accountant reviewed closing statement
- [ ] Lawyer reviewed final reps/warranties
- [ ] Insurance: director & officer (D&O) policy active during reps period
- [ ] Personal estate planning: will updated (estate plan?)
- [ ] Emotional check: are you ready?

**Call your lawyer & say:**
"I'm about to wire/close. Final questions:
1. Is there anything you'd change in the docs?
2. What's the #1 risk I should understand?
3. Are we protected post-close if we discover X liability?"

---

## Signing Day: How It Works

**Timeline (typical):**

```
8 AM: Closing call with buyer's counsel + your lawyer
9 AM: Final document review (3–4 hours)
1 PM: All parties initial sections, sign full docs
2 PM: Docs scanned, transmitted to escrow agent
3 PM: Wire funds (if cash transaction) or stock transfer (if all-stock)
4 PM: Closing documents recorded/filed (if required)
5 PM: Email: "Closing is complete."
```

**What happens after:**
- Buyer takes control of assets/company
- Escrow account funded (you have claim on it)
- Earnout clock starts (if applicable)
- Non-compete begins
- You can tell team/customers

---

## Post-Signing: First Week

- [ ] Announce to team (in-person is better than Slack)
- [ ] Announce to customers (transparent > rumors)
- [ ] Freeze personal email (switch to buyer's domain)
- [ ] Document any transition work (hours logged for billing if contractor)
- [ ] Schedule monthly earnout tracking meetings
- [ ] Get first escrow release scheduled (6 months post-close)
- [ ] Celebrate 🎉 (you earned this)

---

## Red Flags That Mean "Don't Sign"

| Flag | Action |
|------|--------|
| Financing still contingent | Walk or extend closing deadline 30d |
| Customer consent not obtained | Renegotiate contracts now |
| Material liability discovered in DD | Reduce price by liability amount |
| Buyer's indemnity >1.5% of price | Reject or add reps insurance |
| Non-compete >18 months | Renegotiate to 12m |
| Earnout metrics buyer can control | Remove or reduce % |
| Founder feels pressured/rushed | Take 48h, reconsider |

---

## Final Decision: Go or No-Go?

**You sign if:**
- ✓ Price is acceptable (≥0.9 × BATNA)
- ✓ Terms are reasonable (reps cap, earnout control, timeline)
- ✓ Buyer seems reliable (not likely to fail post-close)
- ✓ You're comfortable with future role
- ✓ Team/board aligned

**You walk if:**
- ✗ Price <0.85 × BATNA
- ✗ Terms are egregiously unfavorable
- ✗ Buyer seems unreliable (financing shaky, counsel playing games)
- ✗ You feel coerced/rushed
- ✗ New material risk discovered

**Remember:** The only time you have leverage is BEFORE you sign.
After signing, you're exposed for years (reps survival).

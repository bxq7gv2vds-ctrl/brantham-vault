# PITCH DECK STRUCTURE — ACQUISITION CONTEXT

**Timeline:** 30 minutes deck + Q&A  
**Audience:** CTO, CFO, BD lead, CEO  
**Goal:** Establish credibility + mutual interest → NDA + full teaser by D7

---

## SLIDE 1: TITLE SLIDE (30 seconds)

```
[YourCo] — Strategic Acquisition Overview

Your Name, Founder & CEO
[Date]
[Optional: Logo of acquirer if warm intro]
```

*What it does:* Sets tone. Professional, not overly polished (signals "real company, not pitch").

---

## SLIDE 2: THE PROBLEM (1 minute)

**One sentence problem statement + data point**

```
[VERTICAL] teams still spend [X] hours/week on [PAINFUL TASK]
despite [X] existing solutions.

Problem proof:
• [Statistic about problem size: market, frequency, cost]
• [Customer quote about pain]
• [Untapped opportunity: "nobody does X well"]
```

**Design:** 1 visual (photo of pain, not abstract chart) + 2-3 bullets.

---

## SLIDE 3: THE SOLUTION (1 minute)

**What you built + why it works**

```
[YourCo] automates [specific task] in [vertical], reducing time 
from [X] hours to [Y] minutes.

Key differentiators:
• [Feature] — used by [X]% of customers, [Y]% retention
• [Feature] — integrated with [popular tool customers use]
• [Feature] — achieves [specific outcome: "3x faster", "80% cost savings"]

Not just a feature—a platform for [specific workflow].
```

**Design:** 1 product screenshot + 3 bullets (not 6). Avoid jargon.

---

## SLIDE 4: MARKET OPPORTUNITY (1 minute)

**TAM + your addressable segment**

```
Total Addressable Market: $[XB]

Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM): $[XM]
→ [VERTICAL] segment, [GEOGRAPHY], [CUSTOMER PROFILE]

Your current penetration: [X]% of SAM
Acquisition target: [Y]% of SAM by Year 2
```

**Design:** Simple TAM chart (not fancy) + brief explanation.

---

## SLIDE 5: TRACTION (2 minutes)

**Revenue + retention + customers**

```
Financial Performance:
• $[MRR]k MRR, [X]% YoY growth (last 12 months)
• [X]% gross margins, [Y]% EBITDA margins
• [Z]% net retention (customers expanding usage)

Customer Base:
• [X] total customers, [Y] enterprise logos
• [% of revenue] from top 10 (concentration analysis)
• [List 3-5 recognizable customer names]

Team:
• [X] full-time, founded [year]
• Key expertise: [2-3 areas relevant to acquirer]
```

**Design:** Dashboard format (simple bars/tables). Honesty > perfection.

---

## SLIDE 6: WHY NOW? (1 minute)

**Timing + inflection + urgency (softly)**

```
1. Product inflection — shipped [major feature/integration], 
   now seeing [acceleration metric].

2. Market inflection — [competitive threat / new regulation / 
   customer demand change] making consolidation natural.

3. Team appetite — built [X], proven [Y], now ready for 
   [next scale / venture capital / strategic partnership].

This is the right moment for both our companies.
```

**Design:** 3 bullets. Honest but optimistic. No fake urgency.

---

## SLIDE 7: FIT WITH YOUR COMPANY (2 minutes)

**Why acquisition makes sense for acquirer specifically**

```
Strategic alignment:

1. Customer fit
   • [YourCo] customers = [acquirer's] target accounts
   • Opportunity to cross-sell [acquirer's product]
   • Immediate revenue uplift: $[X] in year 1

2. Product fit
   • [YourCo] product complements [acquirer product]
   • Can be deployed as [standalone / add-on] initially
   • Technology stack compatible (keep-separate or integrate)

3. Team fit
   • [YourCo] team brings [specific expertise] to [acquirer team/problem]
   • Retention likelihood: High (align with incentives)

Alternative (build vs. buy):
   • Building [YourCo] in-house: 12-18 months + $[2-3M] in costs
   • Acquiring: immediate revenue + team + customers + code
```

**Design:** Clear alignment matrix. Answer "why us, why now, why [acquirer]?" explicitly.

---

## SLIDE 8: FINANCIALS (1 minute)

**Conservative projections + assumptions**

```
Year 1 (Post-acquisition):
• Revenue: $[ARR]M (conservative, based on [pipeline/cohort analysis])
• Gross Margin: [X]% (same as today)
• EBITDA: [Y]% (improving with scale)

Drivers:
• [X] new logos from [acquirer's customer base / market expansion]
• [Y]% churn (based on [retention data])
• [Z]% ACV expansion (from cross-sell)

Key assumptions:
• No major customer churn (only [X] customers <1 year contracts)
• [1-2] key hires in Year 1 (product, sales)
• Integration with [acquirer platform] starts Month 6
```

**Design:** Conservative. Use actual cohort data, not growth-rate extrapolation.

---

## SLIDE 9: VALUATION & STRUCTURE (1 minute)

**Simple, no negotiation yet**

```
Valuation Rationale:
• Revenue multiple: [X]x ARR (comparable to [Y, Z] acquisitions)
• EBITDA multiple: [X]x (market for [category])
• DCF-based fair value: $[X]M

Proposed structure (conceptual):
• [X]% cash, [Y]% earnout, [Z]% equity rollover (if applicable)
• Earnout tied to: [1-2 key metrics: revenue, retention, customer]
• Earnout period: [12-24 months]

We're open to structure discussion based on your preference.
```

**Design:** Ballpark range, not exact. Leave room for negotiation.

---

## SLIDE 10: NEXT STEPS (30 seconds)

```
1. Today: Alignment on strategic fit
2. This week: NDA + confidential teaser (10 pages, detailed financials)
3. Next 2 weeks: Data room setup, DD team alignment
4. Weeks 3-8: Full diligence (customer calls, tech audit, legal review)
5. Weeks 9-12: Binding docs (LOI, SPA), negotiation, close

Questions?
```

**Design:** Timeline + clear next milestone. No surprises.

---

## 📋 PRESENTATION NOTES

### Delivery
- **Pace:** 30 min presentation, 20-30 min Q&A
- **Questions to expect:**
  - Slide 3: "How do you differentiate from [competitor]?"
  - Slide 5: "Why did revenue slow in [month]?"
  - Slide 7: "What happens to your roadmap post-acquisition?"
  - Slide 9: "Why are you asking $[X]?"
- **Anticipate:** Have data on top 3 Q&A ready

### Design Tips
- **Avoid:** Animated transitions, dense text, bright colors
- **Use:** Actual screenshots, real customer testimonials, honest data
- **Tone:** Founder-to-executive (professional but authentic)

### Backup Slides (Keep in deck, don't present unless asked)
- Detailed tech architecture (for CTO)
- Customer concentration breakdown (for CFO)
- Competitor comparison matrix (for BD)
- Org chart + team bios (for CHRO)
- 3-year financial model with assumptions
- Market research / TAM derivation
- Product roadmap (6-12 months)

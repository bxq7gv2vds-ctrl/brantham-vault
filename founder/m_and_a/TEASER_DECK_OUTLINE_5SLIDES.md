# TEASER DECK — 5-SLIDE OUTLINE (Soft Shop Version)

**What is this?** The first document buyer sees after NDA. NO numbers, NO price, NO hype. Just storytelling + fit.

**Slides:** 5-8 max. Each slide has 1 idea. Design is clean (Figma, Keynote, Pitch deck template).

---

## SLIDE 1: PROBLEM (The pain point)

**Headline:** [1 sentence describing the problem]

**Visual:** Screenshot of frustration OR landscape photo capturing the problem

**Text:** 
- What's broken in the market right now?
- Who feels this pain? (job title, not "everyone")
- What does the status quo cost them? ($X per year, Y hours per week, Z frustration)

**Example:**
```
Title: "Sales teams spend 40% of their week on admin, not selling."
Subtitle: "CRMs are built for IT departments, not sales reps."
```

**Tone:** Serious. Data-backed. No exaggeration.

---

## SLIDE 2: SOLUTION (Your product in 1 sentence)

**Headline:** [1 sentence: what you built]

**Visual:** Product screenshot (clean, not cluttered) OR product in action (GIF)

**Text:**
- What's different about your approach?
- Why is it better than the status quo?
- (Don't list features; explain the shift)

**Example:**
```
Title: "We built the first CRM designed for sales reps, not admins."
Visual: Side-by-side comparison (your UI vs. competitor)
```

**Tone:** Optimistic. Clear. One core idea.

---

## SLIDE 3: TRACTION (Proof it works)

**Headline:** "We're winning with [your customers]"

**Metrics (pick 2-3, no more):**
- ARR or MRR (with growth rate, e.g., "$3M ARR, 40% YoY growth")
- Customer count (e.g., "500+ customers")
- Net Revenue Retention (e.g., "115% NRR")

**Avoid:**
- User signups (vanity metric)
- Press mentions (doesn't prove business)
- "We're the fastest growing..." (unfalsifiable)

**Visual:** Simple bar chart (ARR over time) or logo cloud (top 10 customers, anonymized if needed)

**Example:**
```
Title: "Traction: $3M ARR, 40% growth, 115% NRR"
Chart: ARR growth Q4 2024 → Q2 2026
```

**Tone:** Factual. Boring is good. No hype.

---

## SLIDE 4: MARKET OPPORTUNITY (Why now?)

**Headline:** "A $[X]B market waking up to this problem"

**Narrative:**
- What's changed in the market? (regulation, technology, customer behavior)
- Why is THIS the moment? (not 3 years ago, not 3 years from now)
- Who else is succeeding in this space? (1-2 comp wins, not your wins)

**Avoid:**
- "We have a TAM of $100B" (everyone says this)
- Market share projections (too speculative)
- "AI/blockchain/[hype]" (unless material to your product)

**Visual:** Simple chart (market growth rate) OR timeline (when the market woke up)

**Example:**
```
Title: "The CRM market is shifting from admins to power users"
Facts:
- Remote work grew from 25% → 60% (2020-2024)
- Sales reps demand self-service tools (not IT approval)
- Traditional CRMs losing 5% MRR to point solutions
```

**Tone:** Data-driven. Conversational. No cheerleading.

---

## SLIDE 5: TEAM (Who's running this?)

**Headline:** "Built by [X years] of domain experts"

**Bios:**
- Founder 1: [Title] + [key credential]. (1 sentence max)
- Founder 2: [Title] + [key credential]. (1 sentence max)
- (No more than 3 people. Quality > quantity)

**Avoid:**
- Massive bios (they don't care about your LinkedIn)
- Generic credentials ("MBA from Stanford" — everyone has one)
- Pictures of the team (save for close, not soft shop)

**Visual:** No photos. Just clean names + backgrounds.

**Example:**
```
CEO: Founded 3 B2B SaaS startups. 5 years as sales leader at [CRM Company].
CTO: 10 years at [Big Tech]. Led infrastructure for 100M+ user platform.
```

**Tone:** Credible, not pretentious.

---

## OPTIONAL SLIDE 6: Why [Buyer]? (If targeted)

**Only include if:**
- You're sending to a specific buyer (Strategic or PE fund)
- You have genuine synergies with them (not generic)

**Headline:** "Why [Buyer] is uniquely positioned to win"

**Narrative:**
- What's the synergy? (customer overlap? distribution? technology?)
- Why is this a rare opportunity? (not "we're a good fit for everyone")
- What can [Buyer] do that we can't? (not ego)

**Avoid:**
- Flattery ("Your platform is amazing...")
- Vague synergies ("We both serve SMBs...")

**Example:**
```
Title: "Why TechCorp Inc is uniquely positioned to win"
Narrative:
- TechCorp has 2,000+ enterprise customers in sales ops
- We have 500 power users in those same companies
- Opportunity: 1-2 year path to $50M ARR bundled with TechCorp's platform
- You have the distribution. We have the product.
```

**Tone:** Direct. Specific. Not flattery.

---

## SLIDE 7: (OPTIONAL) Next Steps

**Headline:** "Let's explore together"

**Call to action:**
- "Schedule a 30-min call: [your email]"
- "We're evaluating 5 strategic partners. Timeline is [month]."

**Tone:** Urgency without desperation.

---

## DESIGN TIPS

- **Font:** Clean sans-serif (Helvetica, Arial, Inter). No Comic Sans.
- **Colors:** 2-3 colors max. Dark background (not white). Light text (not hard to read).
- **Spacing:** Lots of whitespace. Not crammed.
- **Charts:** Simple bar charts or line graphs. No 3D pie charts.
- **Length:** 2-3 seconds per slide if spoken. If read silently, 5-10 seconds.

---

## USAGE RULES

**DO:**
- ✅ Send as PDF (not Keynote link)
- ✅ Send AFTER NDA is signed
- ✅ Send with a 1-sentence email: "Thanks for signing the NDA. Here's the teaser. Would love to hop on a call [this week/next week]."
- ✅ Assume they'll read it silently (in a meeting). Design accordingly.

**DON'T:**
- ❌ Include price, valuation, or multiple
- ❌ Include detailed financials (save for CIM)
- ❌ Mention competitors by name (focus on you)
- ❌ Include 10+ slides (keeps momentum, not a thesis)
- ❌ Use stock photos (looks cheap)
- ❌ Animate or auto-advance slides

## EXAMPLE: TEASER DECK FOR TECHFLOW ANALYTICS

**Slide 1: Problem**
```
Title: "Sales leaders are blind to real-time deal momentum"
Subtitle: "Traditional CRMs are reporting systems, not decision tools."
Visual: Screenshot of generic CRM dashboard (grey, boring)
```

**Slide 2: Solution**
```
Title: "TechFlow Analytics: Real-time visibility into your pipeline"
Visual: TechFlow dashboard (colorful, actionable, 3 metrics visible)
Key difference: "Built for sales leaders, not admins. Answers in seconds, not hours."
```

**Slide 3: Traction**
```
Title: "Growing fast with top enterprise sales teams"
Metrics:
- $3M ARR | 40% YoY growth
- 500+ companies (including 20+ Fortune 500 pilot customers)
- 115% NRR
Visual: Bar chart (ARR growth over 8 quarters)
```

**Slide 4: Market**
```
Title: "The $30B sales intelligence market is consolidating"
Facts:
- Sales software spending grew 25% YoY (2021-2026)
- Demand for "single pane of glass" reporting is growing
- Legacy CRMs are losing share to vertical analytics
Visual: Market growth chart (30B → 50B by 2028)
```

**Slide 5: Team**
```
CEO: Founded Salesloft (unicorn). 12 years in sales ops.
CTO: Ex-Salesforce. Led analytics for 50M+ user base.
```

**Slide 6: Why [Strategic Buyer]**
```
Title: "Why TechCorp is uniquely positioned"
- TechCorp serves 5,000+ enterprise sales teams
- TechFlow already integrated with 30% of TechCorp's customer base
- Opportunity: Own the sales intelligence market tier within TechCorp
```

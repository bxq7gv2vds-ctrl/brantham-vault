# Closing & Contingency Guide (Tâches 25-28)

---

## CLOSING CONDITIONS — 40 ITEM CHECKLIST (Tâche 25)

**Conditions that must be met before close:**

| Category | Item | Your Risk |
|---|---|---|
| **Board/Governance** | Seller board approval | Low (if you own company) |
| — | Buyer board approval | Medium (buyer stalls here) |
| **Regulatory** | Antitrust filing / approval (if needed) | Medium (can delay 3-6 months) |
| — | Industry-specific approval (healthcare/fintech) | High (can kill deal) |
| **Customer** | Top 10 customers consent to change of control | Medium (risk of churn) |
| — | No customer terminations between LOI and close | High (changes deal economics) |
| **Employee** | Key employees sign offer letters / retention agreements | Medium (risk of resignations) |
| **Legal** | No pending litigation / injunctions | High (can block close) |
| **Financing** | Buyer has financing in place (if not all-cash) | Critical (deal killer if denied) |
| **Closing Docs** | All purchase agreement, reps, warranties signed | Low (just admin) |

**Rule:** If condition not met, buyer can walk away (unless you negotiated termination fee).

## ESCROW STRUCTURE (Tâche 26)

**How buyer protects themselves post-close:**

| Item | Standard | Your Goal |
|---|---|---|
| **Escrow amount** | 15-20% of purchase price | Push to 15% |
| **Duration** | 12 months | Standard |
| **Who holds** | Neutral third party (law firm) | Standard |
| **Release timing** | 50% at month 6, 50% at month 12 (if no claims) | Push for 100% at month 12 |
| **Claims paid from** | Escrow first, then your pocket | Negotiate: escrow covers first $X |
| **Unclaimed escrow** | Returned to you at month 12 | Standard (good for you) |

**Example:**
- Deal: $10M upfront + $2M earnout
- Escrow: $1.5M (15%)
- You receive at close: $10M - $1.5M = $8.5M + $0.5M bonus = **$9M**
- Month 12: If no claims, + $1.5M escrow returned = **$10.5M total** (+ earnout if earned)

## BUYER FINANCING CONTINGENCY (Tâche 27)

**If buyer says "contingent on financing," this is dangerous.**

| Scenario | Risk | Your Protection |
|---|---|---|
| **All-cash buyer** | None | Safe, close on schedule |
| **Debt-financed buyer** | Lender pulls financing at last minute | Negotiate reverse termination fee |
| **Stock deal** | Buyer's stock price crashes before close | Rare, but negotiate stock price collar |

**Reverse termination fee (fight for this):**
```
"If buyer's financing falls through, buyer pays you $[1-2M] to walk away."
```

**Without it:** Buyer walks, you lose 6-12 months.

## MATERIAL ADVERSE CHANGE (MAC) DECODER (Tâche 28)

**Buyer's escape hatch: "Things changed; we're walking."**

| What Triggers MAC? | Your Risk | Your Response |
|---|---|---|
| "Revenue drops 20%" | High | Propose: MAC only if drop > 30% for 2 consecutive quarters |
| "Key customer leaves" | Medium | Carve out: Customer loss < 10% revenue = not MAC |
| "Market crashes" | Low | Carve out: Market-wide events (recession) ≠ MAC for you alone |
| "Competitor launches product" | Low | Carve out explicitly (normal business) |
| "Regulatory change" | Medium | Carve out: Industry-wide regulatory changes ≠ MAC |

**Golden rule:** Define MAC in LOI, not in purchase agreement (buyer will try to expand it later).

**Your MAC definition (propose this):**
> "MAC = Seller's revenue declines 30%+ for two consecutive quarters, caused by circumstances unique to Seller's business (not industry-wide)."

# LOI Template — Letter of Intent (Seller-Friendly Format)

**Objectif :** Modèle de LOI équilibré mais biaisé seller; à adapte à votre deal.

**Timing :** Après découverte initiale et accord tacite sur prix. LOI = commitment acheteur avant full DD.

---

## LETTER OF INTENT TO ACQUIRE

**[DATE]**

To: Board of Directors, [COMPANY_NAME]

Re: Letter of Intent for Acquisition of [COMPANY_NAME]

---

### 1. TRANSACTION OVERVIEW

**1.1 Parties**
- **Target:** [COMPANY_NAME] ("Company")
- **Buyer:** [ACQUIRER_LEGAL_NAME] ("Buyer")
- **Sellers:** All shareholders as set forth on Schedule A

**1.2 Transaction Structure**
The Buyer shall acquire 100% of the equity of the Company for an all-cash / all-stock / mixed consideration as follows:

**Purchase Price: $[X,XXX,XXX]**
- **Base Consideration:** $[X]M (payable at Close)
- **Earnout Consideration:** $[Y]M (contingent on KPIs; see Section 5)
- **Estimated Proceeds to Shareholders:** $[Z]M after transaction costs

---

### 2. PURCHASE CONSIDERATION & ALLOCATION

**2.1 Base Payment**
- **Cash at Close:** $[X]M (60–70% typical)
- **Timing:** Within 2 business days of closing

**2.2 Holdback / Escrow** *(Seller-friendly terms)*
- **Amount:** $[Z]M (10–15% of base)
- **Duration:** 12 months post-close (not 18–24 months)
- **Release:** Automatic release 12mo; reduced if indemnification claims resolve
- **Earnout not escrowed:** Earnout paid outside of escrow if KPIs hit

**2.3 Earnout Mechanics** *(Critical for Seller*
- **Target:** Achieve [KPI] by [DATE] (e.g., $6M ARR by Dec 31, 2025)
- **Payment:** If target met, earn $[X]M additional within 30 days
- **Calculation:** Based on Buyer's audited financial statements (independently verified)
- **No floor/cap adjustment:** Earnout not reduced if Buyer underinvests; not capped at [X]M
- **Timing:** Measured at calendar quarter-end; paid within 30 days of determination

**2.4 Transaction Costs**
- **Seller pays:** Legal (~$50–100k), accounting, advisor fees (if had broker)
- **Buyer pays:** Integration, due diligence, own legal
- **Deduct from base?:** NO—base price is net to shareholders

---

### 3. REPRESENTATIONS & WARRANTIES

**3.1 Standard Seller Reps** *(Narrow scope—Seller-friendly)*
The Company represents:
- [ ] Duly organized, validly existing Delaware corporation
- [ ] Cap table accurate and complete
- [ ] Financial statements true, accurate, not misleading
- [ ] No undisclosed liabilities >$[100k]
- [ ] No litigation; no regulatory violations
- [ ] IP: all material IP owned by Company or properly licensed
- [ ] Contracts: all material contracts disclosed; no change-of-control terminations
- [ ] Compliance: GDPR, SOC2, state privacy laws in scope

**3.2 Rep & Warranty Limitations** *(Critical Seller Protection)*
- **Materiality Scrape:** $[250k] threshold (no claim <$250k); $[2M] cap (basket)
- **Survival:** 12 months (not 18–24 months)
- **Indemnification Cap:** 10% of purchase price ($[X]M)
- **Exceptions:** No rep warranty for known issues disclosed in data room
- **Buyer's Risk:** If Buyer discovers issues during DD, Buyer can renegotiate/walk; Buyer not forced to close on surprise issues

**3.3 Buyer Representations**
- [ ] Authority to execute
- [ ] Financing committed (show proof)
- [ ] No litigation preventing close

---

### 4. CONDITIONS PRECEDENT

**4.1 Conditions to Buyer's Obligation to Close** *(Seller must satisfy)*
- [ ] Representations & warranties true as of Close
- [ ] No material adverse effect (MAE) on Company
- [ ] No law/regulatory action prohibiting transaction

**4.2 Conditions to Seller's Obligation to Close** *(Buyer must satisfy)*
- [ ] Buyer's reps true
- [ ] Financing available and committed
- [ ] No governmental action prohibiting close
- [ ] No regulatory/antitrust issues (Buyer bears this risk)

**4.3 MAE Definition** *(Narrow, Seller-friendly)*
Material Adverse Effect excludes:
- General economic/market conditions
- Changes in applicable laws (broad-based)
- Changes in INTEREST RATES or stock market (excludes buyer-specific)
- Pandemics, acts of God
- Any impact <$500k cumulatively

**Translation:** Buyer can't use "market downturn" to walk or renegotiate.

---

### 5. EARNOUT TERMS

**5.1 KPI Definition** *(Clear, objective, auditable)*

| Metric | 2025 Target | 2026 Target | Earnout Payout |
|--------|---|---|---|
| ARR | $[6.0]M | $[7.5]M | $[2.5]M if hit by Dec 31, 2025 |
| NRR | >115% | >120% | Bonus $500k if NRR >120% |
| Customer Retention | <5% churn | <4% churn | Bonus $250k per 1% beat |

**5.2 Earnout Governance**
- **Measurement:** Audited by Buyer's independent accountant; Seller right to audit working papers
- **Dispute:** If Buyer/Seller disagree, independent auditor decides (split cost 50/50)
- **No reduction for underinvestment:** Even if Buyer doesn't invest in sales/product, KPI still measured; Seller not penalized
- **Seller audit rights:** Quarterly access to Buyer's financials to track progress toward KPI

**5.3 Earnout Payment**
- **Timing:** Within 30 days of KPI confirmation
- **No caps/collars:** Earnout paid in full if KPI hit; not reduced
- **If earnout not hit:** No payment; no "partial credit"

---

### 6. CLOSING & TRANSITION

**6.1 Closing Date**
- **Date:** [DATE] or as mutually agreed (target: 90–120 days post-LOI)
- **Earlier:** If diligence completes faster, close sooner (not later mandatory)

**6.2 Employment & Retention**
- [ ] CEO/Founder: [X] year employment agreement; $[salary]; +[equity earnout]
- [ ] CTO: [X] year; $[salary]; +[equity earnout]
- [ ] Key engineers: [X]x base salary retention bonus (paid at close)

**6.3 Transition Services Agreement**
- **Duration:** 60 days (not 6 months)
- **Scope:** Seller assists with customer onboarding, contract transition, employee questions
- **Cost:** Buyer pays $[X]/hour for transition labor (not free)

---

### 7. EXCLUSIVITY & STANDSTILL

**7.1 Exclusivity** *(Narrow—Seller-friendly)*
- **Duration:** 60 days from LOI signing (not 90 days)
- **Scope:** Seller talks to Buyer only; doesn't solicit other bids
- **Termination:** Either party can terminate + pursue other options if deal doesn't close by [DATE]

**7.2 Standstill**
- **Duration:** 12 months post-close (or if deal terminates, 12 months)
- **Restriction:** Seller/team doesn't compete directly in [specific vertical only]
- **Exception:** Allows adjacent products; doesn't restrict consulting

---

### 8. DEAL TERMINATION

**8.1 Either Party Can Walk If:**
- [ ] Financing fails (Buyer)
- [ ] Material DD finding (either party, >$[X] impact)
- [ ] Regulatory/antitrust block (Buyer)

**8.2 Fees on Termination**
- **No reverse break fee** (Seller doesn't owe Buyer if walks)
- **If Buyer walks for no valid reason:** $[X]k termination fee (reimburse Seller's legal/advice costs)

---

### 9. CONFIDENTIALITY & NDA

- [ ] Both parties sign mutual NDA (already in place)
- [ ] Survives termination: 2 years
- [ ] Public announcement: Joint press release within 5 business days of close

---

### 10. GOVERNING LAW & DISPUTES

- **Jurisdiction:** Delaware law (neutral)
- **Disputes:** Mediation first (30 days); if unresolved, arbitration (not litigation)
- **Costs:** Loser pays reasonable attorneys' fees (incentivizes settlement)

---

### 11. SCHEDULES & EXHIBITS

**Schedule A:** Capitalization Table (list all shareholders + their shares)
**Schedule B:** Material Contracts (list customer/vendor contracts >$50k/year)
**Schedule C:** Intellectual Property (patents, trademarks, copyrights)
**Schedule D:** Employees & Equity Grants
**Schedule E:** Litigation / Regulatory Issues (if any)
**Schedule F:** Customer List (name, ARR, contract terms, renewal date)

## KEY SELLER-FRIENDLY NEGOTIATION POINTS

| Term | Seller asks for | Buyer typically offers | Compromise |
|------|---|---|---|
| **Escrow Duration** | 12 mo | 18 mo | 12 mo (non-negotiable) |
| **Escrow Holdback %** | 10% | 15–20% | 10–12% |
| **Rep Warranty Survival** | 12 mo | 18 mo | 12 mo |
| **Indemnification Cap** | 10% | 15% | 10% |
| **Earnout Adjustment** | No caps | -30% if underinvest | +/- 10% max on defined KPI |
| **MAE Threshold** | $500k | $250k | $350k |
| **Exclusivity Period** | 60 days | 90 days | 75 days |

---

## What NOT to Accept

❌ **Reps & Warranties > 18 months:** Indefinite liability post-close  
❌ **Escrow > 15%:** Too much seller money at risk  
❌ **No earnout if KPI hit:** Buyer bait-and-switch  
❌ **Full indemnification with no caps:** Seller bankrupt by claims  
❌ **Reverse break fee if Seller walks:** Disproportionate  
❌ **Change-of-control termination on key customers:** Kills deal economics  

---

## Red Flags in Buyer's LOI Counter

🚩 **Reps tied to "best of knowledge"** (Seller liable for unknowns)  
🚩 **MAE defined broadly** (Buyer has escape hatch)  
🚩 **Earnout reduction if Buyer cuts budget** (Buyer controls KPI hit)  
🚩 **No walk-away price** (Buyer can keep renegotiating)  
🚩 **Buyer audit rights 24/7** (Fishing expedition post-close)  

**If Buyer includes these, push back hard or walk.**

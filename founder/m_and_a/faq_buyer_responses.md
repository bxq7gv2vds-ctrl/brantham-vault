# FAQ Buyer — 10 Questions Fréquentes & Réponses

**Use pour sales calls avec acheteurs. Adaptez à votre contexte.**

---

## Q1: Pourquoi vendez-vous maintenant?

**Bad answer:** "Je m'ennuie." / "J'ai une meilleure idée."  
**Good answer:** "We've proven product-market fit ($XM ARR, 98% NRR). To maximize impact and acceleration, joining [buyer type] unlocks [specific synergy: distribution, tech, team]. The timing is right for both parties."

**Why it works:**  
- Positions exit as *strategic* not *desperate*
- Shows you understand buyer's value-add
- Suggests you'd stay & execute post-close

---

## Q2: Quelle est votre concentration client?

**Format:**
- Top 1 customer: X% of ARR
- Top 5: X%
- Top 10: X%
- Median customer size: $[ACV]
- Customers with >20% YoY churn risk: [X]

**If concentration > 30% in top 1:**  
Don't hide it. Say: "Our largest customer is X% of ARR. Here's why it's low-risk: [contract length = 3y, NPS = 9/10, auto-renewal, cross-sell potential]. Even if they churn, we'd replace it in [X] months with pipeline velocity."

**Why transparency wins:**  
- Buyer will find it anyway in DD
- Ownership of the risk kills buyer's negotiating leverage
- Shows you know your business

---

## Q3: Quel est votre churn, et pourquoi?

**Good format:**
- **Monthly churn:** X% (SaaS standard benchmark: 2–5% for Enterprise, 5–8% for SMB)
- **Annual net revenue retention:** X% (target: >100%)
- **Reason for any uptick:** [2024 Q2 spike = 2 customers in [industry] hit economic headwinds; both since re-engaged]

**If your churn is high (>8%):**  
Explain root cause, not excuse: "We've been optimization-focused rather than customer-success-focused. Post-acquisition, we're adding [specific hire/team] to reduce to [target]."

**Why it matters:**  
- Churn determines buyer's valuation (1% churn delta = ~1x multiple delta)
- Demonstrates operational maturity

---

## Q4: Pourquoi est-ce difficile de reproduire votre produit?

**Good answer (moat):**
- **Network effects:** "Each customer onboarded improves [specific metric] for all others."
- **Data advantage:** "We've collected [specific dataset] that takes competitors [X years] to replicate."
- **Switching costs:** "Migration cost = [X hours], making switching non-trivial for customers."
- **Brand/trust:** "We have [X% market share] in [vertical]; brand is [defensible metric]."

**If you DON'T have a moat:**  
Honesty: "We compete on execution speed, customer intimacy, and community. Our moat is team—and that's why joining [buyer] strengthens it."

---

## Q5: Quel est votre chiffre d'affaires réel vs. ce que vous revendiquiez?

**Bring the proof:**
- Last 12 months of bank statements (shows money actually came in)
- Customer contract PDFs (tied to ARR claims)
- Monthly P&L (tracks growth quarter by quarter)
- SaaS metrics (MRR, new, expansion, churn broken down)

**If there's a discrepancy:**  
Address it before buyer finds it: "Our ARR projection was X. Current cash-collected is Y. Gap is Z because [contracts staggered, 1 customer delayed payment, etc.]."

---

## Q6: Qui sont vos 3 biggest risks post-close?

**Smart founder answer** (shows self-awareness):
1. **[Specific employee] retention:** They own [X%] of [product/sales]. Post-close, we'd include [equity/bonus] to retain for [duration].
2. **Integration with your platform:** We're [tech stack]. You're [stack]. Plan: [X weeks] to [merge/integrate/deprecate] features.
3. **Customer perception:** Some customers bought us because we're independent. Plan: [communication strategy, transition team, retained brand or sunset?]

**Why it wins:**  
- Shows maturity
- Preempts buyer concerns
- Demonstrates you have solutions

---

## Q7: Pourquoi devriez-vous rester en tant que Founder/CTO?

**Good reasons to stay:**
- "Product vision is [X-year roadmap]. I stay to [ ensure delivery / own customer strategy / lead engineering]. Here's the deal structure: [equity / earnout tied to] product milestones."
- "The team needs continuity. I'll [1-year transition], then [advisory role / equity vest / exit]."

**If you want to leave:**
Honesty: "I've found a great [replacement CTO / operator]. [Their background], $[salary package] locks them in. My role becomes [advisor / customer relationships / optional]."

---

## Q8: Quel est votre "10-month roadmap" en cas de problème post-close?

**Have a plan:**
- "If [metric] drops, we'll [invest in that team, redirect resources, hire specialist]."
- "If top customer churn, pipeline = [X months of backfill](."
- "If [integration challenge], we pivot to [Plan B: keep as independent product, module, sunset]."

**Example roadmap:**
- Month 1–3: Migrate [system A] to buyer's infrastructure
- Month 4–6: Consolidate customer success teams
- Month 7–10: Deprecate [legacy feature], cross-sell [buyer feature] to [X customers]

---

## Q9: Combien coûterait un acquihire vs. acquisition?

**Quick math:**
- **Acquihire (team only):** $[sum of 12-month salaries] + [severance + equipment] = ~$[X] per engineer
- **Full acquisition (product + team + IP):** $[ARR × multiple] = 10–20x higher

**Your positioning:**  
"Our product has [specific value: recurring revenue, IP, market position]. If you wanted just the team, acquisition cost would be [Y]. Our customers + recurring = worth [Z premium]."

---

## Q10: Quel est votre "Plan B" si cette acquisition échoue?

**Be honest, briefly:**
- "We remain profitable / have [X months] of runway. We'd continue [specific growth strategy: hiring, geographic expansion, new vertical]."
- "We're in talks with [X] other buyers; timeline is flexible."
- "At worst, we IPO in [timeframe] or remain independent and grow."

**Why honesty helps:**
- Removes urgency from buyer's side (less pressure to negotiate)
- Shows you have optionality
- Demonstrates resilience

---

## Master Talking Points to Memorize

1. "We've proven product-market fit. You're buying a cash-flowing business, not a concept."
2. "Our churn/retention shows customer stickiness. That's your predictability post-close."
3. "The team is the moat. Post-close, we'd [stay / transition strategically] to protect that."
4. "Our customers are willing to [specific action: stay, expand, vouch]. That's confidence."

---

**Usage:** Print this, read before each buyer call. Tailor the numbers + specifics to your company. Avoid sounding scripted—this is *training*, not *talking points to read*.

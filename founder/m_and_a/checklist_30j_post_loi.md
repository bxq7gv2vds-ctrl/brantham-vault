# Checklist 30 Jours Post-LOI — Conditions Précédentes

**Entre signature LOI et kick-off legal.** Responsabilité: Seller. Cible: 15-20 jours.

---

## Semaine 1: Opérations & Légal

- [ ] **Notarize cap table** (CertTM ou comptable)
- [ ] **Debt schedule finalized** (tous les prêts, SAFEs, convertibles, dettes opérationnelles)
- [ ] **Customer concentration analysis**: top 10 clients = combien % de l'ARR?
- [ ] **Revenue bucket by cohort**: année d'achat par client (churn pattern)
- [ ] **Compile all customer contracts** (PDFs + metadata: start date, ACV, auto-renew, notice periods)
- [ ] **Customer NPS/CSAT survey** (email to all customers: "How likely to recommend?" 1–10)
- [ ] **Verify all IP assignments**: éventuels co-founder, contractors, employees
- [ ] **Trademark/domain audit**: tous les domaines en possession? TM clearance?
- [ ] **Engage M&A accountant** (si pas déjà) pour due diligence support
- [ ] **Notify CPA/Tax advisor** (commence à penser à impôts de la vente)

---

## Semaine 2: Team & Assets

- [ ] **Key employee retention agreements drafted** (template: manager/CTO/CFO minimum)
- [ ] **Employee non-compete review** (existing clauses? période de non-concurrence?)
- [ ] **Equity schedule audit** (options, vesting, strike prices — all current?)
- [ ] **Org chart + bios** (3-line bio par lead, rôle, tenure)
- [ ] **Employee handbook review** (adhère aux lois 2026? PTO policies, benefits?)
- [ ] **Background check authority consent** (buyeur demandera background checks)
- [ ] **Product roadmap deck** (6-month forward, slide 1: current state)
- [ ] **Tech stack inventory** (languages, frameworks, DBs, APIs, third-party tools)
- [ ] **Code audit readiness** (is GitHub org clean? old branches? do you have decent README?)
- [ ] **Infra cost breakdown** (AWS/GCP/Azure monthly spend, breakdown by service)

---

## Semaine 3: Finance & Compliance

- [ ] **Audited/reviewed financials** (SaaS metrics: MRR, CAC, LTV, payback, ACS — all calculated?)
- [ ] **Monthly P&L last 12 months** (Excel: revenue, COGS, OpEx, EBITDA)
- [ ] **Customer cohort analysis** (retention by vintage: do 2023 cohorts show declining churn?)
- [ ] **CAC payback period** (for acquisition-focused sales, what's payback?)
- [ ] **Gross margin trend** (up or down YoY? why?)
- [ ] **Expense audit** (find 3–5 items to cut before close = better EBITDA story)
- [ ] **Tax returns last 3 years** (1040s, corporate returns, state filings)
- [ ] **Sales compensation plan** (commission structure, quotas, commissions owed at close?)
- [ ] **Contract liability schedule** (any contracts that penalize you if bought? early termination fees?)
- [ ] **Insurance policies** (D&O, general liability, cyber — can buyer assume?)
- [ ] **GDPR/Privacy audit** (CCPA? DPA in place? Privacy policy up to date?)
- [ ] **Security compliance** (SOC 2? ISO? HIPAA? document what you have)

---

## Semaine 4: Dataroom & Materials

- [ ] **Create secure dataroom** (Google Drive folder structure, shared with limited access link)
- [ ] **Index all docs** (spreadsheet: doc name, category, date, page count)
- [ ] **Verify all NDAs** (buyers will sign NDA before dataroom access; draft lightweight NDA)
- [ ] **Compile investor deck** (5-slide summary: problem, solution, traction, team, ask)
- [ ] **Customer reference list** (3–5 happy customers willing to take buyer calls; their titles)
- [ ] **Press/media coverage** (all articles, podcasts, tweets featuring company — compile links)
- [ ] **Pitch deck v2** (refresh 10–15 slides: problem, TAM, competition, why now, why us)
- [ ] **Founder narrative doc** (1 page: origin story, why you built this, what you learned)
- [ ] **Buyer research brief** (1-pager on each of top 3 targets: org, recent deals, possible synergies)
- [ ] **Email template for warm intros** (template your advisors will use to intro to buyers)

---

## Sign-Off Checklist

**Before buyer legal team kicks off:**

- [ ] All docs indexed in dataroom
- [ ] Cap table 100% audited
- [ ] Customer contracts compiled
- [ ] IP assignments confirmed + all prior art cleared
- [ ] Financial statements ready (3y history + last quarter)
- [ ] Key employees aligned + retention agreements drafted
- [ ] Buyer has NDA signed + dataroom access live
- [ ] Seller's counsel engaged + ready for SPA drafting

---

## Red Flags to Catch Now (Kill the Deal Early)

🚩 **Top 10% of customers represent >50% of ARR** → buyer will negotiate harder, add earn-out claws back  
🚩 **IP disputes** (founder dispute, contractor IP not assigned) → can add months to close  
🚩 **Key person earning >50% of team compensation** → retention risk  
🚩 **GDPR violations or security breaches** → compliance risk, indemnity spike  
🚩 **No clean GitHub** (history of large commits, secrets in code) → tech risk  
🚩 **Gross margin < 60%** (for B2B SaaS) → buyer views as unprofitable  
🚩 **Monthly churn > 8%** → unit economics broken  
🚩 **Founder still doing 50% of customer success** → not scalable  
🚩 **No customers willing to reference call** → churn signal  
🚩 **Tax liens or liens on IP** → kills deal until resolved  

---

**Timeline:** Aim to complete 80% of this by day 25, final 20% by day 30.  
**Effort:** ~60–80 hours total (allocate 1 lead, 1 support).  
**Success metric:** Buyer says "docs look clean" after first dataroom review.

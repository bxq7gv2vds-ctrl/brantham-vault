# BUYER TYPE SELECTOR — Which Path = Best Outcome for YOU?

**Utilité** : Choisir strategic vs PE vs venture vs financial en 5 min, basé sur tes priorités.

---

## 1. WHAT MATTERS TO YOU MOST?

Classe par importance (1=most, 4=least):

- **Cash out NOW** (speed, certainty) → Strategic or PE
- **Control/Vision** (stay involved, steer) → Venture or Financial buyer (minority)
- **Passive return** (walk away, no role) → PE or Financial
- **Highest price** (maximize exit value) → Strategic > PE > Venture > Financial

---

## 2. DECISION TREE

```
Q1: Do you want a post-close role?
  YES → Q2
  NO  → Q3

Q2: Do you have a multi-year vision for the product?
  YES → Venture buyer
        (can reject cash, keep control, long term)
  NO  → PE buyer (operational leverage)
  
Q3: Is your main goal maximum $?
  YES → Strategic buyer (synergies = highest price)
  NO  → Financial buyer (passive, ROIC-focused)
```

---

## 3. DETAILED COMPARISON

| Dimension | Strategic | PE | Venture | Financial |
|-----------|-----------|----|---------|-----------| 
| **Who they are** | Competitor/adjacent market | LBO fund (Thoma Bravo, Vista) | Venture fund with M&A arm | PE/alt assets, passive |
| **What they want** | Revenue synergies, cost cuts, moat | Cash flow + 3-5yr exit | Smart capital + talent | ROIC 15-25% |
| **Price they pay** | HIGHEST (synergies premium) | Medium (LBO math) | Medium-Low (growth focus) | LOW (passive yield) |
| **Earnout likelihood** | 20-30% (synergies-based) | 40-60% (operational targets) | 10-20% (optional) | <5% (formula-based) |
| **Earnout difficulty** | Moderate (controllable) | HIGH (buyer-controlled metrics) | LOW (aligned) | Very LOW (automatic) |
| **Your post-close role** | Often required (6-24m) | Optional (nice-to-have) | Preferred (founder) | Rare (passive) |
| **Your control** | Limited (rolled up) | Very limited (PE board) | More freedom | Minimal (dividend play) |
| **Timeline to close** | 60-90 days | 90-120 days | 45-60 days | 30-45 days |
| **Risk of walkaway** | Medium (regulatory/synergies) | Low (financial fit) | Medium (founder culture) | Very Low |

## 4. EXAMPLE SCENARIOS

**Scenario A: "I want MAX cash NOW, no post-close role"**
```
→ Target: Strategic buyer
→ Why: Highest price ceiling
→ Risk: Lower earnout %
→ Mitigation: Negotiate reps cap <1%, holdback 12m max
```

**Scenario B: "I want control of product for 2-3 more years"**
```
→ Target: Venture buyer (minority stake keep, founder operates)
→ Why: Aligned vision, founder-friendly
→ Risk: Possible lower absolute price
→ Mitigation: Negotiate founder role guardrails (budget, headcount, roadmap)
```

**Scenario C: "I'm burned out, want passive exit + income"**
```
→ Target: PE buyer or Financial buyer
→ Why: Step away, get cash + dividend stream
→ Risk: Earnout failure (PE drives hard targets)
→ Mitigation: Front-load cash, keep earnout <20% total
```

**Scenario D: "I'm not sure, want to test market"**
```
→ Target: Soft shop with 2-3 buyer types in parallel
→ Why: Let market tell you the price
→ Decision rule: Pick highest bidder at LOI stage, adjust buyer type
```

## 5. RED FLAGS BY BUYER TYPE

### Strategic Buyer Red Flags
- 🚩 Synergies vague or unquantified
- 🚩 Earnout >40% tied to buyer-controlled metrics
- 🚩 Non-compete >3 years
- 🚩 Your product gets killed within 12m (pivot threat)

### PE Red Flags
- 🚩 Earnout >50% total (too much risk)
- 🚩 EBITDA targets unrealistic vs your 3-yr trend
- 🚩 Founder role undefined (high departure risk)
- 🚩 Debt load >3x EBITDA (overleveraged)

### Venture Red Flags
- 🚩 Founder veto on product decisions (cap your control)
- 🚩 VC firm has portfolio conflict (your competitor in cap table)
- 🚩 Minimum growth targets embedded (earnout trap)
- 🚩 Exit clause vague (could be forced sale down road)

### Financial Buyer Red Flags
- 🚩 Below-market price (they're betting on passive yield, not growth)
- 🚩 Zero post-close role (you're completely out)
- 🚩 Passive management (product stagnation risk → churn)

# Acqui-Hire Playbook: Negotiating Your Survival

**Acqui-hire** = Buyer acquires your company primarily to hire your team.  
Your product may be shelved (killed, integrated, or ignored).

---

## When You're Headed for Acqui-Hire (The Signals)

| Signal | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| "We love your team, not sure about the product" | Acqui-hire is the play |
| "How much of team would stay post-close?" | They're assessing talent cost |
| "We don't need your revenue" | Product isn't the value |
| Low offer (0.5-2x ARR) | Talent pricing, not product pricing |
| Earnout doesn't depend on revenue | They don't care if your customers leave |
| Interest in "integrating" team quickly | Dissolving product into their org |

---

## Acqui-Hire Deal Structure

### Typical Terms
```
Total consideration: $X (usually 0.5-2x ARR)
├─ Signing bonus: 20-30% (cash)
├─ Base salary bump: 10-20%
├─ Signing stock grant: 10-15% (vesting)
└─ New role title + vesting: 4-year new cliff on equity
```

### Example: $500k ARR company, 5 people

```
Traditional M&A (product): $2-3M (4-6x)
Acqui-hire (team): $1-1.5M (2-3x)
  ├─ Signing (cash): $300k
  ├─ Each person gets: $25-50k signing + new equity
  ├─ New 4-year vesting: $150-200k equity
  └─ Integration: Fold into buyer's org
```

---

## Your Power in Acqui-Hire

You have leverage **only if**:
- ✅ Team has credible outside offers
- ✅ You can sell the "keeps team together" value
- ✅ Buyer *really* wants specific people (not generic engineers)

**You have NO leverage if:**
- ❌ Job market is weak (people desperate for jobs)
- ❌ Team is fungible (could hire elsewhere cheaper)
- ❌ You're undesirable (culture, skill mismatch)

---

## Negotiation: The Cash vs. Equity Split

### The Buyer's Play
```
Buyer: "$1M total consideration"
Buyer's thinking: "We can keep the team via vesting. 
                   Give them 30% cash, 70% vesting. 
                   If they leave, we keep upside."
```

### Your Counter
```
You: "That's aggressive. For this quality of team 
      and retention risk, we need 50% cash at signing + 
      4-year single-trigger on remaining 50%."

Reason: "Gives team certainty (they're not taking a risk 
         on your company), keeps them happy."
```

### Realistic Outcome
- 40% cash at close
- 20% signing equity (new grants, new vesting)
- 40% retention bonus (paid out at months 6, 12, 18, 24)

---

## Retaining Your Team (The Real Negotiation)

### Non-Negotiable Asks

1. **Keep team in same group** (don't scatter into 5 orgs)
   - Buyer will try: "Different managers, different teams"
   - You push back: "Team coherence is why you're acquiring us"

2. **Each person gets explicit role, title, reporting line**
   - Buyer will try: "Figure it out post-close"
   - You push back: "All roles defined in writing, agreed before close"

3. **Single-trigger acceleration** (don't claw back equity if things change)
   - Buyer will try: "Double-trigger (you have to perform)"
   - You push back: "Single-trigger (they're paid regardless)"

4. **Manager = founder (or trusted lieutenant)**
   - Buyer will try: "Your new VP of Eng is our COO"
   - You push back: "I'm managing team first 6 months"

5. **Retention bonus paid (don't just vesting)**
   - Buyer will try: "Equity keeps them here"
   - You push back: "50% equity + 50% cash retention bonus"

## Term Sheet Red Lines

| Point | Walk-Away | Acceptable |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| **Cash at close** | <30% | 40-60% |
| **New equity vesting cliff** | >2 years | 1-year cliff |
| **Total retention duration** | <18 months | 24-36 months |
| **Clawback if you leave** | Yes, claws back vesting | No clawback |
| **Founder title/role** | Generic or elimination | VP Eng / CTO |
| **Signing bonus per person** | <$15k | $25-50k+ |

## Preventing the "Slow Fade"

### The Buyer's Game
```
Close deal
  → Assign team to random projects
  → Move them to different managers
  → Give them work they hate
  → Team quits (Buyer only paid $X, made out!)
```

### Your Defense
```
1. Write 90-day integration plan (naming projects + leaders)
2. Define retention metrics (if N people leave, trigger bonus payout)
3. Monthly all-hands (track morale, escalate issues)
4. Founder spot-checks team sentiment (monthly 1:1s)
5. Escalation path to buyer CEO if things drift
```

---

## The Earnout in Acqui-Hire

Typically **NO product revenue-based earnout**.  
Instead:
- "Keep X% of team for Y months" = earn $Z
- OR: "Product hits Z milestone" = team gets bonus

### Example
```
$1M base deal
$250k earnout, split as:
  - "All 5 team members at 6 months" = $100k paid
  - "All 5 team members at 12 months" = $100k paid
  - "Product milestone X hit by month 12" = $50k paid
```

## Founder Role: What to Negotiate

### Option A: Stay in Leadership (Best)
```
Title: VP Engineering / Head of Product
Salary: $180-250k (market rate for acquirer size)
Equity: New 4-year grant (100-200k if public, 0.1-0.3% if private)
Scope: Team management + product direction
Downside: Report to acquirer's execs, may fight constantly
```

### Option B: Graceful Exit (OK)
```
Title: Advisor to CTO
Salary: $0 (but signing bonus was $300k+)
Equity: Vesting normally, no new grants
Scope: Help with transition, leave at month 6
Upside: Freedom to start next thing
```

### Option C: Founder Continues (Rare, Most Acqui-Hires)
```
You disappear from product, help with integration
Stay 6-12 months, then move on
Ideal if you want to start something new
```

## Red Flags in Acqui-Hire Terms

| 🚩 Red Flag | Implication |
|---|---|
| "We'll define roles post-close" | Chaos, people reassigned randomly |
| "No retention bonus, only vesting" | High risk of gut-pulls |
| "You'll report to VP who doesn't know us" | Political disaster |
| "<30% cash at close" | Company wants to claw back via vesting |
| "Team integrated immediately" | People scattered, cohesion lost |
| "Double-trigger acceleration" | You have to perform for acquirer (hard) |
| "Earnout based on our goals, not team retention" | You get nothing if people leave |

---

## Checklist: Before Signing Acqui-Hire LOI

- [ ] Each team member has explicit new role written in LOI
- [ ] Signing bonus is 40%+, paid immediately
- [ ] Retention period is 24+ months (long enough to matter)
- [ ] New manager named and has met your team
- [ ] Earnout tied to team staying, not buyer's random metrics
- [ ] Equity new-grant addressed (not just vesting old equity)
- [ ] Clawback clause removed or minimal
- [ ] Founder role is explicit (leader, advisor, or out?)
- [ ] Integration plan shows team staying together
- [ ] You can escalate to CEO if integration goes wrong

---

## After Close: Your Job in Acqui-Hire

### Month 1: Honeymoon
- Team is excited, new salary, new office
- Check in with each person (morale, concerns)
- Help them integrate into acquirer's culture

### Months 2-6: Monitor & Escalate
- Watch for: reassignments, manager conflicts, boredom
- Escalate morale issues to CEO
- Track who's unhappy (people leave at month 4-6)

### Months 6-12: Plan Exit
- Either: commit to staying longer (re-negotiate if unhappy)
- Or: prepare to leave, document handoff

### Reality Check at Month 12
- If people are leaving → acqui-hire failed (not your fault)
- If team is strong, building → success, can stay or go
- If you're miserable → no value in staying

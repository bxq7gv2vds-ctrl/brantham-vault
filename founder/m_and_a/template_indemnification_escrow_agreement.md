# INDEMNIFICATION & ESCROW AGREEMENT

**This Escrow Agreement** is entered into as of [Date] by and between:
- **Buyer:** [Buyer Company Inc.]
- **Seller:** [Your Company Inc.]
- **Escrow Agent:** [Third-party escrow service, e.g., Wilmington Trust, Computershare]

---

## 1. PURPOSE & ESCROW AMOUNT

**Seller's Representations & Warranties** (as defined in Stock Purchase Agreement) are backed by an escrow holdback.

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| **Base Escrow Amount** | $[X]M ([Y]% of purchase price) |
| **Escrow Release Timing** | 50% at Month 12, 50% at Month 18 |
| **Escrow Agent Fees** | Paid 50/50 by Buyer and Seller |

---

## 2. INDEMNIFIABLE CLAIMS

**Seller indemnifies Buyer for losses caused by:**

- Breach of any Seller representation or warranty (from SPA Schedule [X])
- Breach of Seller's covenants or obligations
- Undisclosed liabilities or contingencies
- IP infringement (third-party claims)
- Employee/customer litigation
- Tax liabilities (if undisclosed)

**Excluded claims (Buyer bears these):**
- Changes in tax law post-close
- General market conditions (industry downturn)
- Buyer's operational decisions (pricing, hiring, strategy)
- Force majeure (pandemic, war, natural disaster)

---

## 3. CLAIM PROCESS

### (a) Notice of Claim
**Buyer** must provide **written notice** to Seller within **[30] days** of discovering a breach.

**Notice includes:**
- Specific representation/warranty alleged to be breached
- Detailed description of the breach
- Dollar amount of loss claimed
- Supporting documentation (customer churn data, financial impact, emails, etc.)

**Timing:** Claims must be noticed before [Month 18 post-close] (end of survival period)

### (b) Seller's Response
**Seller** has **[15] business days** to:
- Admit or deny the claim
- Provide evidence/documentation supporting their position
- Propose settlement amount

### (c) Dispute Resolution
If Buyer and Seller disagree on amount:
1. **Direct negotiation** ([15] days)
2. **Neutral accountant/arbiter** ([30] days)
   - Each side presents evidence
   - Arbiter determines amount within [5] days
   - Cost split 50/50
   - Determination binding

---

## 4. CALCULATION OF LOSS

**Loss = Seller's indemnifiable amount per claim**

**Loss includes:**
- Direct damages (e.g., customer refund of $[50]K)
- Lost revenue (e.g., customer churn → $[200]K lost ARR)
- Cost to remediate (e.g., legal fees for IP defense → $[30]K)
- Reasonable attorney/expert fees (up to [X]% of claim)

**Loss excludes:**
- Consequential damages (lost profits, punitive damages, reputational harm)
- Punitive or exemplary damages
- Damages for which Buyer has insurance coverage (or proceeds available)

---

## 5. BASKETS & CAPS

### (a) Basket (Threshold)
**Buyer cannot make claims unless aggregate losses exceed:**
- **Aggregate Basket:** $[50]K (1% of purchase price)
- **Per-Claim Basket:** $[10]K (minor claims below this are Buyer's responsibility)

*Once aggregate basket threshold is crossed, ALL losses (including those below $[10]K per claim) are covered.*

### (b) Cap (Ceiling)
**Total indemnification is capped at:**
- **General Cap:** $[X]M ([Y]% of purchase price), except:
- **IP Cap:** Uncapped (intellectual property is a critical asset)
- **Fraud Cap:** Uncapped (seller fraud has no limit)
- **Tax Cap:** $[Z]M (tax liabilities capped lower due to audit risk)

---

## 6. ESCROW RELEASE SCHEDULE

| Timing | Release Amount | Condition |
|---|---|---|
| **Month 12 post-close** | $[500]K (50% of escrow) | No material claims pending |
| **Month 18 post-close** | Remaining balance minus claim holdback | Final claims resolved |

**Holdback for pending claims:**
- If Buyer has notice of pending claim at Month 12, amount is held until resolution
- Unclaimed funds released to Seller regardless of potential future claims after Month 18

**Interest:**
- Escrow earns interest at [Federal Funds Rate or 0% per agreement]
- Interest distributed with principal upon release

---

## 7. INDEMNIFICATION MECHANICS

### (a) Escrow-First Approach (Standard)
1. **Buyer submits claim to escrow agent**
2. **Escrow agent holds funds pending resolution**
3. **If claim approved, escrow agent pays Buyer**
4. **Seller can dispute; goes to arbitration**
5. **Post-arbitration, escrow releases per determination**

### (b) Example Scenario

| Event | Action |
|---|---|
| Month 3: Customer churn discovered | Buyer notices claim ($100K) |
| Month 8: Arbitration complete, Buyer wins | Escrow releases $100K to Buyer |
| Month 12: No new claims; Seller receives $400K | Remaining $400K held for claims |
| Month 18: All claims resolved | Remaining balance released to Seller |

---

## 8. KEY PERSON INDEMNITY (Optional)

**If [key employee] departs within 12 months post-close without Buyer's permission:**
- Seller indemnifies Buyer for $[100]K (lost productivity, replacement cost)
- OR Escrow holds $[100]K until Month 18 if employee has not yet departed

---

## 9. MATERIALITY THRESHOLDS

**Certain breaches only trigger indemnification if:**
- Individual item >$[10]K, OR
- Aggregate items >$[50]K

**Example:** If Seller failed to disclose a $[5]K vendor contract, it doesn't trigger a claim (below threshold). But if Seller failed to disclose three $[20]K contracts (aggregate $[60]K), all three are covered.

---

## 10. EXCLUSIVE REMEDY

**Indemnification via escrow is Buyer's exclusive remedy for breach of representations/warranties** (except for fraud or willful breach, which have no cap).

This means:
- Buyer cannot sue Seller directly post-close
- Buyer must work through escrow claim process
- Buyer cannot claim damages beyond escrow balance
- Buyer cannot pursue personal assets of Seller

*Rationale: Keeps litigation out of court; escrow amount is the agreed-upon maximum liability.*

---

## 11. ESCROW AGENT DUTIES

**Escrow Agent [Name] agrees to:**

1. **Hold funds in trust** — no interest or claims by either party until release authorized
2. **Maintain records** — log all claims, notices, communications
3. **Disburse per instructions** — release funds only upon written joint authorization from Buyer and Seller, or per arbitration determination
4. **Provide statements** — monthly account statements to both parties
5. **Hold for [18] months** — then release per settlement or arbitration

**Escrow Agent is neutral** — does not take sides on disputes; merely executes authorized releases.

**Escrow Agent fees:**
- $[5]K annual administration fee (split 50/50)
- $[1]K per claim processed (paid by claiming party or split if disputed)

---

## 12. DISPUTE RESOLUTION FOR ESCROW CLAIMS

**Step 1 — Direct Negotiation ([15] days)**
Buyer and Seller meet to discuss claim; attempt settlement.

**Step 2 — Neutral Arbitration ([30] days)**
If no settlement, each side submits:
- 2-page brief (facts, legal arguments)
- Supporting documents (maximum 50 pages)

**Neutral arbitrator** (selected from [AAA panel / Big 4 accountants / mutual agreement]):
- Reviews submissions
- Determines loss amount within [5] days
- Amount is binding; no appeal

**Costs:** Each side bears 50% of arbitrator fees (~$[2–5]K per claim)

---

## 13. TAX TREATMENT OF ESCROW

**For tax purposes:**
- Escrow funds held in trust; not taxable to Seller until released
- Claim payments to Buyer are deductible (damage compensation)
- Earnout payments (if separate) are seller's income (ordinary or capital gain)

*Seller should consult tax counsel on escrow accounting.*

---

## 14. TERMINATION & FINAL RELEASE

**At Month 18 post-close:**
- All claim periods expire
- Remaining escrow (minus pending claims) released to Seller via wire
- Escrow account closed

**After Month 18:**
- No new claims can be filed
- Seller has recourse only for fraud (separately litigated)

# Exit Timeline Roadmap — 18 Months Pre-Sale Execution

**Objectif :** Planification orchestrée de 18 mois, segmentée par trimestre et fonction.

---

## Timeline Overview (Mois -18 to -1)

```
PHASE 1: FOUNDATION (M-18 to M-12)  — Prepare business for sale
PHASE 2: OPTIMIZATION (M-12 to M-6)  — Maximize valuation metrics
PHASE 3: ACCELERATION (M-6 to M-0)   — Run parallel buyer conversations
PHASE 4: CLOSE (M-0 to M+6)          — LOI → Due Diligence → Close
```

---

## PHASE 1: Foundation (Months -18 to -12)

### Quarter 1 (M-18 to M-15): Audit & Plan

**Finance**
- [ ] **Financial audit** (external auditor, $20-40k)
  - FY-2, FY-1 financials reviewed
  - GAAP compliance check
  - Red flags documented (so buyer doesn't discover them)

- [ ] **SaaS metrics review**
  - ARR, NRR, churn calculation standardized
  - 36-month dashboard built
  - Reconciliation: ARR = actual customer contracts (no fudging)

- [ ] **Tax planning consult** ($5-10k lawyer)
  - Stock sale vs asset sale implications
  - Founder personal tax impact
  - State jurisdiction analysis

**Operations**
- [ ] **Customer concentration analysis**
  - Top 10 customers = [X]% of ARR
  - If >40%, plan diversification

- [ ] **Customer health audit**
  - NPS survey
  - Contract renewal timeline
  - Any at-risk customers identified?

- [ ] **IP audit**
  - Patents filed? (TM, copyright)
  - Open-source license compliance scan
  - Any disputed IP?

**Team**
- [ ] **Org chart review**
  - Any employment disputes pending?
  - Non-competes in place for key employees?
  - Equity vesting structure clear?

---

### Quarter 2 (M-15 to M-12): Fix Critical Issues

**Fix High-Priority Items Found in Q1 Audit:**

- [ ] **If customer concentration too high:** Launch new product line or vertical to diversify revenue
- [ ] **If NPS < 50:** Customer success overhaul (hire, train, process)
- [ ] **If churn > 5%:** Root cause analysis + mitigation
- [ ] **If key IP ambiguous:** Secure via assignment, patent, or trademark filing
- [ ] **If open-source risk:** Conduct compliance audit, remediate if needed
- [ ] **If team departures expected:** Negotiate retention with key employees (20% equity bonus? Stock acceleration?)

---

## PHASE 2: Optimization (Months -12 to -6)

### Quarter 3 (M-12 to M-9): Metrics Improvement

**Goal: Maximize valuation multiples**

**Revenue**
- [ ] **ARR growth:** Target +20-30% YoY (if you're not there, accelerate GTM)
- [ ] **NRR improvement:** Target 100%+ (upsell/expansion focus)
- [ ] **New customer acquisition:** Establish predictable sales funnel

**Profitability**
- [ ] **Gross margin optimization**
  - If <70%: reduce COGS (automation, vendor negotiation)
  - Target: 75%+ for valuation appeal

- [ ] **Rule of 40 score**
  - ARR growth % + gross margin % = target ≥ 40
  - If <40: need to either grow faster OR improve margin

**Customer Quality**
- [ ] **Reduce concentration risk**
  - No single customer >10% of ARR (ideal: <5%)
  - Diversification plan executed

- [ ] **Increase NRR via expansion**
  - Upsell/cross-sell program launched
  - Target: +15-20% expansion revenue this quarter

---

### Quarter 4 (M-9 to M-6): Buyer Mapping & Soft Outreach

**Strategic Planning**
- [ ] **Buyer map created**
  - 5-10 strategic buyers identified
  - 2-3 PE firms researched
  - Likely multiples estimated

- [ ] **Banker engagement** (if using one)
  - M&A advisor retained (top-tier firms like Evercore, Goldman, or boutique specialized)
  - Banker fee: typically 0.75-1.5% of deal value
  - Banker timeline: Q4 (soft intro), Q1 (formal process)

- [ ] **Soft outreach to 2-3 strategic buyers**
  - Warm intro via mutual contact (not cold pitch)
  - Message: "Exploring strategic opportunities; would love to connect"
  - Goal: Get on their radar (no formal process yet)

- [ ] **Board/investor alignment**
  - If you have board, get buy-in on exit strategy
  - Investor consent for M&A process required?
  - Liquidation preference implications understood?

---

## PHASE 3: Acceleration (Months -6 to -1)

### Quarter 5 (M-6 to M-3): Formal Process Launch

**Sales Process**
- [ ] **Teaser deck created**
  - 1-2 slide overview (company, growth, customers, valuation range)
  - NDAs signed with buyers

- [ ] **Data room prepared**
  - Folder structure: Financials, Legal, Contracts, IP, Org, Customer
  - ~50-100 documents uploaded
  - Version control (dated files, no confusion)

- [ ] **Buyer outreach (formal)**
  - Banker/advisor sends "soft teasers" to 5-10 buyers
  - Response tracking (who is interested?)
  - Schedule management (don't overload yourself with meetings)

- [ ] **Management presentation prep**
  - 20-30 min executive summary
  - Deep-dive slides (financials, unit econ, roadmap)
  - You + CFO/CTO practice pitch

---

### Quarter 6 (M-3 to M-0): Closing Phase

**LOI Negotiation**
- [ ] **Top 2-3 buyers give indications** (non-binding bids)
  - Typical: "We'd value this at $[X]M"
  - Track each buyer's offer & terms

- [ ] **LOI negotiation** (4-6 weeks)
  - Price, earnout %, escrow hold, survival periods, reps & warranties
  - Lawyer review (outside counsel $10-30k)

- [ ] **LOI signature** (M-1, roughly)

**Due Diligence Prep**
- [ ] **Legal holds issued**
  - Email preservation (litigation risk)
  - No document destruction during deal

- [ ] **Final financial restatement**
  - Confirm accuracy of numbers shared with buyer
  - Reconcile any discrepancies

- [ ] **Customer notification prep**
  - Draft customer announcement (after close only)
  - Key customer intro calls scheduled (post-LOI, pre-close)

## Key Decision Gates

### Gate 1 (M-12): Ready to Sell?

**Checklist:**
- [ ] ARR ≥ $1M (or market shows interest <$1M)
- [ ] NRR ≥ 100% (or path to it clear)
- [ ] Churn ≤ 3%/month
- [ ] Rule of 40 ≥ 30 (acceptable for buyer)
- [ ] No major IP/legal issues outstanding
- [ ] Team stable (no key departures expected)

**Decision:** PROCEED to Phase 2, or delay 6-12 months?

---

### Gate 2 (M-6): Pull the Trigger?

**Checklist:**
- [ ] 3+ buyers expressing interest
- [ ] Banker believes valuation achievable
- [ ] Board/investors aligned on exit timeline
- [ ] Founder ready to sell (personal readiness!)
- [ ] No major negative news in pipeline (company health OK)

**Decision:** LAUNCH formal process (M-3), or reconsider?

---

### Gate 3 (M-2): Accept Best Offer?

**Checklist:**
- [ ] 2+ LOI offers received
- [ ] Best offer = fair valuation (matches your expectations or exceeds)
- [ ] Buyer = good cultural fit
- [ ] Earnout terms acceptable
- [ ] You're comfortable with buyer team

**Decision:** SIGN LOI (proceed to close), or walk away?

---

## Red Flags: Pause or Reconsider

🚩 **Rule of 40 < 25** → Buyer will lowball; improve metrics first
🚩 **Churn rising** (>3%/mo) → Suggests deeper issue; address before sale
🚩 **Key customer announced churn** → Delay sale 6 months, stabilize
🚩 **No buyers interested after soft outreach** → Maybe not ready; revisit M-12
🚩 **Founder ambivalence** → Don't force sale if unsure; costs are real (earnout, lockup, culture)

---

## Post-Close Transition (M+0 to M+6)

- **M+0 to M+2:** Integration planning (founder + buyer ops)
- **M+2 to M+6:** Earnout monitoring, customer retention focus
- **M+6:** Founder decides next role (stay on, advisor, leave?)

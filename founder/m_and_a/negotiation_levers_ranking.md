# Negotiation Levers — Ranking by Impact

**Levier fort = utilisez tôt. Levier faible = discardez ou utilisez en dernier resort.**

---

## TIER 1 : NUCLEAR (Utilisez une fois max, très tôt)

### Lever 1A : "Multiple Buyers Competing"

**Tactique :** Vous avez 2 sérieux buyers en même temps. Buyer A sait que B existe.

```
Buyer A: "Valuation 3x ARR?"
You: "Buyer B offers 4x, mais votre synergy story est meilleure.
      Voulez-vous matcher?"
```

**Impact :** +20-40% valuation si crédible
**Cost :** Brûlez B (abandonnez B ou B se venge)
**Timing :** LOI negotiation ou plus tôt
**Red flag :** Si vous feignez avoir 2 buyers → buyer découvre mensonge, deal dies

### Lever 1B : "Walk Away (Credibly)"

**Tactique :** Vous avez une autre option (job offer, another buyer, or "actually not selling")

```
Buyer: "3x ARR, c'est notre max."
You: "OK, merci. We'll wait for next buyer or pass."
[Close laptop, stand up, leave.]
```

**Impact :** Massive (buyer réalise vous ne désespéré pas)
**Cost :** Deal dies (peut-être)
**Timing :** LOI negotiation ou earnout terms
**Red flag :** Fake walk = career killer (buyer never trusts you again)

### Lever 1C : "Customer Retention Guarantee"

**Tactique :** Top 3 clients commettent "We'll stay 3 years if deal closes"

```
You: "My 3 biggest customers (40% revenue) have signed letters
     saying they'll stay 3 years post-close.
     That's your downside protection."
```

**Impact :** Eliminates customer concentration risk, +15-25% valuation
**Cost :** Customer letters are hard to get (clients don't like being named)
**Timing :** LOI ou DD phase
**Red flag :** Letter avec exit clause = worthless

## TIER 2 : STRONG (Utilisez 2-3x pendant negotiation)

### Lever 2A : "Synergy Quantification"

**Tactique :** Articulate exactement combien votre deal vaut à buyer.

```
You: "Conservative estimate:
     - Cross-sell to 30% of your customer base = $X/year
     - Platform efficiency = $Y/year
     - Talent retention = $Z/year
     Total = $X+Y+Z. We'll take 40% of Year 1 in earnout."
```

**Impact :** Justifie une valuation plus haute, earnout structure fairer
**Cost :** Buyer challenges votre math, must defend
**Timing :** Early LOI
**Red flag :** Synergies que buyer can't control (vous contrôlez) = discount

### Lever 2B : "Earnout Structure Favorable"

**Tactique :** Earnout based on YOUR control, not buyer's whims.

```
Bad: "Earnout if revenue reaches $X"
     (Buyer can starve product, earnout dies)

Good: "Earnout if revenue $X AND we hit these 3 OKRs
       (which you control: hires, marketing, pricing)"
```

**Impact :** Earnout becomes more likely → higher effective valuation
**Cost :** Buyer may reject (prefer traditional revenue earnout)
**Timing :** LOI phase
**Red flag :** Earnout targets buyer won't commit to = impossible

### Lever 2C : "Tax Optimization"

**Tactique :** Structure deal to minimize tax hit (e.g., stock vs. cash, 338(h)(10) election)

```
Your tax guy: "If buyer is strategic, ask for 50% stock
              (deferral = less tax immediately)"
```

**Impact :** +5-15% effective valuation (tax savings)
**Cost :** Requires sophisticated tax planning
**Timing :** LOI or later (tax detail)
**Red flag :** Buyer won't commit to specific allocation

### Lever 2D : "Team Retention Bonus"

**Tactique :** Build retention into deal terms (equity vesting, bonus for staying)

```
You: "My CTO must stay 2 years. I'll commit him if he gets:
     - $500k signing bonus
     - Equity vest on schedule
     - No surprise changes to his role in first 6 months"
```

**Impact :** Removes key person risk, valuation +10%
**Cost :** Reduces your cash/earnout
**Timing :** Before LOI, during data room
**Red flag :** Team says no = non-negotiable

## TIER 3 : MEDIUM (Utilisez 3-5x, more flexible)

### Lever 3A : "Competitive Threat"

**Tactique :** Buyer realizes they should acquire you now vs. lose to competitor.

```
You: "Company X in the space just raised Series B.
     If you don't move now, they'll be 2x bigger next year.
     Acquisition window closes."
```

**Impact :** +10-20% valuation if credible
**Cost :** Competitor must be real threat
**Timing :** Early prospection
**Red flag :** Buyer thinks you're overplaying threat = credibility loss

### Lever 3B : "Founder Key Person Risk"

**Tactique :** "If I leave, product dies" (only if true).

```
You: "Our platform is built on [X proprietary tech]
     that lives in my head. I commit to staying
     2 years, knowledge transfer + mentorship."
```

**Impact :** +5-10% valuation
**Cost :** You must stay, no quick exit
**Timing :** LOI
**Red flag :** Buyer does tech audit, finds your tech is standard = risk gone

### Lever 3C : "Process Efficiency"

**Tactique :** Speed is your lever (you move fast, buyer doesn't).

```
You: "We're closing by [date], whoever moves fastest gets it.
     Next buyer gets access [tomorrow]."
```

**Impact :** +5-10% valuation if buyer fears FOMO
**Cost :** Creates adversarial mood
**Timing :** Prospection → LOI
**Red flag :** Buyer calls your bluff, you have no other buyer = loss

### Lever 3D : "Product Roadmap Optionality"

**Tactique :** "Our roadmap gives you options buyer didn't have before"

```
You: "We have 6-month roadmap with [X feature] that
     opens up [new market/segment] for you.
     Acquisition gives you that 6 months faster."
```

**Impact :** +10-15% valuation if roadmap valuable
**Cost :** Must deliver roadmap post-close
**Timing :** LOI or management meetings
**Red flag :** Roadmap overpromised = earnout failure

## TIER 4 : WEAK (Avoid, or use as throwaway)

### Lever 4A : "Market Timing"

**Tactique :** "Market cycle is good right now"

```
You: "Q3 2026 is hot for SaaS M&A, multiples are 5-7x.
     Next cycle might be 3-4x. Buy now."
```

**Impact :** +0-5% (usually ignored)
**Cost :** None, but sounds weak
**Timing :** Never, too generic
**Red flag :** Buyer thinks you're desperate

### Lever 4B : "Customer Testimonials"

**Tactique :** "Customers love us"

```
You: "NPS 70, retention 95%. Customers will tell you we're great."
```

**Impact :** +0-5% (table stakes, not a lever)
**Cost :** None
**Timing :** Teaser, not negotiation
**Red flag :** If NPS/retention aren't exceptional → backfires

### Lever 4C : "Founder Reputation"

**Tactique :** "I'm a known founder, asset to your team"

```
You: "I've been featured in [Publication], speak at conferences.
     I can accelerate your go-to-market."
```

**Impact :** +0-5% (unless you're actually famous)
**Cost :** Sounds like ego
**Timing :** Never in negotiation
**Red flag :** Buyer thinks you'll be problem child post-close

### Lever 4D : "Tax Loss Carryforwards / Accounting Quirks"

**Tactique :** "Deal structure lets you save on taxes"

```
You: "Our losses can be carried forward, structure optimizes for you."
```

**Impact :** +2-5% (depends on buyer's tax situation)
**Cost :** Requires tax lawyer, complex
**Timing :** LOI or later
**Red flag :** If buyer's tax guy doesn't agree → non-starter

## Sequencing: When to Use Each Lever

```
PHASE           LEVER STRATEGY
═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Prospection     - Weak levers only (customer testimonials, market)
                - Start building 1A (multiple buyers) quietly

Teaser/CIM      - Lever 3A (competitive threat)
                - Lever 3D (product roadmap)

First meetings  - Lever 2A (quantified synergies)
                - Lever 3A (competitive threat) if appropriate

LOI negotiation - Lever 1A (multiple buyers) if have
                - Lever 1B (walk away) if credible
                - Lever 2B (earnout structure)
                - Lever 2C (tax optimization)
                - Lever 2D (team retention)

Earnout terms   - Lever 1C (customer retention guarantee)
                - Lever 2B (earnout OKR structure)
                - Lever 3B (key person commitment)

Red flags       - Lever 1B (walk away) if deal goes south
```

## Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why it fails | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| **Using all levers at once** | Buyer thinks you're desperate | Use 1-2 strong, hold back rest |
| **Weak lever in LOI** | Buyer ignores it, wastes credibility | Save weak levers for throwaway |
| **Fake multiple buyers** | Buyer discovers bluff, deal dies | Only use if 100% real |
| **Demanding earnout you can't hit** | Earnout dies, relationship sours | Be realistic on targets |
| **Holding back walk-away leverage** | Buyer senses you can't leave, lowballs | Signal credibly you can walk (early) |
| **Synergy math you can't defend** | Buyer's team rips it apart, trust lost | Have CFO / advisor vet first |

## Nuclear Option: The "No Deal" Close

If buyer's offer is insulting and won't budge:

```
You: "Thanks for the offer. We're going to pass.
      [Reason: valuation gap / earnout impossible / timeline broken]
      
      Here's what would make us say yes:
      - [Specific % higher valuation]
      - [Specific earnout structure]
      - [Specific retention commitment]
      
      If you want to talk, my door is open."
```

**Result :** 40% chance buyer improves offer (realizes you're serious). 60% deal dies (but you kept integrity).

**Better than :** Accepting bad deal you'll regret for 3 years.

# 100-Day Integration Brief — Post-Close Roadmap

Fill this BEFORE signing to align buyer expectations. Update daily first 30 days.

---

## Day 1: Go-Live Prep (1 week before close)

### Communications (All hands must be scheduled)
- [ ] **Founder/CEO all-hands** (sold company + what's next)
- [ ] **Customer announcement email** (CEO co-signed)
- [ ] **Investor/board update** (if applicable)
- [ ] **Team 1:1s scheduled** (all reports, by day 3)

### Legal/Admin (Done during closing week)
- [ ] Email migrations (if consolidating)
- [ ] VPN/SSO access (buyer's directory)
- [ ] Slack/Teams invite to buyer workspace
- [ ] Benefits transfer (healthcare, 401k handoff)
- [ ] Org chart update (who reports to whom)

### Technical (Dry-run, no cutover yet)
- [ ] System access lists created (API keys, database, infra)
- [ ] Integration checklist (CRM, analytics, data warehouse)
- [ ] Backup strategy confirmed (if moving to buyer's infra)
- [ ] Downtime window agreed (if any cutover needed)

### What Happens Day 0 Morning
```
Timeline (assume 10am ET close):

10:00  Legal signing ceremony + wire transfer confirmation
10:30  CEO sends announcement to team (pre-written)
11:00  Email: Buyers to all customers (trust + transition plan)
12:00  CEO town hall (45 min Q&A, recorded)
14:00  IT provisioning (VPN, email, tools)
16:00  Manager 1:1s start (retention reassurance)
Next day: Office/culture visit (if co-located) OR video calls
```

## Days 1-30: Stabilization Phase

### Week 1: Meet & Greet
- [ ] All-hands intro (buyer CEO + integration lead)
- [ ] Product integration call (product team side-by-side)
- [ ] Engineering kickoff (architecture review, tech stack alignment)
- [ ] Sales/customer success handover (no policy changes yet)
- [ ] Finance: Consolidate P&L, sync reporting

### Week 2-3: Deep Dives (Functional Leads)
- [ ] **Product**: Feature roadmap merge (buyer priorities + yours)
  - Decision: Keep separate product? Merge? Integrate over time?
  - First decision: UI/UX changes? (minimize for 60 days)
- [ ] **Engineering**: Code review standards, deployment process
  - Decision: Keep separate repos? Merge repos? Shared infra?
  - First call: Any critical tech debt buyer needs to know?
- [ ] **Sales/Success**: Customer transition plan
  - Decision: Keep separate sales motions or cross-sell?
  - Action: List top 20 customers, assign joint success plans
- [ ] **People/Culture**: Org structure, retention bonuses, role clarity
  - Decision: Consolidate teams? Parallel teams? Cross-functional?

### Week 4: Quick Wins
- [ ] Ship 1-2 small product improvements (morale)
- [ ] Close 1 customer "stuck in transition" (show progress)
- [ ] Announce 1 hiring plan ("we're investing in your growth")

### Key Metrics (Week 4 checkpoint)
| Metric | Target | Action if miss |
|--------|--------|---|
| Attrition | 0% (engineering), <5% (others) | Retention calls start |
| Customer NPS trend | Stable or +5 | Issue post-close comms |
| Revenue run-rate | On plan | Pricing/contract review |
| **Morale** (1-10) | >7 (team survey) | CEO listening tour |

---

## Days 30-60: Integration Phase

### Month 2: Execute Roadmap
- [ ] **Product**: First merged feature launched
  - Assign cross-functional team (3-4 people)
  - Target: Demo to both CEOs day 45
- [ ] **Engineering**: Infra consolidation (if planned)
  - Parallel run first 2 weeks (safety net)
  - Cutover plan written + dry-run (day 50)
- [ ] **Sales**: 
  - Cross-sell trial launched (3-5 customer pilots)
  - Pricing alignment decision (keep separate or merge?)
- [ ] **Operations**: Cost consolidation begins
  - Tool audit (kill redundant software)
  - Lease/office decisions (by day 50)

### Retention Critical Path
- [ ] All key people have: new title, reporting line, 1-year vesting bonus
- [ ] Public commitments (announce promotion, new opportunity)
- [ ] Skip-level lunches (buyer exec meets teams, not just leads)

### Customer NPS / Churn Watch
```
Red flags:
  - Any customer threatening to leave (call personally)
  - NPS drop >10 points (survey + root cause)
  - Support ticket volume spike (integration issues)
  
Action:
  - List "at-risk" customers (>50% churn risk) by day 35
  - CEO + buyer CEO personal calls to top 10 by day 40
```

## Days 60-90: Stabilization II

### Month 3: Realize Synergies
- [ ] **Product**: Merged roadmap locked (next 6 months)
- [ ] **Engineering**: Full integration complete (one codebase or clear seams)
- [ ] **Sales**: First 3 cross-sell wins (revenue recognized Q2/Q3)
- [ ] **Finance**: Full P&L integration, consolidated reporting

### Team Confidence Checks
- [ ] Pulse survey (anonymized): "Are you staying? Why/why not?"
- [ ] Promotion announcements (if planned, do by day 75)
- [ ] New hire kickoff (show buyer is investing)

### Buyer Board Prep
By day 90, your buyer needs to show:
- ✅ No attrition (or very minimal)
- ✅ Revenue on track
- ✅ First synergy wins visible
- ✅ Roadmap merged + next product launch locked

## The Integration Risk Matrix

| Risk | Probability | Mitigation | Owner |
|------|-------------|-----------|-------|
| Key engineer leaves | Medium | Retention bonus + new title | CTO + buyer CTO |
| Customer churn spike | Medium | Proactive outreach + stability comms | VP Sales |
| Product roadmap conflict | High | Decide Week 1 (not week 8) | CEO + buyer Product |
| Tech stack mismatch | Low | Engineering kickoff flags early | CTO |
| Culture clash | Low-Medium | Transparent communication, skip-level meetings | CEO + HR |
| Acquisition financing delays | Very Low | Pre-signed with clear close date | CFO |

**Escalation**: Any "Medium+" risk → CEO conversation by day 5.

## Red Flags = Escalate Immediately

⚠️ **If you see these, call buyer CEO that day:**

- Key engineer puts in notice (why? compensation? cultural?)
- Customer with >10% of revenue goes dark
- Product roadmap conflict (team sides forming)
- "We think we made a mistake" chatter (address morale)
- Finance numbers don't reconcile (audit issue?)

## Quick Wins (Small Visible Successes, Days 1-30)

Pick 2-3 of these to launch in first 30 days:

1. **Product**: Bugs from buyer's roadmap fixed (shows responsiveness)
2. **Sales**: First cross-sell close (validates synergy)
3. **Engineering**: Performance improvement visible (e.g., API latency -20%)
4. **Team**: Promotion or new hire announced (shows investment)
5. **Customer**: Pain point solved together (team collaboration win)

**Timing**: Announce day 20, deliver day 25-30. Morale matters.

## Quick Reference: Integration Decision Tree

**Product integration?**
- Keep separate 12+ months → lower risk, slower synergy
- Merge in 90 days → faster synergy, integration debt
→ Decide week 1 with buyer product lead

**Engineering?**
- Separate repos, shared infra → good default
- Merge repos → only if tech stacks align
→ Decide week 1 with buyer CTO

**Sales?**
- Keep motions separate → lower risk
- Cross-sell immediately → faster revenue
→ Try cross-sell pilots day 30, full push day 60

**Operations?**
- Keep separate → lower risk
- Consolidate tools → immediate cost savings
→ Audit day 5, consolidate by day 60

**Use case**: New integration lead arrives day 1 → print this, execute this.

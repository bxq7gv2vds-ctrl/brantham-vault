# Valuation Defense Playbook — How to Win Price Negotiations

Use this in LOI discussions when buyer says your price is too high.

---

## The Setup: Price Anchoring

### Your Opening Anchor (Week 1-2 conversations)
**Don't put a number in email.** Let them ask.

When they ask "What's your valuation expectation?":

```
"We're not shopping, but we've modeled this a few ways:
- DCF with base case assumptions: $X to $Y range
- Comparable multiples (median SaaS: 4-6x ARR): $A to $B range
- Strategic value (synergies + IP): $C to $D range

We're open, but we need to be in a range that reflects our position.
What's your initial thinking?"
```

**Why this works:**
- You anchor 3 different methods (they can't say "your math is wrong")
- You don't say "final number" (leaves room to negotiate)
- You ask first (control the framing)

---

## The Defense: Arguments by Buyer Objection

### Objection 1: "Your multiple is too high"
**They said:** "4x ARR is crazy for [your company]"

**Your response (pick strongest):**

1. **Growth argument**
```
"We're not 4x of a mature 10% growth SaaS. We're:
  - 40%+ YoY growth (not 10%)
  - 95%+ NRR (expansion, not churn)
  - [Name customer segment] is hot right now ($XB TAM)
  
Comp analysis: [Similar company] closed at 5.2x with slower growth.
We should be 4.5-5x given our trajectory."
```

2. **Retention argument**
```
"Our magic number is [X] (ideal SaaS = >0.75).
Our customer lifetime = [Y] years (comps = [Z] years).
That durability justifies higher multiple."
```

3. **Synergy argument**
```
"For YOU, there's [specific synergy]:
  - [Your customers] can upsell to [their product] = $2-3M net-new ARR
  - [Your tech] solves [their problem] = $1M operational savings
  
Fair value = our standalone + 50% of synergy = $[X]M"
```

### Objection 2: "Your growth isn't sustainable"
**They said:** "You peaked; growth will decelerate"

**Your response:**
```
"Fair concern. Here's our evidence it's sustainable:
  - Trailing 3-month growth = 35% (consistent with 40% YoY)
  - Net expansion (NRR 102%) covers churn + adds new
  - Product roadmap adds [3 features] targeting [new vertical] = runway for 24+ months
  
If you're worried, suggest: earnout on [metric] hit in year 2.
That way we both win if growth continues."
```

### Objection 3: "You're losing money"
**They said:** "Negative EBITDA kills your valuation"**

**Your response:**
```
"Understood, but context:
  - We're in growth phase (investing in [sales/product/people])
  - Unit economics: CAC payback = [X] months (healthy)
  - Path to 20% EBITDA margin = [Y] revenue level (= $XM in 2 years)

Strip out growth investments:
  - Actual EBITDA margin (core ops) = 5-10%
  - DCF with 20% terminal margin = $[Z]M enterprise value
  
Comparable: [Name co] was burning $[A]M when acquired at [multiple]x"
```

### Objection 4: "Customer concentration is too high"
**They said:** "If [big customer] leaves, you're dead"**

**Your response:**
```
"True, but:
  - Top customer is [% ARR]. If they left, remaining business = $[X]M (still valuable)
  - They're multi-year contract + love us (NPS 85)
  - Even with 50% scenario (they leave), value = $[Y]M
  
Suggest: Earnout tied to Top 3 customer retention through year 1.
We're confident + incentivized to keep them happy."
```

### Objection 5: "Your TAM is too small"
**They said:** "SaaS TAM is saturated; limited upside"**

**Your response:**
```
"TAM for [our focus] = $[XB] (cite report).
We're at [% penetration] with <5% market share of addressable base.

But for you, TAM doesn't matter as much as:
  1. Unit economics are proven
  2. Churn is low (customers stick)
  3. Synergy opportunity (your TAM is $YB, we can cross-sell)

We're not a 10x unicorn, but a stable cash-generative asset."
```

## The Offense: Arguments That Increase Price

### Move 1: Emphasize Earnout as Upside
```
"We see mutual interest in price. What if we structure it as:
  - Cash at close: $[X]M (covers our downside)
  - Earnout: $[Y]M over 2 years (covers your uncertainty)
  
If we hit targets, you pay the earnout. If we overperform, we celebrate together.
Best of both worlds."
```

**Why:** Buyer feels less risk, you get closer to asking price.

### Move 2: Highlight Alternative Buyers
```
"We're talking to [name generic type of buyer: strategic player, PE firm].
Each has different synergies, but your [specific synergy] is our #1 reason 
to do this with you. So let's find a price that works for both."
```

**Why:** Creates urgency without burning bridges.

### Move 3: Reframe "Cost of Delay"
```
"Every month we don't close:
  - We're capturing [X]% more ARR (organic growth)
  - Your synergy window gets [smaller / bigger] with [market condition]
  
At our current growth, in 12 months we'd be worth 30% more.
Might make sense to do this now?"
```

**Why:** Flips frame from "you're overpriced" to "we're undervalued."

### Move 4: Strategic Value Calculation
```
"If you integrate us:
  - Cross-sell revenue capture: $[2-3]M in year 2-3
  - Engineering reuse = [X]% cost savings
  - Customer wallet share increase = [Y]%

Fair allocation: You get 50% of synergy value.
That's worth $[Z]M premium over standalone valuation."
```

**Why:** Moves conversation from "your business value" to "our joint value."

## Price Negotiation Script (Real Conversation)

### Buyer's first offer (usually 20-30% below your ask)

**You:** "Thanks for the offer. Can you walk me through your math?"

**Buyer:** "We see $[X]M revenue, but 40% growth might slow. We'd model $[Y]M exit value."

**You:** "Fair. I respect that. A few things:
  1. Our trailing 3-month growth is [Z]% — actually up from last year
  2. NRR [>100] means we're expanding into existing customers
  3. Comps: [Company A] at [growth rate] sold at [multiple]x

So I think [multiple] is low. What if we split the difference at [higher multiple]?"

**Buyer:** "Let me talk to the team. We'll come back."

---

## The Flinch

**If buyer's offer is surprisingly low:**

```
You: "Wow. [Pause 5 seconds]. Can I be honest? 
     That's [X]% below where I thought we'd land. 
     Walk me through your thinking?"

[Listen. Don't negotiate yet.]

Then: "I need to think about this. Can you come up [Y amount]? 
      If yes, I'm interested in moving forward."
```

**Why flinch:** Anchors them that their offer was too low. Forces them to justify.

---

## Red Flags: When to Walk

❌ **Walk away if buyer says:**
- "You're overvalued. No one will pay this."
  → Find next buyer. This person isn't serious.
- "We'll lowball you now, improve terms post-close."
  → Bait-and-switch. Walk.
- "We need to cut price to hit Board approval" (AFTER shaking hands)
  → Negotiating in bad faith. Walk.
- "We'll do earnout only; zero cash at close"
  → Too much risk transferred to you. Walk.

✅ **Stay engaged if:**
- "We love it but need to model conservatively"
  → Rational buyer, deal works
- "Can you hit these metrics, and we'll close at price X?"
  → Earnout conversation, normal
- "We want a discount for [specific risk]. Can we de-risk it?"
  → Legitimate concern, solvable

## The Close: How to Say Yes

**Buyer says:** "OK, we'll do $[X]M at LOI. Cash at close, earnout structure TBD."

**You say:** "Great. I'm comfortable at that valuation. Let's lock the key terms:
  - Cash at close: $[X]M
  - Earnout: [Y months], tied to [metric], [% of base]
  - Cap on earnout: [Z]
  - Founder role: [description]
  
If those are right, I'm ready to sign LOI next week."

**Why:** You're making the buyer's deal, not just accepting. You retain agency.

---

## Quick Reference: Price Defense Framework

| Buyer Says | Your Counter | Tone |
|------------|--------------|------|
| "Too high" | Show comps at your multiple | Confident, not defensive |
| "Growth slowing" | Show trailing growth + NRR | Data-driven |
| "Profitability matters" | Show path to 20% EBITDA | Optimistic but realistic |
| "Concentration risk" | Show earnout, customer stickiness | Problem-solving |
| "We'll wait for better price" | Show cost of delay | Urgent but not panicked |

## The Playbook, Compressed (One Page for Your Board)

**My ask:** $[X]M cash at close
**Walk-away:** $[Y]M (includes earnout)
**Earnout:** 2-year $[Z]M tied to [metric] + cap

**If they offer <$[X]:**
1. Show comps (we're not outlier)
2. Show synergies (worth $[A] to them)
3. Offer earnout (de-risk their position)
4. If <$[Y] even with earnout, walk

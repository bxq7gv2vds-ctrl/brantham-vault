# Narrative Building for SaaS M&A

## Why Narrative Matters

A buyer doesn't just buy financials—they buy **growth story + defensibility + team**.  
Your narrative is the 1-pager or pitch deck they'll use to pitch *their* board.

---

## Core Narrative Framework (1 Page)

### 1. Market Hook (1 sentence)
- Problem: [Market size + why urgent]
- Example: "SMB manufacturing wastes $40B/year on manual inventory" (TAM=real, not "we're in a $1T market")

### 2. Why You (2-3 sentences)
- What's your unfair advantage?
  - Founder expertise + customer locks (switching costs)
  - Proprietary data / network effects
  - IP / patent fortress
- *Avoid:* "best team" (everyone claims this) → be specific: "only team to solve X"

### 3. Traction Proof (3 bullets)
- **Growth:** 120% YoY, $500k ARR, 45% MoM (pick strongest)
- **Defensibility:** Net Retention 110%, avg contract length 24m, churn <5%
- **Market fit:** NPS 65+, repeat purchase rate 80%

### 4. Category Position (1-2 bullets)
- Where you sit vs. alternatives
- *Not:* "we're unique" → *Yes:* "only vendor with API-first + 0-config setup"

### 5. Why Now (1 bullet)
- Market tailwind: e.g., "API-first movement", "shift-left security", "remote work"
- Timing must feel inevitable, not lucky

### 6. Buyer Benefit (1-2 sentences)
- **If strategic buyer:** What synergies? Revenue cross-sell? Cost reduction?
- **If PE buyer:** What's the playbook? Add sales team + CAC reduction?
- *Tailor this per buyer type*

---

## Narrative Variations by Buyer Type

### For Strategic Buyer (e.g., Salesforce, HubSpot)
```
"They have 50M+ SMBs, we have 2,000 customers converting at 3x their rate.
Bundled, this is $200M+ new revenue stream + upsell to existing base of $X."
```

### For PE Buyer
```
"$500k ARR, 50% margins, stable churn.
Add 3-person sales team, we hit $2M ARR in 24mo.
Exit in 4 years at $15M+ (6x EBITDA)."
```

### For Venture/Growth Buyer
```
"Markets moving 10x in our favor. With capital, we can outrun competitors.
$1B market, we're capturing 1%, path to 10% is clear."
```

---

## Common Narrative Mistakes (And Fixes)

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Fix |
|-----------|--------|
| "We're the Uber of X" | "We're gaining $X revenue/month in category worth $YB" |
| "Huge market opportunity" | "$500M TAM we can realistically reach in 5 years" |
| "Best-in-class product" | "Customers churn to us at 5x rate vs. competitor Y" |
| "Well-capitalized team" | "Founder has 10 years in this exact vertical + 3 exits" |
| "Lots of inbound interest" | "3 strategic buyers + 2 PE firms in active LOI talks" |

## Stress-Testing Your Narrative

Before soft shop, ask yourself:

1. **If I were the buyer, would I believe this?**
   - Or does it sound like founder hype?

2. **What's the #1 objection?**
   - "Why should I build vs. buy?" → Answer it in narrative
   - "Commodity market?" → Prove defensibility

3. **What does this buyer actually care about?**
   - Strategic = cross-sell + customer synergy
   - PE = margin + leverage
   - Venture = growth + market share
   - **Tailor narrative accordingly**

4. **Can I prove every claim in 5 minutes?**
   - ARR: show Stripe dashboard
   - Churn: show MRR cohorts
   - NPS: show raw survey data

## When to Update Your Narrative

- [ ] Before each new buyer conversation
- [ ] After major customer win
- [ ] After product milestone
- [ ] After market event (competitor IPO, layoffs signal)
- [ ] Before LOI discussion (add urgency if needed)

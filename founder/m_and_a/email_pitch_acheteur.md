# Email Pitch Acheteur — Scripts adaptés

## **VERSION 1 : Approche directe (CEO-to-CEO)**

**Sujet :** Strategic acquisition opportunity — [Company] + [Buyer]

---

```
Hi [CEO Name],

I'm reaching out because [BUYER] and [YOUR COMPANY] solve similar problems 
for [TARGET MARKET], and I think there's a significant acquisition opportunity 
that could benefit both organizations.

[YOUR COMPANY] has built:
  • [Key differentiator 1] — achieving [proof point, e.g., "3% churn vs 7% market avg"]
  • [Key differentiator 2] — serving [segment with proof]
  • [Key metric] — €[X] ARR with [Y%] growth

Our customers include [name 3-5 recognizable names or verticals], with 
[Z%] retention. We believe there's a strategic fit with [BUYER]'s [platform/vision].

I'd like to explore whether a combination could:
  ✓ Expand your total addressable market (TAM)
  ✓ Eliminate a competitor
  ✓ Add [specific capability, e.g., "European customer base"]
  ✓ Improve unit economics (consolidation benefits)

If you see merit, I'd be happy to share a brief overview and customer metrics 
under NDA. No pressure if timing isn't right — we're executing well independently.

Looking forward to hearing from you.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
[YOUR TITLE]
[PHONE]
```

## **VERSION 2 : Approche par acheteur financier (PE/VC)**

**Sujet :** Investment/Acquisition opportunity — SaaS, [€X] ARR, [Y%] growth

---

```
Hi [Partner Name],

[PARTNER FIRM] focuses on [vertical/stage], and [YOUR COMPANY] is a strong 
acquisition target in this space.

Quick snapshot:
  • €[ARR] ARR, [growth%] YoY growth
  • [X]% gross margin, [Y]% EBITDA margin
  • [Z] customers, [retention]% retention
  • [Founder names + pedigree] leading

Why now:
  ✓ Market expansion opportunity (new geography, product line)
  ✓ Team ready to scale aggressively post-acquisition
  ✓ Recurring revenue base + profitability de-risks acquisition

We'd welcome a conversation with you about whether this fits your thesis. 
We're exploring strategic alternatives, and the right financial partner 
could accelerate our growth significantly.

Are you open to a brief call this week?

Best regards,
[YOUR NAME]
[COMPANY]
[PHONE]
```

## **VERSION 3 : Approche via broker/intermediary**

**Sujet :** Potential acquisition — [Company], SaaS, €[X]M EV

---

```
Dear [Broker/Intermediary],

I'm reaching out because you've represented acquisitions in our space, 
and I'd like to explore working with you on a potential exit.

[YOUR COMPANY] is a profitable SaaS business (€[X] ARR, €[Y]k EBITDA, 
[Z%] growth). We've been approached by [3-5] potential acquirers, but 
we want professional guidance to maximize value.

We're looking for:
  • Professional process management (LOI → closing)
  • Access to strategic & financial buyers
  • Deal structuring advice (earnout, seller note, etc.)
  • Tax optimization guidance

Typical fees for deals at this stage: 4-6% of EV. We're ready to move 
quickly and can provide full transparency.

If this is a fit for your practice, let's talk this week.

Best,
[YOUR NAME]
```

## **VERSION 4 : Inbound response (buyer contacted you first)**

**Sujet :** RE: Potential acquisition interest

---

```
Hi [Buyer Name],

Thanks for reaching out — we appreciate the interest.

We're honored that [BUYER] sees strategic value in [YOUR COMPANY]. 
We've built something special, and we want to partner with the right 
organization if we pursue an exit.

We're at a point where we could consider acquisition, but before we 
explore terms, I'd like to understand:

1. What's your strategic thesis for [OUR SPACE]?
2. How would you integrate our team / product?
3. What does your typical acquisition process look like?
4. Rough timeline in mind?

If there's genuine alignment, we'd be happy to share preliminary metrics 
under NDA. But we want to make sure cultural & strategic fit is there first.

Can we schedule a call with your CEO / M&A lead in the next week?

Best regards,
[YOUR NAME]
```

**Sujet :** RE: Acquisition interest — curious about your vision

---

```

I'll be direct: we're not actively shopping [COMPANY], and we're proud 
of what we're building independently.

That said, the right partner at the right terms could change that. 
If you have a compelling vision for how [OUR COMPANY] fits into your

Why I'm interested in talking:
  • You respect founder culture (vs full integration)
  • You're serious about [our vertical], not just roll-up
  • You can accelerate our international expansion (our constraint)

What would make this interesting: [€X] cash + [structure] that gives 
us [upside/rôle/X]. And speed — we only want a 60-day process.

If that resonates, let's talk.

[YOUR NAME]
```

## **KEY PRINCIPLES FOR ALL EMAILS**

### ✓ DO

1. **Short & punchy** — 200–250 words max
2. **Show proof points** — Numbers, customers, growth
3. **Specific ask** — "Can we talk Thursday?" not "Let me know if interested"
4. **Signal strength** — You're doing well, not desperate
5. **Personalize** — Use CEO name, mention specific acquisition they did
6. **Social proof** — Drop 2–3 customer names (with permission)

### ✗ DON'T

1. **Long rambling narrative** — They'll skim, not read
2. **Generic → looks mass-mailed** — Even if it is
3. **Desperate tone** — "We really need a buyer" = weak negotiation
4. **Exaggerate metrics** — Due diligence will uncover truth
5. **Multiple asks** — Pick ONE (call, video, data room access)
6. **Cc: lawyers** — Too formal for first reach-out
7. **Vague value prop** — "Synergies possible" = nonsense

## **FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCE**

```
Email 1 (Day 0)  : Initial pitch
Email 2 (Day 3)  : "Checking in — did you see my note?"
Email 3 (Day 7)  : Final attempt with specific date/time
Email 4 (Day 14) : Move on, other prospects

↪ If any response: schedule call within 48h
```

---

## **PROFILE-SPECIFIC ADJUSTMENTS**

### For **Strategic Buyers** (competitors/platforms)

Add: "Natural fit because... [shared customers, complementary tech, geographic expansion]"

### For **Financial Buyers** (PE/VC)

Add: "EBITDA margin: [X]%, FCF conversion: [Y]%, leverage-ability: [Z]"

### For **Vertical Acquirers** (build-vs-buy)

Add: "Faster to acquire than build (6m to product parity, 18m to your standard)"

## **If you get interest, next email is:**

```
Thanks for the interest. Before we dive deeper, here's what we're looking for 
in a partnership:

1. Timeline: We'd like to move from LOI → closing in 90 days
2. Structure: [X]% cash at closing + [Y]% earnout (contingent on retention)
3. Team: We'd need [CEO/CTO] to stay [X] years post-closing
4. Process: NDA → data room access → DD → LOI → SPA → closing

Does that align with your process? If so, I'll set up our lawyer and 
we can move to the next step.

Best,
[YOU]
```

# EARNOUT MILESTONE SCORING RUBRIC

**Use this rubric to define clear, measurable earnout milestones that align buyer and seller interests post-acquisition.**

---

## FRAMEWORK: PRINCIPLE OF SHARED MEASUREMENT

- **Clarity:** Both parties agree on measurement method pre-close
- **Auditability:** 3rd party can verify achievement without ambiguity
- **Incentive Alignment:** Seller's upside depends on post-close execution
- **Achievability:** Realistic targets that don't require buyer's failure

---

## SCORING MATRIX: MILESTONE TYPES

| Type | Metric | Pro | Con | Auditor |
|------|--------|-----|-----|---------|
| **Revenue-based** | ARR, bookings, MRR | Easy to measure; seller driven | Influenced by buyer pricing, discounting | Buyer's CPA |
| **EBITDA-based** | EBIT, operating profit | Reflects profitability; better for sellers | Requires agreement on accounting | Buyer's CPA |
| **Milestone-based** | Product launch, customer milestones | Objective; less gaming | Hard to define; open to interpretation | Mutual agreement on specs |
| **Customer-based** | Logo count, ARR per customer | Seller-controllable | Depends on product-market fit | Customer list audit |

## TEMPLATE: REVENUE-BASED EARNOUT

### Year 1 (Months 13–24 post-close)

| Revenue Target | Earnout Payment | Payment Timing |
|---|---|---|
| $[5]M (50% above current) | $[500]K | Q1 Year 2 |
| $[6]M (60% above current) | $[700]K | Q1 Year 2 |
| $[7]M+ (70%+ above current) | $[1]M | Q1 Year 2 |

**Measurement Method:**
- Audited by Buyer's CPA within [30] days of anniversary
- ARR = committed annual contracts as of [end of Year 1]
- Excludes one-time services, implementation fees
- New customer acquisition at Buyer's direction (at seller's request) counts toward target

**Guarantee:** If [key employee] departs within 12 months, earnout reduced by [50]%

## TEMPLATE: EBITDA-BASED EARNOUT

### Year 1 (Months 13–24 post-close)

| EBITDA Target | Earnout Payment | Payment Timing |
|---|---|---|
| $[1]M (at 25% margin) | $[250]K | Q1 Year 2 |
| $[1.2]M (at 27% margin) | $[400]K | Q1 Year 2 |
| $[1.5]M (at 30%+ margin) | $[600]K | Q1 Year 2 |

**EBITDA Definition:**
```
EBITDA = Operating Income (from audited P&L)
         + Depreciation
         + Amortization
         + Stock-based compensation (if non-recurring)
         + One-time integration costs (if approved)
         − Earnout payments (if applicable)
```

**Add-backs (pre-agreed):**
- Integration costs: $[50]K (one-time)
- Key hire bonus (if external): $[30]K
- Product replatforming (if necessary): $[100]K

**Exclusions (not added back):**
- Ongoing S&M spend (seller controls this)
- Salaries of existing team
- Product roadmap investments

## TEMPLATE: MILESTONE-BASED EARNOUT

### Example: Product Integration Milestones

| Milestone | Description | Target Date | Evidence | Earnout |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **M1: API Integration** | Bidirectional data sync between Buyer platform and Seller product | Month 6 | Functional test; customer can toggle on | $[100]K |
| **M2: Customer Migration** | 80% of existing customers migrated to Buyer platform | Month 12 | Customer list signed-off by Buyer and Seller | $[150]K |
| **M3: Feature Parity** | [Top 5 features] rebuilt on Buyer stack, performance meets SLA | Month 18 | Feature acceptance by Buyer PM | $[100]K |
| **M4: Support Handoff** | Customer support fully transitioned to Buyer support team | Month 12 | Support ticket SLA met for 30 days | $[50]K |

**Total Earnout:** $400K over 18 months

**Measurement:**
- Buyer and Seller jointly assess each milestone
- Disputes resolved via mutual agreement; no third-party auditor (higher subjectivity)
- "Signed-off" means written acceptance from Buyer's designated stakeholder

**Risk:** Milestones are often subject to interpretation; consider 3rd party arbitration for disputes >$50K.

## TEMPLATE: CUSTOMER-BASED EARNOUT

### Year 1 (Months 13–24 post-close)

| Metric | Target | Earnout | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Logo Retention** | Retain 95% of top 20 customers (>$25k ARR) | $[150]K | Failure to retain any top customer reduces by $[15]K |
| **Customer Growth** | Add 10+ net new customers | $[100]K | Self-serve/sales-driven; any source counts |
| **Expansion Revenue** | $[200]K net expansion from existing customers | $[100]K | Measured YoY; expansion = new use case, increase in seats/usage |
| **NPS Improvement** | Achieve NPS >50 (from current [40]) | $[50]K | Measured via 3rd party survey |

**Total Earnout:** $400K

**Measurement:**
- Audited by Buyer's CS team
- Customer retention = contract renewal OR demonstrated continued usage
- Expansion tracked via Buyer's billing system

## SCORING RUBRIC: EARNOUT QUALITY (SELLER'S PERSPECTIVE)

### Rate each earnout component:

| Criterion | Low Risk | Medium Risk | High Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Auditability** | 3rd party can verify within [5] days | Requires [10–15] days of analysis | Ambiguous; disputes likely |
| **Seller Control** | Seller's actions drive 80%+ of outcome | Seller controls 50–80% | Buyer can sabotage (pricing, discounting) |
| **Achievability** | Conservative targets; 80%+ confidence | Realistic targets; 60% confidence | Stretch targets; <50% confidence |
| **Timing** | Annual measurement; clear calendar | Quarterly; mixed timing | Ongoing; hard to close book |
| **Dispute Risk** | <5% chance of meaningful dispute | 20–30% chance | >30% chance of litigation |
| **Payment Likelihood** | >95% confidence of full payout | 70–80% confidence | <60% confidence |

**Seller Action:** If any dimension rates "High Risk," negotiate to reduce earnout or add seller-favorable adjustment provisions.

---

## TYPICAL DISPUTE TRIGGERS (AND HOW TO AVOID)

| Dispute | Root Cause | Prevention |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue doesn't meet target | Buyer cuts prices or slows sales | Pre-agree minimum ASP floor; seller reviews pricing decisions |
| EBITDA miss | Buyer inflates costs or reallocates headcount | Pre-agree COGS and S&M caps; auditor reviews P&L adjustments |
| Customer churn | Buyer neglects customer success | Include customer retention guarantee clause; credit for seller's CS team |
| Product milestone delayed | Buyer deprioritizes integration work | Tie earnout to calendar date, not completion; penalty if Buyer delays >30 days |
| Accounting ambiguity | Buyer's accountant interprets rules differently | Use auditor's calculation as binding; pre-agree what counts as cost |

## SAMPLE EARNOUT STRUCTURE (BALANCED)

**Total earnout:** $[600]K (30% of base purchase price)

**Year 1 (Months 13–24):**
- Revenue target: $[6]M → $[250]K
- EBITDA margin target: >25% → $[150]K
- Customer retention: >90% → $[100]K
- Product milestone: API integration → $[100]K

**Year 2 (Months 25–36):**
- Expansion revenue: $[200]K → $[100]K (requires Year 1 payout success)

**Seller-favorable terms:**
- Earnout not subject to buyer's post-close discounting (revenue floor pre-agreed)
- Any earnout earned but unclaimed after [90] days paid out regardless of conditions
- If Buyer significantly underinvests (determined by arbitration), seller can trigger early close-out at [X]% of target

**Arbitration:**
- For disputes >$[50]K, neutral accountant selected from [Big 4] firm
- Cost split 50/50; determination binding
- Earnout payment held pending resolution

---

## RED FLAGS IN EARNOUT DESIGN (SELLER SHOULD AVOID)

🚩 **Vague metrics:** "Achieve sales growth" (vs. "$[10]M ARR by month 24")  
🚩 **Buyer-controlled variables:** Revenue tied to buyer's pricing/discount decisions  
🚩 **Long survival periods:** Earnout payouts delayed >18 months post-close  
🚩 **Clawback triggers:** Vague breaches like "material breach of reps" trigger full clawback  
🚩 **Unachievable targets:** Seller has <30% confidence in payout  
🚩 **No dispute mechanism:** Buyer unilaterally decides if target hit; no 3rd party arbitration

## APPROVAL CHECKLIST

Before accepting earnout structure:

- [ ] All metrics have clear, objective definitions
- [ ] Seller controls >60% of outcomes
- [ ] 3rd party can audit/verify within 30 days
- [ ] Earnout is payable within 90 days of measurement
- [ ] Dispute mechanism exists (arbitration for >$50K)
- [ ] Seller has >70% confidence in payout
- [ ] Clawback triggers are specific, not vague
- [ ] Key person departure doesn't automatically trigger full clawback

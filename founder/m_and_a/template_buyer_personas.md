# Buyer Personas — M&A Target Mapping

## 1. **Strategic Buyer** (60% of deals by $)
- **Profile:** Large competitor or adjacent vertical (10–100x your size)
- **Motivation:** Eliminate competitor, expand TAM, acquire tech/team
- **Price leverage:** High (synergy justifies premium)
- **Timeline:** 12–16 weeks (board approvals needed)
- **Diligence depth:** Deep (tech + customer overlap risk)
- **Red flag:** "We'll integrate your team into ours" = culture clash risk
- **Earnout likelihood:** 20–30% (they can afford it)
- **Contacts:** CEO, VP Corp Dev, Product SVP

## 2. **Financial Buyer (PE)** (20% by count, 30% by $)
- **Profile:** 250M–2B AUM, owns 5–15 SaaS companies
- **Motivation:** Leverage, roll-ups, multiple arbitrage
- **Price leverage:** Low (multiples are standardized)
- **Timeline:** 8–12 weeks (standardized process)
- **Diligence depth:** Thorough + accounting focused
- **Red flag:** "We'll improve margins 200bps" = labor cuts
- **Earnout likelihood:** 30–50% (high — they protect downside)
- **Contacts:** Operating partner, investment lead, sponsor

## 3. **Tuck-In Acquirer** (10% by count, 15% by $)
- **Profile:** Larger SaaS company looking to consolidate or cross-sell
- **Motivation:** Cross-sell to their base, consolidate duplicated features
- **Price leverage:** High (you fill their feature gap)
- **Timeline:** 6–10 weeks (usually fast)
- **Diligence depth:** Moderate (product + customers)
- **Red flag:** "We'll sunset your product" = dead-end
- **Earnout likelihood:** 10–20% (they have cash, high certainty)
- **Contacts:** VP Product, VP M&A, Head of Integration

## 4. **Strategic Investor Pivot** (5% by count, 10% by $)
- **Profile:** VC firm with portfolio company synergy (they own your distribution)
- **Motivation:** Maximize portfolio value, justify follow-on checks
- **Price leverage:** Moderate–High (they control the narrative)
- **Timeline:** 10–14 weeks (partner alignment)
- **Diligence depth:** Moderate (they already know you)
- **Red flag:** "We bought your competitor last year" = redundancy
- **Earnout likelihood:** 40% (structure funding round as acquisition)
- **Contacts:** Partner leading the investment, CFO

## 5. **Founder / Angel Acquirer** (5% rare, high variability)
- **Profile:** Successful founder looking to build roll-up or expand existing tool
- **Motivation:** Build empire, acquire recurring revenue
- **Price leverage:** Very high (emotional buyer, no corporate processes)
- **Timeline:** 4–8 weeks (can be very fast)
- **Diligence depth:** Light to moderate (gut-driven)
- **Red flag:** "I'll integrate it into my existing company" = total control
- **Earnout likelihood:** 60% (they don't have treasury, use your cash flow)
- **Contacts:** The founder directly, maybe their COO

## Buyer Confidence Signals (Rank them by acquisition probability)

| Signal | Weight | Example |
|---|---|---|
| They own a competitive or adjacent product | 10 | "We have 3 similar companies in portfolio" |
| 2+ call rounds, product demo requested | 8 | "VP Product wants a deep tech review" |
| Data room access granted to 3+ team members | 8 | They're doing real diligence |
| Legal engaged (LOI draft language shared) | 9 | Legal team = budget allocated |
| CFO involved in calls | 9 | They're modeling the deal |
| Reference calls with your top customers | 9 | Final stage before offer |
| They accelerate timeline ("close by Q3") | 7 | Pressure to do the deal |
| They ask about earnout structure | 7 | They're serious about price negotiations |
| They inquire about key employee retention | 8 | Post-close integration planning |
| Multi-stakeholder sign-off mentioned | 6 | Longer close, but committed |

**Scoring:** 0–30 = exploratory, 30–60 = interested, 60–80 = serious, 80+ = likely close

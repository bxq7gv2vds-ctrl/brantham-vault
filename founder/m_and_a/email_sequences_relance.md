# Email Sequences — Buyer Relance (Progression vers LOI)

**Strategy**: Each sequence = escalating urgency + clear CTAs. Send 1 email every 5–7 days.

---

## SEQUENCE 1: INITIAL MEETING RECAP + SOFT PUSH (3 emails)

### Email 1: Same Day (Within 2 Hours of Call)

```
Subject: [Company] — follow-up from our call today

Hi [Buyer Name],

Thanks so much for taking the time this afternoon. Really appreciated 
your thoughtful questions about [specific topic they asked about].

A few key points we discussed:
- [Synergy 1 you both agree on]
- [Timeline interest: "exploring options in Q3"]
- [Next step: product demo + customer ref call]

I'm attaching:
1. Pitch deck (the 30-slide version)
2. One-pager on unit economics (shows our margin profile)
3. Cap table + funding history (transparency)

No ask here — just want you to have the context.

Let me know if you'd like to set up that product demo for [specific date].

Best,
[Founder]
```

**Key**: Send same-day while momentum is hot. Lower stakes; mostly FYI.

---

### Email 2: +5 Days ("Checking In" Mode)

```
Subject: Quick follow-up — demo scheduled for [DATE]?

Hi [Buyer Name],

Just checking in on that product demo we discussed. 

Our engineering team has some slots open:
- [Tuesday, 10am PT]
- [Wednesday, 2pm PT]
- [Thursday, 11am PT]

We can also do it asynchronously if timing is tight (30-min recording + Q&A call).

Either way, I'd love to get this on your calendar before end of week.

Best,
[Founder]
```

**Key**: Offer specific time options (lowers friction). Asynchronous option shows flexibility.

---

### Email 3: +10 Days (Escalate Slightly)

```
Subject: [Company] — let's move forward with next steps

Hi [Buyer Name],

Following up on the demo — haven't heard back, but I assume you're busy.

Quick reality check: are we still interesting to you?

If yes, let's lock in a call with your technical team + finance lead 
(I'm thinking [DATE range]). This gets us to the point where we can 
discuss serious terms (LOI structure, timeline, etc.).

If you've decided to pass, no problem — just let me know so I'm not 
hanging.

Either way, I'll follow up once more next week.

Best,
[Founder]
```

**Key**: Direct question ("are we still interesting?"). Forces a response (yes/no/maybe).

---

## SEQUENCE 2: MOVING TO LOI STAGE (2 emails)

### Email 4: +15 Days (Serious Conversation)

```
Subject: [Company] — ready to move toward LOI

Hi [Buyer Name],

I realize we've been circling a bit. Let me be direct.

We're serious about exploring this transaction, and we think you could be 
the right acquirer. Here's why:

1. [Your strategic synergy specific to them] → worth $[X]M to your business
2. [Cultural fit / team alignment]
3. [Timeline alignment: you're raising capital / expanding segment]

I'd like to propose we move to the next phase:

**Week of [DATE]:**
- 60-min call with you + your CFO/strategist
- We present: product deep-dive, financials, key metrics, earnout structure
- You present: acquisition criteria, integration approach, 100-day plan

**Outcome of that call:**
- If there's mutual interest, we draft LOI (target: signed by [DATE])
- If not, we shake hands and move on (no hard feelings)

Does this make sense? Can you make [specific date] work?

Best,
[Founder]
```

**Key**: Specific value prop to buyer. Clear 2-step: deep call → LOI. Removes ambiguity.

---

### Email 5: +22 Days (Last Chance / Walk-Away Indicator)

```
Subject: [Company] — final check-in before we explore other options

Hi [Buyer Name],

I haven't heard back, so I'm going to assume this isn't a priority for you right now.

That's okay — not every deal works out.

Here's my question: **Should we stay on your radar, or should I assume you're 
moving on?**

Options:
A) You're interested — set the call this week [I'll adjust my schedule]
B) You're not ready now, but check back in [X months] — I'll set a calendar reminder
C) You've decided to pass — I'll focus elsewhere

Just reply with A, B, or C, and we'll move forward accordingly.

No pressure either way.

Best,
[Founder]
```

**Key**: Explicit three-option close. Forces clarity. Shows you have alternatives (implies other buyers).

---

## SEQUENCE 3: ADVANCED STAGE (2 emails — Only Use if Progressing)

### Email 6: After LOI Intent Confirmed (+30 Days)

```
Subject: [Company] — LOI draft + diligence kickoff

Hi [Buyer Name],

Great news — our legal team and CFO have worked with [your advisor name] 
to draft a LOI that I think works for both sides.

**Key terms:**
- Base price: $[X]M (cash)
- Earnout: $[Y]M on [specific metric] over [12 months]
- Closing timeline: [45 days from signature]
- Key employee retention: [Founder names + terms]

**Next steps:**
1. You review LOI with your counsel (target: feedback by [DATE])
2. We iterate [1–2 rounds] (expect by [DATE])
3. Both sign LOI (target: [DATE])
4. Diligence kicks off immediately (data room opens [DATE])

I've attached the LOI + a summary of open points.

Can we schedule a call [DATE] to walk through this?

Best,
[Founder]
```

**Key**: Concrete terms. Clear timeline. Removes guesswork.

---

### Email 7: Final Push to Close (After Months of Negotiation)

```
Subject: [Company] — closing in [10] days. Final offer.

Hi [Buyer Name],

We've been at this for [2–3 months]. We've negotiated hard, and I think 
we've landed on terms that work for both of us.

Here's where we are:
- LOI signed [DATE]
- Diligence: 95% complete
- Open items: [list of 2–3 minor things]
- Outstanding condition: [financing / regulatory approval / key employee signing]

**Final ask:**
Let's set a close date of [DATE]. This gives us [10] business days to wrap 
up the final items (diligence objections, financing docs, SPA sign-off).

If we don't close by then, I'll need to re-evaluate our commitment to this 
path, as it's consuming a lot of management bandwidth.

So: **Can we commit to [DATE] closing, or should we pause this?**

I'll follow up tomorrow for an answer.

Best,
[Founder]
```

**Key**: Ultimatum (respectful, but clear). Creates urgency. Forces decision.

## ALTERNATIVE SEQUENCES (Special Cases)

### If Buyer Goes Silent for 2+ Weeks

```
Subject: [Buyer], checking in — no pressure

Hi [Buyer Name],

I realize I haven't heard from you in a couple of weeks. Life gets busy!

Just want to check: are we still on your roadmap?

If yes, let me know what info would help move this forward.

If no, all good — I'll stop bugging you. 😊

Best,
[Founder]
```

---

### If Multiple Buyers Are Competing

```
Subject: [Company] — interested parties and timeline

Hi [Buyer Name],

Quick heads up: we're receiving interest from [Acquirer 2] and [Acquirer 3], 
and we're likely to move the process forward with one of them by [DATE].

If you're seriously interested, I'd recommend we schedule a call this week 
to make sure we're aligned.

No pressure — just wanted you to know the context.

Best,
[Founder]
```

**Key**: Create FOMO without being aggressive. Shows other demand.

---

### If Buyer Asks for Discount After Months

```
Subject: [Company] — response to pricing question

Hi [Buyer Name],

I got your note about adjusting the price down to $[X] (from our agreed $[Y]).

I understand the instinct, but here's why we're sticking to $[Y]:

1. We've hit every metric in our projections (or exceeded them)
2. [Competitive buyer] has already offered $[Y + $Z]
3. Our earnout structure already aligns our interests (you only pay full price 
   if we hit targets)

I'm confident $[Y] is fair. But if price is truly the blocker, let's talk 
about adjusting the earnout structure or timing instead.

Are you still interested at $[Y]?

Best,
[Founder]
```

**Key**: Acknowledge the ask. Defend with logic. Offer creative alternatives (not just price cuts).

## METRICS TO TRACK

| Phase | Time | Email | Healthy Response | Red Flag |
|-------|------|-------|------------------|----------|
| **1. Initial** | Day 0–7 | Recap call | Demo scheduled | Radio silence |
| **2. Engagement** | Day 8–14 | Demo confirm | Demo completed | Keeps delaying |
| **3. Serious** | Day 15–30 | LOI proposal | LOI review started | "We'll get back to you" |
| **4. Committed** | Day 31–60 | LOI signed | Diligence active | Slow Q&A responses |
| **5. Final** | Day 61–90 | Close push | Closing scheduled | New objections surface |

---

## Red Flags (When to Walk Away)

| Red Flag | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| **No response to 2 consecutive emails** | Not serious; move on |
| **Keeps asking for discount after LOI** | Bad faith negotiation |
| **"Financing will come through" (promised for 2+ months)** | May not have cash; risk it never closes |
| **Keeps deferring decision to "committee" / "board"** | Slow decision-making; dead deal |
| **New objections after LOI signed** | Buyer having cold feet; consider walking |

---

## Pro Tips

✓ **Keep emails short** (3–4 sentences max)  
✓ **End with a specific ask** (demo date, call time, decision deadline)  
✓ **Use names** ("Hi [Name]", not "Hi Team")  
✓ **Respond to their emails within 2 hours** (shows you're serious)  
✓ **Follow up exactly 5–7 days** (not random; creates rhythm)  
✓ **Track opens & clicks** (use Intercom/HubSpot; adjust if they're not opening)

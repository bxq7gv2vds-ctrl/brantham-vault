# Soft Shop Preparation Checklist

**Timeline:** 2-4 weeks before first outreach

---

## Governance & Advisors
- [ ] Chose investment banker vs. lone founder outreach (I recommend banker if ARR > $300k)
- [ ] Signed NDA with banker (if using one)
- [ ] Informed board / key shareholders of soft shop intent
- [ ] Set up separate email for buyer inquiries (not personal)
- [ ] Chose 2-3 trusted advisors (lawyer, banker, mentor) for feedback loop

## Messaging & Materials
- [ ] 1-pager draft (narrative, traction, market position)
- [ ] List of 5-10 strategic buyer candidates (fill in: contact, synergies, why now)
- [ ] Identified "safe" 1-2 buyers to contact first (practice conversations)
- [ ] CEO talking points (3 minutes, why we're exploring options)
- [ ] Confidentiality agreement (template ready)

## Financial Prep
- [ ] Last 24 months financials cleaned (no inconsistencies)
- [ ] Normalized EBITDA calculation (add back founder costs, one-time items)
- [ ] Churn cohort analysis (prove it's stable or declining)
- [ ] CAC/LTV calculation (show unit economics are positive)
- [ ] Revenue waterfall (show growth drivers, not surprises)

## Operational Prep
- [ ] Key customer list (top 20, with ARR, churn risk, NPS)
- [ ] Org chart (founder-dependent areas flagged honestly)
- [ ] Tech stack overview (debt level: low/med/high, clear description)
- [ ] Customer concentration memo (largest customer % of revenue)
- [ ] Pricing model explanation (clear, defensible, not in flux)

## Legal/IP Prep
- [ ] IP audit (patents, trademarks, open source licenses listed)
- [ ] Cap table clean (all option grants documented, no overhangs)
- [ ] Material contracts flagged (customer, vendor, partner agreements with change-of-control clauses)
- [ ] Founder agreements review (non-compete, IP assignment, all signed)

## Mindset Prep
- [ ] Written your personal "exit goals" (money, role, next chapter)
- [ ] Defined your walk-away price & BATNA
- [ ] Prepared for "no" (what's your plan if soft shop fails?)
- [ ] Briefed your team (or not, depending on strategy—but decide now)
- [ ] Committed to secrecy (not chatting in Slack about this)

---

## Before First Buyer Call

- [ ] Researched buyer's recent M&A (what did they pay, for what?)
- [ ] Identified 2-3 specific synergies (personalized, not generic)
- [ ] Practiced 3-minute pitch (record yourself, listen back)
- [ ] Prepped 3 tough questions (customer concentration, tech debt, competition)

---

## Stop & Decision Gate

**Before you contact even 1 buyer:**

**Question:** Are you 80%+ committed to exploring this?

- YES → proceed
- NO → wait 3-6 months, revisit

(Soft shops are visible. Backing out = founder looks flaky.)

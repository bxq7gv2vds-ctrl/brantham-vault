# Template LOI (Letter of Intent) — Acquisition SaaS

**Version**: Seller-friendly (but negotiable) | **Duration**: 3–5 pages

---

## LETTER OF INTENT

**Date**: [DATE]

**From**: [BUYER COMPANY] ("**Buyer**")  
**To**: [SELLER COMPANY] ("**Seller**")

**Re**: Proposed acquisition of [Seller]

---

## 1. Transaction Structure

**[Seller] agrees to explore a transaction whereby:**

- Buyer will acquire 100% of the equity of [Seller Company Name] for aggregate purchase price of:
  - **Base consideration**: $[X] million
  - **Earnout** (optional): Up to $[Y] million based on [revenue/EBITDA growth targets] over [12/24 months]
  - **Payment method**: [% Cash] / [% Stock] / [% Vendor financing]

**Commentary**: 
- Lock in base price + earnout mechanics early to avoid disputes later.
- Stock component = buyer wants you aligned post-close; push for collar & escrow rights.

---

## 2. Exclusivity

**For [45/60] days from this letter**, Seller agrees to:
- **Stop** all M&A conversations with other parties
- **Share** complete financials, customer list, and cap table with Buyer only
- **Negotiate** diligently with Buyer in good faith

**Seller retains the right to:**
- Continue operating the business normally
- Discuss with existing investors/board (if required)
- Accept a **Superior Proposal** (higher price/better terms) if received; must notify Buyer with 3 business days to match

**Commentary**:
- Exclusivity = dead time if buyer stalls. Get explicit timeline milestones (LOI signed → SPA in 15 days).
- "Superior Proposal" carve-out protects you if better offer surfaces.
- Don't accept >60 days unless you get a deposit/commitment fee.

---

## 3. Confidentiality & No-Shop

All information shared is **Confidential** and may not be disclosed to competitors without written consent.

Breaking confidentiality = **Liquidated damages of $[50K–250K]** (reasonable estimate of harm).

**Exception**: Required disclosures to advisors, lenders, board, and regulatory authorities.

**Commentary**:
- Lock in penalty amount upfront (avoids later disputes about "reasonable" damages).
- Make sure buyer's advisors sign separate NDAs.

---

## 4. Representations & Warranties Scope

**Buyer acknowledges** that all diligence will be conducted in the **Data Room**, and:

- **No representation** is made regarding future revenue, growth, or product roadmap
- **Seller makes no warranty** regarding competitive risk, market changes, or third-party IP (will be addressed in final SPA)
- **Financials** are prepared on [accrual/cash basis] and represent [company's best estimate]

**Commentary**:
- In LOI, keep reps & warranties **minimal** — save detailed reps for SPA.
- Add a carve-out for "matters disclosed in the Data Room" = lower your liability later.

---

## 5. Conditions to Closing

Buyer's obligation to close is **conditional upon**:

1. **Satisfactory diligence** (financial, legal, technical, customer concentration)
2. **No material adverse change** (MAC clause — loss of >20% revenue, major customer churn, founder departure)
3. **Key employee retention** agreements signed (list: [Founder, CTO, VP Sales])
4. **Customer consents** (if >X% of contracts require assignment approval)
5. **Financing approval** (if all-cash deal)
6. **Regulatory approval** (if applicable; e.g., GDPR, anti-trust)

**Seller's obligation to close is conditional upon:**
1. **Accuracy of Buyer's reps & warranties** at closing
2. **Buyer's financing** in place (if not all-cash)

**Commentary**:
- MAC clause = buyer's escape hatch. Define it tightly (e.g., >20% revenue loss, not "market downturn").
- Financing contingency = if buyer doesn't have cash, you want **priority lien** on assets or deposit.
- Keep key employee list **short** (3–5 people max); over-indexing gives buyer leverage.

---

## 6. Diligence Timeline & Data Room

**Buyer agrees to**:
- Access Data Room from [DATE] to [DATE] (30–45 days recommended)
- Provide Q-list (questions) on rolling basis
- Complete diligence by [TARGET DATE] (e.g., 30 days after LOI)
- Issue LOI objections within [5 business days] of discovering issues

**Seller agrees to**:
- Designate single point of contact for all Q&A
- Update Data Room as new docs are ready (weekly, or asap for critical items)
- **Not require NDAs from Buyer's advisors** if they sign a side-letter

**Commentary**:
- Slow diligence = dead deal. Enforce strict timeline in LOI.
- "Rolling Q-list" prevents buyer from burying issues until the last minute.
- Appoint a diligence champion on your side (not founder) = faster, less distraction.

---

## 7. Purchase Price Adjustment (Working Capital)

Base purchase price assumes **working capital of $[X]** (calculated as: current assets − current liabilities − debt).

If actual closing WC is:
- **Higher than target**: Buyer pays Seller the difference (dollar-for-dollar)
- **Lower than target**: Seller reimburses Buyer

**True-up process**: Final WC calculated within [30] days post-close; disputes resolved by independent accountant.

**Commentary**:
- WC adjustment = post-close clawback. Lock in target now to prevent surprise.
- Use a "normalized" approach (e.g., exclude one-time items, normalize receivables).
- Get independent accountant language in SPA to avoid disputes later.

---

## 8. Earnout Structure (if applicable)

| Year | Trigger | Target | Payout |
|------|---------|--------|--------|
| **2026** | ARR > $[X]M | $[Y]M earnout | 50% if hit; full if exceed by 10% |
| **2027** | ARR > $[Z]M | $[Y]M earnout | 50% if hit; full if exceed by 10% |

**Earnout governance**:
- Monthly P&L reporting (by [5th of month])
- Buyer cannot actively **harm** earnout metrics (e.g., defund sales)
- Disputes → independent auditor determination (split cost 50/50)
- If earnout not fully hit, Seller gets a consolation tier ($[min amount])

**Commentary**:
- Earnout = buyer-friendly (less upfront cash) but risky for seller (depends on buyer execution).
- Add **consolation tier** (e.g., 20% payout if only 50% of target hit) to reduce risk.
- Restrict buyer's ability to reduce headcount, cut marketing, or acquire competitors during earnout period.

---

## 9. Indemnification & Escrow

- **Escrow amount**: [15–20%] of purchase price, held for [12–18 months] post-close
- **Seller indemnifies Buyer** for breaches of reps/warranties (capped at escrow amount)
- **Fraud/IP indemnification**: Uncapped, [36-month] tail
- **Basket**: First $[50K–100K] of claims not paid; aggregate threshold $[250K]

**Commentary**:
- Escrow = insurance for buyer. Lock in amount + duration early.
- Fraud/IP = always uncapped (you can't limit liability for lying or stolen code).
- Basket prevents buyer from filing micro-claims ($5K breach, costs $50K to dispute).

---

## 10. Founder / Key Talent Retention

- **[Founder Name]** to remain CEO for minimum [12 months] post-close at salary $[X]
- **[CTO Name]** to remain as [Title] for minimum [12 months] at salary $[X]
- **Equity acceleration**: [50%] of unvested equity accelerates upon close (single-trigger); remaining [50%] vests upon [2-year anniversary] if employed (double-trigger)

**Commentary**:
- Single-trigger acceleration = protects you if buyer forces you out immediately.
- Lock in titles + comp in LOI to avoid surprise downleveling post-close.
- Negotiate "good leaver / bad leaver" language in SPA (e.g., resignation = forfeit vesting).

---

## 11. Non-Compete & Non-Solicitation

**[12–24] months post-close**:
- Founder/key employees agree not to compete in [specific product category] in [specific geography]
- Cannot solicit customers or employees for [12 months]

**Enforcement**: Breaches = liquidated damages $[X per violation], plus injunctive relief.

**Carve-outs**: 
- You can work in [adjacent sector] or [different product]
- You can consult for academic/non-profit work

**Commentary**:
- Don't agree to overly broad non-competes (unenforceable in CA anyway).
- Limit to your actual competitive threat (specific product + geography).
- Ensure "bad leaver" definition matches, or you're bound forever.

---

## 12. Financing & Closing Conditions

**All-cash deal**:
- Buyer provides proof of funds from [investment bank / private equity fund] by [DATE]
- If financing falls through, deal is void (no penalties to either party)

**Partially-financed deal**:
- Buyer assumes [X%] debt; secures commitment letter by [DATE]
- If lender pulls out, Buyer has [5 business days] to find alternative lender or deal terminates
- Seller has **priority lien** on [specify assets] until full payment

**Commentary**:
- All-cash = best case; get proof of funds early.
- Partially financed = ask for **earnest money deposit** ($[X], applied to purchase price at close; forfeited if buyer walks).
- Don't close without lender approval or explicit buyer contingency waiver.

---

## 13. Closing Mechanics & Timing

**Target closing date**: [45–60 days] from LOI signature

**Closing steps** (in order):
1. Final SPA & ancillary documents drafted & approved ([21 days])
2. Regulatory filings (if required) submitted ([14 days])
3. Final diligence items resolved ([7 days])
4. Escrow agreement signed
5. Wire transfer of purchase price; equity transfer
6. **Closing condition check-in**: Both parties confirm no MACs, all reps true & accurate

**Post-close**: Transition services agreement, data export, access handoff (executed simultaneously)

**Commentary**:
- 45–60 days is realistic for simple SaaS deal; add time if regulatory/financing involved.
- Front-load drafting (SPA should be 50% done by LOI signature).
- Set hard deadline for closing (e.g., "if not closed by [DATE], deal terminates without penalty").

---

## 14. Expenses & Break Fees

**Each party bears its own expenses** (legal, accounting, advisors). No break fee unless deal terminates due to:

- **Buyer breach**: Buyer pays Seller $[X] (typically 3–5% of deal value as penalty)
- **Seller breach**: Seller pays Buyer $[X] (same amount)

**Exception**: If buyer's financing falls through, no break fee to Buyer.

**Commentary**:
- Break fees incentivize good faith; should be symmetric.
- Exclude financing failures from break-fee penalty (not seller's fault).

---

## 15. Termination & Walk-Away Rights

This LOI terminates **without penalty** if:
- Parties don't sign final SPA within [60] days of LOI
- Diligence reveals [material issues] that can't be resolved
- Regulatory approval is denied
- Financing (if applicable) is unavailable

**Surviving obligations**:
- Confidentiality (survives [3 years])
- Non-solicitation of Buyer's employees (survives [12 months])
- Exclusivity (survives [until LOI termination date])

**Commentary**:
- Walk-away clause = you're not trapped if diligence goes sideways.
- Set a hard date (not vague "until resolved").
- Lock in survival periods for confidentiality + non-solicitation.

## Key Negotiation Points (Quick Ref)

| Point | Seller Push | Buyer Push | Compromise |
|-------|-------------|-----------|-----------|
| **Exclusivity duration** | 30 days | 90 days | 45 days + milestones |
| **Base price** | Higher | Lower | Earnout adjusts gap |
| **Earnout payout** | 100% if close to target | Tie to aggressive growth | Consolation tier @ 50% hit |
| **Indemnity cap** | 10% / 12 months | 25% / 24 months | 15% / 18 months |
| **MAC clause** | Narrow definition | Broad | >20% revenue loss, clear metrics |
| **Financing contingency** | Not acceptable | Full contingency | Proof of funds by day 20 |

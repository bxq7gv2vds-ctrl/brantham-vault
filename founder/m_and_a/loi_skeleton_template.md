## 5. Representations & Warranties

Buyer's obligations are subject to:
- Satisfactory financial/legal due diligence
- No material adverse change (MAC)
- Accuracy of seller representations (standard set per SPA)
- Key person retention agreements in place

---

## 6. Conditions Precedent to Closing

- [ ] Cap table audit complete
- [ ] Customer concentration review <[%]
- [ ] Debt schedule finalized
- [ ] IP assignments confirmed
- [ ] Key employee agreements signed
- [ ] Reps & warranties insurance bound

---

## 7. Exclusivity & Confidentiality

- **Exclusivity:** [30/60/90 days from LOI date] — Seller will not solicit or negotiate with other buyers.
- **Confidentiality:** Both parties agree NDA remains in effect.

---

## 8. Expenses

Each party bears its own expenses. [Option: "Buyer will reimburse reasonable seller legal fees up to $[X]"]

---

## 9. Binding vs. Non-Binding

**BINDING:**
- Exclusivity clause
- Confidentiality obligations

**NON-BINDING:**
- Price, payment terms, earnout structure
- Timeline (subject to DD findings)

---

## 10. Termination

This LOI terminates:
- Automatically at end of exclusivity period if SPA not signed
- By mutual written consent
- If material facts change materially adverse to either party

**Notes for seller:**
- Keep LOI lightweight; heavy terms go in SPA.
- Exclusivity is the only negotiable item here.
- If buyer balks at 60-90d timeline, that's a yellow flag (they're not serious).
- Earnout targets should be achievable 80% of the time under buyer ownership.

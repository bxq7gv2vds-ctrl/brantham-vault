# LOI Review Checklist — 30 Critical Points

## Price & Deal Structure (6)
- [ ] **Purchase price in cash** clearly stated (e.g., "$5M at close")
- [ ] **Earnout amount, formula, timeline** explicitly defined (e.g., "$500k over 24m if ARR ≥ $10M")
- [ ] **Seller indemnification cap** stated (typical: 1-2x EBITDA; any less = risk)
- [ ] **Reps & warranties insurance** — who pays? (Buyer pays = favor to you)
- [ ] **Escrow amount & holdback** specified (typical: 10-15% of purchase price, 12-18m)
- [ ] **Earnout holdback account** — where/how held? (Must be third-party escrow, not buyer's account)

## Condition of Close (6)
- [ ] **Material adverse change (MAC) clause** defined — is it buyer-friendly or balanced?
  - Buyer-friendly: Any < $X revenue drop = buyer can walk
  - Balanced: Must be > 20-30% of EBITDA loss
  - **Action:** Negotiate tight definition or remove
- [ ] **Seller representations & warranties** listed — how broad?
  - Red flag: "Seller reps all facts are true" = way too broad
  - Better: Specific, knowledge-qualified reps (e.g., "to seller's knowledge, no lawsuits")
- [ ] **Buyer due diligence completion deadline** set (e.g., "90 days from LOI")
  - Anything open-ended = buyer can drag, then walk
- [ ] **Third-party consents required** identified (e.g., customer contracts, software licenses)
  - Check if any require consent; if yes, start conversations NOW
- [ ] **Employee retention/equity** vesting plan attached (earnout depends on it)
- [ ] **Financing contingency** — is buyer financing the deal?
  - If yes, ask: "Are you *committed* financing or *best efforts*?" (huge difference)

## Seller Obligations Post-Close (5)
- [ ] **Founder role & duration** defined (e.g., "CTO, 24 months post-close")
  - How many hours/week? Reporting to whom?
- [ ] **Non-compete & non-solicitation** terms
  - How long? How broad? What's in scope?
  - Example: 24m global non-compete + 36m non-solicit (tight)
- [ ] **Transition services agreement (TSA)** mentioned?
  - Who pays for seller's transition support? (Buyer should)
  - How long? (30-90 days typical)
- [ ] **Knowledge of business** — who must stay?
  - If only founder, you can't leave
  - If CEO + 2 engineers required, that's more balanced
- [ ] **Access to data/employees post-close**
  - Can seller talk to customers? Staff?
  - Restrictions = buyer wants clean integration

## Seller Indemnification (5)
- [ ] **Cap on indemnification claims** (e.g., $2M = 40% of deal)
  - Below 1x EBITDA = you're absorbing all post-close surprises
  - Propose: 1-2x EBITDA (balanced)
- [ ] **Basket/deductible** (minimum claim to trigger indemnity)
  - Typical: $25-50k per claim, $100k aggregate
  - Higher = fewer nuisance claims on you
- [ ] **Escrow as sole remedy for reps** for [X months]
  - Good for you (escrow expires, no more claims)
  - Typical: 12-18 months (not 24m or longer)
- [ ] **Specific carve-outs from indemnity**
  - Taxes, environmental, IP, permits — any exceptions?
  - Flag: "Reps & warranties insurance" should cover most
- [ ] **Seller indemnity vs. Buyer indemnity** — is it one-way?
  - One-way (seller only) = very buyer-friendly
  - Propose mutual (buyer also indemnifies seller for undisclosed liabilities)

## Governance & Control (4)
- [ ] **Board seat post-close** — any rights for seller?
  - Especially important if earnout is contingent on performance
- [ ] **Approval rights** on material decisions (e.g., "seller approval on 50%+ price changes")
  - Protects earnout integrity
- [ ] **Financial reporting** requirements (monthly, quarterly?)
  - Critical for earnout tracking
- [ ] **Dispute resolution** mechanism (arbitration vs. litigation?)
  - Arbitration = faster, cheaper

## Tax & Legal (4)
- [ ] **Tax treatment** specified (C-corp sale = capital gains; S-corp = different)
  - Consult CPA before signing
- [ ] **Reps & warranties insurance cost** allocated (Buyer pays = savings for you)
- [ ] **Legal fees** responsibility (typically: each party pays own)
- [ ] **Closing costs** (title, escrow, title insurance) — who pays?
  - Standard: Buyer pays

## Timeline & Conditions (5 remaining)
- [ ] **Exclusivity period** defined (e.g., "90 days, no shop to other buyers")
  - Use to lock buyer in & finalize diligence
- [ ] **Diligence access** — what data room access? How long?
  - Limit to: Finance, customer list, contracts, IP, legal/compliance
  - Exclude: Private emails, board minutes, salaries
- [ ] **Closing date target** set (e.g., "60 days post-LOI")
  - Open-ended = buyer controls timeline
- [ ] **Outside date** (walk-away if not closed by X) — is there one?
  - Propose: 120–150 days from LOI
  - Protects you from indefinite delays
- [ ] **Definitive purchase agreement** timeline — when drafted?
  - Typical: Buyer's counsel drafts within 2 weeks of LOI

## Red Flags 🚩

| Red Flag | Action |
|---|---|
| LOI has "subject to financing" with no committed capital | Push back: "You need committed financing in LOI" |
| MAC clause says "any material revenue loss" with no $X threshold | Redline: "MAC only if 30%+ EBITDA loss" |
| Seller reps are "to seller's actual knowledge" but knowledge = 0 carve-outs | Redline: "Knowledge means facts that [name] could have discovered" |
| Earnout vests monthly but buyer can unilaterally adjust targets | Rewrite: "Targets locked at close; earn out formula in schedule" |
| Seller must stay 36+ months for 25%+ earnout | Negotiate: "24m max" or reduce earnout % |
| Indemnity cap is less than $500k for $5M deal | Redline: "1x EBITDA minimum" |
| No outside date; "close when ready" | Add: "Must close by [date] or either party can walk" |

## Priorities Before Signing

1. **Get your lawyer to review** (non-negotiable)
2. **Finalize purchase price structure** (cash + earnout, not "to be determined")
3. **Lock earnout formula in writing** (no handshakes)
4. **Confirm escrow details** (amount, duration, release schedule)
5. **Verify third-party consents** (can you actually close, or are there blockers?)
6. **Confirm key employee retention** (if earnout requires them to stay, get signed agreements)

# Customer Retention Playbook — Post-Close (Days 1-60)

**Ces 60 jours déterminent si vous touchez l'earnout ou pas.**

---

## Why This Matters

When acquisition closes:
- 5-15% of customers churn in first 30 days (fear, uncertainty)
- If churn >10%, earnout target unreachable
- Buyer will blame you, earnout disputes begin

**Goal :** Hold churn <2% Days 1-60 → earnout track on.

---

## DAY 1 — Closing Day

### Morning (Before announcement)
- [ ] Prepare customer email (draft approved by both teams pre-close)
- [ ] Verify: all customers have same message (no contradictions)
- [ ] Customer success team prepped (they field calls today)

### Email Template
```
Subject: [Your Product] is joining [Buyer Company] — Excited to announce

Hi [Customer],

Effective today, [Your Product] is now part of [Buyer].

What this means for you:
✅ Same product, same team, same roadmap
✅ [New benefit 1: feature X from buyer's platform]
✅ [New benefit 2: integration with buyer's ecosystem]
✅ Dedicated account manager: [name, email, phone]

Nothing changes:
❌ Price (same for 12 months guaranteed)
❌ Contract (auto-renews as agreed)
❌ Support SLA (if anything, better)

[Optional: 1 sentence about founder continuing as [role]]

Questions? Reply here or call [CSM name]: [phone]

Cheers,
[Founder name]
[Your Company name] now part of [Buyer]
```

### Afternoon
- [ ] Buyer's CS team calls all top 5 customers (warm intro)
- [ ] Your team listens in (show continuity)
- [ ] Document any customer concerns

**Critical:** No surprises, no "we're changing things."

---

## DAYS 2-7 — First Week

### Customer Success Blitz
- [ ] Scheduled check-in calls: ALL customers (top 20 first)
  - Format: "How's integration going? Any issues?"
  - Not a sales call, not asking for renewal
  - Pure: "We care, we're still here"

- [ ] Resolve any urgent issues within 24 hours
  - Bug found? Fix it.
  - Feature broken? Escalate immediately.
  - Account issue? Resolve same day.

### Playbook: "Customer calls for re-assurance"
```
Customer: "Are you discontinuing the product?"
You: "No, we're investing MORE. New features coming."

Customer: "Will pricing go up?"
You: "Guaranteed same price for 12 months. Then reassess."

Customer: "Is [founder] leaving?"
You: "I'm staying as [role], committed to product success."

Customer: "Why'd you sell?"
You: "Strategic partnership. Better resources, faster roadmap."
```

### Documentation
- [ ] Create issue tracker: every customer concern → ticket
- [ ] Triage: critical (fix today), urgent (fix week), nice-to-have
- [ ] Assign owner to each issue

---

## DAYS 8-14 — Second Week

### Proactive Product Updates
- [ ] Announce first "post-acquisition" improvement (within 14 days)
  - Can be small (faster API response, UI improvement)
  - Shows momentum, shows buyer is investing

- [ ] Roadmap adjustment meeting with top 10 customers
  - Show: "Here's what's coming" (2-3 features buyer will enable)
  - Don't promise too much
  - Get feedback

### Onboarding New Features (if integration with buyer tech)
- [ ] If buyer adds feature (e.g., integration, new API), beta test with top customers
- [ ] Ease path: "Here's how to use new stuff" not "You must migrate"

---

## DAYS 15-30 — First Month

### Retention Metrics Dashboard
```
Metric                 Target    Status
═════════════════════════════════════════════════
Churn (first 30 days)  <2%      [your number]
NPS (survey sent)      >60      [your score]
Support tickets opened <15%     [your %]
On-time payment        >95%     [your %]
Expansion (upsell)     >0%      [# accounts expanding]
```

Track these religiously. Show buyer every week.

### Implementation: Did customer sign "retention letters" pre-close?
- [ ] If YES: Verify they're still committed (soft touch call)
- [ ] If NO: Now's the time (ask "Can I count on you 12 months?")

### What NOT to do
- ❌ Change pricing
- ❌ Change support SLA
- ❌ Move customer to new product/platform without permission
- ❌ Reduce team (looks like cost-cutting)
- ❌ Skip check-ins (looks like you forgot them)

---

## DAYS 31-60 — Second Month

### Win-Back for At-Risk Customers
- [ ] Identify at-risk: customers with issues unresolved, haven't logged in, etc.
- [ ] Personal call from you or CSM (not automated email)
- [ ] Offer: "What can we do to make this right? Credit? Feature?"

### Expansion Play (if applicable)
- [ ] Now that integration is stable, pitch buyer's complementary products
- [ ] Example: "You use Product A, here's how Product B from our new parent accelerates..."
- [ ] Don't be aggressive (50% of customers not ready)

### Retention Calls (Deep dives)
- [ ] Schedule quarterly business reviews with top 10
- [ ] Agenda: ROI, product fit, roadmap alignment, expansion opportunities
- [ ] Document outcome & action items

### Team Continuity
- [ ] By day 60, all customer-facing team should be in place post-acquisition
- [ ] No surprises: "CSM Jane is leaving"
- [ ] If someone is leaving: introduce replacement 2 weeks early (overlap)

## Churn Prevention Framework

### If customer indicates churn interest:

```
Customer: "We're thinking of switching to [competitor]"

Your response:
1. "What problem are we not solving?"  [Listen]
2. "Here's how we address that..."     [Solution]
3. "New from acquisition: [benefit]"   [Unique value]
4. "Let's do a win-back plan"          [Concrete]

Win-back options:
- Price reduction (offer 20% discount next 6 months)
- Feature fast-track (we build what they need)
- Dedicated engineering (assignee for their account)
- Executive sponsorship (Buyer CEO calls them)
```

**Only use price reduction if you've tried everything else.**

## Week 9-12 (Beyond 60 Days)

### Measure Success
```
If churn <2% and NPS stable/improving:
  ✅ You're on track for earnout
  ✅ Buyer is happy with acquisition
  ✅ You're in good standing

If churn >5%:
  ❌ Red flag for earnout risk
  ❌ Escalate to buyer leadership
  ❌ Implement emergency retention plan
```

### Earnout Documentation
- [ ] Monthly retention report to buyer
  - Customer churn rate
  - NPS trend
  - Revenue health
  - Issues log (fixed vs. open)

- [ ] Escalation protocol
  - If customer at risk: notify buyer within 2 days
  - If churn happens: root cause analysis within 1 week
  - If pattern emerges: joint working group to fix

## Post-Close Retention Checklist

### Communication
- [ ] Day 1: Customer announcement email (approved by buyer)
- [ ] Week 1: Top 5 customer calls (founder or CSM intro)
- [ ] Week 2: Roadmap adjustment call (top 10 customers)
- [ ] Weeks 3-8: Weekly check-ins (CS team, rotating customers)
- [ ] Week 9+: Quarterly business reviews

### Product
- [ ] First update shipped within 14 days (shows momentum)
- [ ] Integration with buyer tech (if applicable) in beta
- [ ] No feature removals or breaking changes first 60 days
- [ ] Support SLA maintained or improved

### Team
- [ ] CSM team stable (no departures first 60 days)
- [ ] Founder visible (customer calls, not disappeared)
- [ ] Buyer integration team meets with your CS monthly

### Metrics
- [ ] Churn <2% (Days 1-60)
- [ ] NPS maintained (no downward trend)
- [ ] Support response time <4 hours
- [ ] Zero escalations to legal/dispute
- [ ] 0% "stop payment" disputes

### Finance
- [ ] 100% on-time payment (if monthly billing)
- [ ] No refund requests (except legitimate)
- [ ] Expansion deals: 1-2 customers upsold

## Earnout Risk Mitigation

### If customers are churning (>5%)

**Week 1-2 of churn detection:**
1. Call buyer head of sales/product → "Here's issue, here's fix"
2. Propose joint customer win-back (buyer CEO + you on calls)
3. Offer retention credit / acceleration (from your earnout if needed)

**Negotiation play:**
```
You: "Churn is due to [X: integration delay / messaging / support].
     We're fixing [Y], which requires [Z: buyer marketing / resources].
     Once fixed, we'll recapture [N customers]."
     
Buyer: "OK, we'll help with [Z]"

Result: Churn stops, earnout back on track.
```

**If buyer won't help:**
```
You: "If churn continues and earnout fails, I'm flagging this
     as joint responsibility, not my failure.
     Earnout dispute will be:
     - 70% your responsibility (you cut support)
     - 30% my responsibility (I didn't push back)
     
     Let's agree on this upfront."
```

## One More Thing: Culture

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Badmouthing buyer to customers ("They're messing with our product")
- ❌ Making promises buyer can't keep ("We'll never change pricing")
- ❌ Playing sides (customers vs. buyer)

**Do:**
- ✅ Be honest: "Integration is happening, there will be some change"
- ✅ Be optimistic: "This is good for you long-term"
- ✅ Be a bridge: Help buyer and customers understand each other

**Remember:** Customers want to believe in the acquisition. Make it easy for them.

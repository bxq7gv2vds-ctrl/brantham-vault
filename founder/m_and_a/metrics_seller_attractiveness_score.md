## DETAILED SCORING

### 1. NET REVENUE RETENTION (NRR)

**Definition:** `(Beginning MRR + Expansion - Churn) / Beginning MRR`

**Why buyers care:** Predicts revenue predictability and upsell potential.

**Scoring:**
- **> 120%** = 🟢 Elite (Slack, Figma level) — Buyer pays premium
- **100-120%** = 🟢 Strong (HubSpot, Calendly level) — Buyer pays 8-10x revenue
- **95-100%** = 🟡 Acceptable (most SaaS) — Buyer pays 6-8x revenue
- **90-95%** = 🟠 Weak (red flag for buyer) — Negotiate down or explain
- **< 90%** = 🔴 Dealbreaker unless exceptional TAM — Discount 20%+

**Your NRR:** _____%

**If NRR < 95%, fix this before approaching:**
- [ ] Expand existing customer revenue (upsell roadmap?)
- [ ] Reduce churn with retention program (NPS survey? health scoring?)
- [ ] Combine both for 12+ weeks, then re-approach

---

### 2. CAC PAYBACK PERIOD

**Definition:** `(Customer Acquisition Cost) / (Monthly Gross Profit)`

**Why buyers care:** Efficiency + bankability of growth.

**Scoring:**
- **< 6 months** = 🟢 Elite efficiency — Buyer confident in scaling
- **6-12 months** = 🟢 Strong — SaaS gold standard
- **12-18 months** = 🟡 Acceptable but stretched — Flag as "investment phase"
- **18-24 months** = 🟠 Concerning — Buyer will demand slower growth post-acquisition
- **> 24 months** = 🔴 Broken — Fix unit economics or discount heavily

**Your CAC Payback:** _____ months

**If > 12 months, explain in buyer narrative:** "We're still ramping S&M spend; at profitability CAC payback is X months."

---

### 3. MONTHLY CHURN RATE

**Definition:** `(MRR Lost to Churn) / (Beginning MRR)` or `(Customers Lost) / (Beginning Customers)`

**Why buyers care:** Revenue runway + integration risk (churn often spikes post-close).

**Scoring:**
- **< 2%/mo** = 🟢 Exceptional — Lock-in or strong defensibility
- **2-5%/mo** = 🟢 Good — SaaS standard (60%/yr annualized = 45% customers gone/yr)
- **5-10%/mo** = 🟡 Acceptable — Churn is elevated but manageable
- **> 10%/mo** = 🔴 Crisis — Fix before selling; buyers will overly discount

**Your Monthly Churn:** _____%

**Annualized Churn (rough):** 1 - (1 - monthly churn)^12 = _____%

**If churn > 5%/mo, buyer will:**
- Assume 50% customer attrition post-close (integration risk)
- Model revenue with 3x churn rate in first 6 months
- Apply 15-25% discount to deal value

---

### 4. GROSS MARGIN

**Definition:** `(Revenue - COGS) / Revenue`

**Why buyers care:** Scalability + profitability at scale.

**Scoring (SaaS):**
- **> 75%** = 🟢 Exceptional — Pure software
- **60-75%** = 🟢 Excellent — SaaS standard
- **50-60%** = 🟡 Acceptable — Some COGS (hosting, support)
- **40-50%** = 🟠 Concerning — Buyer will model aggressive cost cuts
- **< 40%** = 🔴 Dealbreaker — Not software

**Your Gross Margin:** _____%

**If < 60%, explain:** "Improving to X% post-integration via [cost cuts]."

---

### 5. MAGIC NUMBER (Growth Efficiency)

**Definition:** `(ARR Growth Last Quarter) / (S&M Spend Last Quarter)`

**Why buyers care:** Proves marketing/sales scalability without diminishing returns.

**Scoring:**
- **> 1.0x** = 🟢 Exceptional — Every $1 S&M → > $1 new ARR
- **0.75-1.0x** = 🟢 Good — SaaS benchmark
- **0.5-0.75x** = 🟡 Stretched — Growth is costing more than usual
- **< 0.5x** = 🟠 Concerning — Your S&M team is underpowered or market saturated

**Your Magic Number:** ______ x

**If < 0.75x, flag:** "We're in investment phase; magic number returns to 0.8x post-Q3 based on pipeline."

## ACTION PLAN: FIX BEFORE APPROACHING

**If you scored < 10/15:**

1. **Churn** (worst offender): Implement retention program this quarter
2. **NRR**: Launch upsell playbook for top 20% customers
3. **Magic Number**: Pause S&M spend for 30 days (or identify poor-performing channels to cut)
4. **CAC Payback**: Reduce acquisition spend temporarily (redirect to retention)
5. **Margins**: Audit COGS line-by-line; cut bottom 10% of costs

**Timeline:** 8-12 weeks to re-score, then approach.

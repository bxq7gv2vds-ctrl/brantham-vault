# Customer Announcement Email — 3 Variants

Send these AFTER employee announcement, ideally same day.

---

## VARIANT A: Enterprise/Strategic Customers (Largest ARR)

**Send to:** Direct relationship owner (CEO, Head of Procurement) + CSM
**Timing:** Call first, then email 1 hour after (personal touch)

**Subject:** [ANNOUNCEMENT] Strategic Partnership with [Buyer] — What This Means for You

> Hi [CEO/Contact],
>
> I wanted to personally share exciting news: [Company] has been acquired by [Buyer Name] for $[X].
>
> **Why this matters to you:**
> [Buyer] is the leading [market/product category] with [X customers / $XXM ARR]. By joining forces, we're combining our strengths to deliver [specific product improvement relevant to THIS customer].
>
> **What this means for your service:**
> - **No disruption.** Your service continues uninterrupted. [Buyer]'s infrastructure will ensure [99.99% uptime / better SLA / faster features]
> - **Your dedicated team stays.** [Name] (your CSM) and [Name] (your technical contact) are part of [Buyer] now and will continue supporting you
> - **New capabilities.** You'll gain access to [specific feature/integration] that [Buyer] built for [similar large customer use case]
> - **Better together.** We're committed to [specific outcome important to customer: faster releases, better support, deeper integration, global reach]
>
> **Next steps:**
> - [Buyer's CSM lead] will reach out [this week] for a brief intro call
> - Your current contract continues as-is (no changes to terms, pricing, or service level)
> - We'll host a [30-min webinar on Zoom] on [date] to walk you through integration benefits
>
> I'm grateful for your partnership over [X years]. The best is ahead.
>
> [Your name]
> CEO, [Company]
>
> P.S. If you have any concerns or want to discuss this live, I'm available for a call [specific times]. Just reply.

## VARIANT B: Mid-Market Customers (Medium ARR)

**Send to:** Primary contact + any backup users
**Timing:** Email only (no call necessary unless they request)

**Subject:** We've Been Acquired by [Buyer] — Here's What It Means for You

> Hi [Name],
>
> We're excited to announce that [Company] has been acquired by [Buyer Name].
>
> **What this means for your account:**
> - **Your service continues as-is.** No interruptions, no price changes, same support level
> - **You'll get faster feature releases.** [Buyer] has invested in product development, so expect new features every [month/quarter]
> - **Better support infrastructure.** [Buyer] has a global support team in [timezones], so you'll get faster response times
> - **New integrations coming.** [Buyer] will be connecting [product] to [relevant ecosystem: CRM, data warehouse, etc.]
>
> **Your next steps:**
> - Check your email for a message from [Buyer's CSM] ([date]) — they'll intro themselves and answer questions
> - Your contract stays the same; no action needed from you
> - Optional: Join our webinar [date/time] to see the product roadmap (sign up link)
>
> Thanks for being a customer. We're looking forward to serving you even better.
>
> [Your name]
> CEO, [Company]

## VARIANT C: Small/SMB Customers (Transactional)

**Send to:** All users who have email on file
**Timing:** Automated email (can send to many at once)

**Subject:** Exciting News: [Company] + [Buyer]

> Hi [Name],
>
> [Company] has joined [Buyer]! 
>
> **Good news:** Nothing changes for you. Your account, pricing, and service level all stay the same.
>
> **The benefit:** You'll now have access to [Buyer]'s tools for [specific use case relevant to SMB: reporting, integrations, premium support].
>
> **What to expect:**
> - New product features every [month/quarter]
> - [Buyer]'s support team is available [hours/availability]
> - Integrations with [tools SMBs use: Slack, Google Workspace, Zapier]
>
> Have questions? Reply to this email or visit [help center link].
>
> Excited to serve you,
> [Company] team

## If Acquisition is a "Down Round" or Customer Lost Value

**Variant D: Transparency Play (For customers who might perceive downside)**

**Subject:** An Update on [Company] — We're Joining [Buyer]

> Hi [Name],
>
> I want to be transparent with you. [Company] was acquired by [Buyer Name] for $[X].
>
> **Why this happened:**
> [Honest narrative: market downturn, we needed scale, competition was fierce, founder burnout, capital requirements, etc.]. The best path forward for [product you love] was to join [Buyer], who has [resources, market, team] to make it what you deserve.
>
> **What this means for you:**
> - Same service, better future. [Buyer] will invest in [specific improvements you've asked for]
> - Pricing stays the same through your renewal
> - You'll keep your CSM / support contact
>
> **If you're concerned:**
> I get it. Consolidations can feel risky. But [Buyer] is [profitable / well-capitalized / market leader], and this actually improves [product reliability / roadmap / support quality] for you.
>
> If you want to discuss live, I'm available [times].
>
> Thanks for your trust,
> [Your name]

## DON'T Say (Anti-Patterns)

| Don't | Why | Say Instead |
|---|---|---|
| "We were failing, so we sold" | Undermines confidence in your product | "[Buyer]'s market position creates better opportunities for our product" |
| "Pricing may change post-integration" | Customers immediately look for alternatives | "Pricing stays the same through [your renewal / 12 months]" |
| "This was a forced sale" | Sounds desperate; customers doubt long-term viability | [Don't mention it; move on] |
| "Your data may be transferred" | Privacy panic | "Your data stays secure; [Buyer] has [SOC 2 / ISO 27001]" |
| "Some features may be deprecated" | Customer assumes their use case will be dropped | "[Buyer] is committed to [roadmap item relevant to them]" |
| "You'll be assigned a new CSM" | If you have a good CSM, don't spook them | "[Your CSM] is now part of [Buyer], and you'll keep them" (if true) |

## Sending Checklist

- [ ] Segment customers by size (enterprise / mid-market / SMB)
- [ ] Map each customer to template (A/B/C or custom)
- [ ] Draft customer-specific personalizations (mention their use case, not generic)
- [ ] Get buyer approval on all customer-facing messaging (legal + marketing)
- [ ] Coordinate timing with internal announcement (same day, after all-hands)
- [ ] Prepare follow-up cadence:
  - [ ] Day 0: Email announcement
  - [ ] Day 1: CSM 1-on-1 intro calls
  - [ ] Day 3: Customer webinar (optional, but valuable)
  - [ ] Week 2: Check-in email asking for feedback
- [ ] Monitor replies for churn risks (flag immediately to retention team)
- [ ] Document any customer concerns in shared sheet (for integration team)

---

## Churn Predictions

**Most likely to churn:**
- Customers with termination-for-convenience clauses
- Customers who wanted to sell to you (competitive buyers they don't trust [Buyer])
- Customers on month-to-month contracts
- Customers who've complained about product direction

**Action:** Proactive outreach from founder or CSM within 48 hours to at-risk segment.

---

**Pro Tip: Track "customer sentiment" by tier post-announcement. If enterprise customers are quiet, that's usually good (acceptance). If they're asking questions, answer fast (concern mitigation).**

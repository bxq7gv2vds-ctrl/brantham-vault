# M&A Pitch Deck Script (5 Minutes)
**Structure**: 30 slides, 10 seconds per slide

---

## SLIDE 1: COVER PAGE (10 sec)

**Script**:  
"Thanks for taking the time today. We're [Company], and we've built [1-line product description]. This deck explains why we're an attractive acquisition target and the synergies we see together."

**Key**: Smile, steady voice, eye contact.

---

## SLIDES 2–3: THE PROBLEM (30 sec)

**Slide 2**: Market pain point (visuals: customer quote or market data)  
**Slide 3**: Existing solutions are inadequate (table: competitor comparison)

**Script**:  
"Our customers face [specific pain]. Most companies try [common workaround], but that's slow, expensive, and error-prone. We saw an opportunity here."

**Key**: Concrete example (don't generic "pain point").

---

## SLIDE 4: OUR SOLUTION (10 sec)

**Script**:  
"We built [product name] to [solve that pain] in [X minutes] instead of [old time]. Here's what sets us apart: [3 bullets]."

**Key**: Benefit, not feature list.

---

## SLIDES 5–7: GO-TO-MARKET & TRACTION (45 sec)

**Slide 5**: Who we sell to (customer segments, logos)  
**Slide 6**: How we sell (pricing model, sales motion)  
**Slide 7**: Early traction (ARR growth chart, CAC/LTV)

**Script**:  
"We're selling to [segment] at $[X] per customer. We've signed [# customers] in [timeframe], growing to $[ARR]M ARR. Our CAC payback is [# months]—very healthy for this category."

**Key**: Show momentum (even if early-stage; e.g., "3 paying customers, $50K ARR, 25% MoM growth").

---

## SLIDES 8–9: PRODUCT & TECH (20 sec)

**Slide 8**: Product screenshot / demo (what it looks like)  
**Slide 9**: Tech stack & IP (patents, unique tech, moat)

**Script**:  
"The product is [describe in plain English]. Our secret sauce is [1 technical differentiator]. We have [patent/trademarks/proprietary data] that's hard to replicate."

**Key**: Highlight defensibility (why competitors can't just copy you).

---

## SLIDES 10–12: MARKET SIZE & OPPORTUNITY (30 sec)

**Slide 10**: TAM (Total Addressable Market) — $[X]B globally  
**Slide 11**: SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market) — $[X]M (your addressable segment)  
**Slide 12**: Market growth (CAGR, trends, tailwinds)

**Script**:  
"The TAM is $[X]B. Our SAM is $[X]M—large enough to build a $[100M–1B] business. This market is growing [X]% annually driven by [regulatory change / customer demand / automation trend]."

**Key**: SAM, not TAM (TAM is almost always huge; SAM shows focus).

---

## SLIDES 13–14: TEAM & ADVISORS (20 sec)

**Slide 13**: Founding team (photos, brief bios)  
**Slide 14**: Advisors / Board (if any heavyweight names; leave blank if weak)

**Script**:  
"We're led by [Founder 1, background] and [Founder 2, background]. [Key credential—e.g., 'Together, we've built 2 companies to exit, combined 40 years in this space.']"

**Key**: Emphasize relevant experience (sales, ops, fundraising). If advisors are weak, skip this slide.

---

## SLIDES 15–17: FINANCIAL PROJECTIONS (45 sec)

**Slide 15**: Revenue forecast (3-year projection, conservative)  
**Slide 16**: Path to profitability (when EBITDA positive)  
**Slide 17**: Unit economics (LTV, CAC, payback period, gross margin %)

**Script**:  
"We're profitable [or 'on path to profitability in [Q/year]']. Our unit economics are strong: $[LTV] customer lifetime value, $[CAC] customer acquisition cost, payback in [X months]. Gross margins are [X]%—sustainable long-term."

**Key**: Conservative projections (e.g., 50% YoY growth, not 300%). Show clear path to profitability.

---

## SLIDE 18: KEY METRICS DASHBOARD (10 sec)

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **ARR** | $[X]M |
| **Customer Count** | [X] (growing [X]% MoM) |
| **CAC Payback** | [X] months |
| **Gross Margin** | [X]% |
| **Churn Rate** | [X]% MoM |
| **NPS** | [X] |

**Script**: *Point to dashboard.* "These are the metrics that matter most to us. [Call out the one you're most proud of]."

---

## SLIDES 19–21: COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE (30 sec)

**Slide 19**: Competitive matrix (price vs. features)  
**Slide 20**: Competitor comparison table  
**Slide 21**: Our advantage (why we win)

**Script**:  
"In this space, you have [Competitor A—expensive but powerful] and [Competitor B—cheap but limited]. We're positioned as [Goldilocks / leader / disruptor]. Our advantages: [3 bullets]."

**Key**: Acknowledge competitors (don't pretend they don't exist); focus on your differentiation.

---

## SLIDES 22–24: WHY NOW? (MARKET TIMING) (30 sec)

**Slide 22**: Tailwind #1 (e.g., regulatory change, technology maturity)  
**Slide 23**: Tailwind #2 (customer demand, adoption curve)  
**Slide 24**: Tailwind #3 (competitive threat, urgency)

**Script**:  
"The timing is perfect because: [tailwind 1], [tailwind 2], and [tailwind 3]. This is a [2–3]-year window to capture market share before consolidation."

**Key**: Create urgency without desperation.

---

## SLIDES 25–26: SYNERGIES & STRATEGIC FIT (30 sec)

**Slide 25**: Synergies with [Buyer] (revenue synergies, cost synergies)  
**Slide 26**: Why [Buyer] specifically (customer overlap, distribution, tech fit)

**Script**:  
"If you acquire [Seller], here's what you gain: You reach [X] new customers. Your [existing product] gets enhanced with our [feature]. You realize [cost synergy: shared ops, R&D]. Combined, we're [market leader / defensible / 2x faster growth]."

**Key**: Make it **buyer-specific** (not generic "synergies").

---

## SLIDE 27: USE OF PROCEEDS / POST-ACQUISITION ROADMAP (15 sec)

**Slide 27**: What we'll do post-close (product roadmap, hiring, integration)

**Script**:  
"In the first 100 days, we'll [integrate product / hire sales team / launch to your customer base / expand to adjacent markets]. Year 2, we target [milestone]."

**Key**: Show you have a plan; you're not just coasting post-close.

---

## SLIDE 28: FUNDING & CAPITAL EFFICIENCY (10 sec)

**Slide 28**: Capital raised to date, burn rate, runway

**Script**:  
"To date, we've raised $[X]M from [investors]. We're unit-profitable [or 'on path to positive unit economics']. This deal lets you acquire us before [alternative: Series C, IPO track, competitor acquisition]."

**Key**: "Before Series C" = lower valuation. "We're profitable" = less risk.

---

## SLIDE 29: VALUATION & DEAL TERMS (OPTIONAL—USUALLY IN FOLLOW-UP) (10 sec)

**If pressed**:

"We're valued at $[X]M, reflecting: [3-year revenue forecast × X multiple] + [IP value] + [team]. We're open to discuss earnout structures, retention agreements, and integration milestones."

**Key**: Don't anchor too hard in first pitch. Save this for LOI discussion.

---

## SLIDE 30: CALL TO ACTION (15 sec)

**Script**:  
"We're excited about the possibility of working together. Here's what we'd like next: [Option A: tour our product], [Option B: meet with our team], [Option C: schedule a deep-dive financial review]. What works best for you?"

**Key**: Clear next step. Make it easy to say yes.

---

## DELIVERY TIPS (CRITICAL)

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| **Maintain eye contact** | Read from slide |
| **Pause at key moments** | Rush through numbers |
| **Let buyer ask questions** | Defend your projections unprompted |
| **Show confidence** | Apologize for limitations |
| **Smile, even on camera** | Look nervous or over-rehearsed |
| **10 sec per slide = 5 min** | Go over 5 minutes without permission |

---

## TIMING BREAKDOWN

```
Slides  1–3  : Problem & Solution        (40 sec)
Slides  4–7  : Product + Traction       (75 sec)
Slides  8–12 : Tech, Market, Team       (65 sec)
Slides 13–18 : Financials + Metrics     (75 sec)
Slides 19–24 : Competition + Tailwinds  (60 sec)
Slides 25–27 : Synergies + Roadmap      (60 sec)
Slides 28–30 : Valuation + CTA          (35 sec)
               ─────────────────────────────
               TOTAL: ~5 minutes (300 sec)
```

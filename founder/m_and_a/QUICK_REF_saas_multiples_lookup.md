## Adjustments (Applied to base multiple)

| Factor | Impact | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Growth rate** | +0.5–1x per 10% YoY | <20% growth: -1x; >50%: +1x |
| **Profitability** | +1–2x if EBITDA >30% | Negative EBITDA: -1x |
| **Churn rate** | -0.5x per 2% annual | <3% gross churn: +0.5x; >8%: -1x |
| **Net retention** | +0.5–1x if >110% | NRR <100%: -0.5x |
| **Customer concentration** | -0.5–1x if Top 3 >30% | Diversified (no customer >5%): +0.25x |
| **Buyer synergies** | +1–3x if strategic | Strategic buyer exploiting product fit |
| **Market position** | +0.5–1x if category leader | Fragmented market: no premium |

## How to Use

1. **Find your ARR bracket** → baseline multiple
2. **Apply adjustments** → realistic range
3. **Know your buyer type** → strategic can pay 2–3x more than PE
4. **Anchor high** → begin negotiation 20–30% above range

## Interpretation Notes

- **PE buyers**: Target 8–10x EBITDA max (need ROI target in 4–5 years)
- **Strategic buyers**: Can pay 10–15x (synergy extraction, market defense)
- **Founder acquirers**: Typically 5–7x (limit financial runway)
- **Public acquirers**: 8–12x typical (stock currency, growth ambitions)

# HIDDEN COSTS OF M&A — FULL BUDGET

**M&A isn't free. Plan for [5–15]% of deal value in total costs. Here's the breakdown.**

---

| Item | Cost Range | Notes |

## BREAKDOWN BY CATEGORY

### Legal Fees (30–40% of total costs)
- **Your counsel:** $[150–300]K (drafting SPA, negotiations, closing)
- **Escrow/trust setup:** $[20K]
- **Employment agreements:** $[10–20]K
- **IP assignments:** $[10–15]K
- **Earnout documentation:** $[10]K

**How to reduce:** Use smaller firm (vs. BigLaw); negotiate flat fee vs. hourly

---

### Accounting & Tax (20–25% of total costs)
- **Financial statement review/audit:** $[30–50]K
- **Tax planning/structuring:** $[25–75]K
- **Diligence response:** $[20–40]K
- **Closing working capital adjustment:** $[10–15]K

**How to reduce:** Clean up books BEFORE sale (reduces audit time)

---

### Advisors (15–20% of total costs)
- **Investment banker:** $[250]K–$[2]M (if hired; 1–2% of deal value)
- **Business advisor:** $[20–50]K
- **Consultant for tech diligence:** $[30–50]K

**How to reduce:** Skip banker if you have good inbound (only needed if shopping deal); rely on board advisors

---

### Opportunity Cost (15–20% of total costs)
- **Your time (6 months of salary):** $[150–300]K
- **Team distraction:** $[50–100]K (engineers pulled for tech diligence)
- **Lost productivity:** $[50–100]K

**Real but often ignored.**

---

## HIDDEN COSTS NOT INCLUDED

Also budget for:

- **Earnout clawback disputes:** $[50–100]K in legal fees if there's a dispute
- **Post-close integration:** $[100–300]K (systems, people, tools)
- **Transition service costs:** You might work 3–6 months earning small fee; opportunity cost high

---

## WHO PAYS WHAT

| Cost | Paid By | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Buyer's legal | Buyer | They fund their own counsel |
| Your legal | You or escrow | Standard to pay from closing proceeds |
| Escrow agent | 50/50 split | Standard arrangement |
| Investment banker | You | Commission comes from proceeds |
| Your accountant | You | But can include in due diligence requests |
| Management time | You | You're not paid during diligence (opportunity cost) |

## HOW TO BUDGET FOR COSTS

**Rule of thumb:**
- **Small deals:** Reserve [1–2]% for costs
- **Mid-market:** Reserve [1–2]% for costs
- **Large deals:** Reserve [0.5–1.5]% for costs (scale benefits)

**Safety margin:** Always add [+20]% buffer (things always cost more than expected)

---

## WAYS TO REDUCE COSTS

1. **Use your board's legal advisor** (vs. hiring new counsel)
2. **Keep books clean** (reduces audit/accounting time)
3. **Document IP upfront** (vs. rushing during diligence)
4. **Respond quickly to diligence** (faster close = lower admin costs)
5. **Skip banker if you have direct buyer** (saves 1–2% of deal)
6. **Flat-fee arrangements** with lawyers (vs. hourly)

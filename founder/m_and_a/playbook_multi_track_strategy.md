# PLAYBOOK: MULTI-TRACK STRATEGY — RUNNING MULTIPLE BUYER CONVERSATIONS

**Never negotiate with a single buyer. Run 3–5 parallel conversations to create FOMO, maintain optionality, and maximize price.**

---

## PRINCIPLE: COMPETITION DRIVES PRICE

**In M&A, the best leverage is alternatives.**

- 1 buyer → They dictate terms (you're desperate)
- 3 buyers → You have optionality (they compete for you)
- 5 buyers → Multiple offers → You pick the best

**The math:**
- Single buyer offering $[100]M = You accept
- 3 buyers (offers at $[100]M, $[120]M, $[110]M) = You pick the $[120]M, or negotiate up to $[130]M

---

## STEP 1: IDENTIFY 3–5 BUYER CATEGORIES

### Buyer Archetypes (Pick 1–2 from Each Category)

| Category | Profile | Advantage | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Strategic #1** | [Big competitor] | High synergies; good price; long-term partnership | May kill your product; integrate aggressively |
| **Strategic #2** | [Customer's customer] | Cross-sell opportunity; large ecosystem | May be distracted; slow integration |
| **PE Firm #1** | [Growth PE, $[X]M fund] | Financial discipline; keeps you as CEO; earnout realistic | Aggressive margin targets; may sell again in 3 years |
| **PE Firm #2** | [Mid-market PE, $[Y]M fund] | Similar to PE #1; different operators | Lower price than Strategic; but good operators |
| **Tuck-in Buyer** | [Smaller company looking to acquire] | Fast integration; less bureaucracy | Lower price; less brand prestige |

---

## STEP 2: TIER YOUR BUYERS (YOUR PREFERENCES)

**Rank your 3–5 buyers by preference (honestly):**

| Rank | Buyer | Why Preferred | Strategic Fit | Price Expectation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1st Choice** | [Strategic Buyer A] | Best product fit; CEO stays; synergies high | 9/10 | $[120–130]M |
| **2nd Choice** | [PE Firm A] | Growth operator; good capital partner | 8/10 | $[100–115]M |
| **3rd Choice** | [Strategic Buyer B] | OK fit; risk of product kill | 6/10 | $[95–110]M |
| **4th Choice** | [PE Firm B] | Less aligned; more financial | 5/10 | $[85–100]M |
| **Fallback** | Keep growing independently | No acquisition | — | N/A (valuation grows 30%/year) |

**Your strategic goal:** Negotiate with #1 choice, but have #2–4 as real alternatives to push price up.

---

## STEP 3: THE OUTREACH SEQUENCE (STAGGERED)

**DO NOT contact all buyers simultaneously.** Stagger your outreach so you get signals from multiple buyers without making it obvious you're running a process.

### Timeline (Weeks 0–6)

| Week | Action | Who | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Week 0** | Warm intro to Buyer #1 (via advisor/board member) | Strategic #1 | Start conversation; set tone |
| **Week 1** | Send 1-pager; request meeting | Buyer #1 | Initial interest |
| **Week 2** | First call with Buyer #1; gauge interest | Buyer #1 | Understand their thesis; soft anchor price |
| **Week 2** | Warm intro to Buyers #2–3 (other advisors) | Strategic #2, PE Firm #1 | Start parallel conversations |
| **Week 3** | Buyer #1 sends LOI | Buyer #1 | Formal offer |
| **Week 3–4** | First calls with Buyers #2–3 | Buyers #2–3 | Gauge interest; request prelim offers |
| **Week 5** | Preliminary offers from Buyers #2–3 | Buyers #2–3 | Compare options |
| **Week 6** | Play offers against each other; push top choice on price | All buyers | Maximize valuation |

---

## STEP 4: THE CONVERSATION SCRIPT (DIFFERENT FOR EACH BUYER)

**Key: Be honest that you're talking to multiple buyers. Don't hide it.**

### To Buyer #1 (Your Top Choice)

> "Thanks for your interest in acquiring us. We're excited about the strategic fit with your platform.
>
> We're in the early stages of exploring options. We're talking to [2–3] other potential acquirers to understand market valuations and make sure we pick the right partner.
>
> We prefer your company because of [specific strategic fit]. But we need the valuation to reflect our growth trajectory and the synergies you'd capture.
>
> Can you share an initial indication of interest (price range and terms)? We can then schedule more detailed conversations."

**Why this works:**
- You're transparent (they know you're shopping)
- You signal preference (them > others)
- You ask for their number (forces them to anchor)
- You hint that others are interested (creates FOMO)

---

### To Buyers #2–3 (Secondary Choices)

> "Thanks for the intro. We're exploring strategic options for the company and would like to have a conversation with you.
>
> We've had preliminary conversations with [1–2] other parties and want to understand multiple perspectives on how acquisitions like ours could create value.
>
> Are you interested in a 30-minute call to understand our product, metrics, and strategic fit with [Your Company]?"

**Why this works:**
- You mention other parties (they know they're not the only one)
- You're exploring "options" (not desperate)
- You're asking if THEY'RE interested (not begging for interest)

---

## STEP 5: MANAGING THE MULTI-TRACK PROCESS

### The Delicate Balance

**DO:**
- ✅ Tell buyers you're talking to others (transparency builds trust)
- ✅ Give each buyer a timeline ("We'll make a decision by [date]")
- ✅ Play offers against each other (indirectly; "Other offers are in the $[X]M range")
- ✅ Ask for preliminary offers from all buyers simultaneously (creates deadline pressure)
- ✅ Negotiate terms with top choice, using others as leverage

**DON'T:**
- ❌ Lie about other offers (buyers will discover the truth; kills trust)
- ❌ Share confidential info from Buyer A with Buyer B (unethical; illegal if under NDA)
- ❌ Play games or string buyers along (sours relationships; they'll withdraw)
- ❌ Negotiate exclusively with buyer #1, then surprise them with offers from #2–5 (they'll feel manipulated)

---

## STEP 6: THE PUSH (WEEK 5–6)

**Once you have 2–3 preliminary offers, use them to push your top choice's price higher:**

### Example Scenario

| Buyer | Offer | Your Response |
|---|---|---|
| **Buyer #1 (your 1st choice)** | $[100]M | "Thanks. We prefer your fit, but [Buyer #2 is at] $[110]M. Can you get to $[105]M+?" |
| **Buyer #2** | $[110]M | "We like your offer. [Buyer #1] is offering $[100]M and has better product fit. Would you go to $[115]M to secure this?" |
| **Buyer #3** | $[95]M | "Thanks for the interest. We're focusing on Buyers #1–2 whose offers are higher." |

**Result:** Through competition, you push offers from $[100]M → $[105]M → $[115]M.

---

## STEP 7: EXCLUSIVITY (The Endgame)

**Once you've selected your winner, you'll sign an LOI with them. The LOI typically includes:**

**Exclusivity clause:**
> "During the [60]-day diligence period, Seller agrees to work exclusively with Buyer and not solicit or accept offers from other parties."

**This is normal.** Once you sign LOI with a buyer, you stop talking to others.

**But before signing LOI:**
- Get all offers on the table (step 6)
- Negotiate your top choice to the highest price possible
- THEN sign LOI (exclusivity kicks in)
- Then full diligence and closing

## RED FLAGS IN MULTI-TRACK PROCESS

🚩 **You lose leverage:**
- Buyers find out you're talking to competitors (gossip, industry rumors)
- You lose credibility by sharing confidential info or appearing to string them along
- Buyers withdraw ("We'll wait; they'll get back to us after other options fall through")

🚩 **You damage relationships:**
- Buyer #1 finds out Buyer #2 offered more; feels betrayed
- Buyer discovers you lied about other offers ("There are no other offers; you made them up")
- Post-close, buyer is resentful (bad for earnout cooperation)

---

## TIPS FOR RUNNING CLEAN MULTI-TRACK

### Transparency Rule
**Buyers PREFER transparency.** Tell them:
- "We're exploring options with [2–3] other parties"
- "We'll make a decision by [date]"
- "We prefer your strategic fit, but price matters"

They'll respect this. What they won't respect is:
- Lying about other offers
- Stringing them along indefinitely
- Making exclusive commitments you don't honor

### Information Firewall
**Keep each buyer conversation separate. DO NOT:**
- Tell Buyer A what Buyer B offered
- Share Buyer A's price with Buyer B
- Mix up confidential info between conversations

**DO:**
- Generalize: "Other offers are in the $[100–120]M range" (no names)
- Negotiate independently with each buyer
- Let them compete on quality, not on knowing others' details

### Timeline Discipline
**Set a decision date and stick to it:**
> "We'll make a decision by [date]. Please submit your best offer by [date −5 days]."

This creates urgency and prevents indefinite stalling.

## EXAMPLE: MULTI-TRACK IN ACTION

### Setup
- You: $[10]M ARR, 40% growth, 120% NRR
- Fair value: ~$[80–100]M
- You want to reach $[120]M through competitive pressure

### The Play

**Week 1:** Contact 3 buyers
- Buyer A (strategic competitor): "Interested; send us a 1-pager"
- Buyer B (PE firm): "Interested; let's schedule a call"
- Buyer C (customer's customer): "Let's talk"

**Week 2:** First calls
- Buyer A: "We see strategic fit; preliminary thought is $[100]M"
- Buyer B: "Solid company; preliminary thought is $[110]M"
- Buyer C: "Let's do deeper diligence first"

**Week 3:** You respond
- To Buyer A: "Excited. Can you get to $[110]M? [Buyer B] is at that range."
- To Buyer B: "Great offer. [Buyer A] has better strategic fit and product integration story. Would you go to $[120]M to secure this?"
- To Buyer C: "Thanks. Let's continue conversations, but want to focus on top 2 offers."

**Week 4:** Results
- Buyer A increases to $[115]M (fighting to win)
- Buyer B increases to $[120]M (doesn't want to lose)
- You pick Buyer B @ $[120]M (price achieved!)
- You sign LOI with Buyer B (exclusivity begins)

**Result:** Multi-track competition netted you +$[20–40]M vs. single-buyer negotiation.

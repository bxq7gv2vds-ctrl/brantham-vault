# BUYER TARGETING PLAYBOOK — Who to Call First

**Purpose:** Rank potential buyers by conversion likelihood + value  
**Use case:** Before you send Emails 1, you have 10-20 suspects. Which 5 do you prioritize?  
**Time:** 30 min to prioritize, then execute in order  

---

## QUICK FILTER: TIER BUYERS BY WARMTH + FIT

| Tier | Warmth | Fit | Conversion % | Call first? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1** | Warm intro | Perfect fit | 40-60% | ✅ YES |
| **Tier 2** | Warm intro | Good fit | 30-40% | ✅ YES |
| **Tier 3** | Cold | Perfect fit | 15-25% | ✅ NEXT |
| **Tier 4** | Cold | OK fit | 5-15% | ⏭️ Later |
| **Tier 5** | No context | Weak fit | <5% | ❌ Skip |

---

## TIER 1: WARM INTROS + PERFECT FIT

**These are your golden tickets.** Someone introduced you. They're in your market/adjacent.

**Who falls here:**
- ✅ Existing investor introduced you to them
- ✅ Competitor (that they acquired) already inside
- ✅ Customer referred them (they know your product)
- ✅ Board member has a relationship
- ✅ Your advisor has worked with them before

**Action:**
1. **Prep the intro** — Ask your connector: "What does [Buyer] care about most?"
2. **Intro email** — "I know [Buyer] loves [market]. [Your company] is similar."
3. **Expect response:** 48 hours
4. **Goal:** First call within 1 week

**Script for your connector:**
```
"I'm exploring a potential M&A conversation with [Buyer]. 
Would you be willing to introduce us? They'd probably be interested in our 
[market positioning / growth trajectory / customer base]."
```

---

## TIER 2: WARM INTROS + GOOD FIT

**Someone introduced you. They're in an adjacent market (not direct competitor).**

**Who falls here:**
- ✅ VCs on your cap table know the founder
- ✅ Other portfolio companies introduced you
- ✅ Advisor worked with their team before
- ✅ You met them at a conference, had a real conversation
- ✅ LinkedIn connection with 500+ shared connections

**Action:**
1. **Warm intro is CRITICAL** — Don't go cold for Tier 2
2. **Intro email** — Reference mutual connection + context
3. **Expect response:** 3-5 days
4. **Goal:** Call within 2 weeks

**Script:**
```
"[Mutual contact] mentioned [Buyer] is exploring the [market segment]. 
We've built something in that space that you might find interesting.

Would you be open to a quick conversation?"
```

---

## TIER 3: COLD + PERFECT FIT

**No warm intro, but they're your exact target (same market, similar customer).**

**Who falls here:**
- ✅ Direct competitor (that you're NOT afraid of)
- ✅ Buyer in your vertical SaaS category
- ✅ You found them via Crunchbase (they're acquiring in your space)
- ✅ They recently raised $$ (have capital)
- ✅ You read about their expansion into your market

**Action:**
1. **Research them deeply** — LinkedIn, recent news, team
2. **Personalize your outreach** — Mention something specific
3. **Expect response:** 10-20% (cold email reality)
4. **Goal:** First call within 4 weeks (takes multiple touches)

**Script:**
```
"I saw [recent news about Buyer]. We're doing something similar 
in [market segment]. 

Would love to share our data + see if there's a fit."
```

---

## TIER 4: COLD + ADJACENT FIT

**No warm intro. They're in a related space (not direct competitor, but potential acquirer).**

**Who falls here:**
- ✅ Buy the category you're in but not the exact segment
- ✅ PE firm focused on SaaS (but not your vertical)
- ✅ Strategic acquirer with a platform (you could be vertical 4 of 6)
- ✅ Buyer exploring new verticals

**Action:**
1. **Mass research** — Build a list of 20-30
2. **Batch outreach** — Send in waves (not all at once)
3. **Expect response:** 5-10% (cold, lower fit)
4. **Goal:** First call within 8 weeks (play the long game)

**Script:**
```
"I've followed [Buyer] because you're building in [market]. 
We've had strong traction in [adjacent segment].

Curious if you'd ever expand into [our market]."
```

---

## TIER 5: HAIL MARY / OPPORTUNISTIC

**Weak fit, but they have capital + track record of acquisitions.**

**Who falls here:**
- ✅ Large platform acquirer that buys anything (Hubspot, Salesforce)
- ✅ PE mega-fund with lots of dry powder
- ✅ Strategic acquirer famous for roll-ups (but different vertical)
- ✅ Venture capital investor (could lead secondary round)

**Action:**
1. **Only after Tiers 1-4 are exhausted**
2. **Teaser only** — No personal email (waste of time)
3. **Expect response:** <5%
4. **Goal:** Backup list only

**Why low priority:**
- Large acquirers get 100+ inbounds per year
- They'll take meetings if YOU have traction (they'll find you)
- Not worth time unless you're desperate

## PRIORITIZATION MATRIX

```
         |  COLD  |  WARM
---------|--------|--------
Perfect  | Tier 3 | Tier 1 ← START HERE
Good     | Tier 4 | Tier 2 ← THEN HERE
Weak     | Tier 5 | (rare)
```

**Execution order:**
1. Tier 1 (target: 5 warm intros)
2. Tier 2 (target: 5-10 warm intros)
3. Tier 3 (target: 10-15 cold emails)
4. Tier 4 (target: 20-30 cold emails)
5. Tier 5 (if needed, otherwise skip)

---

## BUYER TYPE PRIORITIZATION (Secondary filter)

**After you've tiered by fit/warmth, prioritize by buyer type:**

| Buyer Type | Conversion speed | Earnout reliability | Autonomy post-close | Target first? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Strategic (large SaaS co)** | Slow (60-90 days) | High | Medium | ✅ YES |
| **PE fund** | Medium (45-60 days) | Medium | Low | ✅ YES |
| **Founder / Acqui-hire** | Fast (30-45 days) | Low | High | ⏭️ AFTER |
| **Startup acquirer** | Medium (45-60 days) | Low | High | ⏭️ AFTER |
| **Private equity** | Slow (90-120 days) | High | Low | ✅ YES |
| **Financial buyer** | Very slow (120+ days) | Medium | High | ❌ AVOID |

**Rule of thumb:**
- **Start with Strategic** (fastest, cleanest)
- **Then PE** (if you want maximum value)
- **Avoid Financial buyers** (takes forever)

## RED FLAGS BY TIER

| Tier | Red flag | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1** | Warm intro is slow to respond | Chase your connector (they're flaky) |
| **Tier 2** | Takes >1 week to reply | Move down. Not as interested as you thought. |
| **Tier 3** | <10% response rate after 3 touches | Move to next batch. This one isn't biting. |
| **Tier 4** | No response to teaser | Expected. Keep trying. Law of large numbers. |
| **Tier 5** | Can't get anyone's attention | Skip. Focus on Tiers 1-3. |

---

## EXECUTION CALENDAR

**Week 1:**
- Identify 5 Tier 1 candidates
- Ask for warm intros
- Prep personalized emails

**Week 2:**
- Warm intros sent
- Tier 1 first calls happening
- Start Tier 2 outreach

**Week 3:**
- Tier 1 → LOI phase (hopefully)
- Tier 2 first calls
- Start Tier 3 cold outreach

**Week 4+:**
- Tier 1/2 in negotiation
- Tier 3/4 first calls coming in
- Keep feeding pipeline

- **Advisor/lawyer:** Tier 2 (they have network)
- **BD person:** Tier 3-4 (cold outreach, mass emails)
- **Finance:** Compile data room once buyer is qualified

## SUCCESS METRICS

**Target per tier:**

| Tier | # to contact | # of calls | # of LOIs |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | 5-10 | 3-5 | 1-2 |
| Tier 2 | 5-10 | 2-4 | 0-1 |
| Tier 3 | 15-20 | 2-3 | 0-1 |
| Tier 4 | 20-30 | 1-2 | 0 |

**Math:** For every 20 cold emails sent (Tiers 3-4), expect 1 LOI

---

## COMMON MISTAKES

| Mistake | Why it's bad | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| **Blasting cold emails to 100 Tier 5 buyers** | Low conversion, wastes time | Focus on Tiers 1-3 first |
| **Ignoring warm intros** | Cold is 5% conversion, warm is 40% | Ask your network FIRST |
| **Not personalizing Tier 3 emails** | Looks like spam | Mention something specific |
| **Waiting for responses before moving to next tier** | Deal takes 3 months, can't wait | Start Tier 2 while Tier 1 is thinking |
| **Not tracking responses** | Can't see what's working | Build a simple tracker |

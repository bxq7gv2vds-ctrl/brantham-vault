# Retention KPI Framework — Earnout Milestones

**Objectif :** Définir les 3-5 KPI mesurables qui déterminera si vous touchez 100% de l'earnout.

---

## Tier 1: Core Revenue KPI

| Metric | M1 | M6 | M12 | M24 | Earnout% |
|--------|----|----|-----|-----|----------|
| **ARR Growth** | No decline | +5% YoY | +10% YoY | +15% YoY | 40% |
| **Net Revenue Retention** | 90%+ | 95%+ | 100%+ | 105%+ | 30% |
| **Customer Churn (monthly)** | ≤3% | ≤2% | ≤2% | ≤1.5% | 20% |
| **Gross Margin** | Maintain | Maintain | +2% | +3% | 10% |

**Total Earnout: 100%** if 3+ of 4 metrics hit.

---

## Tier 2: Operational Milestones (Bonus, if revenue targets hit)

| Milestone | Trigger | Timeline | Bonus |
|-----------|---------|----------|-------|
| Customer Satisfaction | NPS ≥ 60 | M3 + M12 | +5% (if revenue on track) |
| Product Adoption | 80% feature usage | M6 | +3% (if revenue on track) |
| Sales Team Retention | 80%+ of team stays | M12 | +5% (if revenue on track) |

---

## Negotiation Guidance

**What to push for:**
- ✅ **Revenue-only metrics** (ARR/NRR) — you control these via retention + sales
- ✅ **NRR ≥ 100%** — signals healthy expansion (not just churn protection)
- ✅ **Lower churn threshold** (2%/mo = 24%/yr) — realistic, but tight
- ⚠️ **Avoid EBITDA targets** — buyer's margin controls this, not you
- ⚠️ **Avoid headcount milestones** — buyer may reduce team post-close

**What to push back on:**
- ❌ New customer acquisition targets — buyer controls GTM, pricing, CAC
- ❌ Product roadmap dependencies — you don't own roadmap post-close
- ❌ Market share targets — external factors (competition, macro)

## Risk Scenarios & Contingencies

### Scenario A: Revenue miss -10% (e.g., macro downturn)

**Trigger:** ARR drops below target by >8% at M6.

**Playbook:**
1. Immediate review: Is churn elevated or just new sales weak?
2. If churn = root cause → customer success surge (60-day check-in blitz)
3. If sales weak → negotiate with buyer for temporary pricing promo (recover ARR without churn)
4. Document all efforts; request "force majeure" carve-out for macro conditions

**Earnout impact:** -15% at M12 unless recovery plan executed

### Scenario B: Key customer churns (>5% ARR loss)

**Trigger:** Single customer ≥ 5% ARR leaves post-close.

**Playbook:**
1. Win-back: Founder + buyer ops do 2-week blitz
2. If unrecoverable → negotiate replacement deal (new customer acquisition credit towards NRR target)
3. Document customer health pre-close (was churn imminent?)

**Earnout impact:** -5-10% unless recovered within 60 days

### Scenario C: Buyer reduces support spend (churn rises)

**Trigger:** Support team cut → churn rises to 4%+/mo.

**Playbook:**
1. Escalate to buyer leadership: "This action breaches implicit earnout conditions"
2. Request buyer fund temporary outsourced support ($20-30k/mo)
3. Document all customer complaints tied to support reduction
4. If refused → invoke "Best Efforts Clause" in earnout agreement

**Earnout impact:** Negotiate down to 70-80% of target if buyer cost-cuts

## Red Flags (Renegotiation Trigger)

🚩 **Buyer tries to change KPI post-LOI** → Walk away
🚩 **Earnout >50% of deal** → Demand lower % or all-cash alternative
🚩 **KPI tied to buyer's actions** (e.g., sales spend, product roadmap) → Push back
🚩 **Churn target <2%/mo** → Unrealistic for most SaaS (ask for proof)

---

## Pre-Close Checklist

- [ ] Agree on measurement methodology (SaaS metrics standard = MRR/ARR/net churn calc)
- [ ] Define "customer" (min annual contract value? Stale customers?)
- [ ] Define "NRR" (expansion vs contraction vs churn-only)
- [ ] Agree on audit rights (buyer can spot-check customer contracts, but requires notice)
- [ ] Monthly reporting cadence (1st of month? Last day?)
- [ ] Bonus mechanics (if metric beats target by >5%, do you get % bonus?)
- [ ] Threshold = earnout void (if revenue drops >20%, does whole earnout disappear or partial?)

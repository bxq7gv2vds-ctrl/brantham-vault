# Founder Q&A Script — Appels Pré-LOI

**Use case:** Soft shop, first buyer calls, strategy calls with advisors  
**Length:** 30-45 min calls  
**Goal:** Position company favorably without overselling; establish credibility

---

## Part 1: Self-Introduction (60 sec)

**Buyer asks:** "Tell us about yourself and the company"

### Your script:
> "We started [COMPANY] in [YEAR] to solve [CUSTOMER PAIN]. I'm [YOUR NAME], founded [#] companies before, [RELEVANT BACKGROUND]. 
> 
> We've grown from [$X] to [$Y ARR] in [TIMEFRAME] with [GROWTH %] YoY growth. Our retention is [X%/month], and we're profitable on [METRIC: operating cash flow / GAAP depending on stage].
> 
> We serve [VERTICAL: SMBs/mid-market/enterprise] in [GEOGRAPHY], and our top customers include [NAME 2-3 recognizable names — anonymize if NDA'd]. 
> 
> The market opportunity is [$Z billion], and we have a defensible moat via [IP / network effects / switching costs]. We're here because [we want to accelerate / align with stronger distribution / focus on product]."

### Calibration
- **If you're in distress:** Focus on team quality, TAM, product-market fit—not revenue trending
- **If you're hot:** Lead with growth, unit economics, and founder track record
- **If you're in-between:** Emphasize predictable revenue, happy customers, and team bench

**What NOT to say:**
- ❌ "We're raising another round" (signals desperation)
- ❌ "Our last fundraise was hard" (shows weak positioning)
- ❌ "Honestly, we're just trying to get acquired" (kills valuation)

## Part 2: Financial Questions

### Q: "What's your current run rate?"

**Script:**
> "[ARR] in trailing twelve months. Last quarter was [$X], which is [+5-15%] sequential. We're on pace for [$Y] full-year."
> 
> **(If declining):** "Last two quarters were flat as we refocused from [XYZ] to [more profitable segment]. Now seeing [+5%] MoM re-acceleration."

**Pro tip:** Always give trailing twelve months (smooths volatility) + sequential to show momentum.

---

### Q: "What's your churn?"

**Script:**
> "[X%] monthly net churn at company level, [Y%] gross churn. This is [better/in-line with/higher than] SaaS median because [reasons: sticky product / long contracts / low CAC].
> 
> Our largest customer is [% of ARR], top 5 are [% of ARR]. No [single] customer >25% of ARR."

**If churn is high (>5%):**
> "We've been focused on [feature XYZ] and [sales retention] recently. Last cohort shows [X%] improvement. We've hired a VP Customer Success to reduce this to [target] by Q[X]."

**Red flag to avoid:** Never admit churn data is unavailable or "we haven't modeled it yet."

---

### Q: "What's your customer acquisition cost?"

**Script:**
> "Our blended CAC is $[X], payback in [Y] months at [Z%] gross margin. We acquire through [channel: self-serve / SMB sales / partnerships], and [CHANNEL] is our highest-efficiency channel at $[cost] CAC."
> 
> **LTV:CAC ratio:** [ratio] (we target >3:1, currently at [X:1]).

**If CAC is high (>12 month payback):**
> "We're in heavy land-and-expand mode (land at $X, expand to $Y ARR). Our LTV is $[total], which exceeds CAC 3.5x. We've seen [15-20%] net revenue retention from expansion."

---

### Q: "Are you GAAP profitable?"

**Script (if yes):**
> "Yes, we've been GAAP profitable since [DATE] with [X%] operating margin. We're reinvesting [some/most] of cash flow into [R&D / hiring]."

**Script (if no, but positive cash flow):**
> "We're GAAP negative at [X%] operating margin, but [positive/break-even] on operating cash flow. Our path to profitability is [specific plan + timeline]. With your scale, we can achieve [MARGIN%] by [DATE]."

**Script (if cash-burning):**
> "We're currently investing [X%] into [specific growth initiatives: sales team / product roadmap]. We have [Y] months of runway and hit [metric: unit economics / growth target] that trigger profitability."

---

### Q: "What's your gross margin?"

**Script:**
> "[X%] gross margin, with [breakdown: Y% COGS, Z% support/infra]. This is [improving / stable] as we [optimize / scale infrastructure]."
> 
> **By segment:** [SEGMENT A] is [X%] margin, [SEGMENT B] is [Y%], etc."

**Trajectory matters:** 
> "(If declining) We're seeing gross margin pressure from [reason]. By [DATE], we'll achieve [target%] through [specific action]."

---

## Part 3: Product & Market Questions

### Q: "What's your competitive advantage?"

**Script:**
> "Three things:
> 1. **Product:** [Specific feature / UX that competitors lack]. This took [X] engineering years to build.
> 2. **Customer base:** [Large installed base / brand / network effects] that competitors can't easily replicate.
> 3. **Team:** [Founder expertise / VP of Product's background] — we've done this before."
> 
> Market is [competitive / consolidated / emerging]. We don't compete on price; we [solve problem X / move faster / go deeper]."

**Red flags to avoid:**
- ❌ "We're the only player who does X" (probably untrue; buyer will fact-check)
- ❌ "We're cheaper than alternatives" (commoditized thinking)
- ❌ "Our team is the best" (subjective; back with facts)

---

### Q: "What's your market opportunity?"

**Script:**
> "TAM is [$X billion] globally, and we serve [sub-segment worth $Y billion]. We currently have [X%] market share, with [top 5 competitors holding Y%]."
> 
> **Path to $100M ARR:** [specific strategy: geographic expansion / verticals / product extensions]."

**Sources:** Name them (Gartner, IDC, internal research).

---

### Q: "What's your product roadmap for the next 12 months?"

**Script:**
> "[High-level, 3-4 initiatives without proprietary detail]
> 
> 1. **[Feature A]** — Customer request from top 5 accounts; estimated impact [$X additional ARR]
> 2. **[Integration B]** — Enables workflow that [outcome]; expands TAM by [%]
> 3. **[Performance improvement C]** — Reduce latency / increase throughput — requested by enterprise segment"
> 
> We're flexible on exact prioritization based on combined feedback."

---

## Part 4: Customer & Retention Questions

### Q: "Tell us about your top customers"

**Script:**
> "[CUSTOMER 1]: [$X ARR] in [VERTICAL], [since DATE]. They use [key feature]. Net retention: [Y%]. Expansion potential: $[Z] if we add [feature]."
> 
> "[CUSTOMER 2]: [$X ARR], [use case]. They're [happy / asking for feature XYZ]. Renewal expected [DATE]."
> 
> All top 20 are renewals; no customer lost in [last X months]."

**If you have churn risk:**
> "We had [one] customer churn in [DATE] because [reason]. We've addressed [specific fix], and similar customers now have [better outcome]."

---

### Q: "What's your NRR (net revenue retention)?"

**Script:**
> "We're at [X%] NRR, driven by [Y% expansion within existing customers, Z% from upsell/cross-sell / new cohort improvements]."
> 
> "By [DATE], we expect [X+5%] NRR as we [implement feature / hire CSM / improve onboarding]."

**If <100% (contraction):**
> "We've been at [X%] NRR due to [downsizing customers / market pressure]. However, [recent cohorts] show [improvement]. We expect to reach >100% by [DATE]."

---

## Part 5: Team & Scaling Questions

### Q: "What's your org structure?"

**Script:**
> "We have [X] people across [departments]:
> - Engineering ([Y] people, led by CTO/VP with [relevant exp])
> - Product/Design ([Z] people)
> - Sales/CS ([A] people)
> - Finance/Ops ([B] people)
> 
> We have [#] level-1 reports to me. Key bench: [next 2 hires that unlock [X growth threshold]]."

---

### Q: "How founder-dependent is this company?"

**Script:**
> "I'm the founder/CEO, but [COMPANY] is **not** founder-dependent because:
> 1. **VP/Head of [X]** makes all [product/technical] decisions; I'm mostly advisory on strategy
> 2. **Our top 10 customers** have [separate contracts / direct relationships] with [team member], not just me
> 3. **No single person** has >$[threshold] customer relationships or critical IP only they know
> 
> My role post-acquisition would be [advisory / board / interim head of X]."

**If you ARE critical:**
> "Today, I'm critical for [specific reason: investor relationships / strategic decisions]. My transition plan is [X-month role, then [advisory / board]]. We'll hire a [replacement] immediately post-close."

---

### Q: "What's your retention plan for the team?"

**Script:**
> "All [X] employees are critical. Our retention strategy:
> - $[X total earnout pool] for key roles
> - [X]-year [equity lock-up / continued vesting]
> - [Specific growth opportunity post-close: enter new market / expand product]
> 
> Our historical attrition: [X%] annual (below [Y%] SaaS median), mostly [specific reason: equity vesting / natural career moves]."

---

## Part 6: Risk & Challenge Questions

### Q: "What keeps you up at night?"

**Script:**
> "[Vulnerability + solution strategy]
> 
> 1. **Largest risk:** [Customer concentration / churn trend / market saturation]
>    - **Mitigation:** [specific plan + timeline]
> 
> 2. **Market risk:** [Competitive pressure / changing buyer needs]
>    - **Mitigation:** [recent hiring / product direction]
> 
> 3. **Team risk:** [Key person departure potential / skill gaps]
>    - **Mitigation:** [documentation / cross-training / backfill plan]
> 
> Overall, our business is [resilient / accelerating] because [reason]."

**Avoid:**
- ❌ "We don't really have risks" (not credible)
- ❌ "The market is shrinking" (why sell?)
- ❌ Dwelling on risks without solutions

---

### Q: "Have you been hacked or have any security issues?"

**Script:**
> "[Honest answer]:
> - **Zero breaches** — or —
> - **[Small incident, DATE]:** Compromised account credentials. We [remediated], notified [customers], and [implemented fix]. No data exfiltrated. Insurance claim [filed / resolved]."
> 
> Recent security posture:
> - SOC2 Type II certified (as of [DATE])
> - [CISO / VP Security] hired to manage compliance
> - Zero P1 vulnerabilities in last [X months]"

---

## Part 7: Deal Mechanics Questions

### Q: "What's your asking price / valuation?"

**Script:**
> "We haven't set a specific number yet, but here's our thinking:
> - **Comparable multiples:** SaaS companies at our growth rate trade at [X-Y] times ARR. We're at [lower/upper] end due to [unit economics / retention / defensibility].
> - **DCF:** Our base case DCF suggests [$X–Y million] enterprise value with [Z%] discount rate.
> - **Our expectations:** We'd like to see [$A–B million] all-in consideration. We're flexible on structure [cash / stock / earnout].
> 
> Happy to do a deep-dive on valuation once we've explored strategic fit."

**Avoid naming a number first** (anchors them low). Let THEM bid.

---

### Q: "What would keep you from selling?"

**Script:**
> "Our BATNA (best alternative):
> - **Raise Series [X]** — we have [Y] VC interest at [valuation]
> - **Continue independent** — path to profitability in [X months]
> 
> We'd walk if:
> 1. Price <[lower bound] (we have [capital / options])
> 2. Earn-out >50% of total comp (too risky)
> 3. Founder role is [specific unacceptable outcome]
> 4. Retention packages don't protect the team
> 
> So we're serious about exploring this IF [strategic fit / valuation / terms] make sense."

---

## Part 8: Questions TO ASK Buyer

**Don't just answer; ask back:**

1. **"What's driving your interest in [this sector / us specifically]?"**
   - *Signals* whether they're serious or just exploring

2. **"How does this fit into your strategy?"**
   - *Helps you understand integration risks and upside potential*

3. **"What's your timeline for closing?"**
   - *Determines urgency and walk-away date*

4. **"Have you acquired companies in our space before?"**
   - *Flags if they've successfully integrated or had post-close disasters*

5. **"What does success look like for you post-close?"**
   - *Reveals whether they'll push you to achieve earnout or deprioritize*

6. **"What due diligence would you need?"**
   - *Gauge intrusiveness and their seriousness level*

---

## General Rules

| Do ✅ | Don't ❌ |
|------|-------|
| Lead with narrative & momentum | Read from notes (seems unprepared) |
| Provide data to back claims | Make up numbers / project unrealistic growth |
| Acknowledge challenges honestly | Pretend everything is perfect |
| Ask questions back | Be a passive interviewee |
| Leave time for their questions | Talk for 40min straight |
| Follow up within 24h | Go silent for a week |
| Introduce advisors early | Surprise them with lawyers later |
| Emphasize defensibility & moat | Focus only on size of market |

---

## Post-Call Checklist

- [ ] Send thank-you email within 2 hours (shows eagerness)
- [ ] Share materials they asked for by next morning
- [ ] Circle back after 5 days if no follow-up (keep momentum)
- [ ] Loop in your M&A advisor with call summary (help with negotiation later)
- [ ] Document buyer's priorities / concerns (influence LOI draft)
- [ ] Assess cultural fit (Can you work with these people for 12-24 months?)

# Team & Counsel Guide (Tâches 29-30)

---

## NON-SOLICITATION CLAUSE (Tâche 29)

**What buyer wants:** Prevent you from hiring away their employees / customers.

| Clause | Buyer's Ask | Your Counter | Compromise |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Employee non-solicit** | 3 years, all employees | 12 months, direct reports only | 18 months, top 10 employees |
| **Customer non-solicit** | 3 years, all customers | 12 months, top 20 only | 18 months, top 10 customers |
| **Scope** | "Can't work in [broad industry]" | "Direct competitors only" | "Direct competitors, 18 months" |

**Your position:** This is not your job to negotiate. Buyer shouldn't demand you don't hire people or customers after the deal. It's unfair if you're staying 24 months. Reject broad non-solicit.

**Red line:** Don't accept non-compete > 18 months or > direct competitors. You need to work after this.

---

## SELLERS' COUNSEL BRIEF (Tâche 30)

**What to discuss with your M&A lawyer before LOI:**

| Topic | What To Cover |
|---|---|
| **Deal Structure** | Cash vs stock, earnout formula, hold-back amount |
| **Reps & Warranties** | What you're comfortable representing (and what you're not) |
| **Indemnity** | Cap, basket, survival period, escrow-funded |
| **Closing Conditions** | Key risks (financing, consents, MAC) |
| **Tax** | Installment sale treatment, Section 338 election, any tax traps |
| **Earnout Disputes** | How disputes get resolved, audit rights, timeline |
| **Non-Solicitation** | What's reasonable, what's not |
| **Budget & Timeline** | How much DD costs, how long to close, when do you get paid |

**Lawyer fees:** Expect $30-50K in legal fees (or 0.5-1% of deal value for larger deals).

**Three lawyers to have:**
1. **Your M&A lawyer** (primary, leads negotiation)
2. **Your accountant** (validates numbers, tax implications)
3. **Your personal financial advisor** (post-close planning, earnout risk)

## FINAL PRE-CLOSE CHECKLIST (Quick)

- [ ] All reps reviewed by counsel
- [ ] Earnout formula is clearly defined (not vague)
- [ ] Indemnity cap is reasonable (15% max)
- [ ] Non-solicitation is narrowly scoped (18m, direct competitors)
- [ ] Closing conditions are documented
- [ ] Financing is confirmed (if applicable)
- [ ] Escrow amount negotiated down to 12-15%
- [ ] Retention bonus is cash at close (not escrowed)
- [ ] Tax plan reviewed (installment sale? Section 338?)
- [ ] Earnout dispute resolution mechanism defined

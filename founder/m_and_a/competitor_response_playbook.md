# Competitor Response Playbook

**Trigger** : Buyer says "We're worried your product will cannibalize ours" or "Competitor will copy this."

---

## Response Framework

### Scenario 1: "Your product will cannibalize ours"

**Buyer concern** : They sell Product X at $100/mo. You sell Product Y at $50/mo (simpler, cheaper). Customers will switch.

**Your response** :
1. **Acknowledge** : "Valid concern. Here's why it's not an issue."
2. **Positioning** : "We serve [niche/use case] your product doesn't prioritize. Example: [customer]. They tried your product, it was overkill. Ours is simpler."
3. **Upsell path** : "More likely: we upgrade customers to your product. 30% of our users request [your feature]. Roadmap: upsell them to you."
4. **Data** : "3-year data: 0 customers switched from Y to X. But 5 tried X first, found it too complex, chose Y."

**Deal clause** : Offer to price your product **above current if needed** → Removes cannibalization risk. Example: $50 → $75/mo.

---

### Scenario 2: "Competitor will copy this"

**Buyer concern** : Competitor launches Product Z (clone of yours) in 6 months. Why pay for original?

**Your response** :
1. **IP protection** : "Patents/trade secrets in place. 18-month head start on clones."
2. **Switching cost** : "Data lock-in + customer success = high friction to switch. NRR 95%+ = stickiness."
3. **Execution moat** : "You + our team = speed. Competitor will take 12+ months to match. We'll have 3 new features."
4. **Market proof** : "We've already done the hard work: product validation, customer acquisition, market education. Competitor has to replicate all of that."

**Deal clause** : Tie earnout to **customer retention (NRR ≥95%)**, not just revenue. → Buyer invests in keeping customers happy, not just volume.

---

### Scenario 3: "We can build this ourselves cheaper"

**Buyer concern** : "Why buy you for $1.5M when we can hire 3 engineers for $300K/year?"

**Your response** :
1. **Time to market** : "3 engineers = 18 months to feature parity (with bugs). You're behind competitors 18 months."
2. **Opportunity cost** : "Those 3 engineers were supposed to build [next product]. Now they're rebuilding ours."
3. **Risk** : "Custom build = 40% failure rate (scope creep, engineer churn, tech debt). Acquiring proven product = 85% success."
4. **Data** : "We've already invested $[X]M in R&D. You get that for $1.5M."

**Example math** :
```
Build yourself:
├─ 3 engineers × $100K × 1.5 years = $450K salary
├─ Tech debt + rewrites = $100K+ in extra costs
├─ Opportunity cost (features they're not building) = $200K+ revenue loss
└─ Total hidden cost = $750K+

Acquire us:
└─ $1.5M upfront, $500K earnout = $2M total (but with proven customers, IP, team)
```

**Messaging** : "Cheaper than building, faster to market, lower risk. You win."

## Objection Handling (Quick Scripts)

| Objection | Your Response |
|-----------|---------------|
| "We can do it cheaper/faster" | "You're right you *could*. But risk is high; acquisition is 3x faster." |
| "Your product will cannibalize ours" | "Different market positions. Data: 0 cannibalization in 3 years. We actually upsell customers to you." |
| "Competitor will copy this in 6 months" | "Possible. But 18-month head start = defensible. Market leaders ship faster. You + us = unstoppable." |
| "We don't want distraction" | "Then acquire us, let us run independently. No integration headache. Pure revenue add." |
| "We need to think about it" | "Of course. Key timeline: market is moving fast. In 12 months, your competitors will have similar product. Faster to acquire than build." |

---

## Deal Sweetener (If Buyer Still Hesitant)

If buyer worried about ROI:
- **Lower upfront price** in exchange for **higher earnout** (tied to retention/revenue).
- Example: $1.2M cash + $300K earnout (if NRR ≥95% and ARR ≥ $[X]).
- Shifts risk to you (you ensure retention), removes buyer's risk.

---

## Avoid These Mistakes

❌ "Our product is way better than yours"  
✅ "Ours serves [niche], yours serves [enterprise]. Complementary."

❌ "Competitors can't copy us"  
✅ "We have 18-month head start. We'll stay ahead with continuous innovation."

❌ "You HAVE to acquire us or competitors will"  
✅ "Acquiring us is faster/cheaper than building. Your choice, but window is open 6 months."

---

## Prep Before Buyer Calls

- [ ] Competitor landscape: Who will copy you? Timeline?
- [ ] Your defensibility: IP, switching cost, execution speed?
- [ ] Buyer's product strategy: Where do you fit?
- [ ] Upside for buyer: Revenue add? Cost save? Market expansion?

**Messenger** : You, founder. (Buyer trusts founder narrative.)

---

## Follow-Up

After handling objection:
- Send 1-pager: "Why this acquisition succeeds" (backs up your verbal response).
- Data point: 3 case studies of similar acquisitions (didn't cannibalize, worked out well).
- Offer trial: "Let's do 30-day integration pilot with 1 customer. If it works, full acquisition."

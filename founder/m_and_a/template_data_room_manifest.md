# Data Room Manifest — 50+ Documents Buyers Expect

## Financial Documents (10–12 docs)

**Core financials:**
- [ ] P&L statements (3 years monthly, last 3 months weekly)
- [ ] Balance sheets (3 years annual, last 6 months monthly)
- [ ] Cash flow statements (3 years annual, last 6 months monthly)
- [ ] ARR/MRR detail (by customer cohort, monthly trend)
- [ ] Revenue schedule (breakdown by product, customer segment)

**Analysis:**
- [ ] EBITDA normalization (add-backs for non-recurring items)
- [ ] Churn analysis (12-month cohort retention, seasonality)
- [ ] Unit economics (CAC, payback period, LTV by cohort)
- [ ] Synergies analysis (estimated COGS reduction post-acquisition, if any)
- [ ] Tax filings (last 3 years 1040/1120-S, any back taxes?)

**Budget & forecasts:**
- [ ] Next 3 years forward revenue budget (assumptions documented)
- [ ] Headcount plan (roles, titles, salaries for next 12m)

---

## Customer Documents (6–8 docs)

**Contracts & relationships:**
- [ ] Master customer list (Name, ARR, monthly usage, churn risk, renewal date)
- [ ] Top 10 customer contracts (with change-of-control clauses highlighted)
- [ ] NDA/MSA templates (all versions used)
- [ ] Channel/reseller agreements (if any)
- [ ] Customer references (optional, but 3–5 willing to speak to buyer = gold)

**Health metrics:**
- [ ] NPS survey results (last 2 administrations)
- [ ] Churn detail by customer (reasons, cohort analysis)
- [ ] Support ticket volume & CSAT (if tracked)

---

## Product & Technology (6–8 docs)

**Product:**
- [ ] Product roadmap (12-month forward view, with timeline)
- [ ] Feature adoption metrics (which features drive retention?)
- [ ] Roadmap dependencies (if feature X relies on tech debt removal, flag it)
- [ ] Competitive positioning (how do you differ from [competitor])

**Tech stack & architecture:**
- [ ] Tech stack inventory (databases, APIs, languages, tools, licensing)
- [ ] Architecture diagram (how does data flow? What's the bottleneck?)
- [ ] Infrastructure costs (AWS, Stripe, etc. monthly burn)
- [ ] Deployment process (CI/CD pipeline, testing, rollback)
- [ ] Known tech debt (be honest; buyers will find it anyway)
- [ ] Security/compliance status (SOC2? GDPR? Penetration test? Be proactive)

---

## People & Organization (4–6 docs)

**Team:**
- [ ] Org chart (names, titles, reports, salary bands)
- [ ] Key employee retention agreements (if requiring post-close stays)
- [ ] Employee option pool (vesting schedule, strike price)
- [ ] Employment agreements (sample 2–3, showing equity/bonus terms)
- [ ] Headcount history (hires, departures, why people left)

**Employment liability:**
- [ ] Any pending/resolved employment claims? (Yes = disclose upfront)

---

## Legal & Compliance (8–10 docs)

**Core legal:**
- [ ] Certificate of incorporation & bylaws
- [ ] Cap table (fully diluted, including options with 4-year vest)
- [ ] Board resolutions (major decisions: funding, grants, policies)
- [ ] Articles of incorporation (Delaware C-corp standard)

**Contracts & commitments:**
- [ ] All material contracts (>$10k/year or strategic importance)
  - Vendors, SaaS subscriptions, contractors, partnerships
  - **Key action:** Flag contracts with change-of-control clauses
- [ ] Debt schedule (any notes, loans, lines of credit? Amounts, terms, covenants)
- [ ] Insurance policies (E&O, general liability, D&O)

**Compliance:**
- [ ] Privacy policy & terms of service (any audits or complaints?)
- [ ] SOC2 report (if obtained; demonstrates security/compliance)
- [ ] GDPR/CCPA compliance memo (any audits? Data handling practices?)
- [ ] Security policies (incident response, bug bounty, penetration test results if any)

**IP & licensing:**
- [ ] IP ownership documentation (who owns code, trademarks, patents?)
- [ ] Open-source audit (what OSS licenses are you using? Any GPL? Copyleft?)
- [ ] Trademark registrations & applications
- [ ] Domain registrations & certificates

**Litigation & claims:**
- [ ] Any pending/threatened litigation? (Be upfront; nondisclosure kills deals)
- [ ] Tax liens or audits? (Any back taxes owed?)
- [ ] Regulatory inquiries? (FTC, state AG, industry bodies?)

---

## Operational Documents (4–6 docs)

**Financial & operations:**
- [ ] Accounting policies (revenue recognition, expense capitalization, etc.)
- [ ] Banking & payroll information (accounts, frequency, approval authority)
- [ ] Spreadsheets critical to business (e.g., if your forecasts live in Excel, share them)

**Operational processes:**
- [ ] Onboarding process (how do customers get up and running?)
- [ ] Support/SLA documentation (response times, uptime commitments)
- [ ] Quarterly business review (QBR) templates / customer success processes

---

## Optional But Recommended (2–4 docs)

- [ ] Pitch deck (2–3 versions; shows your positioning over time)
- [ ] Press releases & media mentions (social proof)
- [ ] Customer case studies (3–5; shows impact)
- [ ] Founder bio & vision statement (helps buyer understand culture)

---

## What NOT to Include

❌ Private emails or Slack DMs  
❌ Founder personal financial info  
❌ Board meeting minutes (summarize decisions instead)  
❌ HR disciplinary records (keep private unless relevant to acquisition)  
❌ Salaries of individual employees (give ranges or bands, not names)  

---

## Data Room Platform & Access

**Recommended platforms:**
- Intralinks (enterprise favorite)
- Firmex (mid-market standard)
- Google Drive (simple, free; adequate for small deals)
- Dropbox (less ideal; no granular access control)

**Access control:**
- Buyer & counsel: Full access
- Buyer's accountant/banker: Limited to financials & strategy
- Buyer's technical lead: Limited to tech stack & architecture
- Log all views (audit trail required)

**Release strategy:**
- Phase 1 (after LOI): Core financials, customer list, cap table
- Phase 2 (week 2): Contracts, compliance docs
- Phase 3 (week 3): Tech architecture, operational detail
- Hold back: Salary details, employee names until final weeks

---

## Pre-Data Room Preparation Checklist

- [ ] Redact/anonymize what's sensitive (employee names, specific salaries)
- [ ] Organize with clear folder structure (Finance, Customers, Legal, Tech, People, Ops)
- [ ] Create index/manifest (this document)
- [ ] Have your counsel pre-review docs (remove privilege if needed)
- [ ] Test access to make sure links work
- [ ] Prepare FAQ doc (answers to likely Q's about red flags)
- [ ] Brief your team: "Data room goes live Monday; don't panic if strangers ask questions"

---

## Expected Timeline

- **Days 1–3:** Buyer reviews financials, customer list, top contracts
- **Days 4–7:** Buyer & counsel review legal, cap table, IP
- **Days 8–14:** Buyer's technical lead digs into tech stack, architecture
- **Days 14–30:** Detailed diligence questions, reference calls, site visits
- **Days 30–45:** Final questions, representations & warranties insurance quote
- **Days 45–60:** Purchase agreement finalized, closing readied

# Reps & Warranties Negotiation (Seller's Playbook)

## What Are Reps & Warranties?

**Reps** = Representations (claims about your company)  
**Warranties** = Guarantees (you stand behind these claims in escrow)

### Common Reps (Seller Must Claim)
- "Business is organized and operates validly"
- "Financial statements are accurate"
- "No pending litigation"
- "IP owned or properly licensed"
- "Customers are not concentrated (top 10 < 50%)"
- "No material contracts undisclosed"
- "No environmental/labor violations"

**If wrong** → You pay from escrow (indemnification).

---

## The Money: Escrow & Basket

### Escrow = Your Money Held Hostage
```
Deal price:         $10M
Escrow holdback:    10% = $1M (held 18 months)
Cash at close:      $9M

After 18 months:
  ✓ No claims → You get $1M
  ✗ Claims filed → Escrow pays them, you get less
```

### Basket = Threshold Before Escrow Pays
```
Basket = $50k
  → Claims under $50k = you don't get paid
  → Claim #1: $30k (doesn't hit basket)
  → Claim #2: $40k (total $70k, now escrow pays from first claim)
```

**Larger basket = better for you (claims need to add up to $50k to sting).**

---

## Negotiation Anchors (What to Ask For)

| Item | Buyer Wants | You Should Ask For | Negotiating Compromise |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Escrow %** | 15% | 5% | 10% |
| **Escrow Duration** | 24 months | 12 months | 18 months |
| **Basket** | $250k | $100k | $150k |
| **Cap on Total Claims** | Unlimited | 50% of escrow | 75% of escrow |
| **Survival Period** | 24 months all reps | 12-18 months (18 for IP/tax) | Tiered (IP longer, others shorter) |

---

## Tiered Survival Example

```
Standard reps (org, capitalization, employees):
  → Survive 12 months

Operational reps (financials, customers, contracts):
  → Survive 18 months

Long-tail reps (IP, tax, litigation):
  → Survive 24 months
```

**Why this works:** Buyer gets protection on risky items, you get earlier release on known-good items.

## Red Line Reps (What to Resist)

### ❌ Too Broad
- "All financial statements are accurate in all material respects"
  - **Problem:** "Material" is vague. Buyer interprets as ANY error.
  - **Counter:** "...except as would not reasonably be expected to have a Material Adverse Effect" (MAE defined as $200k+ impact)

### ❌ Impossible to Warranty
- "No pending or threatened litigation"
  - **Problem:** Seller can't know if someone's *thinking* of suing.
  - **Counter:** "...of which company has received written notice"

### ❌ Retroactive Liability
- "All tax filings are accurate and timely"
  - **Problem:** You could owe IRS back taxes discovered years later.
  - **Counter:** "Tax reps survive 6 years" (statute of limitations), "Escrow for tax = $100k max"

## How to Respond to Buyer's Reps List

### Step 1: Accept Non-Controversial Ones
```
Buyer: "Business validly organized?"
You: "Yes, accepted."

Buyer: "No pending litigation?"
You: "Correct, accepted."
```

### Step 2: Negotiate the Fuzzy Ones
```
Buyer: "All financial statements accurate in all material respects?"
You: "Define 'material.' We accept 'materially accurate' if material = 
      greater than $200k or 5% of revenue individually and in aggregate."
```

### Step 3: Add Carve-Outs
```
Buyer: "No material adverse changes"
You: "Agreed, except for industry-wide events (market downturn, 
      regulatory change, acts of God)."
```

### Step 4: Cap Your Indemnity
```
Buyer: "Seller indemnifies buyer for all breaches"
You: "Seller indemnifies up to X% of purchase price, 
      and only if claims exceed basket of $Y."
```

## Escrow Deal Killer: Walk-Away Points

### Walk Away If Buyer Demands
- [ ] Escrow > 15% of deal price
- [ ] Escrow duration > 24 months (except for tax, which can be 6 years)
- [ ] Basket > 1-2% of deal price
- [ ] Cap on total claims > 50% of escrow
- [ ] Reps survive longer than 24 months (except tax & IP)
- [ ] Personal reps about your character/reputation
- [ ] Unlimited indemnification (cap it at least to escrow amount)

## Practical Escrow Math

**Deal: $10M, $1M escrow (10%), 18-month hold**

### Scenario A: Clean Exit
```
Customer files claim: "Vendor contract not properly transferred"
  Amount: $30k
  Basket: $150k
  
Result: Escrow absorbs it. You still get $1M at 18 months.
Status: ✓ Happy
```

### Scenario B: Multi-Claim Hits Basket
```
Claim 1: Vendor contract error = $80k
Claim 2: Tax filing late in small market = $120k
Total: $200k
  
Basket: $150k
Escrow cap: 75% of escrow ($750k)

Result: Escrow pays $200k - $150k = $50k from your escrow.
Final: You get $1M - $50k = $950k at 18 months.
Status: ✓ Acceptable
```

### Scenario C: Big Claim
```
IP claim (buyer didn't have clean license to patent):
  Amount: $500k
  Escrow: $1M
  Cap: 75% = $750k
  
Result: Buyer loses $500k claim against $1M escrow.
Final: You get $1M - $500k = $500k at 18 months.
       Buyer ate remaining $500k (no tail insurance).
Status: 😕 Not great, but you're only on hook for escrow
```

## Negotiation Tactics

### Move 1: Trade Escrow % for Basket
```
Buyer: "We need 15% escrow, 24-month hold"
You: "How about 12% escrow, but we define basket at $200k + tiers?"
```

### Move 2: Parcel Out Reps by Confidence
```
You: "Reps we're very confident in (ops, headcount) = 12m survival.
      Reps with some uncertainty (customer concentration) = 18m.
      Tax/IP/litigation = 24m (as defined by law)."
```

### Move 3: Add Carve-Outs for Industry Events
```
Buyer: "Revenue must stay above $X through close"
You: "Agreed, except we're carving out [product launch delay, 
      customer request for feature], normal ops."
```

### Move 4: Tail Insurance (D&O Insurance)
```
If buyer insists on long survival/high cap:
"We'll buy 12-month tail D&O insurance (covers us both) 
 for up to $X. That covers the gaps."
```

---

## Checklist: Before Signing Reps

- [ ] Read every single rep (takes 2 hours)
- [ ] Mark which ones you 100% stand behind
- [ ] Mark which ones need carve-outs
- [ ] Discuss with lawyer (not optional)
- [ ] Calculate: worst-case escrow loss under reps
- [ ] Compare to walk-away price (is escrow amount worth the risk?)
- [ ] Ensure survival dates are reasonable (not indefinite)
- [ ] Confirm indemnification CAP (don't accept "unlimited")

# Playbook Négociation — Perspective Vendeur

**Utilité** : tactiques et leviers pour maximiser exit value en tant que fondateur.

---

## Phase 1 : Préparation (3 mois avant)

### Define Your BATNA (Best Alternative to Negotiated Agreement)
```
- Plan B: rester indépendant? → valeur annuelle actuelle
- Plan C: levée de capital next round? → dilution + timeline
- Plan D: IPO? → timeline 18–24m, coûts $5M+
- Plan E: maintenir status quo? → croissance organique

BATNA chiffré = votre walk-away number
```

### Position de Force = Créer Compétition
```
✓ Tête-à-tête avec 1 buyer = buyer dicte prix (0 leverage)
✓ 2 buyers sérieux = +15% multiple
✓ 3+ buyers en due diligence parallèle = +25–30% multiple

Tactique: "soft shop" (avocats à 2–3 buyers sélectionnés, pas de data room yet)
```

### Prepare Narrative
```
Deck de 10 slides:
1. Market opportunity (TAM)
2. Product/solution unique
3. Growth trajectory (3 ans)
4. Unit economics (CAC/LTV/NRR)
5. Competitive moat
6. Team depth
7. Synergies pour acquéreur
8. Revenue pipeline
9. Risk mitigants
10. Use of proceeds post-deal

Notes: chiffres vs storytelling (60/40 split)
       focus acheteur priorities, pas tes miennes
```

## Phase 2 : Early Discussions

### First Conversation
```
OBJECTIVE = get meeting, not close deal.

❌ Talk price (too early, anchors you low)
❌ Admit time pressure ("need to raise in Q3")
❌ Share cap table (gives away options upside)

✓ Ask buyer about criteria, timeline, strategy
✓ Understand their synergies
✓ Share vision (ambition, not current state)
✓ Hint at competitive interest (soft, not aggressive)
```

### LOI Negotiation (Letter of Intent)

**Key Terms to Fight For:**
```
1. VALUATION: specify enterprise value, not price per share
   (buyers love to lowball via diluted cap table)
   
2. CONSIDERATION MIX:
   - Cash now? → leverage
   - Stock in buyer? → tread carefully (illiquid)
   - Earnout? → only if <20% of total price, metrics seller-controllable
   
3. REPS SURVIVAL:
   ❌ "All reps survive 24 months"
   ✓ "General reps 18 months, tax reps 6 years"
   (tax stuff you can't control; general = things you knew)
   
4. CAP ON INDEMNITY:
   ❌ "Uncapped"
   ✓ "€1M basket, €5M cap, 0.5% of price deductible"
   (protects you against trivial claims)
   
5. TERMINATION FEES:
   If buyer walks = reverse termination fee
   Example: you agree 90-day exclusivity, buyer leaves day 91
   → Reverse fee = 1% of deal price
   
6. FINANCING CONDITION:
   ❌ "Buyer financing not approved → deal dies, no recourse"
   ✓ "Buyer financing condition dropped after 45 days"
   (forces commitment, or you can shop again)
   
7. GO-SHOP PERIOD:
   Negotiated exit = you get 40–60 day "go-shop"
   (can solicit other offers, only matched if buyer matches)
```

**LOI Template Negotiation Sequence:**
```
Round 1: Buyer sends LOI (inevitably favoring them)
         → You return marked-up (20–30% changes)

Round 2: Buyer comes back with counter
         → Negotiate key 5 terms (price, reps cap, earnout, fees)

Round 3: Sign LOI when misaligned on <2 issues
         (leave for due diligence to resolve)
```

## Phase 3 : Due Diligence

### Information Asymmetry is Your Leverage
```
They ask: "Why did top customer almost leave in Q2?"
You answer: "Normal contract negotiation. Renewed at 20% uplift."
(Don't volunteer that you almost lost them.)

Rule: Answer the question asked, no more.
      If they don't ask about X, don't disclose X.
      (Unless explicitly required by reps, LOI, or law)
```

### Manage Data Room
```
✓ Organize clearly (financial docs, legal, technical, customers)
✓ Summarize complex docs (1-page summary on technical debt, litigation, etc.)
✓ Offer "office hours" for Q&A (control the narrative)

❌ Don't include random emails, slack threads, internal plans
❌ Don't volunteer risk (let them find it if they can)
❌ Don't answer hypotheticals ("what if we did X?")
```

### Red Flag: Buyer Keeps Extending Timeline
```
Week 6: "Need 2 more weeks for technical audit"
Week 8: "Need board approval on revised price"
Week 10: "Need financing confirmation"

Signal: Buyer losing interest or financials deteriorating

Action: Send email: "We're excited but need path to close.
        Can we set hard closing deadline? Else we'll continue pursuing
        other opportunities."
```

## Phase 4 : Price Negotiation

### Anchoring
```
Buyer's opening: "We think $15M is fair."
Your counter: DON'T say "$25M." 
Instead: "We're at $28M based on comparable transactions 
         and our growth trajectory."
(Start high, justified. Gives room to come down.)

Rule of thumb: Start 30–40% above your BATNA.
```

### Multiple Expansion Arguments

| Metric | Impact | Argument |
|--------|--------|----------|
| 50%+ growth | +25% | "Defying market growth, premium justified" |
| NRR >120% | +20% | "Rare for our market, signals durability" |
| <5% churn | +15% | "Sticky customers = predictable revenue" |
| $X installed base | +10% | "Switching costs create moat" |
| Team depth | +15% | "Founders aren't bottleneck" |

**Cumulative**: 4 factors = 50–70% multiple uplift justified.

## Phase 5 : Closing Negotiations

### Earnout Mechanics (If Offered)
```
❌ Bad earnout: "If you hit $50M revenue in Y2, +$5M"
   Problem: you don't control revenue (buyer can cut budget)

✓ Good earnout: "If customer retention >80% at end of Y1 AND
   net revenue uplift >$10M, +$3M"
   (Metric YOU control, can deliver on)
```

### Escrow & Holdback
```
Typical: 15–20% holdback for 12–18 months

Negotiate:
- Basket: exclude claims <$50k (stops nuisance claims)
- Cap: limit total holdback → don't drag on forever
- Release schedule: 50% at 6m, 50% at 12m
```

### Reps Insurance (Alternative to Escrow)
```
If deal >$10M, buyer may buy "rep insurance"
Cost: 1–3% of deal price (one-time premium)
Covers: breaches of reps up to cap

For seller: prefer to let buyer buy insurance (removes holdback friction)
For buyer: reduces your escrow, but pays premium
```

## Phase 6 : Closing

### Final Leverage: 11th-Hour Discoveries
```
Buyer discovers: "You have $200k of unpaid invoices from Q1."

❌ You panic: "Let me discount the price."
✓ You respond: "These are normal DSO (45–60 days).
              Factored into EBITDA. Not a surprise."

Rule: If issue is material, it should be discovered in week 4 of DD.
      If found week 11, buyer should have vetted. Not your fault.
```

### Signing Day
```
- Cash wire: get confirmed, not "ACH tomorrow"
- Releases: all shareholders signed indemnity waivers
- Lockup: understand your non-compete (usually 6–12 months)
- Retention: if you're staying, clarify role/comp post-close
```

---

## Summary: Leverage Checklist

- [ ] Created competitive tension (2+ bidders)
- [ ] LOI caps your reps to 18 months general, 6 years tax
- [ ] Earnout <20% of price, metrics seller-controllable
- [ ] Financing condition expires after 45 days (or unconditional)
- [ ] Reverse termination fee if buyer walks
- [ ] Reps insurance in place (or minimal escrow)
- [ ] Walk-away price defined (BATNA)
- [ ] Board alignment (no internal veto risk)
- [ ] Founder post-deal role clear (avoid limbo)

**Negotiation ceiling**: You can rarely get 100% of what you want.
                         Pick your 3 most important items. Fight for those.
                         Concede on the rest.

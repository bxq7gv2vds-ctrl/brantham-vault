# Timeline Réaliste: LOI à Close — Jalons & Durées

**Expectation setting pour buyers. La plupart des deals prennent 90–120 jours. Voici pourquoi.**

---

## Timeline Standard (90 Jours)

```
Day 0–5: LOI Signature
├─ Seller draft & buyer review (~2 days)
├─ Negotiation (exclusivity period, price) (~3 days)
└─ Sign

Day 5–35: Initial Due Diligence (30 days)
├─ Financial audit (3y statements, tax returns, SaaS metrics)
├─ Legal audit (cap table, IP, contracts, compliance)
├─ Tech audit (GitHub, architecture, security scan, code review)
├─ Customer reference calls (top 5–10 customers)
└─ Management interviews

Day 35–50: Reps & Warranties Insurance Quotes (15 days)
├─ RWI broker engages (parallel to legal diligence)
├─ 3 quotes received (~2–4% of deal value)
└─ Buyer selects insurer

Day 35–65: SPA Drafting (30 days)
├─ Buyer's counsel drafts (10 days) — send seller first draft
├─ Seller's counsel review & redline (~5 days)
├─ Negotiation (reps scope, indemnity cap, escrow %) (~10 days)
├─ Schedules & disclosure attachments finalized (~5 days)
└─ Final SPA signed

Day 50–75: Deep Diligence (25 days)
├─ Follow-up questions from initial DD (10 days)
├─ Key employee interviews & retention agreements (5 days)
├─ Board resolution & shareholder approvals (5 days)
├─ Final management presentation (day 70)
└─ Buyer internal sign-off (day 75)

Day 75–90: Closing Prep (15 days)
├─ Closing document checklist (10 days)
├─ Tax filings & escrow setup (5 days)
├─ Final walkthrough (day 88)
└─ **Day 90: CLOSE**

```

## Actual Timing (What Slows Things Down)

| Slowdown | Typical Delay | How to Prevent |
|----------|---------------|----------------|
| **Buyer's legal team slow** | +10–20 days | Push for parallel DD/SPA drafting, not sequential. |
| **Customer concentration issue** | +15–30 days | Disclose upfront. Have mitigation plan ready. |
| **IP disputes** (contractor, co-founder) | +30–60 days | Resolve *before* LOI. Cost buyer's trust. |
| **Reps insurance hard to place** | +7–14 days | Engage broker on Day 5, not Day 35. |
| **Key employee won't sign retention** | +10–21 days | Negotiate with team *before* LOI. Pre-commit. |
| **Tax issue discovered in DD** | +14–30 days | Get tax counsel review *before* LOI. Disclose. |
| **Buyer's board approval slow** | +14–21 days | Know their decision cadence. Aim for pre-board review. |
| **Multiple buyer offers** | +14–60 days | Run parallel tracks. Create urgency (auction mentality). |

**Pro tip:** The *single biggest delay* is sequential legal review. Insist on parallel diligence + SPA drafting from day 5 onward.

## Timeline by Buyer Type

### Strategic (Vertical Consolidator)
- **Typical:** 90–120 days
- **Why longer:** Board approval, integration planning, customer retention strategy
- **Accelerator:** Clear integration roadmap, customer retention proof

### Strategic (Adjacent/Tech)
- **Typical:** 100–130 days
- **Why longer:** Need to prove technical compatibility, engineering integration time
- **Accelerator:** Clean codebase, clear architecture, DevOps ready

### Financial (PE)
- **Typical:** 120–150 days
- **Why longer:** Deeper financial DD, operational improvements pre-close, multiple rounds of due diligence
- **Accelerator:** Audited financials, clean SaaS metrics, EBITDA clarity

### Corporate (Large tech)
- **Typical:** 140–180 days
- **Why longer:** Legal complexity, multiple approvals, antitrust concerns possible, integration bureaucracy
- **Accelerator:** Alleviate legal risk, show no antitrust issues, have exec champion

### Acquihire (Team Only)
- **Typical:** 30–60 days
- **Why faster:** Less DD, simpler legal, focus is speed to value

## Month-by-Month Breakdown

### Month 1 (Days 0–30)
**Objective:** Anchor deal economics, kick off due diligence.

| Week | Action | Owner |
|------|--------|-------|
| W1 | LOI drafted, reviewed, signed | Seller + Buyer legal |
| W2 | Data room structure finalized; buyer gets access | Seller ops |
| W3–W4 | Initial financial audit begins; legal questions sent | Buyer | 

**Seller effort:** 80 hours (prep docs, answer initial questions, manager interviews)

**Buyer effort:** 120 hours (financial review, legal audit, customer calls)

---

### Month 2 (Days 30–60)
**Objective:** DD diligence 80% complete, SPA in advanced draft stage.

| Week | Action | Owner |
|------|--------|-------|
| W5–W6 | Deep diligence; follow-up questions intense | Buyer |
| W6–W7 | SPA first draft from buyer counsel | Buyer legal |
| W8 | SPA redline from seller; negotiation begins | Seller + Buyer legal |

**Seller effort:** 100 hours (diligence responses, SPA negotiation, interviews)

**Buyer effort:** 150 hours (deep dives, SPA drafting, internal review)

---

### Month 3 (Days 60–90)
**Objective:** SPA finalized, key conditions precedent met, closing documents prepped.

| Week | Action | Owner |
|------|--------|-------|
| W9–W10 | SPA final negotiation; reps insurance bound | Both counsel |
| W10–W11 | Closing document prep (cap table transfer, IP assignments) | Seller + Both counsel |
| W12 | Final walkthrough; closing day prep | Both parties |
| W12 (close) | **Signing & wire transfer** | All |

**Seller effort:** 60 hours (final negotiations, closing doc prep)

**Buyer effort:** 100 hours (final legal, board sign-off, integration planning)

## What to Communicate to Buyers

**At LOI signature:**

> "Based on our experience, typical acquisitions close in 90–120 days from LOI. Here's the timeline:
> - Days 0–30: Initial DD, SPA kick-off
> - Days 30–60: Deep DD, SPA drafting & negotiation
> - Days 60–90: Final DD, closing prep
>
> We've identified 3 potential slowdowns (customer concentration, [key person], [specific item]) and have mitigation plans. Parallel work (DD + SPA drafting, not sequential) will keep us on track."

**If buyer says "Can we close in 60 days?"**

"Unlikely. Here's why:
- RWI insurance alone takes 14–20 days to quote
- SPA negotiation typically 20–25 days
- Board/legal review ~15 days
- Parallel work gets us to 90. Sequential takes 150+."

*Then:* "What if we prioritize [specific item]? We can accelerate by [X days]."

## Risk Mitigations (Pre-LOI)

🟢 **Pre-sign LOI:**
- [ ] All cap table questions answered (audited cap table)
- [ ] IP assignments confirmed (no disputes)
- [ ] Tax returns pre-reviewed (no surprises)
- [ ] Key employees pre-committed to stay
- [ ] RWI broker identified & ready to quote (day 5)

**Impact:** Saves 10–15 days

🟡 **Insist on parallel work:**
- [ ] Due diligence *and* SPA drafting simultaneously, not sequentially
- [ ] Financial DD doesn't block legal DD
- [ ] Customer calls don't wait for buyer's internal approval

**Impact:** Saves 15–20 days

🔴 **Red flag if buyer insists on:**
- Sequential work (DD first, then SPA) = bad sign. They're not serious.
- No timeline commitment = they're still exploring.
- Buyer's counsel can't start until DD complete = process blocker.

## Extensions & Why They Happen

**Common reasons for going 120–180 days instead of 90:**

1. **Buyer's board only meets quarterly** → Add 30 days if LOI falls between meetings
2. **Tax audit surface unexpectedly** → Add 20–40 days
3. **Key customer threatens to leave** → Add 14–21 days for retention negotiation
4. **Multiple buyer competition** → May extend to run mini-auction (actually good for you)
5. **Buyer's integration team slow** → Add 10–30 days if they're redesigning product

**Mitigation:** Build in a 30-day buffer. If you target day 90, you'll likely close day 100–110. If you target day 60, you'll miss it and look disorganized.

## How to Speed Up

**Target 75 days instead of 90:**

1. **Pre-LOI:** Resolve all IP/tax/cap table issues. (Saves 10–15 days)
2. **Insist on parallel diligence:** Day 5, both legal AND financial teams start simultaneously. (Saves 15 days)
3. **RWI broker on Day 5, not Day 35:** (Saves 10 days)
4. **SPA template prepared pre-LOI:** (Saves 5 days)

**Reality:** Even with optimization, 75–80 days is floor. If buyer pushes harder, something's wrong or they're not serious.

## Closing Checklist (Final 7 Days)

- [ ] All schedules finalized
- [ ] RWI policy bound
- [ ] Escrow account setup & wired
- [ ] Cap table transfer paperwork signed
- [ ] IP assignments executed
- [ ] Final walkthrough completed
- [ ] All closing docs ready for signature
- [ ] Wire instructions confirmed (fraud prevention)
- [ ] Announcement drafted (not sent until post-close)

---

**One final thought:** The longest deals happen when communication breaks down. Weekly calls (buyer + seller + counsel) prevent misunderstandings. If comms slow, timeline stretches.

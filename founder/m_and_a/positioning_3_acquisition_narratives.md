# THREE ACQUISITION NARRATIVES — POSITION FOR YOUR BUYER TYPE

**Different buyers want different stories. Use the right narrative based on buyer type.**

---

## NARRATIVE 1: THE HIGH-GROWTH STORY (For Strategic Buyers & Growth PE)

**Hook:** "We're a high-growth company adding significant revenue to your platform"

### The Pitch
"[Your Company] is growing [X]% YoY with [NRR Y]% expansion. We're capturing [Z]% of a [TAM]M market that's still in early adoption. By acquiring us:

1. **You add $[X]M revenue immediately** (our ARR) + $[Y]M over next 3 years (based on growth trajectory)
2. **You enter new customer segments** we've already cracked (list: [segments])
3. **You get [N] customers** that would cost you $[estimated CAC × customers] to acquire independently
4. **Your growth story improves** — investors will value the combined entity higher

**Valuation implication:** Growth premium justifies [8–10x] multiple on our ARR."

### Buyer Profile
- Strategic buyer (competitor or adjacent)
- Growth PE firm
- Looks for revenue accretion

### Key Metrics to Emphasize
- YoY growth % (35%+ is premium)
- NRR >120% (expansion story)
- Customer momentum (net new customers)
- TAM size (market is big, still early)

---

## NARRATIVE 2: THE EBITDA-ACCRETIVE STORY (For Larger Corporates & PE)

**Hook:** "We're a profitable business generating cash; you get immediate margin expansion"

### The Pitch
"[Your Company] is approaching profitability with [X]% EBITDA margins and is capital-efficient. By acquiring us:

1. **Your combined EBITDA improves immediately** — we're generating [X]M EBITDA; adds [Y]bps to your margins
2. **We're not capital-intensive** — no heavy CapEx required; cash flow positive on Day 1
3. **Cost synergies are real:** Consolidated back-office (sales, finance, HR) saves **$[X]M annually**
4. **We de-risk your business** — we have stable, predictable revenue (vs. startup volatility)

**Valuation implication:** EBITDA multiple of [X] justifies our pricing. Conservative than growth-based multiple, but reflects cash generation."

### Buyer Profile
- Larger corporate buyer
- PE firm focused on profitability
- Wants margin-accretive add-ons

### Key Metrics to Emphasize
- EBITDA margin % (path to 30%+)
- FCF (free cash flow positive)
- CAC payback (<12 months)
- Cost synergies quantified ($[X]M savings)

---

## NARRATIVE 3: THE STRATEGIC FIT STORY (For Product Companies & Competitors)

**Hook:** "We fill a critical gap in your platform; your customers desperately need us"

### The Pitch
"[Your Company] solves a critical problem for your customers:

1. **Your customers ask for our functionality** — support tickets mentioning [feature/capability] are your top request
2. **We already serve [X]% of your customer base** (list which accounts use us)
3. **Integration is seamless** — we plug into your platform; customers see unified experience
4. **You prevent competitive disruption** — if you don't acquire us, [Competitor] will, and then you lose feature parity

**Valuation implication:** Strategic synergies justify premium pricing above pure financial multiples."

### Buyer Profile
- Product competitor or platform company
- Wants to plug feature gap
- Defensive acquisition (prevent competitor from getting us)

### Key Metrics to Emphasize
- Customer overlap (% of your customers also use us)
- Feature gap (what we provide that you don't)
- Integration potential (how easily we combine)
- Competitive risk (if we go to competitor, impact on you)

---

## HOW TO SELECT YOUR NARRATIVE

| Buyer Type | Use Narrative | Key Argument |
|---|---|---|
| **Strategic Competitor** | #3 (Strategic Fit) | "We fill your product gap; your customers want this" |
| **Growth PE** | #1 (High-Growth) | "We're fast-growing; add revenue + improve your story" |
| **Large Corporate** | #2 (EBITDA) | "We're profitable + we save you money; margin accretive" |
| **Customer's Customer** | #3 (Strategic Fit) | "We help you serve our customers better" |

---

## EXAMPLE: POSITIONING THE SAME COMPANY THREE WAYS

**Your Company:** $[10]M ARR, 40% growth, 25% EBITDA margin, serves [10]% of strategic buyer's installed base

### Narrative #1 (To Growth PE): High-Growth
"We're a [40]% growth business. In 3 years, we'll be $[24]M ARR. By acquiring us now at [8x ARR] = $[80]M, you're getting access to a growth engine that generates 3x+ on your investment."

### Narrative #2 (To Large Corp): EBITDA-Accretive
"We're already profitable at [25]% EBITDA margin. We contribute [$2.5]M EBITDA today. Plus, you consolidate our sales team with yours and save [$1]M/year. Total value creation: $[3.5]M/year = [14x] on your investment."

### Narrative #3 (To Competitor): Strategic Fit
"[10]% of your customer base uses us (that's [100] of your [1000] accounts). If you don't acquire us, a competitor will, and you lose feature parity. Our integration adds [key capability] your customers demand; acquisition justifies [$80]M price."

---

## MIXING NARRATIVES

**Pro tip:** You can blend narratives in the same conversation:

> "[Your Company] is both high-growth AND profitable. We're adding $[X]M revenue to your platform (growth story) while generating $[Y]M EBITDA (margin story). Your customers overlap with ours (strategic fit). This makes us a rare acquisition: growth + profitability + strategic value."

---

## RED FLAGS IN YOUR NARRATIVE

🚩 **If growth story is weak:** Don't lead with growth. Lead with profitability/strategic fit instead.
🚩 **If margins are low:** Don't lead with EBITDA. Lead with growth/strategy.
🚩 **If strategic fit is unclear:** Don't claim fit; focus on financial returns.

## NEXT STEPS

1. **Pick your buyer type** (strategic vs PE vs corporate)
2. **Choose the matching narrative** (high-growth, EBITDA, strategic)
3. **Build data** to support your narrative (comps, metrics, customer examples)
4. **Practice your pitch** in 2 minutes (concise, confident)
5. **Use narrative consistently** in LOI, discussions, negotiations

# Playbook — Multiples Acquéreurs en Parallèle

**Strategy**: Running a competitive auction drives price & terms. Here's how.

---

## PHASE 1: TARGETING (WEEK 1)

**Goal**: Identify 8–12 potential buyers across 3 tiers.

| Tier | Type | # Target | Strategy |
|------|------|----------|----------|
| **Tier 1 (Strategic)** | Large competitor / platform | 2–3 | Perfect synergy; willing to overpay |
| **Tier 2 (Strategics)** | Adjacent strategics | 3–4 | Good synergy; solid offers expected |
| **Tier 3 (Financial)** | PE funds / growth equity | 3–4 | Financial return focus; disciplined |

**Why 3 tiers?** If Tier 1 lowballs, you have Tier 2/3 backups.

---

## PHASE 2: SIMULTANEOUS OUTREACH (WEEK 1–2)

**Send initial call script to all 8–12 within 48–72 hours** (creates sense that others are interested).

**Template email:**

```
Hi [Buyer],

We're exploring strategic options and think you could be an ideal partner.
[Specific synergy]. 

A few other acquirers are also evaluating us this week.

I'd love to schedule 15 min to introduce the opportunity. 
Available [TIME 1] or [TIME 2]?

Best, [Founder]
```

**Key**: "A few other acquirers are evaluating" = FOMO. Creates urgency without saying it explicitly.

---

## PHASE 3: ORGANIZING INTO COHORTS (WEEK 2–3)

**Separate buyers into cohorts to manage momentum:**

**Cohort A** (Fast-track): Serious buyers who moved quickest
- Schedule deep-dive calls
- Provide complete data room access
- Set specific LOI target date

**Cohort B** (Parallel track): Other strong candidates
- Standard diligence track
- Hold data room access until Cohort A results in
- Bring in if Cohort A stalls

**Cohort C** (Reserve): Secondary options
- Light-touch engagement
- No data room access yet
- Reactivate only if needed

---

## PHASE 4: TIMELINE DISCIPLINE (WEEK 3–8)

**Week 3**: Cohort A deep-dive calls completed
**Week 4**: LOI prep (Cohort A buyers draft terms)
**Week 5**: LOI feedback rounds (1–2 iterations)
**Week 6**: LOI signed (fastest Cohort A buyer)
**Week 7**: Initiate Cohort B intensive engagement (backup)
**Week 8**: Cohort B provides LOIs (competitive bids)

**Why this timeline?** If Cohort A LOI stalls in diligence, you move Cohort B into active negotiation with leverage ("Cohort B is offering $[X]M").

---

## PHASE 5: CREATING COMPETITIVE TENSION

### When Buyer Asks, "Are You Talking to Others?"

**Response**:
```
"Yes, we are exploring with [#] acquirers. Each brings different 
strengths to the table.

We're trying to find the best fit—not just the highest price.

You should compete on:
1. [Synergy A] where you have advantage
2. [Timeline/integration approach]
3. [Product fit/vision alignment]

What's most important to you in this deal?"
```

### When One Buyer Asks for Exclusivity

**Respond**:
```
"We're happy to move fast with you, but we can't go exclusive yet. 
Here's what we can do:

- Prioritize your diligence (you get first access to team, data room)
- LOI by [DATE] (lock in terms, then you have exclusive diligence period)
- Agree on key terms within [X] days

Does that work for your timeline?"
```

---

## PHASE 6: PRICE SIGNALING

### Email #1 (Week 4): Soft Signal

```
Hi [Buyer 1],

Quick update: the feedback from our conversations has been really positive.
A few buyers are asking about earnout structures and retention packages.

I'm thinking: $[X]M base + $[Y]M earnout on [metric] over 12 months.

Does that ballpark feel reasonable to you?

Best, [Founder]
```

**Effect**: Signals to Buyer 1 that others are interested in similar price points.

### Email #2 (Week 5): Competitive Signal

```
Subject: [Company] — update on buyer interest

Hi [Buyer 1],

A quick heads-up: we're getting solid interest from [Buyer 2] 
(similar strategic fit to you).

They've indicated interest at $[X]M base. We're still evaluating 
whether their integration plan aligns with our vision.

Where do you stand? I want to make sure you have a clear picture 
of what we're exploring.

Best, [Founder]
```

**Effect**: Buyer 1 now knows they're competing and needs to sharpen offer.

---

## PHASE 7: NEGOTIATING THE CLOSING

**Key principle:** Always have a backup.

### Scenario: Buyer 1 Lowballs

```
Buyer 1 offers: $15M
You wanted: $20M

Response:
"Buyer 2 is at $18M. That feels more aligned with our metrics and synergies.

Can you match that, or are you going to step back?"
```

**This works because Buyer 1 knows you have Buyer 2.**

### Scenario: Buyer 1 Wants to Renegotiate After LOI

```
Buyer 1: "We want to reduce price to $16M based on diligence."
You: "That's not on the table. Buyer 2 confirmed their $18M offer 
is still valid. Let's move forward at the agreed $17M, or we'll 
engage with Buyer 2."
```

---

## PHASE 8: FINAL DECISION

**When you have 2+ LOI offers**, use **Buyer Scoring Matrix** to decide:

| Criterion | Buyer 1 | Buyer 2 |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| Price | $17M | $18M |
| Strategic fit | 4 | 3 |
| Earnout terms | Fair | Aggressive |
| Founder retention | Good | OK |
| **Score** | **3.4** | **3.3** |

**Conclusion**: Buyer 1 at $17M > Buyer 2 at $18M (holistically).

---

## ANTI-PATTERNS (Don't Do This)

❌ **Share LOI from Buyer A with Buyer B** (breaches confidentiality; kills deal flow)  
❌ **Artificially inflate buyer count** ("5 buyers interested" when only 2 real)  
❌ **Move all buyers to LOI stage simultaneously** (impossible to manage)  
❌ **Let negotiations drag >8 weeks** (buyers lose interest; energy fades)  
❌ **Accept LOI, then shop for better price** (break trust; deal falls apart)

---

## TIMELINE SUMMARY

```
Week 1: Outreach to 8–12 buyers
Week 2: Deep-dive calls with Tier 1 (Cohort A)
Week 3: Cohort A data room opens
Week 4: Cohort A drafts LOI
Week 5: LOI negotiation / Cohort B engagement
Week 6: LOI signed (Cohort A winner)
Week 7: Cohort B presented competitive bid (if needed)
Week 8: Close decision
```

---

## CHECKPOINTS (When to Walk Away from a Buyer)

| Warning Sign | Action |
|--------------|--------|
| **No response to 2 emails** | Move to Cohort B |
| **Delays diligence >3 weeks** | Red flag; slow decision-making |
| **Asks for 30%+ price cut mid-diligence** | Likely bad faith; consider walking |
| **Won't commit to timeline** | Not serious; deactivate |
| **Demands exclusivity but moves slowly** | Reject; lock up your optionality |
